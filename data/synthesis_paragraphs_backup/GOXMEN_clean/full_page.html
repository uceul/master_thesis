Polyhedron

Volume 85, 8 January 2015, Pages 445-449

Synthesis, structure and topological analysis of a novel three dimensional Co(II) coordination polymer constructed by an unsymmetrical ligand

Author links open overlay panelRashmi A.AgarwalParimal K.Bharadwaj
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.poly.2014.09.002Get rights and content

Abstract

Through in situ basic hydrolysis of cyanide functional group of 3-(1H-benzo[d]imidazol-1-yl)-5-(1H-imidazol-1-yl)benzonitrile a new three dimensional porous coordination polymer {[Co2(BIIBA)3]·(NO3)·5H2O}n (1) (BIIBA = 3-(1H-benzo[d]imidazol-1-yl)-5-(1H-imidazol-1-yl)benzoate) have been isolated under hydrothermal conditions and characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction. Topological analysis of 1 revealed a (3,3,4,5)-connected 4-nodal unprecedented pkb8 net with point symbol {4.62}2{42.66.82}{63}{65.8}.

Graphical abstract

A novel 2-fold interpenetrated three dimensional coordination polymer of Co(II) have been synthesized by employing 3-(1H-benzo[d]imidazol-1-yl)-5-(1H-imidazol-1-yl)benzonitrile (BIIBN) under hydrothermal conditions. This complex has (3,3,4,5)-connected 4-nodal pkb8 net with point symbol {4.62}2{42.66.82}{63}{65.8}.

1. Introduction

Owing to their intriguing molecular topologies, metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) have recently attracted a lot of attention [1]. Different types of coordination networks have been produced with novel topologies by intelligent design of the organic spacers and judicious choice of the central metal atoms. In the past decade because of unique advantages in enhancing stability, gas sorption, specific surface area and molecular dynamics as well as their general structural aesthetics [2] considerable effort has been invested in interpenetrating coordination polymers [3]. It reveals the entanglement between a limited numbers of motifs with identical topology, in which each motif is, interweaved with all the other ones. A lot of work has been done on the rational design of multidimensional infinite supramolecular architectures under which the construction of transition metal-carboxylate polymers is a successful paradigm [4], [5], [6] by controlling the favorable geometry of ligands and metals. However the governing principles in these systems are less ascertained and remain elusive due to great diversity of the resulting structures.

Herein we report the synthesis of new coordination polymer {[Co2(BIIBA)3]·(NO3)·5H2O}n (1) by in situ hydrolysis of unsymmetrical ligand 3-(1H-benzo[d]imidazol-1-yl)-5-(1H-imidazol-1-yl)benzonitrile (BIIBN) at high temperature, predicting new topological structure.

2. Experimental

Reagent grade 3,5-difluorobenzonitrile, and metal salt Co(NO3)2·6H2O were acquired from Aldrich and used as received. All solvents, imidazole, benzimidazole and K2CO3 were procured from S.D. Fine Chemicals, India. Solvents were purified prior to use following standard procedures.

2.1. Physical measurements

Infrared spectra were obtained (KBr disk, 400–4000 cm−1) using a Perkin-Elmer model 1320 spectrometer. 1H NMR and 13C NMR spectra were recorded using a JEOL-ECX 500 FT (500 MHz and 125 MHz, respectively) instrument in CDCl3 with Me4Si as the internal standard. ESI mass spectra were recorded using a WATERS Q-TOF premier mass spectrometer. Thermograms were recorded on a Mettler Toledo (heating rate of 10 °C/min) TGA instrument. Microanalyses for the compounds were obtained using a CE-440 elemental analyzer (Exeter Analytical Inc.). Powder X-ray diffraction (Cu Kα radiation, scan rate 3°/min, 293 K) patterns were obtained on a Bruker D8 Advance Series 2 powder X-ray diffractometer.

2.2. Synthesis of ligand (BIIBN)

Ligand have been synthesized by following procedure: a mixture of benzimidazole (1.7 g, 14.4 mmol), imidazole (1 g, 14.4 mmol), 3,5-difluorobenzonitrile (2 g, 14.4 mmol) and K2CO3 (6 g, 43.2 mmol) in 10 mL of dry DMF was heated at 80 °C for 24 h with constant stirring. After cooling to room temperature, the mixture was poured slowly into ice-cold water (100 mL) with constant stirring to obtain a white solid that was collected by filtration and air-dried. The ligand BIIBN is separated by column chromatography with 80% ethyl acetate as the eluent (Scheme 1). Yield: 1.0 g, (35%). IR (cm−1, KBr pellet): 2234(w), 1730(s), 1603(s), 1498(s), 1309(s), 1271(s), 733(s), 1233(s) (Fig. S1). 1H NMR (CDCl3, 500 MHz) δ (ppm): 8.16(m, 2H; HAr), 7.99(d, 1H; HAr), 7.90(m, 1H; HAr), 7.84(m, 1H; HAr), 7.77(s, 1H; HAr), 7.55(m, 1H; HAr), 7.39(m, 3H; HAr), 7.29(d, 1H, HAr) (Fig. S2). 13C NMR (CDCl3) δ (ppm): 141.80, 141.41, 139.15, 135.56, 135.42, 132.08, 131.54, 123.28, 121.45, 121.48, 120.30, 118.06, 117.83, 110.22, and 109.81 (Fig. S3). ESI-MS: m/z (100%) [M+1]+ 286; calculated 285 (Fig. S4); Anal. Calc. for C17H11N5: C, 71.57; H, 3.86; N, 24.56. Found: C, 71.6; H, 3.95; N, 24.60%.

2.3. Synthesis of complex 1 {[Co2(BIIBA)3]·(NO3)·5H2O}n

A mixture of Co(NO3)2·6H2O (0.082 g, 0.28 mmol), BIIBN (0.04 g, 0.14 mmol), and dilute sodium hydroxide (0.1 mL) in H2O (6 mL) was placed in a Teflon lined stainless steel autoclave and heated at 180 °C for 1 day under autogenous pressure. Then it was allowed to cool to room temperature at the rate of 1 °C/min to afford pink colored needle-shaped crystals. Yield: ∼40%. Anal. Calc. for C51H43N13O14Co2: C, 51.92; H, 3.67; N, 15.43. Found: C, 54.8; H, 3.88; N, 15.14%. IR (cm−1, KBr pellet): 3399(s), 3108(s), 1562(s), 1503(s), 1422(s), 1384(s), 1313(s), 1239(s), 1068(s), 743(s) (Fig. S5).

In the presence of dilute base (NaOH), CN– group of BIIBN hydrolyzed to COO– group of BIIBA and coordination of the ligand (BIIBA) to the Co(II) metal takes place by which an interesting structure is acquired.

2.4. X-ray determination of complex 1

Single crystal X-ray data on complex 1 were collected at 100 K on a Bruker SMART APEX CCD diffractometer using graphite-monochromated Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) as described earlier [7]. Due to the disorder contents of the pores in the framework, identification of guest (solvent and nitrate anion) entities within the voids was impossible, so squeeze refinement have been performed using platon [8] that shows one NO3 anion and five lattice water molecules per formula weight. Due to disordered structure several DFIX commands were given to fix bond distances between C–C in benzimidazole part of ligand. Atoms C1, C3, C13, C14, C16, C20, C23, C32, C33, C40, C43, C44, C46, N2 and N8 were refined isotropically. Contributions of all atoms of the guest molecules have been incorporated in both the empirical formula and formula weight in Table 1. The crystal and refinement data are collected in Table 1 while selected bond lengths and bond angles are given in Table S1.

3. Results and discussion

Through basic hydrolysis of asymmetric ligand BIIBN, under hydrothermal condition it reacts with Co(II) salt, by which complex 1 have been synthesized. In IR spectrum, this ligand shows the characteristic nitrile stretching at ∼2230 cm−1. This peak is absent in the coordination polymer indicating hydrolysis of the cyanide group. The strong peaks in the region, 1350–1550 cm−1 are due to coordinated carboxylate [9]. While peaks in the region 1305–1385 cm−1 are corresponding to free NO3− anions and IR stretching at 3399 cm−1 revealing presence of water molecules (Fig. S5).

3.1. X-ray structural description of complex 1

X-ray crystallography reveals that complex 1 crystallizes in the monoclinic space group Cc. The asymmetric unit consists of two Co(II) ions with different coordination environment, three BIIBA, one nitrate anion and five lattice water molecules. The angles N(4)−Co(1)−N(5) and N(5)−Co(1)−O(1) are 177.6(7)° and 95.0(5)° on the other hand N(8)−Co(2)−O(6) and N(10)−Co(2)−N(8) angles are 156.6(5)° and 94.9(3)°. These bond angles predict that both metal centers lie in a distorted octahedral coordination sphere, where Co1 ion is bonded through two nitrogen atoms of imidazole, two nitrogen atoms of benzimidazole (Co1–N = 2.058(15)–2.218(13) Å) and one carboxylate group (Co1–O = 2.172(12)–2.228(11) Å), from five different BIIBA ligand units. While Co2 ion is coordinated with one nitrogen atom of imidazole, one nitrogen atom of benzimidazole (Co2–N = 2.030(12)–2.083(13) Å) and two carboxylate groups (Co2–O = 2.079(15)–2.219(14) Å), from four different BIIBA as shown in Fig. 1. Bond distances involving metal ions are not of high accuracy due to disorder present in the structure [10].

Co1 ions are connected with each other by two BIIBA ligands as shown in Fig. 2, extending 1D metal chain along c direction with Co1⋯Co1 distance 10.40 Å. While Co2 ions are bridged together, by one BIIBA ligand, leading to zigzag chain in crystallographic c axis, as shown in the Fig. 3, where two metal ions are also separated by a distance of 10.40 Å.

These 1D chains are further connected to each other by carboxylate group of the ligand, generating the overall three dimensional structure (Fig. 4). Distance between Co1 and Co2 is 10.68 and 11.42 Å alternatively. Viewed along c axis, three dimensional hexagonal networks are present consisting of large voids. But mutual interpenetration of two independent hexagonal networks results in a 2-fold interpenetrated structure leading to small size dumbbell shaped cavities (Fig. 5). The solvent accessible volume is ∼18% of the unit cell volume calculated from the crystal structure using the platon program [8].

3.2. Topological analysis

Topologically [11] 1 can be simplified as novel (3,3,4,5)-connected 4-nodal pkb8 net, by considering Co1 ions as 5-connected nodes, Co2 ions as 4-connected nodes and BIIBA ligand as 3-connected nodes in two different ways (Fig. 6). This novel net from our laboratory presents a point symbol {4.62}2{42.66.82}{63}{65.8}.

3.3. Thermogravimetric analysis

High thermal stability of complex 1 is observed in thermograms. Weight loss of 7.1% is found in the temperature range 30–300 °C, which corresponds to loss of five lattice water molecules (calcd. 7.6%). Framework is stable up to 350 °C beyond this temperature ligand is decomposed (Fig. S6).

Further to confirm the stability of this 3D framework PXRD measurements have been taken, that shows PXRD pattern at 350 °C temperature matches well with the as-synthesized phase suggesting that framework is quite rigid (Fig. S7).

4. Conclusion

In summary, we have successfully synthesized a new three-dimensional 2-fold interpenetrated coordination polymer of Co(II) by utilizing unsymmetrical ligand. This complex has novel topology and named pkb-8.
