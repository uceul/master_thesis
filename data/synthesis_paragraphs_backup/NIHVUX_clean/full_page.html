A 3d–4f heterometallic 3D POMOF based on lacunary Dawson polyoxometalates†

A novel compound, Na2[Ce4(H2O)22(dpdo)5](Mn2HP2W15O56)2·8H2O (1) (dpdo = 4,4′-bipyridine-N,N′-dioxide) with 3d–4f heterometallic 3D POMOF that can be described as a self-penetrating (4,4,6)-connected net topology, has been isolated via a stepwise method, and its magnetic and electrocatalytic properties have been investigated.

Introduction

The design and synthesis of inorganic–organic hybrids, for example, the construction of novel metal–organic frameworks (MOFs), have attracted considerable attention, because of their diverse topologies and potential applications in storage and separation of chemical matter.1 Polyoxometalates (POMs),2 as a large family of metal–oxygen clusters, have been extensively studied due not only to their unmatched structural types and attractive properties such as a controllable shape, size, high-negative charge, and nucleophilic oxygen-enriched surface but also various potential applications in catalysis, medicine and materials science.3 Recently, a new class of hybrid materials has emerged by introducing POMs into MOF systems, so-called POMOFs.4,5 The POMOFs incorporate two important research fields (POMs and MOFs) and open up new possibilities in the pursuit of multifunctional materials that not only exhibit anticipated functional properties, for example, the improved surface area, dispersion degree and orientation of POMs, resulting in enhanced performance such as catalytic efficiency,6 but also reveal aesthetic topological structures.7

On the other hand, the 3d–4f heterometallic compounds8 have received considerable attention due to their interesting physical properties, such as conductivity and magnetic ordering that result from two distinct metal centers connected by suitable linkers. Introducing the 3d–4f heterometals into the POMOF system will be significant to enrich the potential properties of POMOFs, as generally there exists a correlation between their complexity of structure and functionality. Nevertheless, the POMOFs are usually with monometallic systems,5 and the studies of POM-based d–f heterometallic compounds are an emerging field as recently well documented by Reinoso.9 Furthermore, most of the POM-based 3d–4f heterometallic compounds are 0D aggregates rather than 3d–4f heterometallic 3D POMOFs.10 Thus, the construction of new 3d–4f heterometallic 3D POMOFs is still an intriguing challenge.

In general, there are two synthetic strategies to approach 3d–4f heterometallic POMOFs, namely a one-pot procedure and stepwise assembly. The one-pot procedure, as shown in Scheme S1,† is an in situ reaction of simple starting materials (d and f cations, POMs and organic ligands), which is facile and has been extensively employed. However, it was difficult to control and predict the nuclearity and dimensionality of the resultant structures owing to the low stereochemical preferences as well as the strong competitive reaction between the 3d and 4f metals. To overcome these synthetic drawbacks, the stepwise assembly depending on pre-constructed precursors is promising. The stepwise assembly contains two ways: (I) reaction of d–f heterometal precursors and other synthons, (II) reaction of preformed d or f substituted/sandwich clusters and other synthons. Nevertheless, the stepwise assembly is far less developed than the one-pot procedure up to now. Since the pioneering work by Fang and Kögerler, only three novel POM-based 3d–4f heterometallic compounds with 0D aggregate structures have been isolated by using method I11 (Scheme S2†). And method II has not been used for the synthesis of any POM-based 3d–4f heterometallic compounds, not to mention the 3d–4f heterometallic 3D POMOFs, which inspires us and opens up new opportunities. In addition, to our knowledge, all of the previously reported POM-based 3d–4f heterometallic compounds are only constructed by small organic ligands, such as acetic acid and ethylenediamine.9 These small organic ligands are unfavorable to form 3D expansion structures. By introducing some ligands with long and rigid “spacers” into the reaction systems, it may be a good strategy to form the 3d–4f heterometallic POMOFs with ease. The 4,4′-bipyridine-N,N′-dioxide (dpdo) ligand (Chart S1†) possesses a long and rigid “bipyridine spacer” as well as terminal oxygen atoms in nitrogen heterocycle systems. The terminal oxygen atoms are in favor of coordinating to 4f cations, which effectively reduces the competitive reaction from 3d cations, as the 3d cations prefer to bind to soft N atoms, whereas the 4f cations as hard acceptors have a particular affinity to oxygen donors according to the hard–soft acid base theory.12

In this context, by choosing three lacunary Dawson-type POMs Na12[α-P2W15O56]·24H2O (P2W15), CeIII ions, MnII ions, dpdo ligands, a novel 3d–4f heterometallic 3D POMOF compound Na2[Ce4(H2O)22(dpdo)5](Mn2HP2W15O56)2·8H2O (1) has been isolated via stepwise method II (Scheme 1). To the best of our knowledge, 1 not only represents the first 3d–4f heterometallic 3D POMOF compound based on Dawson POMs13 but also provides a feasible route for us to approach 3d–4f heterometallic 3D POMOFs by using a step-by-step assembly process.

Experimental section

Materials and physical methods

The dpdo ligand was synthesized according to the literature methods.14 All other reagents were purchased commercially and were used without further purification. Elemental analyses of C, H and N were performed on a Perkin-Elmer 2400 CHN Elemental Analyzer and that of Ce, Mn and W were carried out with a Leaman inductively coupled plasma (ICP) spectrometer. IR spectra on KBr pellets were recorded on a Nicolet 170SX FT-IR spectrophotometer in the range 400–4000 cm−1. TG analyses were performed with a Perkin-Elmer TGA7 instrument in a N2 atmosphere at a heating rate of 10 °C min−1. The X-ray powder diffraction (XRPD) patterns were recorded on a Siemens D5005 diffractometer with Cu-Kα (λ = 1.5418 Å) radiation. Magnetic susceptibility measurements were carried out in the temperature range 2–300 K with an applied magnetic field of 0.5 T on polycrystalline samples of compound 1 with a Quantum Design MPMS-XL-5 SQUID susceptometer. The isothermal magnetizations were performed on the same samples at 2 K with magnetic fields up to 5 T. The susceptibility data were corrected for the sample holders previously measured using the same conditions and for the diamagnetic contributions of the salt as deduced by using Pascal's constant tables (χdia = −1912.15 × 10−6 and −1873.15 × 10−6 emu mol−1).15

Synthesis of Na2[Ce4(H2O)22(dpdo)5](Mn2HP2W15O56)2·8H2O (1)

Step 1: the precursor Na16[Mn4(H2O)2(P2W15O56)2]·53H2O cluster was prepared via a conventional method that has been previously reported in the literature.16 Step 2: a mixture of Na16[Mn4(H2O)2(P2W15O56)2]·53H2O (0.4839 g), dpdo (0.16 g), Ce(NO3)3·6H2O (0.15 g) and H2O (18 mL) was stirred and its pH value was ca. 4.0. The resulting suspension was sealed in a 30 mL Teflon lined reactor and kept at 160 °C for 4 days. After cooling the autoclave to room temperature, dark orange block crystals were separated, washed with water, and air-dried (Yield: 40% based on W). Calcd for Na2[Ce4(H2O)22(dpdo)5](Mn2HP2W15O56)2·8H2O (9740.94): C 6.17, H 1.06, N 1.44, Na 0.47, Mn 2.26, P 1.27, W 56.62, Ce 5.75; found: C 6.10, H 1.13, N 1.35, Na 0.40, Mn 2.18, P 1.36, W 56.72, Ce 5.83%.

X-ray crystallographic study

Single-crystal X-ray diffraction data collection of compound 1 was performed using a Bruker Smart Apex CCD diffractometer with Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at 293 K. Multi-scan absorption corrections were applied. The structure was solved by the directed methods and refined by full-matrix least-squares on F2 using the SHELXTL crystallographic software package.17 The water molecules were highly disordered and could not be modeled properly, thus the SQUEEZE routine of PLATON was applied to remove the contributions due to scattering from the solvent molecules. The reported refinements are of the guest-free structures using the *.hkp files produced by using the SQUEEZE routine. The amount of water came from the EA and TG experiments. Results and discussion

Syntheses

In fact, during synthesis of the 3d–4f heterometallic POMOF compounds, it was difficult to control and predict the nuclearity and dimensionality of the resultant structures owing to the low stereochemical preferences as well as the strong competitive reaction between the 3d and 4f metals. To overcome these synthetic drawbacks, the stepwise assembly depending on pre-constructed precursors is promising. Thus, the choice of appropriate precursors is a key factor to isolate 3d–4f heterometallic POMOF compounds. The sandwich-type polyoxoanion [Mn4(P2W15O56)2]16− (abbreviated to Mn4(P2W15)2) is a good candidate as precursor based on the following considerations: (i) the Mn4(P2W15)2 is available, which can be obtained by a simple reported method18 (reaction of Mn2+ cations and P2W1512− anion in suitable solution). (ii) The Mn4(P2W15)2 polyoxoanion possesses 98 potential coordination oxygen atoms (32 terminal O atoms and 66 bridge O atoms), and higher charge density than those of the sandwich-type Keggin polyoxoanions, which increase the coordination tendency with f metals and thus approach the novel 3d–4f heterometallic POMOFs with ease.

Description of the crystal structures

Single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis reveals that 1 crystallizes in the triclinic space group P. Compound 1 consists of a sandwich precursor of formula [Mn4(HP2W15O56)2]14−, two Na+ ions, two crystallographically independent Ce3+ cations (Ce1 and Ce2), and three dpdo ligands (L1, L2 and L3). In the [Mn4(HP2W15O56)2]14− unit, both of the two α-[P2W15O52]12− units share seven oxygen atoms with the same central set of four edge-sharing MnO6 octahedra, respectively. The four Mn atoms lie in the same plane and form a centrosymmetric regular rhomb-like cluster (Fig. S1†). Both of the Ce3+ cations are coordinated to nine oxygen atoms in a monocapped square-antiprism geometry. The Ce1 is coordinated by three oxygen atoms from three different dpdo ligands, one oxygen atoms from a [Mn4(HP2W15O56)2]14− anion and five oxygen atoms from five water molecules. The Ce2 is coordinated by two oxygen atoms from two different dpdo ligands, two oxygen atoms from two [Mn4(HP2W15O56)2]14− anions and five oxygen atoms from five water molecules (Fig. S2†). The bond lengths around the Ce atoms are in the range of 2.382(15)–2.647(19) Å, and the O–Ce–O angles are in the range of 63.3(6)–154.2(7)°, which are within the normal ranges for nona-coordinate cerium system.19

One structural feature for 1 is the unusual 3d–4f heterometallic 3D POMOFs, which is described in detail as follows: firstly, two Ce1 and two Ce2 link with four dpdo ligands (two L1 and two L2) to form a 44-membered macrocycle with the each edges of 13.20 Å, and twisting angles of 94.03° and 142.46°, indicating a distorted square geometry. The macrocycles are connected with each other by L3 ligands via Ce–O bonds to result in a 1D zigzag chain (Fig. S3,† left). Each of the [Mn4(HP2W15O56)2]14− anions is incorporated into the macrocycles of these 1D chains (Fig. S4†) and further connects another five macrocycles from the neighboring chains to generate a complicated 3D framework (Fig. S3†). To the best of our knowledge, this framework represents the first 3d–4f heterometallic 3D POMOFs assembly from lacunary Dawson clusters.

Another fascinating structural feature of 1 is its novel self-penetrating (4,4,6)-connected topological structure. The topological analysis of the structure has been performed by considering each [Mn4(HP2W15O56)2]14− unit as a six-connected node (Fig. 1a), each Ce cation as a four-connected node (Fig. 1b), which can be symbolized as a (4,4,6)-connected network with (4 62 8 102)(42 6 8 102)(42 62 89 102) topology (the first symbol for Ce1; the second for Ce2 and the third for [Mn4(HP2W15O56)2]14−). After further investigation of the structure, it is clear to see that 1 is a self-penetrating structure (Fig. 1c and Fig. S5†). In fact, a few self-penetrating nets have been observed in MOFs,20 and the self-penetrating POMOFs are even rarer. To date, only three examples21 showing the self-penetrating topological structure, to the best of our knowledge, have been characterized, and all of them are constructed from the octamolybdate or Keggin clusters and copper complexes. Therefore, compound 1 represents the first exceptional case assembled from Dawson clusters and 3d–4f heterometals.


Fig. 1 Schematic view of the topological structure of 1: (a and b) the coordination relation between the [Mn4(HP2W15O56)2]14− cluster and macrocycle, (c) the overall 3D self-penetrating (4,4,6)-connected net.

Analyses of IR, PXRD, TG and BVS

IR spectra. In the IR spectrum of 1 (Fig. S6†), absorption peaks at 933, 838, and 776 cm−1 are respectively attributed to ν(WOt), νas(W–Ob–W) and νas(W–Oc–W) vibrations of P2W15 (Ot = terminal oxygen, Ob = bridged oxygen of two octahedral sharing a corner, Oc = bridged oxygen of two octahedral sharing an edge).22 The characteristic peaks in the 508–1629 region are indicative of the vibrations of the dpdo molecules and the peak at 3417 cm−1 can be assigned to the vibrations of water molecules.
Powder X-ray diffractograms. The PXRD patterns for 1 are presented in Fig. S7.† The diffraction peaks of both simulated and experimental patterns match well, indicating that the phase purities of 1 are good.
Thermogravimetric analyses. The TG curve of 1, which was performed in air to explore its thermal stability, exhibits two weight loss steps (Fig. S8†). The first weight loss step of 5.76% (calc. 5.55%) below 200 °C corresponds to the loss of water molecules. The second weight loss step of 13.62% (calc. 13.45%) occurred in the range of 200–500 °C is ascribed to the decomposition of organic dpdo molecules and collapse of Mn4(P2W15)2 clusters. The total weight loss is 19.38%, consistent with the calculated value of 19.00% and also supporting the chemical composition of 1.

In 1, all W, Ce and Mn atoms are in the +VI, +III and +II oxidation state, respectively, by bond valence sum (BVS) calculations23 (see Table S1†). Since 1 was isolated from acidic aqueous solution, one proton is attached to the P2W15 cluster to compensate charge balance.

Cyclic voltammetry (CV)

Compound 1 is insoluble in water and common organic solvents. Thus, the bulk-modified carbon paste electrode (CPE) becomes the optimal choice to study the electrochemical properties, which is inexpensive, easy to prepare and handle.24

The cyclic voltammetric behavior for 1-CPE has been investigated in 0.5 M H2SO4–Na2SO4 buffer solution (pH = 2.5). As shown in Fig. S9,† it can be seen that there are three pairs of reversible redox peaks (III–III′, IV–IV′ and V–V′) appeared in the negative potential range, and the mean peak potentials E1/2 = (Eap + Ecp)/2 were −0.065, −0.45 and −0.73 V, which can be ascribed to two-electron redox processes of W6+/W5+.25 The redox peaks II–II′ correspond to the one-electron redox process of Mn(II)/Mn(III).26 In addition, there exists one irreversible anodic peak (I) at +0.93 V, which is attributed to a one-electron redox process of Ce(III)/Ce(IV).27

The electrocatalytic properties of 1-CPE have also been investigated. Notably, the electrochemical studies have shown that 1-CPE has bifunctional electrocatalytic activities towards not only reduction of normal inorganic molecules – iodate (KIO3) ascribed to W-centers in P2W15, – but also oxidation of biological molecules – ascorbic acid (AA) and dopamine (DA) ascribed to Ce and/or Mn centers in 1. Fig. 2 shows the electrocatalytic activity of 1-CPE towards H2O2 and KIO3 in 0.5 M H2SO4–Na2SO4 buffer solution (pH = 2.58). With addition of H2O2, these peaks show no change (Fig. 2, left), and with addition of KIO3, the reduction peak currents for IV and V increase gradually while the corresponding oxidation peak currents decrease. The nearly equal current steps for each addition of IO3− demonstrate stable and efficient electrocatalytic activity of 1-CPE (Fig. 2, right) As shown in Fig. 3, the 1-CPE also exhibits a remarkable electrocatalytic effect toward the oxidation of ascorbic acid (AA) and dopamine (DA). With addition of AA and DA, the anodic peak current of the Mn(II) and Ce(III) wave substantially increases, suggesting that 1 can electrically catalyze the oxidation of AA and DA.

The CAT (catalytic efficiency) of 1-CPE can be calculated by using the CAT formula.28 The CAT of 1-CPE towards 2.0 mM IO3−, AA and DA were calculated to be 197.50%, 154.8% and 176.85% respectively, which suggests that 1 has potential applications in the detection of IO3−, AA and DA (Fig. 4). For comparison, the stability experiment for 1-CPE has been investigated scanning for 40 cycles in 0.5 M H2SO4–Na2SO4 solution (pH = 2.5). As shown in Fig. S10,† it can be seen that the electrode exhibits almost no loss in the current signal after 40 cycles, which suggests that the catalyst of 1-CPE has a high stability.


Fig. 4 Chart of CAT of 1-CPE vs. concentration of H2O2, IO3−, AA and DA.

For comparison, the electrocatalytic properties of Na12[α-P2W15O56]·24H2O and Na16[Mn4(H2O)2(P2W15O56)2]·53H2O have been performed. The P2W15-CPE only has an electrocatalytic reduction activity towards KIO3 and the Na16[Mn4(H2O)2(P2W15O56)2]·53H2O-CPE has no electrocatalytic activity towards AA, DA, H2O2 and KIO3. The results show that compound 1 possess a much higher electrocatalytic activity than Na12[α-P2W15O56]·24H2O and Na16[Mn4(H2O)2(P2W15O56)2]·53H2O) (please see details in ESI, page S6†).

Magnetic properties

The magnetic susceptibilities (χ) of compound 1 was measured in the temperature range of 2.0–300 K at a 0.1 T magnetic field (Fig. 5). In the χmT vs. T plots of 1, the T value slowly decreases from 300 K to 50 K, and then rapidly drops to a value of 4.28 cm3 K mol−1 revealing antiferromagnetic behavior in 1. The χmT product of 24.22 cm3 K mol−1 at 300 K is a little higher than the expected value of 20.71 cm3 K mol−1 for four Mn(II) ions (g = 2.0, S = 5/2) and four Ce(III) ions (g = 6/7, J = 5/2) non-interacting ions.29 The χm−1versus T plot (Fig. S11†) is fitted to the Curie–Weiss law [χm = C/(T − Θ)] in the whole temperature range with C = 25 cm3 K mol−1 and Θ = −7.00 K. The negative Weiss temperature suggests the presence of antiferromagnetic interactions over the whole crystal structure. More detailed magnetic research is under way.
Fig. 5 Plot of χm and χmT vs. T for 1.

Conclusions

In summary, a new compound has been synthesized by using a step-by-step assembly process, which not only possesses a unique self-penetrating heterometallic 3D POMOF structure with bifunctional electrocatalytic activities, but also provides a feasible route to effectively restrain the competitive reaction between the 3d and 4f metals and thus to approach the novel 3d–4f heterometallic POMOFs with ease. With hindsight, we can imagine that other new compounds could be prepared by this synthetic strategy. Further work in this field is underway in our laboratory.
