Abstract

Two novel three-dimensional vanadium phosphites |(C10H10N2)| V2IVO2(HPO3)2(H2PO3)2 1 and |(C4H16N3)| V2IVVIIIO2F2(HPO3)4 2 have been synthesized hydrothermally in the presence of structure-directing agents. Single-crystal structural analysis reveals that compound 1 has a three-dimensional open-framework with two different types of intersecting channels (monoclinic, P21/n, a = 6.3541(14) Ã…, b = 10.460(2) Ã…, c = 14.769(3) Ã…, Î² = 90.412(5)o, V = 981.6(4) Ã…3, Z = 2 with R1 = 0.0523), while compound 2 (orthorhombic, Pbca, a = 13.612(3) Ã…, b = 9.5753(19) Ã…, c = 14.022(3) Ã…, V = 1827.6(6) Ã…3, Z = 4 with R1 = 0.0441) presents a three-dimensional open-framework that consists of a novel {V3O14F2} trimer with mixed valence V2IVVIII linked by two bridging F atoms. In addition, it is worth noting that the organic templates play important roles in the formation of both novel structures. Further characterization of the two compounds has been performed, including X-ray powder diffraction, ICP, CHN, IR and TGâ€“DTA analyses.

1. Introduction

Among various microporous compounds, open-framework metal phosphates have aroused wide concern because of their rich structural chemistry and potential applications in catalysis, biology, optical and electromagnetic functions [1], [2], [3], [4]. Huge numbers of these materials have been reported, showing various architectures with one- (1D), two- (2D) and three-dimensional (3D) connections between inorganic and organic species [5], [6], [7]. A study of the literature of inorganicâ€“organic hybrid materials has shown that the diversity in crystal architectures of metal phosphates is mainly derived from the utility of various organic templates and the substitution of metal ions, such as transition metal ions like V [8], [9], [10], [11], [12], [13]. Recently, research interest has also arisen in replacing four-connected {PO4} units with three-connected {HPO3} units to expand the structure variety of the polyhedron-based frameworks. Organically templated transition metal phosphites with extended network structures such as Fe [14], Zn [15], Mn [16], and Al [17] phosphites have been prepared. Although the synthesis of phosphite framework has been accomplished, only a few vanadium phosphites, including 3D open-frameworks [H2N(CH2CH2)2NH2][(VO)3(HPO3)4(H2O)2] [18], (C2N2H10)0.5[V(HPO3)2] [19], [C2N2H10] Â· [(VO)3(H2O)(HPO3)4] Â· H2O [20] and two-dimensional layer structures [HN(C2H4)3N][(VO)2(HPO3)2(OH)(H2O)] Â· H2O [21], (VO)4(4,4â€™-bipy)2(HPO3)4 [22], (C6H16N2)[VIII(OH)2(VIVO)2(HPO3)4] Â· H3O [23], have been reported.

In the last decade, our group has been worked on the synthesis and structural study of metal phosphates/phosphites, and numerous gallium- [24] and indium- [25] phosphates and zincophosphites [26] have been reported. Our current research is the synthesis and characterization of organic templated vanadium phosphites with novel frameworks. As far as we are concerned, there are few reports of organic templated 3D vanadium phosphites [18], [19], [20] which inspired us to synthesize vanadium phosphites with novel structures. Based on the synthetic strategy of microporous materials and the effects of organic templates on the structural chemistry of open-frameworks, we choose 4, 4â€²-bipyridine (Bipy) and diethylenetriamine (DETA) as templates, V2O5 as metal source, and NH4F as mineralization agents and connectors. Herein, we successfully synthesized two novel vanadium phosphites, |(C10H10N2)| V2IVO2(HPO3)2(H2PO3)2 1 and |(C4H16N3)| V2IVVIIIO2F2(HPO3)4 2, both of which have 3D open-framework structures. In this paper, we mainly introduce the synthesis, structure, and thermal properties of the title compounds.

2. Experimental section

2.1. Synthesis and characterization

Compound 1 was prepared hydrothermally from a mixture of V2O5/H3PO3/Bipy/H2O with a molar ratio of 1/16/1/531. Typically, 0.023 g V2O5, 0.2 mL H3PO3 and 0.025 g Bipy were dissolved in 0.1 mL NH3 Â· H2O and 2 mL distilled water with stirring to form a homogeneous mixture with pH 2.0, then sealed in a 20 mL glass vial and heated under autogenous pressure at 80 Â°C for 12 h, then cooled to room temperature. Green crystals of compound 1 were collected (66% yield based on V2O5) and air-dried for characterization.

Compound 2 was synthesized in the V2O5/H3PO3/DETA/NH4F/H2O reaction system with a molar ratio of 1/24/4/4/664. Typically, 0.046 g V2O5, 0.6 mL H3PO3, 0.11 mL DETA and 0.039 g NH4F were dissolved in 5 mL distilled water with stirring to form a homogeneous mixture with pH 1.0. After stirring for 30 min, the final reaction mixture was transferred into a sealed Teflon-lined steel autoclave and heated at 140 Â°C for 72 h. Upon cooling to room temperature, pure phase of compound 2 in the form of green block-like crystals were collected (62% yield based on V2O5) and air-dried for characterization.

X-ray powder diffraction (XRPD) data were collected on a Rigaku 2550 diffractometer with Cu KÎ± radiation (Î» = 1.5418 Ã…). The elemental analyses were performed on a Perkinâ€“Elmer 2400 LSII CHN analyzer. The inductively coupled plasma (ICP) analyses were carried out on a Perkinâ€“Elmer Optima 3300Dv spectrometer. The Fâˆ’ content was determined by a fluoride ion-selective electrode. The infrared (IR) spectra were recorded within the 400â€“4000 cmâˆ’1 region on a Nicolet Impact 410 FTIR spectrometer using KBr pellets. The thermal gravimetric analyses (TGA) were performed on a NETZSCH STA 449 C thermogravimetric analyzer in air atmospheric environment with a heating rate of 10 C minâˆ’1.

2.2. Determination of crystal structure

Suitable single-crystals of 1 (0.32 Ã— 0.27 Ã— 0.22 mm3) and 2 (0.27 Ã— 0.25 Ã— 0.21 mm3) were selected for single-crystal X-ray diffraction analyses. The intensity data were collected on a Siemens Smart CCD diffractometer for 1 and a Rigaku RAXIS-RAPID IP diffractometer for 2 using graphite-monochromated Mo KÎ± radiation (Î» = 0.71073 Ã…). The numbers of collected reflections and independent reflections were 6965 and 2440 for 1, 16560 and 2056 for 2, respectively. Data processing was accomplished with the SAINT processing program for 1 and the RAPID AUTO processing program for 2. The structures were both solved by direct methods and refined by full-matrix least-squares on F2 using SHELXTL 5.1 software package [27]. All the vanadium and phosphorus atoms were located first. Then the oxygen, fluorine, carbon and nitrogen atoms were subsequently found in difference Fourier maps. For the charge balance, the templates (Bipy and DETA) in compounds 1 and 2 are protonated. The hydrogen atoms of the framework and organic amines were initially located from different Fourier maps, and for the final refinement, were placed geometrically. Finally, the non-hydrogen atoms were all refined anisotropically. It is noteworthy that the P(2) atom in the crystal structure of compound 1 is disordered over two positions with occupancy factors 0.9 and 0.1, respectively. In the structure of 1 (as shown in Fig. 1a), the VO6 octahedra based on vertex sharing oxygen atoms by two P(2) phosphite groups, form a 4-ring chain along the [0 1 0] direction, as shown in Fig. 1b. Such 4-ring chains as building blocks are very common in vanadium phosphites [21], [29] and other metal phosphites [26], [30]. This kind of 4-ring chain perpendicularly stack along the [0 0 1] direction by the P(1) phosphite groups, gives rise to a 3D open-framework. Particularly, there exist two kinds of perpendicular intersecting channels in the framework. One is a 12-ring channel along the [0 0 1] direction, as shown in Fig. 1c, and the other is an 8-ring channel along the [0 1 0] direction (see Fig. 1d), both of which are built of V and P atoms alternately linked via bridging oxygen atoms. The effective width of 12-ring channel and 8-ring channel are 6.11 Ã— 3.87 Ã… and 4.23 Ã— 4.23 Ã…, respectively. The Bipy molecules are located at the center of the 12-ring windows with both N atoms protonated to offer two positive charges. Both C1 and C2 atoms of Bipy are disordered over two positions with occupancy factors of 0.5. There are weak hydrogen bonding interactions between protonated N atoms and O atoms of the open inorganic frameworks (see Supporting Information).

The structure of 2 is shown in Fig. 2a, and it can be understood as follows: there exists an unusual {V3O14F2} trimer unit in the framework, in which two {VO5F} octahedra and a {VO4F2} octahedron linked by two bridging F atoms, further connected by four {HPO3} groups to create the unique {V3P4O18F2} building blocks (see Fig. 2b). These layers further stack in an ABAB sequence to create the 3D open-frameworks.

Comparatively, the trimer in compound 2 has two VO5F octahedra on two ends and a VO4F2 octahedron in the center of the trimer, which further link by four {HPO3} groups to create the unique {V3P4O18F2} building units. Then, three such {V3P4O18F2} building blocks connect via corner sharing to give a regular 10-ring, and this kind of 10-rings further link to create the 3D framework. This kind of trimer in the title compound is a new secondary building unit in the vanadium phosphate or phosphite materials.

3.2. Characterization

3.2.1. Characterization of compound 1

The powder X-ray diffraction pattern of compound 1 is in good agreement with the simulated one based on the data of the single-crystal structure (Fig. 3), suggesting the purity of the synthesized sample.

The IR spectrum of compound 1 (see Supporting Information Figure S2a) is assigned as follows: the band at 3085 cmâˆ’1 is attributed to the stretching vibrations of Arâ€“H groups and the intense bands at 1004 and 1074 cmâˆ’1 are attributed to the asymmetric and symmetric stretching vibrations of Vâ€“O, Pâ€“O bands. ICP analysis gave the contents of V 16.19% (calcd. 16.59%) and P 20.20% (calcd. 20.18%), indicating a V/P ratio of 1/2. Elemental analysis found (wt%): C, 19.48; H, 2.60; N, 4.55, respectively, which is in good agreement with the calculated values (calcd. (wt%): C, 19.56; H, 2.63; N, 4.56) based on the single-crystal structure formula |(C10H10N2)|V2IVO2(HPO3)2(H2PO3)2.

The TGA analysis of compound 1 (see Supporting Information Figure S3a) shows the first mass loss of 5.12% below 420 Â°C is due to the release of partial Bipy molecules. On further heating, a two-step weight loss was observed. The initial weight loss (20.07%) between 560 Â°C and 820 Â°C should correspond to the complete release of residual Bipy molecules. Then, the weight loss occurring between 820 Â°C and 980 Â°C (3.57%) should be attributed to the removal of hydroxyls and structural water of the black solid residue. The powder X-ray diffraction pattern of compound 2 is in good agreement with the simulated one based on the data of the single-crystal structure (Fig. 4), indicating the purity of the synthesized sample.

The IR spectrum of compound 2 (see Supporting Information Figure S2b) is assigned as follows: the bands at 3425 and 3037 cmâˆ’1 are attributed to H-bonded, Nâ€“H and Câ€“H groups. 4. Conclusion

It is noted that compounds 1 and 2 are both synthesized under mild hydrothermal conditions. Structural study shows that they are both novel three-dimensional open-frameworks. Compound 1 presents a structure with 8- and 12-ring intersecting channels while compound 2 has a unique {V3O14F2} trimer unit, which is the first example of fluorine atoms participating in the formation of 3D vanadium phosphites with mixed valence V2IVVIII species. Obviously, both Bipy and DETA molecules serving as templates and counter ions, play important roles for the formation of the two novel structures. Further understanding of the effect of organic templates on the structures of inorganic open-frameworks will be helpful in the rational design and synthesis of inorganic open-frameworks materials.
