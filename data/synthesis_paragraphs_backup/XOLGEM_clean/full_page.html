Introduction

Metalâ€“organic frameworks (MOFs), also known as porous coordination polymers, are a new class of porous crystalline materials with high thermal stability, adjustable chemical functionality, and remarkably high porosity, which can be obtained by self-assembly of metal ions and organic linkers, resulting in tailored nanoporous multidimensional networks.1 Due to their tailored structures and tunable properties, they are promising candidates for gas storage and separation, luminescence sensor and magnetism applications.2â€“4 In particular, cubic-cage-based MOFs show well-defined cavities in the microscale quantum realms and unique properties in the macroscale classical fields.5 A useful but challenging synthetic strategy to design such a MOF is the simultaneous assembly of metal ions as the vertexes and ligands with a specific coordination symmetry as the edges in a single system. This approach could also be termed as edge-directed self-assembly and has been realized in some molecular M8L12 cubes and cubic-cage-based MOFs.5,6 However, it is still a great challenge to rationally synthesize cubic-cage-based MOFs because of the difficulties in the precise control of the structure of the final product during the self-assembly process. As a result, the cubic-cage-based MOF is still rare in coordination chemistry.

Additionally, the predesigned organic ligands have a great influence on the construction of desirable MOFs.7 Our strategy to synthesize a desired MOF is to employ an appropriate ligand with a special coordination symmetry, namely symmetry-guided synthesis approach. This approach was well performed in a series of porous MOFs with interesting magnetic,8 luminescence9 and sorption properties.10 It is well known that 1H-1,2,3-triazole-4,5-dicarboxylic acid (H3TDA), which can afford two carboxylate groups and a triazolate group, is a promising ligand for constructing MOFs with impressive topological structures and significant properties.11,12 H3TDA has been widely employed as a versatile multi-chelating ligand because of its inherent characteristics: firstly, compared with 4,5-imidazoledicarboxylic acid, H3TDA is easier to dissolve in water and other conventional organic solvents; secondly, its 2-site N of the triazole has the potential ability to directly coordinate with metal ions to provide diverse coordination modes; more importantly, it can form a triangular secondary building unit (SBU) which has a superexchange capacity to exhibit interesting magnetic properties.

Results and discussion

Syntheses

It is well known that the self-assembly of MOFs can be influenced by many factors, such as the type of metal ions and ligands, solvents, reaction temperature, pH value of the solution and the counterions.16 As shown in Scheme 1, three novel MOFs based on the same cubic cage were obtained under different reaction conditions. Reaction of Co(ClO4)2Â·6H2O with H3TDA and HTZ in a 1:1 mixture of water and DMF, in the presence of LiOH at 90 Â°C led to the formation of 1, which shows a well-defined cavity with hexahydrated Co2+ ions imbedded in the central cavity of the cubic cages. When the solvent was replaced by water and the reaction temperature was raised up to 140 Â°C, the reaction of Co(ClO4)2Â·6H2O with H3TDA and HTZ afforded the crystals of 2, which has an isostructural framework with 1. The divergences of the two MOFs are their different counter cations. In 1, the counterions are protonated dimethylammonium cations formed from the decomposition of N,N-dimethylformamide and hydronium cations ,whereas the counterions in 2 are hydronium cations. The reaction of Co(OAc)2Â·4H2O with KH2TDA and HTZ in a 1:1 mixture of water and DMF, in the presence of ammonia solution and triethylamine at 90 Â°C, produced the framework of 3, which consists of cubic cages, but no hexahydrated Co2+ ions was imbedded in the cages. Due to the different coordination modes of the alkaline cations, the structure of 3 is quite different from the structures of 1 and 2 and this will be discussed below.

Description of the crystal structures

Single crystal X-ray diffraction studies revealed that 1 and 2 are isostructural and crystallize in the trigonal space group Rc. They exist as novel 2pâ€“3d heterometallic frameworks containing interesting [Co8(TDA)12]20âˆ’ cubic cages, in which hexahydrated Co2+ ions are captured. As discussed above, 1 and 2 exhibit different magnetic parameters even though they are composed of similar [Co8(TDA)12]20âˆ’ cubic cages and a hexahydrated Co2+ ion with Oh symmetry. To access the variation of the coordination environments of the Co2+ ion to ideal Oh influenced by counter cations, the shape values (suggested by SHAPE software) of the corresponding Co1 and Co2 ions in these MOFs are calculated (Table 2).22 The larger the calculated parameter by SHAPE, the more deviation from an ideal symmetry. The results indicate that in 1 and 2 the local environments of Co1 and Co2 have a deviation from the Oh symmetry and the environments of Co3 exactly exhibit the Oh symmetry. The delicate differences of the local Co2+ environments force the Coâ‹¯Co distances and Coâ€“Coâ€“Co angles to be slightly differentiated (Fig. 6). Therefore, the origin of the slight difference in magnetic parameters of 1 and 2 is from the influence of the counter cations. In contrast, 3 consists of four Co2+ environments with similar distorted Oh symmetry. As a result, the magnetic parameters of 3 are slightly different from those of 1 and 2.

Conclusions

In summary, we have successfully assembled three 2pâ€“3d heterometallic MOFs based on interesting cubic cages using a symmetry-guided synthesis approach with 1H-1,2,3-triazole-4,5-dicarboxylic acid. The topologies were well tuned by different alkaline cations: isostructural 1 and 2 display 3D 6-connected pcu nets with Li+ cations; whereas 3 is a 10-connected bct net with K+ cations. The magneto-structural analysis of 1â€“3 indicates that there are dominant antiferromagnetic interactions between Co2+ ions in these systems. This work illustrates a useful approach to synthesize cubic-cage-based MOFs with different topologies and functional properties. Further studies on the synthesis of this type of MOFs with unusual topological structures using this strategy are currently under way in our laboratory.
