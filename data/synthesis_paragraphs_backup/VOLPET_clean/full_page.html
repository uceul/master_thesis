    Abstract

    Three anionic Zn(II)–organic frameworks with 2,5-thiophenedicarboxylate ligand have been synthesized solvothermally. They exhibit different 3D net topologies but all contain trinuclear [Zn3(COO)8] building units. Compound 1 shows an 8-connected bcg net just based on [Zn3(COO)8] nodes, while compound 2 displays an 8-connected bcu net based on two similar [Zn3(COO)8] nodes. Interestingly, compound 3 represents a novel (4,8)-connected flu net based on the trinuclear nodes [Zn3(COO)8] and the binuclear nodes [Zn2(COO)4]. Gas sorption measurements indicate that the desolvated host 1-ht exhibits permanent porosity with Langmuir surface area of 630.5 m2·g–1 and a H2 uptake of 109.5 cm3·g–1 at 77 K and 1 atm, as well as a selective sorption of CO2 over N2 at 0 °C and ambient pressure.

    Synopsis

    The assembly between rigid 2,5-thiophenedicarboxylic acid ligand and Zn2+ ions leads to three anionic Zn−organic frameworks containing trinuclear [Zn3(COO)8] building units and exhibiting diverse net topologies.

    Considerable efforts have been devoted to the synthesis of metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) not only because of their potential application in gas storage/separation,(1) heterogeneous catalysis,(2) and luminescence(3) but also because of their fascinating architectures and desirable topologies. Up to now, a large number of porous MOFs have been synthesized by using the strategy of secondary building units (SBUs), especially related metal-carboxylate MOFs, such as MOF-5, HKUST-1, and a series of MIL-n, etc.(4) As a rigid “V-shaped” dicarboxylic organic linker, 2,5-thiophenedicarboxylic acid (H2thb) has been used to synthesize some porous MOFs,(5-7) which are constructed by various SBUs and exhibit diverse network topologies. For example, Bu and co-workers have reported a homochiral crystallization of microporous framework material, which displays a 4-connected dia net based on mononuclear [In(COO)4] node.(6) Kitagawa et al. reported a 2-fold interpenetrating porous MOF with 6-connected lcy net derived from the classical basic [Zn4O(COO)6] unit(7) and so on. However, the metal–thb framework containing trinuclear [M3(COO)8] unit is not found, and the common feature presented in these MOF structures is only one type of SBU.
    In this work, by employing the H2thb ligand to assembly with Zn2+ ions, we successfully synthesized three anionic Zn–thb frameworks, which all contain trinuclear [Zn3(COO)8] building units and exhibit different net topologies. Compound 1, (Me2NH2)2·[Zn3(thb)4]·χG, shows an 8-connected bcg net just based on [Zn3(COO)8] nodes, while compound 2, (Et4N)2·[Zn3(thb)4]·χG, displays an 8-connected bcu net based on two similar [Zn3(COO)8] nodes. Interestingly, compound 3, (Bu4N)·[Zn3.5(thb)4(DMF)3]·χG, represents a novel (4,8)-connected flu net based on not only the trinuclear nodes [Zn3(COO)8] but also the binuclear nodes [Zn2(COO)4]. The desolvated host 1-ht shows permanent porosity with Langmuir surface area of 630.5 m2·g–1 and a H2 uptake of 109.5 cm3·g–1 at 77 K and 1 atm. In addition, at 0 °C and ambient pressure, 1-ht can separate CO2 from N2.

    Materials and Instrumentation

    All reagents were purchased commercially and used without further purification. The purity of all gases is 99.999%. All syntheses were carried out in a 20 mL vial under autogenous pressure. Diffraction data were collected by using a Oxford Xcalibur diffractometer with graphite monochromated Mo-Ka radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at 293 K. The structures were solved by direct methods and refined by the full-matrix least-squares technique by using SHELXTL.(8) All powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD) analyses were recorded on a Rigaku Dmax2500 diffractometer with Cu Kα radiation (λ = 1.54056 Å) with a step size of 0.05°. Thermal stability studies were carried out on a Netzsch STA-449C thermoanalyzer with a heating rate of 10 °C/min under a nitrogen atmosphere. Gas adsorption measurement was performed in the ASAP (Accelerated Surface Area and Porosimetry) 2020 System.

    Synthesis of (Me2NH2)2·[Zn3(thb)4]·χG (1)

    H2thb (52 mg, 0.3 mmol) and Zn(NO3)2·6H2O (119 mg, 0.4 mmol) were dissolved in 5 mL of DMF/MeCN (4:1, v/v), which were placed in a small vial. The mixture was heated at 120 °C for 48 h and then cooled to room temperature. A mass of colorless sheet crystals were formed and collected by filtration and washed with DMF several times (yield: 72% based on H2thb).

    Synthesis of (Et4N)2·[Zn3(thb)4]·χG (2)

    H2thb (172 mg, 1 mmol), Zn(NO3)2·6H2O (297 mg, 1 mmol), and tetraethylammonium bromide (210 mg, 1 mmol) were dissolved in 5 mL of DEF/MeOH (4:1, v/v), which were placed in a small vial. The mixture was heated at 120 °C for 96 h and then cooled to room temperature. Just a few colorless block crystals were obtained and collected by filtration and washed with DMF several times (yield: 17% based on H2thb).

    Synthesis of (Bu4N)·[Zn3.5(thb)4(DMF)3]·χG (3)

    H2thb (52 mg, 0.3 mmol), Zn(NO3)2·6H2O (80 mg, 0.27 mmol), and tetrabutylammonium bromide (100 mg, 0.31 mmol) were dissolved in 5 mL of DMF/MeCN (4:1, v/v), which were placed in a small vial. The mixture was heated at 100 °C for 48 h and then cooled to room temperature. Only a few colorless block crystals were synthesized and collected by filtration and washed with DMF several times (yield: 15% based on H2thb).

    Solvothermal reaction of H2thb with Zn(NO3)2·6H2O in a mixture of DMF/MeCN (4:1, v/v) led to the formation of colorless sheet crystals of 1 in 72% yield. Crystal structure determination confirms that 1 crystallizes in the orthorhombic system with a space group of Fddd. As shown in Figure 1a, three crystallographically independent Zn2+ ions, Zn1, Zn2, and Zn3 are four-, six-, and five-coordinated, respectively, while thb2– ligands adopt three coordination modes: (κ1)-(κ1)-μ2 (thb-B), (κ1-κ1)-(κ1-κ1)-μ4 (thb-D), and (κ2-κ1-μ2)-(κ1-κ1-μ2)-μ4 (thb-G) (Scheme 1). Three Zn(II) ions are bridged by the carboxylate groups from six different thb ligands to form a trinuclear [Zn3(COO)6], which is quite different from another MOF built from Zn3(OH)(O2CR) trimers.(9) These trimeric units are connected by six thb ligands in ab plane to form a layer with 6-connected hxl net (Figure 1b). Adjacent hxl-type layers are further connected at the trimeric units from both the sides by one thb-B ligand, leading to a 3D pillared-layer framework with two types of channels along the c axis, and the aperture is approximately 7.5 × 8.0 Å2 and 3.5 × 6.0 Å2, respectively (Figure 1c). Viewing of the 3D structure from a direction, there is a parallelogram window with the size of 4.9 × 5.7 Å2 (Figure S1, Supporting Information). The overall framework of 1 is anionic, and each asymmetric unit is corresponding to two (Me2NH2)+ cations formed by the decomposition of DMF solvent molecules. From the viewpoint of topology, the framework of 1 can be described as a bcg network with Schlafli symbol (36·414·57·6) by reducing each trinuclear [Zn3(COO)8] unit as an 8-connected node (Figure 1d). By far, some porous MOFs constructed by trinuclear [M3(COO)8] SBU have been reported.(10, 11) However, there has not been much reported about Zn-thb MOFs, and the reported MOFs are constructed from [Zn4O(COO)6] unit;(5m, 7) the one containing [Zn3(COO)8] unit is not found.
    Compound 2 was prepared by using the solvothermal reaction of Zn(NO3)2·6H2O, H2thb, and tetraethylammonium bromide ((Et4N)Br) in DEF/MeOH (4:1, v/v) mixed solvent. Single-crystal X-ray diffraction reveals that 2 crystallizes in the orthorhombic space group Pnc2. In comparison with 1, 2 also shows an anionic framework, and each asymmetric unit is corresponding to two (Et4N)+ cations. In addition, similar trinuclear [Zn3(COO)8] units are also presented in 2. Interestingly, there are two types of Zn trimeric units (Zn1–Zn3–Zn1a, denoted as A unit, and Zn2–Zn4–Zn2a, denoted as B unit), as seen in Figure 2a. The Zn3 and Zn4 have a perfect octahedral geometry, and the Zn1 (Zn1a) and Zn2 (Zn2a) have similar coordination environments, only the (κ1-κ1-μ2) bidentate bridging carboxylate becomes (κ2-κ1-μ2) bridging, which are four- and five-coordinated, respectively. In 2, thb2– ligands adopt the following coordination modes: (κ1)-(κ1-κ1)-μ3 (thb-C), (κ1-κ1)-(κ1-κ1)-μ4 (thb-D), and (κ2-κ1-μ2)-(κ1)-μ3 (thb-F) (Scheme 1). Three Zn(II) ions are bridged by two thb-C and two thb-F ligands to form a 2D grid sheet on the ab plane (Figure 2b), and the adjacent layers are further interconnected by the thb-D ligands to generate the final 3D framework (Figure 2c). These trimeric units are connected by the following ways: each A unit is connected to eight B units and each B unit to eight A units, as shown in Figure 2d. Thus, the 3D framework represents an 8-connected bcu net with point symbol (424·64). In fact, the similar MOF containing two similar [Zn3(COO)8] SBUs has been reported by Ghosh.(11) The 3D framework of 2 exhibits 1D quadrangle channels along the a direction with dimensions 6 × 6 Å2, occupied by guest molecules and (Et4N)+ cations.
    When the tetrabutyl ammonium bromide ((Bu4N)Br) was added to the reaction system of 1, we obtained a few of crystals 3, which is also a anionic pillared-layer framework but constructed from not only the trinuclear unit [Zn3(COO)8] but also the binuclear unit [Zn2(COO)4]. Thus, it can be seen that cations with varying sizes play a very important role in constructing different MOFs. Crystal structure determination confirms that 3 crystallizes in the triclinic system with a space group of P1̅. The asymmetric unit contains four types of Zn centers. Zn1 has octahedral geometry with full occupancy, while Zn2 has tetrahedral geometry with half occupancy, which is similar to the Zn1–Zn3–Zn1a unit in compound 2. It is worthy to note that three DMF molecules coordinate to the Zn3 ion, and the 6-coordinated Zn3 and 4-coordinated Zn4 form a binuclear unit. Two types of SBUs denoted as C and D unit, respectively (Figure 3a). In 3, thb2– ligands adopt the following coordination modes: (κ1)-(κ1)-μ2 (thb-A), (κ1-κ1)-(κ1-κ1)-μ4 (thb-D), and (κ1-κ1)-(κ1-μ2)-μ4 (thb-E) (Scheme 1). The trimeric units are connected by six thb ligands, and the binuclear unit are linked by three thb ligands in ab plane to form a 2D layer with (3,6)-connected kgd net (Figure 3b). Adjacent 2D layers are further connected at the trimeric units from both the sides by one thb-A ligands and at the binuclear unit from one side by one thb-A ligand, leading to a 3D pillared-layer framework with two types of channels along the a axis (Figure 3c), and the aperture is approximately 6.5 × 6.5 Å2 and 5.5 × 8.5 Å2. Compound 3 is an anionic framework, and each asymmetric unit contains one (Bu4N)+ cation. Topological analysis of the 3D network formed by 3 indicates that it comprises two different nodes (Figure 3d). Each C unit is connected to eight D units, while each D unit is connected to four C units. So the 3D framework represents a novel (4,8)-connected flu net with point symbol (412·612·84)(46)2.(12) Actually, the one containing both [M2(COO)4] and [M3(COO)8] units is highly rarely reported.
    The thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) curve of 1 reveals a weight loss of 25.03% before 250 °C, corresponding to the release of DMF and MeCN molecules (Figure S6, Supporting Information). The host framework of 1 can be stable up to 300 °C. For gas sorption studies, 1 was activated at 150 °C for 7 h under high vacuum and then formed the hollow samples 1-ht. Compared to 1, the peak positions in the powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD) pattern of 1-ht are just slightly changed (Figure S7, Supporting Information). This result indicates that the host framework of 1 underwent a slight change after the removal of guest molecules.
    To investigate the permanent porosity of desolvated 1-ht, the adsorption experiments of N2 and Ar were measured at 77 and 87 K, respectively (Figure 4a). The uptake of Ar for 1-ht is ∼171.6 cm3·g–1 at 1 atm. For N2, it shows reversible and typical type I adsorption isotherm behavior, which is characteristic of a microporous material. The N2 uptake capacity of 1 reaches 146.3 cm3·g–1 at 1 atm. The BET and Langmuir surface areas are 453.2 and 630.5 m2·g–1, respectively. The measured pore volume from the N2 sorption is 0.226 cm3·g–1 at 1 atm. Actually, these measured pore parameters are relatively low. The main factor may be that cations occupy the part of the channel or some even block the pore window, which further prevent N2 to enter into the channel.
    The H2 sorption isotherms at 77 and 87 K for 1-ht were also investigated (Figure 4a). For 1-ht, the H2 uptake capacity reaches 109.5 cm3·g–1 (0.98 wt %) at 77 K and 1 atm, a value closing to those of recently reported MOFs at the same condition.(13) The H2 adsorption enthalpy (Qst) of 1-ht calculated by the virial equation from the adsorption isotherms measured at 77 and 87 K is 7.1 kJ·mol–1. In addition, the CO2 uptake of 1-ht at 273 and 298 K can reach 50.4 cm3·g–1 (2.25 mmol·g–1) and 44.9 cm3·g–1 (2.00 mmol·g–1), respectively (Figure 4b). However, under the same measurement conditions, it can hardly adsorb N2 (1.27 cm3·g–1). At zero coverage, the CO2 adsorption enthalpy for 1-ht is 10.2 kJ·mol–1. In a range of pressures up to 1 atm at 273 K, the adsorption selectivity for CO2/N2 with mole ratios of 20:80 is 41 (Figure 4c), which was calculated using ideal solution adsorbed theory (IAST).(14) Obviously, at 273 K and 1 atm, 1-ht can separate CO2 from N2.

    In summary, by employment of the 2,5-thiophenedicarboxylic acid (H2thb) ligand to assemble with the Zn2+ ions by tuning different organic cations, three anionic Zn–thb frameworks containing [Zn3(COO)8] units and exhibiting different net topologies are presented here. Compound 1 shows an 8-connected bcg net based on [Zn3(COO)8] nodes. While 2 displays an 8-connected bcu net based on two similar [Zn3(COO)8] nodes. Interestingly, 3 represents a novel (4,8)-connected flu net based on not only the trinuclear [Zn3(COO)8] nodes but also the binuclear [Zn2(COO)4] nodes. Gas sorption for 1-ht gives a Langmuir surface area of 630.5 m2·g–1 and a H2 uptake of 109.5 cm3·g–1 at 77 K and 1 atm. In addition, 1-ht demonstrates how it can separate CO2 from N2.
