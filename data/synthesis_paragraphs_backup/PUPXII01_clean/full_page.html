Journal of Molecular Structure

Volume 1127, 5 January 2017, Pages 662-667

Synthesis, structure and properties of a 3D acentric coordination polymer with noninterpenetrated (10,3)-d topology

Author links open overlay panelHuijieLunaXuefeiLiaXiaoWangaHaiyanLiaYaminLiabYanBaia
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.molstruc.2016.08.019Get rights and content

Highlights

•

•

Compound 1 features ferroelectric, NLO and antiferromagnetic behaviors.

Abstract

A new coordination polymer, {[Mn(HPIDC)(H2O)]·2H2O}n (1) (H3PIDC = 2-(pyridin-4-yl)-1H-imidazole-4,5-dicarboxylic acid), has been obtained by hydrothermal method and structurally characterized by X-ray single crystal diffraction, elemental analysis and thermogravimetric analysis (TGA). X-ray single crystal diffraction reveals that compound 1 crystallizing in acentric Pna21 space group, exhibits an ultimate racemic three-dimension framework with rare noninterpenetrated (10,3)-d (or utp) topology due to the alternate array of left- and right-handed helixes. Moreover, compound 1 also features ferroelectric, nonlinear optical (NLO) and antiferromagnetic behaviors.

Graphical abstract

An acentric coordination polymer has been synthesized and characterized by single-crystal X-ray diffraction, IR spectrum, elemental analysis, thermogravimetric analysis, ferroelectric, nonlinear optical and magnetic measurements.

1. Introduction

Emerging attention has been paid to coordination polymers not only due to their interesting topologies and structures, but also for the potential application in the fields such as catalysis, magnetism, gas adsorption, nonlinear optics and ferroelectricity and so on [1]. However, to construct the expected coordination polymers, just like the noncentrosymmetric frameworks, which are the primary requirement for the nonlinear optical and ferroelectric materials [2], the careful selection of organic ligands is particularly important. According to the literature, imidazole-4,5-dicarboxylic acid (H3IDC) has been widely studied and proven to be a good candidate for constructing helical and porous compounds [3]. Therefore, as a 2-position substituted derivative of H3IDC, 2-(pyridin-4-yl)-1H-imidazole-4,5-dicarboxylic acid (H3PIDC) has caught our eyes, which has seldom been researched so far. It is a multi-functional N-heterocyclic carboxylate ligand and possesses many unique advantages in the self-assembly process, which makes it more prominent compared with other ligands. The characteristics are as follows: there are multiple coordination sites in the ligand and it can adopt diverse coordination modes, monodentate, bidentate, bridging fashion for example; the CC bond between imidazole and pyridine rings can suitably rotate to satisfy configuration demands; the ligand can be completely or partially deprotonated due to different pH levels, which is beneficial for the generation of supramolecular structures [4]; the carboxyl and the imidazole ring may be non-coplanar when it coordinates to metal ions, which holds special superiority in constructing acentric coordination polymers [5]. As far as we know, a number of coordination polymers with fascinating structures have been gained with respect to the ligand [3](a), [5], [6].

Taking the aforementioned viewpoints into consideration, we prefer the multidentate ligand and choose it as a starting material, and the outcome is satisfactory. A coordination polymer {[Mn(HPIDC)(H2O)]·2H2O}n (1) has been obtained, crystallizing in acentric Pna21 space group. The three-dimensional structure can be simplified as a rare racemic noninterpenetrated (10,3)-d topology. Moreover, the compound exhibits second-order nonlinear optical property, which may be a good choice as a kind of NLO material. It also displays antiferromagnetic and ferroelectric behaviors.

2. Experimental section

2.1. Materials and physical measurements

All the reagents were purchased commercially and used as received. Infrared spectra were recorded on a Bruker VERTEX-70 FT-IR spectrophotometer in the range of 400–4000 cm−1 using KBr pellets. Thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) was carried out under N2 atmosphere using TGA/SDTA851e with a heating rate of 10 °C/min−1. Elemental analysis was determined on Vario EL III Etro Elemental Analyzer. Powder X-ray diffraction pattern (PXRD) was performed at room temperature using Bruker D8 Advance instrument with Cu Kα radiation (λ = 1.54056 Å) in the range 2θ = 5–50°. Magnetic measurements were performed using a Quantum Design MPMS-XL SQUID magnetometer. The ferroelectric hysteresis loops were carried out on an aix-ACCT TF2000 analyzer at room temperature by using powder sample in pellet. NLO property was performed by a Kurtz−Perry powder SHG test using a Q-switched Nd:YAG laser (1064 nm).

2.2. Synthesis of {[Mn(HPIDC)(H2O)]·2H2O}n (1)

1 mmol MnCl2·4H2O (0.200 g), 0.5 mmol H3PIDC (0.116 g) and 1 mmol KSCN (0.097 g) were mixed and stirred in 6 mL distilled water for 20 min, and then it was transferred to a 25 mL Teflon-lined stainless steel vessel, heated to 160 °C for 3 days. When it was cooled to room temperature, yellow block crystals were obtained, washed by distilled water (Yield: 0.109 g 64.0% based on H3PIDC). Elemental analysis (%): Anal. Calcd. for C 35.31, H 3.26, N 12.35; Found: C 35.28, H 3.34, N 12.29. FT-IR (KBr pellet, cm−1): 3373(s), 1621(s), 1601(s), 1538(m), 1486(m), 1447(m), 1422(m), 1381(s), 1230(w), 1110(w), 1012(w), 864(w), 817(w), 720(w).

2.3. X-ray crystallography

Crystallographic data for compound 1 were collected on a Bruker Apex-II CCD detector diffractometer with Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at 296 (2) K. Data absorption correction and reduction were made with empirical methods. The structure was solved by directed methods using SHELXS-97 [7] and refined by full-matrix least-squares techniques using SHELXL-97 [8]. Anisotropic displacement parameters were refined for most non-hydrogen atoms. Most hydrogen atoms were added in the riding model while the aqueous hydrogen atoms were located from the difference Fourier maps. The crystal data and refinement details for compound 1 are listed in Table 1. The selected bond lengths and angles are listed in Table S1. CCDC number is 1487738 for compound 1. 3. Results and discussions

3.1. The structure of compound 1

X-ray single crystal diffraction reveals that compound 1 crystallizes in orthorhombic crystal system, acentric Pna21 space group. The asymmetric unit consists of one crystallographically independent Mn(II) atom, one doubly deprotonated HPIDC2− ligand, one coordinated water and two lattice water molecules. As shown in Fig. 1, Mn(II) atom is six-coordinated, exhibiting distorted octahedron configuration, in which pyridine-N1 and imidazole-N2 atoms from two individual HPIDC2− ligands locate on the axial positions, three carboxyl oxygen atoms (O1, O2, O3) as well as one water molecule (O1W) lying in the equatorial plane. The Mn−N lengths are 2.278(2) Å and 2.410(2) Å while the Mn−O lengths range from 2.1157(19) Å to 2.1657(19) Å. The distances are similar to the reported values [6g]. In compound 1, each HPIDC2− ligand adopts a μ3-κN,O:κO,O′:κN′ coordination mode to link three Mn(II) atoms (Scheme 1), which has ever seen in several compounds [6b,f,g]. The pyridine and imidazole rings are almost coplanar in the HPIDC2− ligand with the dihedral angle of 11.388°. As well, one carboxyl group in the ligand exhibits larger distortion from imidazole ring with the dihedral angle of 43.261° compared to the other with that of 12.984°. Interestingly, the connectivity manner of HPIDC2− gives rise to two types of helixes along the c axis direction. On one hand, one HPIDC2− ligand links two individual Mn(II) atoms by means of monodentate-N and N,O-chelation patterns, extending to a left-handed {Mn(HPIDC)}n helix (Fig. S1a). Simultaneously, the neighboring right-handed helixes with the same connection are also present (Fig. S1d). On the other hand, two HPIDC2− ligands connect to three Mn(II) ions and generate two kinds of infinite {Mn2(HPIDC)2}n helixes with opposite chirality, respectively by the O,O′-chelating, monodentate-N and N,O-chelating, O,O′-chelating patterns (Fig. S2). All of helixes have paralleled 21 screw axis with the screw pitch of 15.881 Å, consistent with the length of crystallographic c-axis. Further, two types of helixes with opposite chirality are alternately arranged to generate an ultimate racemic 3D framework by sharing Mn(II) atoms and HPIDC2− ligand. From the point of topology, if all the water molecules are ignored, each Mn(II) atom links to three HPIDC2− ligands, and each ligand is coordinated to three Mn(II) atoms. Hence, both of them are considered as three connection nodes and the whole framework can be simplified as a 3D noninterpenetrated (10,3)-d (or utp) topology with the schläfli symbol of {103}, which features left- and right-handed helixes and a final racemic architecture compared to (10,3)-a topology (Fig. 2). According to the literature, the (10,3)-d topology especially the noninterpenetrated type is quite rare and only several cases have been reported up to now [9].

From another perspective to analysis the structure, one carboxyl group in HPIDC2− ligand adopting anti-anti mode connects to adjacent Mn(II) ions, forming a 1D zigzag chains along a axis direction, which is related to the antiferromagnetic coupling. The zigzag chains are further connected by pyridine-N atoms to generate a 3D framework with one-dimensional channels occupied by free water molecules (Fig. 3). Such structure is isostructural with the reported compound {[Fe(HPIDC)(H2O)]·2H2O}n [6b].

3.2. IR, TG, XRD

The IR spectra of compound 1 and the ligand are shown in Fig. S3. By comparison, it is obvious that the peak at 1712 cm−1 in the ligand disappears, while the characteristic absorption peaks at 1621 cm−1 and 1381 cm−1 occurs, which can be attributed to the asymmetric stretching (vas) and symmetric stretching vibrations (vs) of carboxyl groups. The phenomenon provides a further confirmation that the ligand is coordinated to metal ions. Moreover, the broad peak at 3373 cm−1 in 1 derives from the stretching vibration of OH in water molecule. The peaks in the range of 1601−1400 cm−1 are the vibrations of CC, CN in pyridine and imidazole groups [10].

In order to check the purity of compound 1, the powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD) measurement was performed. The PXRD curves (Fig. S4) exhibit that the peaks of experimental patterns are in good agreement with the simulated patterns generated from single-crystal diffraction data, which confirm the pure phase of compound 1. The differences in intensity are because of the preferred orientation of crystalline powder samples.

Thermogravimetric analysis was performed under N2 atmosphere with a heating rate of 10 °C/min to examine the thermal stability of 1. From the TGA curve (Fig. S5), the lattice and coordinated water molecules in compound 1 were prone to loss with the temperature rise and finished at about 150 °C (obsd 14.87%, cacld. 15.89%), and then there was a platform in the range of 150–300 °C. As the heating continued, the weight loss restarted and the HPIDC2− was gradually lost. When the temperature reached to 1000 °C, the whole framework had collapsed.

3.3. Ferroelectric and nonlinear optical properties

Compound 1 crystallizes in noncentrosymmetric space group Pna21, which is associated with C2v point group, one of the ten polar point groups (C1, C2, C3, C4, C6, Cs, C2v, C3v, C4v, C6v) required for ferroelectric materials [11]. Thus, the ferroelectric property of 1 was investigated. From Fig. 4, there are electric hysteresis loops in compound 1 with a remnant polarization (Pr) of ca. 0.37 μC cm−2 and a coercive field (Ec) of ca. 20.1 kV cm−1, which is a typical ferroelectric feature. The saturation spontaneous polarization (Ps) occurring at about 1.09 μC cm−2, is higher than that of the typical ferroelectric compound Rochelle salt (NaKC4H4O6·4H2O, Ps = 0.25 μC cm−2), but small than that found in KDP (KH2PO4, Ps = 5.0 μC cm−2).

As well, the second harmonic generation (SHG) effect of 1 was investigated to evaluate whether it is a potential second-order nonlinear optical material. As shown in Fig. 5, the results indicate that compound 1 displays SHG intensity about 1.5 times of that produced by KDP, which is a good demonstration for possession of optical property.

3.4. Magnetism

The temperature-dependent magnetic susceptibility data of compound 1 was measured in the 2–300 K temperature range under an applied field of 1000 Oe. The χmT and χm versus T plots are depicted in Fig. 6. The room temperature value of χmT is 4.30 cm3 mol−1 K, close to the expected value of 4.375 cm3 mol−1 K for one magnetically isolated spin Mn(II) ions (S = 5/2, g = 2.0). When the temperature is cooled, the χmT value gradually decrease to reach 3.87 cm3 mol−1 K at about 50 K and then followed by a sharp drops to 0.48 cm3 mol−1 K at 2 K on further cooling, which indicate the antiferromagnetic behavior between Mn(II) ions. The magnetic susceptibility of 1 above 25 K obeys the Curie-Weiss law with the value of C = 4.41 cm3 K mol−1 and a negative Weiss constant θ = −7.04 K, which provides a further confirmation for the antiferromagnetic interaction (Fig. S6). Considering the structural characters of compound 1, down the a axis, adjacent Mn(II) ions are bridged by the anti-anti carboxyl groups to form 1D chains, which is the main magnetic exchange pathway between metal centers [12]. Though the neighboring 1D chain are further connected to 3D network, the interchain magnetic interactions can be negligible due to the separation of long HPIDC2− ligands. Therefore, to fit the magnetic data, an attempt was made by using the classical spin Heisenberg model for a one-dimensional chain (eq (1)) [13]:(1)χchain=Ng2β2S(S+1)3kT1+μ1−μWhere μ=coth[JS(S+1)kT]−[kTJS(S+1)]

Herein, N, β, k represents Avogadro's number, the Bohr magneton and Boltzmann's constant, respectively. The good fitting gives g = 2.01, J = −0.79 cm−1 with the agreement factor R = 3.05 × 10−8. The negative value of J is also indicative of the weak antiferromagnetic interaction between Mn(II) ions connected by carboxyl groups [14]. The field dependence of the isothermal magnetization M(H) at 2 K was measured as shown in Fig. S7. When the field reaches 8 T, the magnetization is 4.40 Nβ, without reaching the saturation value of 5.0 Nβ (S = 5/2, g = 2.0) expected for one isolated Mn(II) ions.

4. Conclusion

In summary, an acentric coordination polymer has been obtained by utilizing 2-(pyridin-4-yl)-1H-imidazole-4,5-dicarboxylic acid as organic ligand. The compound features a 3D noninterpenetrated (10,3)-d (or utp) topology, which is quite rare in reported coordination polymers. The SHG signal of compound 1 is 1.5 times of that produced by KDP, and it also exhibits ferroelectric and antiferromagnetic behaviors.
