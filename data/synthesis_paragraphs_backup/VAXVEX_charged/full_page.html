Two novel Cd(II) complexes with unprecedented four- and six-fold interpenetration†

Nan Wang , Jian-Gong Ma , Wei Shi and Peng Cheng *
Department of Chemistry, Key Laboratory of Advanced Energy Material Chemistry, MOE, and TKL of Metal- and Molecule-based Material Chemistry, Nankai University, Tianjin 300071, China. E-mail: pcheng@nankai.edu.cn; Fax: +86-22-23502458

Received 28th February 2012 , Accepted 14th May 2012

First published on 15th May 2012

Two Cd(II) metal–organic frameworks have been synthesized. One of them shows an unusual pcu net with a new four-fold interpenetrating mode Ib, while another one has a rare cdl net with six-fold interpenetration.

Introduction

Today, interpenetration is well established and has been extensively studied in complexes, not only for its potential applications as functional materials, but also for the intriguing variety of architectures and topologies.1,2 However, design and control of interpenetrations for independent motifs are still challenges, since a number of factors significantly affect the final architectures, such as solvents, the coordination geometry of metal centers and the addition of secondary ligands.

In the research of interpenetrating examples, six-connected pcu nets and four-connected dia are the most common interpenetrating nets.3 The pcu nets always adopt a parallel interpenetration mode. To the best of our knowledge, there are only two unusual interpenetrating pcu nets.4 In addition, most interpenetrating pcu networks are two- and three-fold, while only a few four-fold (FECKAA,5a FECJUT,5a PUPNAQ,5b PORWAV5c and UHUPET5d) and one five-fold interpenetrating frameworks6 have been identified till now. Eight kinds (dia, sra, qtz, nbo, cds, gis, lcv and lvt) of four-connected uninodal topologies were observed in the Inorganic Crystal Structure Database7 for three-dimensional coordination networks, among which only nbo and cds have square-planar nodes.

In this paper, we designed a long-chain ligand, bis(4-(4H-1,2,4-triazol-4-yl)phenyl)ethane (btpe), which was coordinated to the Cd(II) salt to afford an unusual four-fold interpenetrating complex {[Cd(btpe)3](ClO4)2·6H2O}n (1). The addition of secondary ligand terephthalic acid (H2bdc) led to a six-fold interpenetrating network with a cdl net [Cd(btpe)(bdc)(H2O)2]n (2). As far as we know, complex 1 shows the first example of class Ib in the four-fold interpenetrating pcu net, while the topology of complex 2 is a rare kind of net with four-connected square-planar nodes. The unusual interpenetrating mode in 1 has been studied through the comparison with typical interpenetrating pcu nets, and the topological character of the cdl type network has been analyzed and compared with nbo and cds nets.

Experimental section

All commercially available solvents and reagents were used as received without further purification. Cd(ClO4)2·6H2O was synthesized by the general method. Bis(4-(4H-1,2,4-triazol-4-yl)phenyl)ethane was prepared in 60% yield by reacting 4,4′-diaminodiphenylethane and N,N′-dimethylformamide azine dihydrochloride according to the literature procedure.8

Physical measurements

The C, H, N elemental analyses were performed on a Perkin-Elmer 240 C H N elemental analyzer. The infrared spectra on KBr pellets were obtained on a Bruker Vector 22 FT-IR instrument in the 400–4000 cm−1 region. The luminescent spectra of the solid samples were recorded at room temperature on an Aminco Bowman Series 2 spectrophotometer with a xenon arc lamp as the light source. The gravimetric analyses were carried out in a simultaneous thermoanalysis apparatus NETZSCH TG 209 instrument under nitrogen atmosphere with a heating rate of 10 °C min−1 in the range 22 to 800 °C. Powder X-ray diffraction patterns were measured at ambient temperature using a D/Max-2500 X-ray diffractometer with Cu-Kα radiation.

X-ray crystallography

The single crystal X-ray diffraction of complexes were recorded on an Oxford Diffractometer Supernova TM, which was equipped with graphite monochromatic Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å). All the structures were solved by direct methods with SHELXS-97 and refined by full-matrix least-squares on F2 using the SHELXL-97 program suite.9 The disordered atoms in 1 (C3 C4 C5 C6 C7 C8 and C9), were refined using C atoms split over two sites, with a total occupancy of 1. All non-hydrogen atoms were found and refined with anisotropic factors. Hydrogen atoms on the aromatic rings and the carbon atoms were placed at calculated positions with an appropriate riding model (AFIX 43 for aromatic CH, AFIX 23 for CH2). CCDC reference numbers are 848257 (1) and 848258 (2), respectively.† The crystallographic data and structure refinement parameters for 1 and 2 are listed in Table 1, selected bond lengths and angles are given in ESI Table S1.† All the measured X-ray powder diffractograms were matched with simulated ones from the single crystal data (ESI Fig. S1)†.

Results and discussion

Structural description

Single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis shows that 1 belongs to the trigonal system with Rc space group. The Cd(II) center has a coordination environment with an octahedral geometry (Fig. 1a), which is constructed by six nitrogen atoms from six btpe ligands with a length of 2.336(3) Å for the Cd–N bond. Neighbouring Cd(II) ions are bridged by btpe to form a 3D framework (Fig. 1b).
Fig. 1 (a) The coordination environment of Cd(II) ion in 1; (b) the 3D framework of 1; (c) pcu topology of 1; (d) the unusual four-fold interpenetration in 1.

From the topological point of view, when btpe and Cd(II) ions are considered as linear connectors and as six-connected nodes, respectively, the framework of 1 becomes a pcu cubic net (Fig. 1c). Four identical 3D nets are interlocked with each other, leading to a unique four-fold interpenetrating architecture (Fig. 1d) which has never been shown before in pcu topology.

We analyzed all the reported four-fold pcu networks and the present structure with the program TOPOS10 to assign the class of interpenetration. In the three classes (see Table 2) with five subclasses independently of the network topology,11 FECKAA and PUPNAQ belong to Class Ia, PORWAV belongs to class II, FECJUT and UHUPET are of the non-equivalent type 4(2 + 2). Interestingly, the structure of 1 shows a new type of four-fold pcu net, which belongs to class Ib.

In the interpenetrating network topology, only six examples (three pairs) belong to class Ib; they are GEHQAL/GEHQOZ as four-fold sra topology,12a HUYJUG/HUYKAN as four-fold srs topology12b and WURMUR/WURNAY as eight-fold srs topology.12c

Normally, when two pcu networks interpenetrate, each cubic cavity interpenetrates the other eight ones in the second network (Fig. 2a) which belongs to class Ia. Different from the common interpenetration mode, a cubic cage of one network is interlocked with four instead of the normal eight different cages of a second network in 1 (Fig. 2b).


Fig. 2 (a) The typical interpenetrating pcu mode; (b) the unusual interpenetrating mode in 1.

Complex 2 crystallizes in the monoclinic system with P2/c space group. The ligand bdc acts not only as an assistant ligand to afford a new type of topology but also as the counter anion to substitute ClO4− in 2. The coordination geometry around the Cd(II) center is a slightly distorted octahedron (Fig. 3a). Two nitrogen atoms from btpe ligands and two oxygen atoms from bdc2− ligands construct the equatorial plane of the octahedron, and the water molecules occupy the axial coordinate sites with Cd–O5 and Cd–O6 bond lengths of 2.312(2) and 2.368(3) Å, respectively. Each btpe ligand bridges two Cd(II) ions by its triazole nitrogen atoms, while each bdc2− connects two Cd(II) ions as well by a bi-monodentate coordination mode to form an infinite three-dimensional (3D) structure (Fig. 3b).


Fig. 3 (a) The coordination environment of Cd(II) ion in 2, (Symmetry codes: (A) −x, y, 0.5 − z; (B) 1 − x, 2 − y, −z; (C) x − 1, 1 + y, z − 1); (b) the 3D framework of 2; (c) cdl topology of 2; (d) the six-fold interpenetration cdl net in 2.

If each Cd(II) ion acts as a four-connected square-planar node, the topology of the 3D framework can be rationalized in terms of a uninodal four-connected topology with the point symbol of (42.6.83) (Fig. 3c). The type of the net displayed by 2 has been named as cdl notation. The single cdl frameworks in 2 contain a large vacant space, which is filled via mutual interpenetration of identical 3D frameworks to generate a six-fold interpenetrating architecture (Fig. 3d). To the best of our knowledge, only one coordination polymer with this topology has been reported till now,13 which shows a three-fold interpenetrating structure. 4-connected network have been intensively investigated. Normally, the planar nodes prefer to assemble into a 2D (4,4) net rather than the less common 3D nbo, cds and lvt. In the four-connected uninodal types only nbo and cds nets have four-connected square-planar nodes.

From the pure topological point of view, in the nbo net each square-planar node is connected to four same neighbors with a 90° twist along every connection. In the cds net, each square-planar node is connected to two kinds of adjacent nodes with the ratio of 2:2. Differently, in cdl nets each square-planar node is connected with three other neighbors with the same type as well as another 90°-twisted square-planar node (Fig. 4).


Fig. 4 One of the square-planar nodes and its four neighbors in nbo net (a), cds net (b) and cdl net (c).

2 possesses a rare topology and appears to be close to the cds net. When two equal nodes in the cdl topology are taken as one new node, the cdl topology will be transformed into cds topology. If the four square-planar nodes are extended to six-connected nodes, the cdl and cds nets will be changed into the well-known six-connected pcu net. The relationships among cdl, cds and pcu nets are schematically illustrated in Fig. 5.


Fig. 5 (a) The relationship between cdl and cds; (b) the relationship between pcu and cdl (breaking the red dotted edges); (c) the relationship between pcu and cds (breaking the red dotted edges).

Thermal and fluorescent properties

TGA of 1 shows a 5.35% weight loss between 88 and 130 °C in agreement with the corresponding calculated values of 5.63% due to the loss of dissociative water molecules. For complex 2, the weight loss of 5.65% in the range of 80–150 °C corresponds to the loss of the weakly coordinated water molecules (calculated, 5.72%). The frameworks of complexes are stable up to about 320 °C based on the TGA plot (ESI Fig. S2).†

The luminescence properties of 1, 2 and free ligands are investigated in the solid state at room temperature (Fig. 6). The emission peaks of the btpe ligand are observed at ca. 403 nm and 424 nm upon excitation of 350 nm, which could be attributed to the π*–π transitions. The emission peak of H2bdc is at 383 nm (λex = 340 nm), which could be attributed to the π*–n transitions. For 1, the emissions occur at 363 nm and 382 nm upon excitation at 320 nm, which shows a blueshift of 38 nm compared with that of btpe ligand. The blueshift relative to the free ligand may be assigned to the emission of ligand-to-metal charge transfer (LMCT).142 exhibits fluorescent emissions at 389 nm and 409 nm upon excitation at 340 nm, which could be assigned to the intraligand charge transfer of btpe. The π*–n transition of bdc2− ligands in both 1 and 2 show almost no contribution to the fluorescent effect compared with the π*–π transition of btpe or fluorescence quenching of carboxyl groups of H2bdc.15


Fig. 6 The emission (red line) and excitation (black line) luminescent spectra of complexes and free ligands in the solid state at room temperature.

Conclusions

Two 3D complexes with different topologies and framework structures have been successfully constructed based on the ligand btpe without and with the introduction of a secondary ligand under solvothermal conditions. The results provide an intriguing pcu net with a new four-fold interpenetrating mode Ib and a six-fold interpenetrating cdl nets. The new ligand, btpe, is proven to be useful for interpenetration and the influence of secondary ligands for the multiple of interpenetrations has been studied as well.
