    Abstract

    Six d10 metal metal-organic coordination polymers, {[Zn2(Htpim)2(tp)2]·2H2O}n (1), {[Cd3(H0.5tpim)4(pa)2(H2O)2]·5H2O}n (2), [Zn(Htpim)2(Htma)]n (3), {[Zn4(Htpim)4(ip)4]·2H2O}n (4), [Cd(Htpim)(ca)]n (5), and {[Cd4(Htpim)2(d-ca)4(H2O)2]·5H2O}n (6) (Htpim = 2,4,5-tri(4-pyridyl)-imidazole, H2tp = terephthalic acid, H2pa = phthalic acid, H3tma = trimesic acid, H2ip = isophthalic acid, H2ca = racemic camphor acid, d-H2ca = d-camphor acid), have been prepared and structurally characterized. In all complexes, the rigid ligand Htpim exhibits different coordination modes and leads to the formation of various architectures. Complex 1 shows a two-dimensional (2D) network with (6,3) topology, where hydrogen-bonding interactions further link these sheets to a three-dimensional (3D) supramolecular structure. Complex 2 exhibits a 2D layer with (53)(54) network topology. Complex 3 is a corrugated 2D layer with (4,4) topology, and the separated layers are connected through weak π−π stacking interactions to generate a 3D supramolecular architecture. Complex 4 is a rhombic 2D layer with (4,4) topology, and the 3D overlapped array is formed by hydrogen-bonding and π−π stacking interactions. A 3D diamondoid framework with 4-fold interpenetration is found in 5. Complex 6 contains a complicated 3D framework, and the vacancy is occupied by the guest water molecules. Their luminescent properties have been investigated in the solid state.

    Synopsis

    A series of new coordination polymers with diverse architectures have been synthesized by mixed multi-pyridyl ligand Htpim and polycarboxylate ligands, where different coordination modes are observed for ligand Htpim. Complexes 1 and 2 show a two-dimensional (2D) network with (6,3) and (53)(54) topology. Complexes 3 and 4 are a corrugated and rhombic 2D layer with (4,4) topology. Complexes 5 and 6 contain a three-dimensional (3D) diamondoid framework with 4-fold interpenetration and a complicated 3D framework. The luminescent properties of these complexes have also been investigated in the solid state.

    Metal-organic coordination polymers on the basis of self-assembly of metal ions and multifunctional ligands have been rapidly developed because of their fascinating structural diversities and potential applications in the fields of catalysis, gas absorption, chirality, luminescence, nonlinear optics, and magnetism.(1-5) The rational design and construction of coordination polymers greatly depends on the judicious selection of the organic ligands and proper choice of metal centers. Polycarboxylate and multipyridyl ligands have attracted long lasting research interest, due to the variety of coordination modes and structural features. For example, the aromatic polycarboxyl ligands, such as phthalic, isophthalic, terephthalic, and trimesic acid, and the rigid multi-pyridyl ligands, such as 4,4′-bipyridine, 2,4,6-tri(4-pyridyl)-1,3,5-triazine, and 4-amino-3,5-bis(4-pyridyl)-1,2,4-triazole have been widely used to construct different coordination polymers.(6, 7) The multi-imidazol ligand, which has diverse coordination fashion resulting from conformation variation, also has been documented.(8)
    The ligand, 2,4,5-tri(4-pyridyl)-imidazole (Htpim), is chosen to construct novel coordination polymers in view of its following characteristics: (1) It has three pyridines for coordination and there will be different coordination modes. As illustrated in Scheme 2, the pyridyl groups can adopt the equivalent bidentate (AA), inequable bidentate (AB), or tridentate coordination modes (AAB), respectively. The angles subtended at the center of the five-membered heterocyclic spacer and two pyridyl N-donors are 82°, 138°, and 140°, respectively. The diverse coordination motif is favored to form a different structure. (2) The imino donor group from imidazole may be completely or partially deprotonated to compensate for the charge. (3) The imidazol nitrogen atoms are useful to form hydrogen bonds and stabilize supramolecular assemblies. (4) π−π stacking interactions resulting from uncoordinated pyridyl and other aromatic rings are good for further stabilizing the solid-state structure.
    Recently, metal-organic coordination polymers built from mixed ligands of carboxylate groups and pyridyl groups have been extensively employed in the construction of polymeric frameworks, which is attributed to incorporating virtues of different functional groups as well as more easily getting architecture controlled by changing one of the above two ligands.(9) However, coordination polymers of the Htpim ligand as the mixed ligands have never been reported so far.
    In this paper, with the use of Htpim and different polycarboxylate ligands as spacers, and d10 metal ions (Zn2+ and Cd2+) as metal centers, six new coordination polymers, {[Zn2(Htpim)2(tp)2]·2H2O}n (1), {[Cd3(H0.5tpim)4(pa)2(H2O)2]·5H2O}n (2), [Zn(Htpim)2(Htma)]n (3), {[Zn4(Htpim)4(ip)4]·2H2O}n (4), [Cd(Htpim)(ca)]n (5), and {[Cd4(Htpim)2(d-ca)4(H2O)2]·5H2O}n (6) (H2tp = terephthalic acid, H2pa = phthalic acid, H3tma = trimesic acid, H2ip = isophthalic acid, H2ca = racemic camphor acid, d-H2ca = d-camphor acid), have been successfully prepared and characterized (Scheme 1). The coordination modes of the ligand have been discussed in detail. In addition, the luminescent properties of the complexes have also been investigated in the solid state.

    General Procedures

    All chemicals were commercially available and used without further purification. The bridging ligand Htpim was synthesized according to the literature method.(10) Elemental analyses for C, H, and N were performed on a CHN-O-Rapid analyzer and an Elementar Vario MICRO analyzer. The IR spectra were taked on a Bruker Vector 22 FT-IR spectrometer with KBr discs in the 4000−400 cm−1 range. Thermogravimetric analyses were collected on a Perkin-Elmer Pyris 1 TGA analyzer from room temperature to 750 °C with a heating rate of 20 °C/min under nitrogen. Fluorescent emission spectra for the solid samples were recorded on an AMINCO Bowman Series2 luminescence spectrometer.

The structures were solved by direct methods and refined with the full-matrix least-squares technique using SHELXTL version 5.1.(12) Anisotropic thermal parameters were assigned to all non-hydrogen atoms.     Syntheses

    Single crystals of 1−6 were synthesized under hydrothermal conditions by treating M(NO3)2 (M = Zn, Cd), Htpim, the corresponding carboxylic acid (H2tp, H2pa, H3tma, H2ip, H2ca, and d-H2ca) and the alkali (NaOH or DMF) in different solvents. By measuring the pH values in the final reaction solutions, we find the pH values of the solutions are associated with the coordination modes of the ligand. For complexes 1−3 with the “AA” coordination mode, the pH values of final solutions are 5.32, 5.00, and 5.90, respectively. For complexes 4 and 5 with “AB” coordination mode, the pH values are 6.57 and 6.68, respectively. However, the final pH value for complex 6 with “AAB” coordination mode is 9.00. All products are air stable and insoluble in common organic solvents, such as methanol, ethanol, acetonitrile, acetone, etc.

    Description of Crystal Structures

    Structure of {[Zn2(Htpim)2(tp)2]·2H2O}n (1)

    The X-ray crystallographic analysis reveals that 1 is a 2D coordination network. In the asymmetrical unit, there are two Zn(II) atoms, two Htpim ligands, two tp2− ligands, and two solvated water molecules. There are two Zn(II) atoms with the same coordination environment (Figure 1a). Each Zn(II) atom is coordinated by two pyridyl nitrogen atoms from Htpim ligands with the “AA” coordination motif, and two oxygen atoms from tp2− ligands to furnish a distorted [ZnN2O2] tetrahedral geometry. The bond lengths and bond angles around Zn1 are somewhat different from those around Zn2. As depicted in Figure 1b, the tp2− ligands bridge to the Zn(II) atoms to form a 1D polymeric chain. Each [Zn(tp)]n polymeric chain is further linked by Htpim ligands to construct a 2D layer motif with (6,3) topology (Figure 1c). The Zn···Zn distances separated by the carboxylate groups of tp and a pair of Htpim are 10.871 and 9.393 Å, respectively. Moreover, two solvated water molecules, the uncoordinated carboxylate oxygen atoms and N atoms of imidazole are interlinked through O−H···O and N−H···O hydrogen bonds, ultimately leading to a 3D supramolecular framework (Figure 1d). The O9 and O10 atoms of water molecules and adjacent carboxylate oxygen atoms (O5, O3a, O2b, O8c) of the tp2− ligands are involved in the formation of O−H···O hydrogen bonds. Simultaneously, the O9d and O10e atoms of water molecules also interact with N2 and N8 atoms of the imidazale to afford N−H···O hydrogen bonds. The hydrogen-bonding interactions play an important role in stabilizing the solid-state structure.

    Structure of {[Cd3(H0.5tpim)4(pa)2(H2O)2]·5H2O}n (2)

    In the asymmetric unit of 2, there are three-quarter Cd(II) atom, one H0.5tpim ligand, half pa2− ligand, half-coordinated water molecule, and one and a quarter solvated water molecules. As shown in Figure 2a, two Cd(II) atoms have different coordination environments. The Cd1 atom has an octahedral coordination sphere, being surrounded by four pyridyl nitrogen atoms from H0.5tpim ligands with the “AA” coordination motif in the basal plane and two oxygen atoms from aqua molecules at the apical sites. The Cd2 atom is also in a distorted octahedral arrangement, in which the equatorial plane is formed by three oxygen atoms (O1, O2, O1c) from two carboxylate groups and one pyridyl nitrogen atom (N3) from the H0.5tpim ligand with the “AA” coordination motif. The axial positions are occupied by the other one oxygen atom (O2c) from the carboxylate group and the pyridyl nitrogen atom (N3c) from the H0.5tpim ligand with the “AA” mode. These Cd atoms are linked by H0.5tpim and pa2− to produce the Cd3(H0.5tpim)4(pa)2(H2O)2 trinuclear unit which extend in four directions around the crystallographic 4-fold axes in the [001] direction (Figure 2b). The repeating units are fused to each other to form a 2D layer with (53)(54) network topology (Figure 2c,d). Some O−H···O and N−H···N hydrogen-bonding interactions are also observed in 2. The intermolecular O−H···O hydrogen bonds are formed by O5 atom of the water molecule and the O1f atom of carboxylate, O7 and O6 atoms of the water molecule, O6 atom and their symmetric equivalents, respectively. The N−H···N hydrogen bond is generated from the N2 atom in imidazale and the hydrogen atom from N5e in the uncoordinated pyridyl rings.

    Structure of [Zn(Htpim)2(Htma)]n (3)

    The asymmetric unit of complex 3 is made up of one Zn(II) atom, one Htpim ligand, and one Htma2− ligand. As illustrated in Figure 3a, each Zn(II) atom adopts a distorted trigonal-bipyramidal coordination sphere consisting of two pyridyl nitrogen atoms from Htpim ligands with the “AA” coordination motif, three oxygen atoms from carboxylate groups. The N3, O2b, and O6c comprise the equatorial plane, and O1 and N4a are located in the axial positions. The carboxylate-oxygen atoms of Htma2− ligand connect two Zn(II) atoms to form the Zn2O2 binuclear unit with an 8-membered metallacycle. The dimers are bridged by carboxylate groups from other Htma2− ligands to give a 1D ladder-like polymer. The Htpim acts as a bridging bidentate ligand and further links such a ladder-like polymer to generate a network that is topologically equivalent to a (4,4) network (Figure 3b,c). Two different metallacycles with 4-connect node are found in 3. The larger one shows the Zn···Zn diagonal distance of 12.465 and 15.119 Å, while the distances are 7.632 and 13.331 Å for the smaller one. Unlike the other 3D supramolecular achitecture formed by π−π stacking interactions from adjacent layers,(13) in 3, the separated layers are self-organized to generate a 3D supramolecular achitecture through weak π−π interactions of the uncoordinated pyridyl rings with a centroid−centroid separation of 3.917 Å (Figure 3d).

    Structure of {[Zn4(Htpim)4(ip)4]·2H2O}n (4)

    In complex 4, the asymmetric unit consists of one Zn(II) atom, one Htpim ligand, one ip2− ligand, and two solvated water molecules. As shown in Figure 4a, each Zn(II) atom sits in a distorted octahedral coordination sphere containing two pyridyl nitrogen atoms from Htpim ligands with the “AB” coordination motif and four oxygen atoms of chelating ip2− ligands. It is noted that the coordination mode of Htpim ligand in 4 is different from those of complexes 1−3. Each ip2− and Htpim ligand connects the Zn2+ ions into a 2D sheet with (4,4) topology (Figure 4b,c). The 2D network structure consists of rhombic grids in which the Zn···Zn distance bridged by ip2− and Htpim are 9.267 and 13.648 Å, respectively. As shown in Figure 4d, the strong hydrogen-bonding interactions are found between carboxylates, imidazole groups, and solvated water molecules (N2−H···O3b, N2 from imidazale and O3b from ip2−; O5−H··· O1c, O5 from the solvated water molecule and O1c from ip2−). Moreover, π−π stacking interactions are found between imidazolyl rings, uncoordinated pyridyl rings, and phenyl rings with centroid−centroid separations of 3.807 and 3.905 Å, respectively (Figure 4e). The above intermolecular interactions induce the 2D sheet to a 3D overlapped array (Figure 4d,e), suggesting that the coeffects of hydrogen-bonding and π−π interactions are in favor of stabilizing the extended structure.(14)

    Structure of [Cd(Htpim)(ca)]n (5)

    The asymmetry unit of 5 contains one Cd(II) atom, one Htpim ligand, and one ca2− ligand. As depicted in Figure 5a, each Cd(II) atom is six-coordinated to two pyridyl nitrogen atoms from Htpim ligands with the “AB” coordination motif and four oxygen atoms of ca2− ligands giving a distorted [CdN2O4] octahedral geometry. The coordination modes of Htpim ligand in complexes 4 and 5 are identical. Given that the chelating carboxylates are treated as one connecting point, Cd atoms also have a pseudotetrahedral environment. Each Cd atom is connected to four adjacent Cd atoms through the Htpim and ca2− bridges to result in a 3-D diamond-like framework containing large adamantanoid cages (Figure 5b). The Cd···Cd distances separated by the ca2− and Htpim ligand are 10.007 and 14.436 Å, respectively, and the Cd···Cd···Cd angles range from 91.8° to 144.9°, which is a significant deviation from the 109.5° expected for an idealized diamond network. In order to minimize the big void cavities and stabilize the framework, the potential void cavities are filled by the other three identical networks, resulting in 4-fold mutually interpenetrating diamondoid array (Figure 5c). A similar 4-fold interpenetrating structure constructed from H2ca and flexible pydidyl ligand tmdpy has been reported before.(15)

    Structure of {[Cd4(Htpim)2(d-ca)4(H2O)2]·5H2O}n (6)

    In the asymmetry unit of 6, there are two Cd(II) atoms, one Htpim ligand, two d-ca2− ligands, and two and half solvated water molecules. As illustrated in Figure 6a, the Cd1 atom lies in a distorted octahedral coordination environment. The apical positions are occupied by one pyridyl nitrogen atom (N3) from Htpim ligand with the “AA” coordination motif and one carboxylate oxygen atom (O2) from d-ca2− ligand. The basal plane is completed by three oxygen atoms (O6, O7c, O8) from d-ca2− ligand and the other one pyridyl nitrogen atom (N5b) from Htpim ligand with the “AA” coordination mode. In contrast to the atom Cd1, the Cd2 atom with octahedral sphere is surrounded by four carboxylate oxygen from d-ca2− ligands, one pyridyl nitrogen atom from Htpim ligand with the “B” coordination motif, and one coordinated water molecule. It is noteworthy that the Htpim ligand has acted as either the bidentate ligand or the ligand with tridentate coordination motif. Meanwhile, the two Cd(II) atoms are linked together by a μ2-O bridge from the carboxylate oxygen atoms of the d-ca2− ligand to afford a Cd2 dimer with Cd···Cd separations of 3.459 Å, which is extended to a 1D zigzag chain. The 2D layer is achieved by the carboxylate groups on two sides of another d-ca2− ligand coordinating to Cd atoms of two adjacent 1D chains (Figure 6b). Then the 2D sheets are further pillared by Htpim ligand to give rise to a complicated 3D framework, in which the shortest Cd···Cd vertical distance between the adjacent layers is 9.114 Å (Figure 6c). Compared with 5, the mutually interpenetration is not found in 6 because of the vacancy of the 3D framework occupied by the guest water molecules. Additionally, the versatile strong hydrogen bonds are found in the cavity (Figure 6d). There are four types of hydrogen-bonding interactions: O−H···O interactions between the guest water molecules, O−H···O interactions between the coordinated aqua molecule and the guest water molecules, O−H···O interactions between the carboxylate oxygen atom and water molecules, and N−H···O interaction between the nitrogen atom of imidazale and the guest water molecules.
    As illustrated in Scheme 3, complexes 1−6 are 2D or 3D frameworks with diversified architectures. In complexes 1−3, the Htpim ligand is in the “AA” bidentate coordination motif which generates a 2D layer with distinct (6,3), (53)(54), and (4,4) topology. Nevertheless, the Htpim ligand with the “AB” bidentate coordination motif affords a 2D rhombic network with (4,4) topology for 4 and a 3-D diamond-like framework for 5. However, the Htpim ligand with the “AAB” tridentate coordination motif produces a complicated 3D framework for 6.

    Coordination Modes of the Ligand

    As illustrated in Scheme 2, the pyridyl groups in Htpim can adopt the “AA”, “AB”, or “AAB” coordination motif, respectively. The main reasons for different coordination modes observed for the Htpim ligand are the following: first, the different coordination modes of the ligand may be influenced by the pH values in the final reaction solutions. Second, different coordination modes may result in the different coordination capability. The different coordination capability further depends on the different electron-donating capability of ligand. For the Htpim ligand, the electron-donating capability of two pyridyl nitrogen atoms in “A” positions is different from the one in “B” position owing to the different electronic effects of nitrogen atom of imidazole,(16) which accounts for the different coordination modes. Finally, the different coordination modes may be also affected by the difference of the steric geometry of ancillary polycarboxylate ligands.(17) Therefore, the structural variation of these mixed-ligand polymers should result from diversified coordination modes and ancillary polycarboxylate ligands with differently oriented carboxyl groups.

    Thermogravimetric Analyses

    Thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) have been measured for all complexes. As shown in Figure 7, for complex 1, a weight loss of 3.78% in the 150−450 °C corresponded to the release of the lattice water molecules (calcd 3.59%). Then the decomposition of the residual components started beyond 500 °C. Complex 2 displays two main steps of weight losses: the first step experienced the loss of the crystalline water molecules between 70 and 253 °C (4.20% weight loss observed; 4.53% calculated). The second step occurred between 254 and 316 °C with the loss of two coordinated water molecules (2.06% weight loss observed; 1.81% calculated). For 3, the network remained intact until it was heated to 460 °C and then began to collapse. The total observed weight loss at 730 °C is 38.90%, and the final residuals were not characterized. The loss of the lattice water molecule for 4 is observed in the ranges 150−200 °C (2.01% weight loss observed; 1.67% calculated), and the decomposition of network occurring at ca. 430 °C. The 3D framework of 5 was stable up to 400 °C. Complex 6 also shows two main steps of weight losses: the weight loss of 5.01% (calcd 4.59%) between 27 and 110 °C resulted from the loss of free water molecules, and then the weight loss of 2.18% (calcd 1.84%) between 111 to 365 °C was attributed to the release of coordination molecules.

    Photoluminescent Properties

    The photoluminescent properties of compounds 1−6 and the free ligand have been carried out in the solid state at room temperature. No obvious photoluminescence was observed for 2 and 3 under the experimental conditions. As shown in Figure 8, complex 1 displays two emission maxima at 427 and 483 nm when excitated at 360 nm. Complex 4 shows a very strong emission band located at 434 nm with excitation wavelength of 360 nm. Upon excitation at 400 nm, the intense emissions are observed at 455 nm for 5 and 478 nm for 6. The free ligand exhibits two emission maxima at 411 and 496 nm upon excitation at 376 nm. The emissions could be attributed to intraligand fluorescent emission.(18) Compared with the other compounds, the significant red shift of the emission occurring in 6 may be ascribed to the differences of the metal ions and the coordination environment around them.(19) The fluorescent intensity for coordination polymers are much stronger compared to the free ligands, possibly resulted from the enhancement of rigidity of the ligand after the coordination effect and the reduction of the loss of energy by radiationless decay.(20) The fluorescent intensity of 4 is much stronger than compound 1. Owing to the analogical emissions of free H2tp and H2ip,(21) it may be mainly assigned to the different coordination mode of the Htpim ligand in these compounds. It is also proved by the structural results as described above. For 1, the dihedral angles between the pair pyridyl rings coordinating to metal ions and the imidazole ring are 40.1° and 41.8°, respectively, while for 4 the dihedral angles of these are 25.6° and 28.7°, respectively. It suggests that the coplanar extent of the Htpim ligand in complex 4 is higher than in complex 1, which is beneficial to the enhancement of the extent of π-conjugated system and the luminescent intensity.(22)

    Six new coordination polymers have been synthesized under hydrothermal conditions by using a versatile multi-pyridyl ligand Htpim and various polycarboxylate ligands, where the rigid ligand Htpim exhibits “AA” bidentate, “AB” bidentate, or “AAB” tridentate coordination modes. The 2D or 3D frameworks with diversified architectures and other interesting features are found, such as supramolecular hydrogen bonding and π−π stacking interactions, network interpenetration, different dimensional framework, and molecular topology. The results suggest that the structural diversification of coordination polymers may result from the different coordination modes and the coeffects from the ancillary polycarboxylate ligands. Our current studies on the rigid ligand with diversified coordination modes are helpful to further understand how to rationally design and construct diversified coordination polymers through the intelligent selection of the organic ligands.
