Abstract

A new protonated borophosphate (H3O)Mg(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·H2O (1) was synthesized under mild hydrothermal conditions and characterized by singleâcrystal Xâray diffraction, FTIR spectroscopy and TGâDTA. The compound crystallizes in the hexagonal system, space group P6(1)22 (No 178), a = 9.4462(7) Ã, c = 15.759(2) Ã, V = 1217.8(2) Ã3, and Z = 6. There exist infinite helical {[BP2O8]3â} ribbons built up from cornerâsharing PO4 and BO4 tetrahedra, which are connected by MgO4(H2O)2 leading to an infinite threeâdimensional openâframework. The H3O+ ions are located at the free thread of the helical ribbons, whereas crystallized water occupy the channels of the helical ribbons. The dehydration of the compound occurs at a higher temperature which is presumably due to the anisotropic hydrogen bonds in the crystal structure. The luminescent properties of the compound were studied.

Introduction

Borophosphates are a new class of materials, which contain BâO groups as well as PâO groups. These groups may be further linked by sharing common oxygen atoms except PâOâP bonds to form different anionic partial structures, such as oligomeric units, chains, ribbons, layers and threeâdimensional frameworks [1â7]. Although systematic investigations on borophosphates started from only a few years ago, considerable progress has been made in both synthesis and potential applications in the fields of molecular sieves, catalyst, nonlinear optical materials, etc [8â13].

Borophosphates of the general formula MIxMIIy(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·zH2O (MI = Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, NH4; MII = Mg, Mn, Fe, Co, Ni, Zn, Cu, Cd; x = 0.35â1, y = 1â1.3, z = 0.2â1) [5, 14â20] are a class of typical examples with 61 or 65 screw axis, which have a characteristic reversible dehydration process to give microporous chiral compounds with CZP topology. Many members of this family have been synthesized, however, to the best of our knowledge, only three of them containing H3O+, namely (H3O)Zn(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·H2O 21 and (H3O)0.5M1.25(H2O)2 [BP2O8]Â·H2O (M = Co2+ and Mn2+), have been reported so far because of the difficulty of the synthesis 22. In this work, we report the synthesis and crystal structure of a new borophosphate (H3O)Mg(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·H2O (1), which contains H3O+ and compare the crystal structure with other analogues. We also discuss the influence of hydrogen interactions on the framework and the ionic radii of MII on the position and occupancies of MI. Moreover, a special thermal behavior was found and the luminescence properties were investigated for the first time in the series of borophosphates.

The interatomic distances and OâPâO angles in the PO4 tetrahedra are in the range 1.502â1.567 Ã and 106.05â111.11Â°, respectively. The MgâO distances are in the range 2.047â2.059 Ã. Two PO4 units and two BO4 units are arranged in an alternating fashion to form fourâmembered rings, whereas the borate groups share oxygen atoms with adjacent phosphate tetrahedra and the PO4 units are connected only to two BO4 units. The connectivity of these fourâmembered rings through common borate groups creates the tetrahedral ribbons of{[BP2O8]3â}, which are arranged around 61 screw axes to form helices. In such a way, each BO4 group lies on the central line of the ribbons, whereas the PO4 groups occupy the borders of the ribbons with two terminal oxygen atoms, which act as ligands along with O3W from two water molecules to complete the octahedral coordination around the magnesium site. Neighboring ribbons are interconnected by MgO4(H2O)2 octahedra, which leads to an infinite threeâdimensional openâframework.

The channels of the helical ribbons in the title compound are fully occupied by water molecules O1W, similar to the water positions in MIxMIIy(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·zH2O (MI = Na, K; MII = Mg, Mn, Fe, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn) [5, 14â20]. In order to balance the negative charge of the helical ribbons, one proton was required per formula. The protons indwell the crystal structure in the form of the protonated water, which was located within the free thread of the helical ribbons and formed strong hydrogen bond with the oxygen atoms of the framework (Figure  2).

In the literature, the influence of the ionic radii of MI on the position and occupancies of MI in MIxMIIy(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·zH2O was discussed [18, 19]. It is clear that the positions of the H3O+ ions in 1 (Figure  3a) are quite similar to the sites of Na+ ions (ionic radii = 112 pm) in NaMg(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·H2O (3) (Figure  3c), whereas in the compound (NH4)Cd(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·0.72H2O (4) (Figure  3d) with intermediate sized NH4+ ions (ionic radii = 143 pm), the ammonium ions are shifted closer to the out wall of helical channels. At the same time, it is noted that the c/a values of this type of structure in the compounds containing Na+ and K+ are about 1.66â1.67, whereas in compound 1 the c/a value amounts to 1.66, which may be another evidence why H3O+ ions in 1 and Na+ ions in 3 have the similar position. It is worth noting that the occupation of the H3O+ ions in the title compound is significantly different from its analogue, (H3O)Zn(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·H2O (2). In compound 2, the H3O+ ions are positioned inside the micropores enclosed by helical ribbons and octahedral (Figure  3b). So, compound 1 is a very good example to prove that the MII ions with different ionic radii also play an important influence on the position and occupancies of the MI ions in MIxMIIy(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·zH2O.

Compared with other analogues with MI = Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, one of the noticeable characteristics is that the H3O+ ions are able to form strong hydrogen bonds through interactions with framework oxygen atoms to stable crystal structure rather than by ionic bonds. Another difference is that the water molecules (O1W) located in the channels of the helical ribbons do not form hydrogen bonds with each other along the helix, but form hydrogen bonds with framework oxygen atoms on the ab plane. Therefore, we speculate that both the H3O+ ions and the water molecules (O1W) play a crucial role in the formation and the stabilization of the structure. Furthermore, the H3O+ ions may act as structure directing ions to control the hydrothermal assembly of polymetric anion network, as with in the compound 2 21. Infrared Spectrum

The FTIR spectrum of the title compound is shown in Figure  4. The bands around 1198, 1159, 959, 576 and 491 cmâ1 are characteristic of PO4. The bands at 1105, 1016 and 940 cmâ1 in the spectra are in agreement with the existence of BO4. The absence of the peaks at about 1400â1200 cmâ1 prove the absence of the BO3 functional group in the structure, whereas the absence of band for PâOâP at around 740 cmâ1 is in agreement with the postulate by Kniep et al. that in borophosphate compounds PâOâP connection is not observed 8. The band at 1643 cmâ1 is assigned to the HâOâH bending mode, which shows that the compound contains crystal water. The OâH stretching vibration is observed at 3348 cmâ1.

Thermal Analysis

The thermal behavior of of (H3O)Mg(H2O)2[BP2O8]Â·H2O (1) is shown in Figure  5. The TG curve of 1 showed that the compound was stable up to about 170 Â°C. On further heating, a twoâstep weight loss was observed. The initial weight loss between 170 and 350 Â°C corresponds to the removal of one crystallized water and one coordinating water molecule (found: 11.28 %; calcd: 12.08 %). The second step, which occurrs between 350 and 700 Â°C, is attributed to the loss of another coordinating water molecule (found: 4.08 %; calcd: 6.04 %). The residue after the calcinations for 1 is amorphous and its phase is unidentified.

This explanation is confirmed by the DTA curve (Figure  5). There are three peaks in the DTA curve. The first endothermic peak, which appears at 246.41 Â°C is related to the dehydration of one crystallized and one coordinating water molecule. The second endothermic peak, which appears at 440.71 Â°C is related to the dehydration of another coordinating water molecule. The exothermic peak at 751.33 Â°C is related to the amorphous phase recrystallization.

If the thermal behavior of 1 is compared with analog compounds, we find that the dehydration temperature in 1 is higher than in other analogues, in which the dehydration begins at about 90 Â°C. This difference may be because all the water molecules form hydrogen bonds with each other or with framework oxygen atoms, which makes the crystal structure more stable.

Luminescence Analysis

The luminescence properties of 1 in the solid state were investigated at room temperature. As shown in Figure  6, compound 1 can be excited by light with a wavelength ranging from 250â300 nm, and two luminescence peaks occur with an emission maximum at 443 nm upon excitation at 268 nm. The luminescence properties may be originated from the oxygen vacancies of BO4 and PO4 units in the helical ribbons. This is the first investigation of the luminescence properties in the series of borophosphates.

Conclusions

A new magnesium borophosphate containing H3O+ was synthesized successfully by a mild hydrothermal reaction. Three kinds of water molecules are connected with framework oxygen atoms or each other by hydrogen bonds, and thus the crystal structure is more stable and the dehydration started at 170 Â°C. The title compound is also a good example to show the influence of MII on the structure. The luminescence properties of this series of borophosphates were studied for the first time. When excited at 268 nm, the compound exhibits two luminescence peaks with an emission maximum at 443 nm. Further work on application of 1 is in progress.
