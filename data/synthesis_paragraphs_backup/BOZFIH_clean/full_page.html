Introduction

Recently, coordination polymers (CPs) constructed from organic ligands and metal ions or clusters have received tremendous attention owing to their powerful attributes in terms of structural versatility and tunable properties in gas adsorption, storage, separation, catalysis, and so on.1,2 However, there have been few reports on the optoelectronic properties of CPs.3,4 The lightâharvesting properties and the absorption edge of CPs can be manipulated by rational design of the organic ligands5 thus to improve the capture of solar radiation and make them good candidates as semiconductors for power conversion in the photovoltaic field.

On this basis, in the present work we synthesized one rigid Nâdonor with a large aromatic conjugated system, 4â[4â(1Hâimidazolâ1âyl)phenyl]â2,6âdi(pyridinâ4âyl)pyridine (L) (Scheme 1, a).6 Usually, the absorption band of a large conjugated molecule with delocalized Ï electrons will shift bathochromically. Then, it is expected that the Lâbased CP will adsorb visible light to achieve visibleâlightâinduced charge separation and optoelectronic conversion. On the basis of L and two carboxylate coligands, 2,5âbis[3â²âcarboxylphenyl]pyridine (H2L1)7 and 1,3,5âbenzenetricarboxylic acid (H3BTC) (Scheme 1, b,âc), two novel complexes formulated as Cd3L(L1)3(H2O) (1) and Zn3L(BTC)2(H2O)Â·7H2O (2) were solvothermally synthesized and structurally characterized by singleâcrystal Xâray diffraction. Photoelectrochemical Behaviors of Complexes 1 and 2

The photoelectrochemical behavior of the two complexes was determined in a threeâelectrode cell with a Pt foil and a Ag/AgCl electrode as the counter electrode and reference electrode, respectively. Si wafers modified by the samples were used as the working electrodes, and they were tested independently under the same conditions. All the photocurrents were measured at the openâcircuit potential of 0 V versus Ag/AgCl. Figure 6 shows the photocurrent time plots for the modified Si wafers under chopped visibleâlight irradiation (650 nm > Î» > 350 nm).

As shown in Figure 6 (and Figures S12 and S13), steady photocurrents were observed if the complex 1â and 2âmodified Si electrodes and the bare Si wafers in neutral buffer solution (0.05 M, pH 6.8) were illuminated by visible light (650 nm > Î» > 350 nm). Complex 1 yielded a photocurrent density higher than that of the bare wafer, whereas the photocurrent density on the complex 2âmodified Si electrode was lower than that on the bare wafer. Herein, the order of photocurrent densities was complex 1 > Si wafer > complex 2. The order of photocurrent densities on complexes 1 and 2 followed the reverse order of their band gaps. However, the band gap of the bare Si wafer was ca. 1.1 eV, which is much lower than those of complexes 1 and 2; this indicates that the generation of the photocurrent is not only associated with the band gap but also with other factors such as the rate of photoinduced charge separation and transfer.

The rate of charge transfer can be analyzed by electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS).14 In the Bode plot, the highâfrequency region is ascribed to charge transfer at the counter electrode; the intermediateâfrequency region demonstrates charge transfer at the sample/electrolyte interface, whereas the lowâfrequency region corresponds to diffusion of ions in the electrolyte.14 In the Nyquist plot, the diameter of the quasiâsemicircle loop in the highâ and intermediateâfrequency range provides the magnitude of the chargeâtransfer impedance at the sample/electrolyte interface.15

Figures S14 and S15 show the Bode and Nyquist plots of the bare Si wafer, the complex 1âmodified Si electrode, and the 2âmodified Si electrode at E = 0 V versus Ag/AgCl with and without visibleâlight illumination (650 nm > Î» > 350 nm). It was found that the chargeâtransfer impedance at the interface of the electrolyte and the complex 1â and 2âmodified Si electrodes with visibleâlight irradiation was lower than that without irradiation, which indicated visibleâlight irradiation could promote charge transfer at the interface in the presence of complexes 1 and 2. Moreover, it was found that the chargeâtransfer impedance at the complex 1/electrolyte interface was lower than that at the bare Si wafer/electrolyte interface and at the complex 2/electrolyte interface, which indicated that interfacial chargeâtransfer impedance is the lowest in the presence of complex 1. Thus, complex 1 showed higher photocurrent density than the bare Si wafer and complex 2.

To investigate the process of photocurrent generation further, the MottâSchottky curves of the samples were measured. As shown in Figure S16, the capacitances of the spaceâcharge layers (SCLs) at the complex 1â and 2âmodified electrodes are different. As expected, the capacitance (C) at the 1âmodified electrode was higher than that at the 2âmodified electrode. Furthermore, the MottâSchottky plots of the two modified electrodes in the dark both show a linear relationship in the range of â0.05 to 0.05 V versus AgCl/Ag for Câ2 versus E with a positive slope (Figure S16), which indicates that complexes 1 and 2 are both nâtype semiconductors.18 Usually, the chargeâcarrier concentration (ND) can be calculated from the slope of the straight line, and it is inversely proportional to the slope.16 Herein, the order of the slopes of the MottâSchottky curves for the three photoelectrodes is complex 2 > Si wafer > complex 1 (Figure S16), which indicates that the chargeâcarrier concentrations in the photoelectrodes follow the reverse order of the slopes, that is, complex 1 > Si wafer > complex 2, which is in agreement with the order of the photocurrent observed on the three photoelectrodes. The difference in the photocurrent responses of the two complexes is ascribed to their different structures. It is probably associated with the different metal centers, such as the Cd3 and Zn2 units, and the different ligands in the structures. The detailed mechanism is under investigation.
