Abstract

A fluorine‚Äêfunctionalized HKUST‚Äê1 (HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF) has been synthesized by a solvothermal reaction between copper nitrate and a potassium salt of 2‚Äêfluoro‚Äê1,3,5‚Äêbenzenetricarboxylic acid (K[H2BTC‚ÄêF]). HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF shows nearly the same crystal structure as that of UHM‚Äê31, a known fluorinated HKUST‚Äê1. Because of its reduced surface area, HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF takes up smaller amounts of gases (N2 , H2 , CO2 ) than UHM‚Äê31 at 1‚Äâbar. After a water vapor adsorption measurement at 298‚ÄâK, HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF turned into an unknown structure, based on powder X‚Äêray diffraction analyses. HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF could be further functionalized with benzylamine in a postsynthetic modification; the nucleophilic replacement of the F atom in BTC‚ÄêF with benzylamine in methylene chloride at room temperature took place in a conversion yield of ~38% based on 1H NMR analyses.

Introduction

A unique nature of crystalline metal‚Äêorganic frameworks (MOFs) as a porous material is that the assembly of simple building blocks produces a variety of network structures which in turn can be modified to execute various functions. As representative examples, a simple ligand, benzene‚Äê1,3,5‚Äêtricarboxylic acid (H3BTC), reacts with Fe(III), Tb(III), or Cu(II) ions to form catalytic MIL‚Äê100(Fe),1 luminescent and ion‚Äêsensing Tb‚ÄêMOF‚Äê76,2 or gas‚Äêseparating HKUST‚Äê1,3 respectively. Furthermore, the organic linkers in MOFs can be chemically modified in the solid state, leading to functionalized frameworks and pores. This strategy, the so‚Äêcalled postsynthetic modification (PSM), has been widely applied to change the framework's surface functionality.4

Wang and Cohen first demonstrated that the ‚ÄîNH2 group of 2‚Äêamino‚Äê1,4‚Äêbenzenedicarboxylate (BDC‚ÄêNH2) in IRMOF‚Äê3 can be reacted with various anhydrides in the solid state.5 The initial success of the PSM approach has made it a general method for the functionalization of various MOFs if the organic linkers contain potentially reactive functional groups. The most sought‚Äêafter functionality is ‚ÄîNH2 which is usually present in the organic ligand or can be generated postsynthetically, as demonstrated in MIL‚Äê101.6 However, the introduction of ‚ÄîNH2 to the BDC linkers of MIL‚Äê101 is carried out by the reduction of the ‚ÄîNO2 groups which are directly generated by a nitration reaction in a very acidic condition, which may not be applicable to most MOFs.

Fr√∂ba and coworkers synthesized 2‚Äêamino‚Äê1,3,5‚Äêbenzenetricarboxylic acid (H3BTC‚ÄêNH2) from 2‚Äêfluoro‚Äê1,3,5‚Äêtrimethylbenzene in a multistep organic reaction, and prepared UHM‚Äê30, an amino‚Äêfunctionalized HKUST‚Äê1 or HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêNH2 via a solvothermal reaction with Cu(II) ion.7 Indeed, UHM‚Äê30 could be functionalized through the coupling reaction with acetic anhydride. Since UHM‚Äê30 has nearly the same structure as HKUST‚Äê1 except for the introduced ‚ÄîNH2 groups, it can be anticipated as one of the versatile platform MOFs that will be able to exhibit improved chemical and/or physical properties compared to unfunctionalized HKUST‚Äê1.

HKUST‚Äê1 (aka CuBTC) formulated as Cu3BTC2(H2O)3 is one of the most investigated MOFs thus far and the prototype structure of various MOFs exhibiting the tbo topology.8 Since HKUST‚Äê1 also shows a large CO2 affinity,9 it has been coated on inorganic supports as thin films useful for the membrane applications.10, 11 Very recently, HKUST‚Äê1 has been in the focus again due to its large methane delivery capacity; its CH4 storage capacity is the largest among the known MOFs.12, 13 Recently, Fr√∂ba and coworkers reported the preparation of fluoro‚Äêfunctionalized HKUST‚Äê1 (UHM‚Äê31) from copper nitrate and 2‚Äêfluoro‚Äê1,3,5‚Äêbenzenetricarboxylic acid (H3BTC‚ÄêF).14 It has an improved CO2 uptake capacity at 298‚ÄâK and 1‚Äâbar, whereas its H2 and CH4 adsorption capacities remain almost the same. Here, we present the water vapor adsorption and PSMs of UHM‚Äê31 (HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF in this work). We also report the crystal structure of a potassium salt of H3BTC‚ÄêF or K[H2BTC‚ÄêF] that has been used for the preparation of HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF (Figure 1).

Experimental

General Methods

All chemicals and solvents used in the syntheses were of reagent grade and used without further purification. Powder X‚Äêray diffraction (PXRD) data were collected on a Rigaku MiniFlex diffractometer with Cu KŒ± radiation (Œª‚Äâ=‚Äâ1.54‚Äâ√Ö). Thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) was carried out using a Scinco TGA‚ÄêS1000 thermal analysis system. Fourier transform infrared (FT‚ÄêIR) spectra of the samples prepared as KBr pellets were measured using a JASCO FT/IR‚Äê4000 spectrophotometer. 1H NMR spectra were obtained on a Bruker 400‚ÄâMHz NMR spectrometer. Elemental analyses were carried out on a Perkin‚ÄêElmer 2400 Series II CHN analyzer. Water Sorption Measurements

The adsorption isotherms of HKUST‚Äê1 and HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF were measured at 298‚ÄâK using the standard volumetric procedure on a BELSORP‚Äêmax instrument (BEL‚ÄêJapan, INC.). The samples were filtered and dried under low vacuum (~10‚àí3‚ÄâTorr) in the Schlenk line before they were loaded into the sample tube of the gas adsorption analyzer. The dried samples (~100‚Äâmg) were further evacuated at 170‚Äâ¬∞C (<1.0‚Äâ√ó‚Äâ10‚àí3‚ÄâTorr) for 10‚Äâh just before the measurement. The dead volume of the sample cell was automatically measured using helium gas. Pressure equilibrium points were also collected automatically by the equipment. After each measurement, weight of the samples was measured by a microbalance. An adsorption/desorption cycle took about 90‚Äâh.

Results and Discussion

As the unfunctionalized H3BTC (benzene‚Äê1,3,5‚Äêtricarboxlyic acid) has been well known for its interesting hydrogen‚Äêbonding network and supramolecular chemistry,19, 20 we were interested in the crystal structure of H3BTC‚ÄêF. Thus, its crystals suitable for single‚Äêcrystal XRD analyses were grown in water when a saturated aqueous solution in an open vial was standing on a bench at room temperature for 3 days. While H3BTC is almost insoluble in water, the ligand was readily dissolved in water, which was curious until its crystal structure was elucidated. The crystal structure revealed that the ligand was not H3BTC‚ÄêF but a potassium salt, K[H2BTC‚ÄêF], exhibiting an extended structure (Figure 2 and Figure S3). Based on the bond distances between C and O atoms, it was possible to identify the C‚ïêO double and C‚ÄîO single bonds of two ‚ÄîCOOH groups: 1.304(6) and 1.218(6)‚Äâ√Ö for C8‚ÄîO3 and C8‚ÄîO4; and 1.325(6) and 1.215(6)‚Äâ√Ö for C9‚ÄîO5 and C9‚ÄîO6, respectively. The deprotonated ‚ÄîCOO‚àí group shows similar C‚ÄìO bond distances: 1.259(6) and 1.271(6)‚Äâ√Ö for C7‚ÄîO1 and C7‚ÄîO2. The COOH groups form hydrogen bonds (Figure S3 and Table S3). The K+ ion is coordinated by seven O atoms and two F atoms. The five K‚ÄîO bond distances ranging from 2.717(4) to 2.909(4)‚Äâ√Ö are similar to those in K(18‚Äêcrown‚Äê6)]+ (2.762(5)‚Äì3.050(4)‚Äâ√Ö)21 and a potassium complex of magneson (2.688(8)‚Äì3.116(6)‚Äâ√Ö),22 but longer than those (2.305(1)‚Äì2.597(1)‚Äâ√Ö) in potassium hydrogen phthalate monohydrate.23 One distance (K1‚ÄîO1‚Äâ=‚Äâ3.275(4)‚Äâ√Ö) is significantly longer than the others, and thus the K1‚ÄîO1 bond strength is weak. It is notable that an F atom bridges two K+ ions with bond distances of 2.870(3) and 2.950(3)‚Äâ√Ö, which is slightly longer than those (2.691(2), 2.861(2)‚Äâ√Ö) in K{HB(C6F5)3}TMEDA2.24 This crystal structure is also supported by the elemental analysis and TGA results. In the TGA curve obtained under air atmosphere, the organic part decomposes near 300‚Äâ¬∞C: the inorganic residue was considered as KO2 (27.0‚Äâwt%, 325‚Äì540‚Äâ¬∞C) and KO (21.0‚Äâwt%, >540‚Äâ¬∞C) (Figure S5). These values match well with the calculated values based on the empirical formula. It is assumed that during the final synthetic step of acidifying the reaction mixture the potassium salt precipitated before the full acidification of the ligand.

HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF was prepared by a solvothermal reaction of K[H2BTC‚ÄêF] and Cu(NO3)2¬∑2.5 H2O in a mixture of DMF/H2O (5.0/5.0‚ÄâmL) at 85‚Äâ¬∞C for 1 day. This reaction condition was different from that employed for UHM‚Äê31, where the solvent was N,N‚Äêdimethylacetamide (DMA).14 In the IR spectrum, the as‚Äêprepared sample showed an Ar‚ÄìF band at 1230‚Äâcm‚àí1, which was not present in the spectrum of HKUST‚Äê1 (Figure S1). Single‚Äêcrystal XRD analysis showed that HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF had the same structure as UHM‚Äê31 with slightly different unit cell parameters (Table S4). The measured PXRD pattern of the as‚Äêsynthesized HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF was coincident with that of the unfunctionalized HKUST‚Äê1, indicating that both MOFs had almost same framework structures (Figure 3).

The occluded guest species, which could not be identified with the single‚Äêcrystal X‚Äêray data owing to severe disorder, were identified as DMF based on the 1H NMR spectrum of the as‚Äêprepared crystals dissolved in DCl/DMSO‚Äêd6 (Figure S2). The TGA curves measured under ambient atmosphere showed a gradual weight loss of 34.8% due to the removal of the occluded DMF guest molecules until 230‚Äâ¬∞C, at which point the framework decomposed abruptly without involving a plateau (Figure S6). The thermal stability of HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF was lower than that of HKUST‚Äê1, which was stable until 300‚Äâ¬∞C at the same measurement condition. For more facile removal of the guest DMF, the as‚Äêprepared crystals were soaked in EtOH for 1 day. As expected, the EtOH in the guest‚Äêexchanged solid could be removed until 150‚Äâ¬∞C, which is much lower than the temperature (230‚Äâ¬∞C) for the removal of the DMF in the as‚Äêprepared sample (Figure S7).

When the activated sample prepared by heating the solvent‚Äêexchanged sample with EtOH under vacuum at 160‚Äâ¬∞C for 6‚Äâh was subjected to the TGA analysis, it quickly adsorbed water from air to give the small bump at 50‚Äâ¬∞C in the TGA trace (Figure S8). This indicates that during the activation process, the coordinated water molecules were detached from the copper centers. A nitrogen adsorption isotherm for the activated sample resulted in a Type‚ÄêI adsorption isotherm (Figure S9). The BET surface area was calculated to be 1404‚Äâm2/g, which is smaller than that (1507‚Äâm2/g) of HKUST‚Äê125 and those of the functionalized HKUST‚Äê1s: 1834‚Äâm2/g for UHM‚Äê30 (HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêNH2)7 and 1802‚Äâm2/g for UHM‚Äê31.14 In spite of the smaller surface area, HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF adsorbed a significant amount of H2 at 77‚ÄâK and 1‚Äâbar; the amount (2.1‚Äâwt%) is slightly smaller than that of HKUST‚Äê1 (2.47‚Äâwt%),25 UHM‚Äê30 (2.28‚Äâwt%),7 and UHM‚Äê31 (2.32‚Äâwt%)14 (Figure S10). The CO2 gas adsorption isotherms were measured at 253, 273, and 298‚ÄâK (Figure S11). The CO2 adsorption capacity of HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF is 4.20‚Äâmmol/g at 298‚ÄâK and 1‚Äâbar, which is slightly smaller than (4.69‚Äâmmol/g) that for HKUST‚Äê126; however, it is much smaller than (5.26‚Äâmmol/g) that for UHM‚Äê307 and (6.56‚Äâmmol/g) for UHM‚Äê31.14 The calculated isosteric heat of CO2 adsorption for HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF based on the three isotherms at 253, 273, and 298‚ÄâK is 27.9‚ÄâkJ/mol (Figure S11). In comparison with UHM‚Äê31, HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF shows reduced gas adsorption properties, presumably due to the smaller BET surface area.

We also conducted water vapor adsorption measurement. HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF exhibited a same water uptake behavior at low pressure (P/P0‚Äâ<‚Äâ0.1) as HKUST‚Äê1, but was saturated with water at a lower pressure (P/P0‚Äâ~‚Äâ0.1) than HKUST‚Äê1 (P/P0‚Äâ~‚Äâ0.2) (Figure 4). HKUST‚Äê1 has a desorption trace that almost overlaps with the adsorption branch at a relatively low pressure, which is a known result.26 In contrast, HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF shows a large discrepancy between the adsorption and desorption traces, implying that water is chemisorbed. HKUST‚Äê1 is known to be unstable in water, leading to a reduced surface area from 1340 to 647‚Äâm2/g after a water adsorption cycle and a changed PXRD pattern.26 HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF was also unstable in water. The PXRD pattern of the water‚Äêsorbed HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF was different from that for the as‚Äêprepared sample (Figure 4(d)); it was not same as that for the water‚Äêsorbed HKUST‚Äê1 either.26 Moreover, the water‚Äêsorbed HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF became nonporous based on a N2 adsorption measurement (Figure S10).

An optical microscope examination showed that the morphology of the water‚Äêsorbed HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF was also different from that of the HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF; block crystals became tiny needle crystals (Figure S13). We have traced the transformation process by measuring the PXRD patterns of the HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF sample with water vapor exposure for 24‚Äâh. Interestingly, the process involved an amorphous phase that turned to a new crystalline phase corresponding to the needle crystals of the water‚Äêsorbed HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF (Figure 4(d) and Figure S14). The nonporosity and observed X‚Äêray intensities at higher diffraction angles of the water‚Äêsorbed HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF suggest that the water‚Äêsorbed HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF would have a condensed structure built from the framework destruction and reorganization in the presence of water.

Fluorobenzene undergoes more readily nucleophilic aromatic substitution reactions than other halogenated benzenes, and the reaction is facilitated if the electron‚Äêwithdrawing groups are located at ortho and/or para positions as in the BTC‚ÄêF linker in HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF. Even though a significant number of studies have been performed on the PSM of MOFs, those on MOFs containing aryl halides are still rare.4, 27 Furthermore, the aromatic nucleophilic substitution has been reported just only once for the postmodification of UiO‚Äê66‚ÄêBr to UiO‚Äê66‚ÄêCN at high temperatures (>140‚Äâ¬∞C).28 We noted that the H3BTC‚ÄêF was susceptible to a nucleophilic attack and used as a precursor for the synthesis of H3BTC‚ÄêNH2 under very mild condition.7 Thus, a similar reaction was anticipated to take place in HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF (Scheme 1).

HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF crystals (100‚Äâmg) were reacted with benzylamine (0.10‚ÄâmL) in 10‚ÄâmL of methylene chloride (MC) at room temperature for 12‚Äâh (Figure 5). The measured PXRD pattern of the product (HKUST‚Äê1‚Äêbam) was same as that of the pristine HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF with an increased intensity at 2Œ∏ = 11.8¬∞ (Figure 3). Unfortunately, the activated HKUST‚Äê1‚Äêbam did not take up nitrogen gas at 77‚ÄâK. The 1H NMR spectrum of HKUST‚Äê1‚Äêbam dissolved in DCl/DMSO‚Äêd6 gave the H3 BTC‚ÄêNHCH2Ph signals along with those of the unreacted H3BTC‚ÄêF (Figure 5). Based on the integration of the signals, the conversion yield of BTC‚ÄêF to BTC‚ÄêNHCH2Ph was ~38% (Figure S9). It was also observed that MC, DMF, and unreacted benzylamine were present in the postmodified sample. The TGA trace of HKUST‚Äê1‚Äêbam showed a large increase in the weight of the organic part. When the weight losses above 200‚Äâ¬∞C, where the thermal decomposition of the organic linkers takes place, are considered, the weight loss ratios between the linkers to inorganic residue (CuO) increased from 57.3/28.7‚Äâ=‚Äâ2.0 for HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF to 57.5/22.9‚Äâ=‚Äâ2.5 for HKUST‚Äê1‚Äêbam, which is due to the introduction of the benzylamine groups. However, we could see 19.8‚Äâwt % of weight loss below 200‚Äâ¬∞C, probably due to the removal of the trapped solvent molecules and benzylamine. Therefore, it is presumed that the bulky benzylamine groups might have blocked the pores and thus the DMF molecules could not be replaced with methylene chloride. Another possible reason is that the generated HF reacts quickly with benzylamine to form benzylammonium fluoride salt. We have tried to conduct the nucleophilic substitutions with other amines such as propylamine, ethylenediamine, maleic anhydride, and p‚Äêxylylenediamine in acetone, chloroform, or methylene chloride. However, the alkylamines and maleic anhydride made HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF crystals amorphous, and the bulky p‚Äêxylylenediamine did not react with HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF.

Conclusion

We have investigated the gas adsorption properties and PSMs of a fluorinated HKUST‚Äê1 (HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF), which was prepared by a solvothermal reaction of Cu(II) and K[H2BTC‚ÄêF] in DMF/H2O. We determined the crystal structures of K[H2BTC‚ÄêF] and HKSUT‚Äê1‚ÄêF. HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF has nearly the same structure as UHM‚Äê31 which was prepared using H3BTC‚ÄêF by Fr√∂ba and coworkers. HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF had noticeably reduced H2 and CO2 uptake at 298‚ÄâK and 1‚Äâbar than HKUST‚Äê1 and UHM‚Äê31. The framework of HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF transformed into an unknown structure during a water vapor adsorption measurement. We demonstrated that HKUST‚Äê1‚ÄêF could react with benzylamine under a mild reaction condition, which is the first example of a PSM via an aromatic substitution reaction in HKUST‚Äê1.
