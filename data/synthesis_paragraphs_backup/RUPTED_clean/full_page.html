A versatile CuII/CuI metal–organic framework for selective sorption and heterogeneous catalysis†

Yu-Jia Ding a, Chun-Pei Zhang a, Yong-Qing Wang a, Xiao-Ming Lin *ab, Ximiao Zhu a, Da-Liang Zhang b, Xian-Jian Duan c and Yue-Peng Cai *a

First published on 24th July 2015

A three-dimensional (3-D) multi-functional mixed valence CuII/CuI-MOF, {[Cu(CuI)2(2-pyzc)2H2O]2·H2O}n (Hpyzc = pyrazine-2-carboxylic acid), has been solvothermally synthesized and successfully characterized by FT-IR spectroscopy, elemental analysis, powder X-ray diffraction, thermal gravimetric analysis (TGA) and single-crystal X-ray diffraction. The structure posesses a 3-D open framework containing 1-D channels and shows a tcj topological network with the Schläfli symbol (43·612)(43)2. This MOF exhibits selective adsorption of CO2/H2 over CH4/N2 and excellent catalytic activity in the oxidation of benzylic compounds and three-component coupling reactions of aromatic alkynes, tosyl azide and diisopropylamine as a cooperative catalyst. The catalyst could be reused several times without significant degradation in catalytic activity.

Introduction

Over the past two decades, metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) as a promising class of porous crystalline materials have attracted tremendous attention owing to their extremely plentiful structures and versatile potential applications, such as gas storage and separation, heterogeneous catalysis, etc.1–3 Above all, porosity is the most important feature in MOFs and plays a significant role in determining their functional properties.4 As is known, pores of appropriate size show great advantages in gas storage or adsorption properties of crystalline structures. However, many MOFs with large pores often tend to have a collapsed framework after the removal of the coordination solvent molecules from the crystalline framework.5 Therefore, the construction of porous MOFs with high thermal and chemical stabilities becomes an active field in coordination and materials chemistry.

On the other hand, well-ordered porous structures of MOFs provide a great platform to develop heterogeneous catalysis. Several efficient strategies have been established to introduce active catalytic centers within the structure, e.g. exploiting coordinatively unsaturated metal sites,6 using functionalized ligands as struts to fabricate MOFs,7 or introducing nanosized metallic clusters or organometallic complexes into the MOFs by post-modification.8 We are interested in the development of catalytic MOFs, in which the metal nodes can act as the catalytic sites and promote the reactions leading to the formation of valuable products.9 Herein, we develop a versatile CuII/CuI-MOF, {[Cu(CuI)2(2-pyzc)2H2O]2·H2O}n (Hpyzc = pyrazine-2-carboxylic acid), which would integrate both selective gas adsorption and catalytic properties.

Experimental

Materials and measurements

All materials were of reagent grade, obtained from commercial sources, and used without further purification; the solvents were dried by standard procedures. The IR spectra were recorded on a Nicolet FT-IR-170SX spectrophotometer in KBr pellets. Thermogravimetric analyses were performed on a Perkin-Elmer TGA7 analyzer with a heating rate of 10 °C min−1 in flowing nitrogen atmosphere. The X-ray powder diffraction patterns were measured on a Bruker D8 Advance diffractometer at 40 kV and 40 mA with a Cu target tube and a graphite monochromator. The 1H NMR spectra were recorded with a Varian Mercury Plus 300 MHz spectrometer. The sorption isotherms were measured with a Quantachrome Autosorb-iQ-MP gas sorption analyzer.

Results and discussion

Structural description of {[Cu(CuI)2(2-pyzc)2H2O]2·H2O}n

Brown crystals of 1 have been obtained by the reaction of pyrazine-2,3-dicarboxylic acid (2,3-H2pzdc) with cuprous iodide in a mixed solvent of CH3CN–H2O (3:5, v/v) under solvothermal conditions.131 crystallizes in the orthorhombic system with the space group Pbcn and possesses a (3,3)-connected three-dimensional (3-D) framework structure consisting of CuII(3)(2-pyzc)2 molecules linking together CuI polymeric chains. In 1, CuI(1) and CuI(2) ions are connected by I− ions to form corrugated double-stranded ladder-shaped inorganic [CuI]n chains with three different Cu⋯Cu distances [Cu(1)–Cu(1A) = 2.6919 Å, Cu(1)–Cu(2) = 2.7006 Å, Cu(2)–Cu(2A) = 2.6574 Å] (Fig. 1b),14 which are slightly shorter than the sum of the van der Waals radii (2.8 Å). Moreover, both CuI(1) and CuI(2) centers are bonded to N atoms of pyrazine-2-carboxylic acid (2-Hpyzc), which comes from the in situ decarboxylation of pyrazine-2,3-dicarboxylic acid. Interestingly, these 1-D [CuI]n chains are further linked together through CuII(3)(2-pyzc)2 molecules, in which the Cu(3) atom is assigned as divalent (based on the valence equivalence), surrounded by two oxygen atoms, two nitrogen atoms in the equatorial position from two different pyzc− ligands, and one coordinated water molecule, leading to a mixed-valence CuI/CuII 3-D metal–organic framework of tcj topology with the Schläfli symbol (43·612)(43)2, showing 1D channels along the [100] direction (Fig. 1c and d). The 1D channel is filled with H2O molecules and PLATON15 calculations suggested a solvent-accessible volume of 742.4 Å3 (approximately 20.2% of the unit cell) by excluding the guest water molecules. A conspicuous feature is that the potentially coordinatively unsaturated metal centers are well-positioned to interact with the guest molecules that enter the framework pores, suggesting that 1 could function as an active heterogeneous catalyst for Lewis acid promoted reactions and exhibit gas sorption properties simultaneously.

Sorption properties

The TG curves show that the weight loss (3.80%) between 200 and 225 °C corresponds to the loss of water molecules (calcd. 3.74%). To activate the sample, the as-made forms of 1 were evacuated at 150 °C for 8 hours to remove the water molecules. It was thermally stable up to 350 °C, as evidenced by thermogravimetric analysis (Fig. S1†). A powder X-ray diffraction study confirmed the phase purity of 1 bulk sample and preservation of its crystallinity after the removal of coordinating molecules (Fig. 2). The permanent porosity of desolvated 1 was confirmed by various gas adsorption measurements. Interestingly, in the case of H2 desorption, a significant hysteresis loop was observed (Fig. 3a). Desolvated 1 showed 97.76 cm3 g−1 (0.86 wt%) H2 uptake at 77 K and 1 atm. The most striking feature is the steep slope in the H2 sorption isotherm at initial pressure, indicating that the pores strongly interact with the H2 molecules. It is interesting to find that desolvated 1 shows selective gas adsorption for CO2 over N2. The sorption measurements for CO2 gave typical type I isotherms for microporous materials (IUPAC classification), and the uptake values around 1 atm are 107.26 cm3 g−1 (17.4 wt%) at 195 K and 59.50 cm3 g−1 (10.5 wt%) at 298 K. Another gas that is often evaluated for MOFs is nitrogen. N2 uptake at ambient pressure was just 6.94 cm3 g−1 (0.86 wt%) at 77 K. The selective sorption of CO2 rather than N2 gas can be attributed to the significant quadrupole moment of CO2 (−1.4 × 10−39 cm2), which generates specific interactions with the host framework.16 As for CH4, only a slight loading of 8.31 cm3 g−1 occurs at 298 K (Fig. 3b). Moreover, desolvated 1 is also subjected to benzene vapor sorption at room temperature. It is noted that it takes up to 70.37 cm3 g−1 (19.7 wt%) benzene molecules, indicative of a diffusion process between the framework and the benzene molecules. This paves the way for testing the catalytic capability of desolvated 1.

Heterogeneous catalysis

It is interesting to note that the coordinatively unsaturated metal centers are exposed on the surface of the framework, suggesting that desolvated 1 might serve as an active heterogeneous catalyst for Lewis acid promoted reactions. To study the catalytic activity of desolvated 1, we first probed the oxidation of benzylic compounds to the corresponding carbonyl functionality.17 Our experiments employed a 1:1 molar ratio of selected benzylic compounds and tert-butylhydroperoxide (TBHP, 70 wt% in water) as the oxidant in CH3CN solvent at 60 °C for 24 h with a loading of 10 mol% of desolvated 1. As shown in Table 2, 89% conversion of tetralin with 92% selectivity to 1-tetralone was detected. GC analysis revealed that 1-tetralone is the major product, a commercially important source of synthetic precursors and an additive to enhance the cetane number in diesel fuels.18 No other by-product was detected aside from 1-tetralol.

To make a comparison, different amounts of the oxidant were used to conduct the catalytic experiment. There is an obvious decrease in the yield of the product when we used 1 equiv. of oxidant and a slight increase when the amount of oxidant reached 2 equiv. We have restricted the amount of oxidant to 1 equiv., which represents a good balance between TBHP cost and high product yield. The satisfying yield of 82% is comparable to that of Cu-MOF-SiF3 (1.5 equiv. of TBHP, yield 89%, selectivity 90%), and the products are 1-tetralone and 1,4-naphthaquinone.19 In another example, the products 1-tetralone, 1-tetralol, 1-naphthol and naphthalene were detected.20 Importantly, removal of 1 by filtration after only 4 h completely shut down the reaction, affording only 43% total conversion after leaving to stand for 20 h (Fig. S2†), which thus demonstrated the heterogeneous catalytic behavior of 1 in nature. Catalyst 1 can be simply recovered by filtration and heated at 150 °C for 8 hours to regenerate the catalytic active site, which was subsequently used in the successive runs without decreasing the catalytic activity by comparison to the catalyst which was reused without heating (Fig. 4). The PXRD pattern of the recovered material showed that the structural integrity of the catalyst was maintained during the catalytic process (Fig. 2d).

In order to probe the size selectivity of this MOF, we took a systematic approach by varying the size and shape of the substrate. When indane and fluorene were employed as substrates at the same experimental conditions, the corresponding ketone conversions were about 80% and 40% (selectivity: 88% and 80%, respectively). The conversion of 9,10-dihydroanthracene to mono-ketone was achieved in a negligible yield (5%). The sole product obtained by oxidation of diphenylmethane is benzophenone with a low yield of 20%.

To make a comparison, a series of control catalysts were used to conduct the catalytic experiments (Table 3, entry 1). When Cu(NO3)·3H2O was used as a catalyst, 2-pyzc ligand and CuI as an additive, no product was detected. In addition, Cu(NO3)·3H2O is less efficient to prompt the catalytic transformation. These results indicate that the catalytic centres might be blocked by the ligands or nitrate anions.21 Moreover, we used Cu(OAc)·H2O as a homogeneous catalyst instead of 1 to conduct the catalytic experiment. The product yield can reach up to 48% after 2 h, which indicated the fact that the Cu(OAc)·H2O promoted oxidation of tetralin is faster than that of 1 (25% after 2 h). However, the Cu(OAc)·H2O catalyzed product yield cannot be significantly improved when the reaction time is prolonged to 24 h. Similarly, the oxidations of fluorene or indane catalyzed by Cu(OAc)·H2O also give a moderate yield of 49% and 47%, respectively. Taking into account the fact that the CuII/CuI-MOF is a self-supported catalyst with reactions taking place in the porous channels, the relatively better catalytic activity of the heterogeneous than the homogeneous analogue is expected. One reason for the higher efficiency may be the fact that the CuII/CuI-MOF is apt to offer coordinatively unsaturated meal centers (CUMCs) as activity sites via the loss of coordinated water molecules as evidenced from TG analysis. This was also verified by a comparison of a heterogeneous catalyst, [Cu2Cl(pyzc)2(H2O)]n (Hpyzc = pyrazine-2-carboxylic acid), which is also a 3-D mixed-valence Cu(I)–Cu(II) coordination polymer but lacks structural porosity and activity sites, showing a negligible yield of 3% conversion.22

Conclusions

In summary, a mixed valence CuII/CuI-MOF containing 1D open channels was synthesized and found to exhibit multiple functions: porosity, high stability, H2/CO2 storage capacity and excellent catalytic activity in oxidation of benzylic compounds and three-component coupling reactions as a Cu2+/CuI cooperative catalyst. This MOF heterogeneous catalyst is very robust and can be recycled several times without losing its activity.
