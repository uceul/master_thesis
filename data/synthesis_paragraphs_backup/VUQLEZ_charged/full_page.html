(C2H8N)9[Eu5(SO4)12]·2H2O: the first europium sulfate open-framework containing two kinds of intersecting extra-large 20-membered ring channels†

Lei Zheng a, Yan Xu *a, Xiaoliang Zhang a, Zhibin Zhang a, Dunru Zhu a, Su Chen a and S. P. Elangovan b
aState Key Laboratory of Materials-Oriented Chemical Engineering, College of Chemistry and Chemical Engineering, Nanjing University of Technology, Nanjing, 210009, China. E-mail: yanxu@njut.edu.cn
bDepartment of Science & Technology, Nippon Chemical Industrial Co. Ltd., 9-11-1 Kameido, Koto-ku, Tokyo, 136-8515, Japan

Received 21st September 2009 , Accepted 2nd October 2009

First published on 15th October 2009

A novel organic amine templated europium sulfate [(C2NH8)9][Eu5(SO4)12]·2H2O 1 has been synthesized using a small organic amine (dimethylamine) as a structural directing agent (SDA). Crystal structural analysis indicates that the inorganic framework of 1 contains two kinds of extra-large 20MR channels.

Great efforts have been made to synthesize new porous materials with pure tetrahedral or mixed polyhedral open-frameworks, while studies of new topological microporous structures of extra-large-pore (more than 12 membered ring channels) open frameworks are extremely intensive because of the widespread applications of these materials in catalysis and the separation of large molecules such as heavy oils or pharmaceuticals. Since the discovery of the first molecular sieve containing extra-large 18-membered rings (18MR), aluminophosphate VPI-5,1 many metal phosphates, aluminosilicates and germanates with extra-large-pore open frameworks such as UTD-1 (14MR),2a,2b CIT-5 (14MR),2c AlPO4-8 (14MR),2d cloverite (20MR),3a JDF-20 (20MR),3b[Zn3(O3PCH2CO2)2(O3PCH2CO2H)][(C2N2H10)(btc)],4a ND-1 (24MR),4b NTHU1 (24MR),4c FD4 (24MR),4d VSB-1, VSB-5 (24MR),4e,4f ASU-16 (24MR),4g FJ-1 (24MR),4h ZnHPO-CJ1 (24MR)4i and SU-M (30MR)5 have been synthesized. Recently, studies focusing on the organic-templated lanthanide sulfates have been an important advancement in the field of solid state materials.6–8 Compared with Ge, Zn and Al, the rare-earth elements can adopt a large range of coordination numbers from 8 (LnO8) to 12 (LnO12) and flexible Ln–O bond lengths.9–16 The high coordination number offers the possibility to generate open-frameworks with high charge densities, which may result in novel topological structures with large pores.4d Unfortunately, to date, extra-large-pore structures of lanthanide sulfate have not been successfully prepared. Recently, we reported that in the presence of larger organic amines, rare-earth elements can be easily coordinated by water to form 1D or 2D architectures.14 Herein, we used a small amine viz. dimethylamine as a structural directing agent (SDA) to successfully synthesize a novel organic amine templated europium sulfate (C2H8N)9[Eu5(SO4)12]·2H2O 1 with two kinds of extra-large 20MR channels.

Compound 1 was prepared by a hydrothermal reaction.‡ Crystal structural analysis indicates§ that the open-framework 1 is the first porous lanthanide sulfate with extra-large 20MR channels and it has a novel inorganic framework with the space group C2/c. Single-crystal structural analysis reveals the 3D open framework is constructed from EuO8, EuO9 and SO4 polyhedra. The asymmetric unit of 1 contains 50 non-hydrogen atoms, 33 of which belong to the open-framework including three Eu atoms and six SO42− groups. As shown in Fig. 1, Eu(1) and Eu(3) are coordinated by 8 O atoms, while Eu(2) is 9-coordinated through O atoms of SO42− groups. The bond distances of Eu–O vary from 2.282(8) to 2.640(6) Å, while the angles O–Eu–O are in between 55.1(2) and 164.6(2)°, that are comparable with other reported Eu compounds. Six crystallographic independent S atoms can be divided into 4 groups: S(1) and S(5) are bonded by one μ3-O, two μ2-O and one Ot (terminal O atoms) to make 4 S–O–Eu linkages; S(2) and S(4) are bonded by four μ2-O atoms to make 4 S–O–Eu linkages, S(3) is coordinated by three μ2-O and one Ot to make three S–O–Eu linkages; S(6) is bonded by two μ2-O and two Ot to make two S–O–Eu linkages.


Fig. 1 The SBU-1 of [Eu5(SO4)12]9− in 1.

The structure of 1 can be described from two types of secondary building units (SBUs). Eu(1) lies on a 2-fold axis (0, y, 0.25), while two Eu3(SO4)6 fragments connect each other by sharing Eu(1) to make the SBU-1 of Eu5(SO4)12. Two adjacent SBUs-1 are linked by bridging O atoms to generate an interesting 20-membered ring (Fig. 2), which consists of 4 EuO9, 6 EuO8 and 10 SO4 polyhedra. The SBU-2 is 10-membered ring constructed by 3 EuO8, 2 EuO9 and 5 SO4 groups. Neighbouring 10 and 20-membered rings are connected by bridging SO4 groups to form an open framework with a 3D channel system. There are two kinds of channels with 10 and 20-members extending along the crystallographic c axis (Fig. 3). Although both 10 and 20-membered channels keep a different size, they can be arranged in an ordered close packing arrangement just like mesoporous materials. Each 20MR channel is surrounding by six 10MR, and these are alternately surrounded by three 20MR and three 10MR channels. The approximate window sizes for 20MR and 10MR are 7 × 11 Å and 5.3 × 5.3 Å, respectively. The zig-zag S–O–Eu chains separate the channels and make the inorganic framework more stable. Interestingly, there are other rectangular 20MR-2 channels along the crystallographic [101] direction, as shown in Fig. 4. Although the 20MR also consists of 10 EuO8 (EuO9) and 10 SO4 polyhedra, the shape is quite different. The pore size is about 5.8 × 14.5 Å. While the rectangle channels of 20MR-2 are separated by helical chains (ESI, Fig. S1),† the central axis for every helical chain is a two-fold screw axis. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first example of a rare-earth sulfate open framework with extra-large 20 MR channels. Moreover, it is one of the rare microporous materials containing two kinds of extra-large channels. Because of the long Eu–O bond distances, its framework density, 10.2 polyhedra per 1000 Å3, is a little smaller than the values for ND-1 (12.1),4b FDU-4 (11.1),4d ZnHPO-CJ1 (11.6)4i and NTHU-1 (11.9).4c

Thermogravimetric analysis shows that the total weight loss of 1 is 63.75%, which is in agreement with the calculated value (62.77%). The weight loss of 3.25% in the range of 50–100 °C corresponds to the removal of free water (the calculated value is 3.05%). The weight loss of 19.32% in the range of 100–300 °C can be attributed to the loss of the organic amine cations (2(CH3)2NH2+ + O2− → (CH3)2NH + H2O; the calculated value is 18.66%). Once the dimethylamine is removed from the structure, the framework will collapse. The final weight loss of 41.23% in the range of 300–750 °C can be attributed to the loss of SO3 (the calculated value is 40.62%). Because of the extra-large pores, the guest molecules and SO3 are removed in the lower temperature region in comparison to the rare earth sulfates reported previously.10–12 Compound 1 shows red luminescence. As shown in Fig. 6, the emission spectrum exhibits the characteristic transition of Eu3+ ion attributed to 5D0 → 7FJ (J = 0, 1, 3, 4) transitions. Comparatively, compound 1 shows a very strong and sharp emission band, 5D0 → 7F2, which is in agreement with the europium compounds reported previously.


Fig. 6 The photoluminescence spectrum of compound 1. The excitation wavelength is 396 nm, and spectrum is taken at room temperature.

In conclusion, a novel organic amine templated europium sulfate (C2H8N)9[Eu5(SO4)12]·2H2O 1 has been synthesized using a small organic amine as the SDA. Interestingly, the inorganic framework includes two kinds of extra-large 20 MR channels. The formation of 1 indicates that small SDAs may template 3D rare earth sulfates with large channels.
