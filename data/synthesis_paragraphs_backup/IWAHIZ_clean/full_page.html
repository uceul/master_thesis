Aluminophosphates prepared using in situ generated tetramethylammonium cations as structure directing agents

Abstract

A series of aluminophosphates (AlPOs) with open architectures, namely [dmdabco][Al2(HPO4)4] (1, dmdabco = N,N-dimethyl-1,4-diazabicyclo[2,2,2]octane), [tmpip]0.5[Al(HPO4)2] (2, tmpip = N,N,N,N-tetramethylpiperazinium) and [tmpip][Al6(PO4)6(HPO4)] (3) have been solvothermally synthesized and structurally characterized. The in situ generated templates (dmdabco2+ and tmpip2+) originated from the methylation reactions between methanol solvent and their corresponding aliphatic organic-amine parents: 1,4-diazabicyclo[2,2,2]octane (dabco) for 1, 1-methylpiperazine for 2 and 3. Such a characteristic in situ methylation is distinct from conventional Eschweilerâ€“Clarke methylation with excessive formic acid and formaldehyde. Compounds 1 and 2 possess the same infinite inorganic chains, which are further linked by hydrogen bonds to form 3D supramolecular framework. Compound 3 is built from Al6P6 and Al6P8 building units, giving rise to an open architecture with three-dimensional channel system.

1. Introduction

Crystalline materials with regular microporous structures have been extensively studied due to their diverse structural chemistry and potential applications in catalysis, absorption, ion-exchange and separation, etc [1], [2], [3]. Since the discovery of the aluminophosphate molecular sieves in 1982 by Wilson et al. [4], aluminophosphates (AlPOs) have become an important family of zeolite materials. To date, a large variety of novel composition of AlPOs with zero-dimensional (0D) cluster, one-dimensional (1D) chains or ladders, two-dimensional (2D) layers and three-dimensional (3D) architectures have been prepared and characterized [5], [6], [7], [8], [9], [10], [11], [12], [13]. They are traditionally synthesized by using hydrothermal or solvothermal synthetic methods with organic amines as the structure directing agents (SDAs). Recently, ionothermal synthesis in which ionic species (an ionic liquid or eutectic mixture) are used as both solvent and template, has been proven to be a promising technique in preparing porous materials with novel structures and special properties [14], [15], [16]. Notable examples include DNL-1(CLO framework type, 20-ring) [17], JIS-3 (18-ring) [18], SIZ-13 containing unusual CoCl bonds in framework [19], NIS-4 with non-centrosymmetric architecture and helical channels [20], NTHU-9 with dual photogenerated features [21], and other SIZ-n type materials with interrupted frameworks [8], [22]. In comparison with those protonated organic amines as SDAs commonly encountered in traditional hydrothermal or solvothermal systems, the organic cations of ionic liquids present in ionothermal reaction usually featured alkyl-substituted groups (e.g. alkyl imidazolium or tetraalkylammonium). Therefore, it would be highly expected to design new alternatives as SDAs for the synthesis of zeolitic and other porous materials. In this aspect, hydrothermal in situ alkylation transformations of readily available organic species may be a promising option. As expected, some crystalline porous solids have been successfully prepared by using in situ formed SDAs, including zinc phosphates/phosphites and gallium phosphates [23], [24], [25]. All these encourage us to further explore the possibility of in situ generated SDAs for making novel aluminium phosphates. Herein, three new organically templated AlPOs, [dmdabco][Al2(HPO4)4] (1), [tmpip]0.5[Al(HPO4)2] (2) and [tmpip][Al6(PO4)6(HPO4)] (3), have been successfully obtained by using in situ template generation route. Notably, the organic dmdabco and tmpip molecules were generated by in situ methylation reactions of CH3OH solvent and dabco and pip precursors respectively. Compound 1 and 2 are isostructural and exhibit infinite inorganic chain built from AlO4 and HPO4 tetrahedra; compound 3 features an rarely observed Al/P ratio of 6/7 and has a three-dimensional open-framework constructed from AlO4, AlO5, PO4 and HPO4 units.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Syntheses

Hydro(solvo)thermal synthesis has been well explored in the synthesis of AlPOs. Among the various factors to affect the structures of AlPOs, organic SDAs undoubtedly played important role in tuning targeted architecture owing to their diverse species, polarity, size, charge and shape, etc. However, the commercially available organic amine is limited and the synthesis of complicated organic amine is time-consuming and high-cost. It is energetic to enlarge the scope of organic SDAs with readily availability from synthetic aspect. The in situ modulation of existing organic amines may be a promising avenue to obtain new SDAs. Recent studies have testified that in situ ligand reactions occasionally occur in hydro(solvo)thermal synthesis [23], [24], [25], [28]. Many novel coordination complexes driven by in situ synthesized ligands has also been reported [29], [30], [31]. There is of course no reason why this in situ strategy cannot be utilized to generate new organically templated AlPOs. Herein, two familiar cyclic aliphatic amines are selected as precursors to investigate the in situ-template-generation of AlPOs. As expected, 1,4-diazabicyclo[2,2,2]octane (dabco) and 1-methylpiperazine are in situ transformed into structure-related N,N -dimethyl-1,4-diazabicyclo[2,2,2]octane (in 1) and N,N,N,N-tetramethylpiperazinium (in 2 and 3). Undoubtedly, solvothermal in situ methylation occurred between methanol and corresponding organic parents (Scheme 1). To our knowledge, in situ alkylation of amine has been previsouly observed in the fabrication of AlPO-CJ8 [32], in which N,Nâ€™-diisopropylethylenediamine template was formed by the dehydration reaction between isopropylamine and the solvent ethylene glycol. The mechanism for alkylation in this work is distinct from that found in AlPO-CJ8. Although compounds 2 and 3 have the same SDAs, their resultant structures exhibit obvious diversities owing to the different initial stoichiometric ratio of reactants and solvent used. Compared with traditional Eschweiler-Clarke methylation of the primary or secondary amine with excess formic acid and formaldehyde [33], the simplicity and environmental friendliness of the in situ direct methylation transformation is noteworthy.

3.2. Structural description

Single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis reveals that both compounds 1 and 2 crystallize in the triclinic P-1 space group, and present similar one-dimensional chain-like structure. As shown in Fig. 2a, the asymmetric unit of 1 contains two Al3+ ions, four HPO42âˆ’ groups and one [dmdabco]2+ cation, while the asymmetric unit of 2 consists of one Al3+ ion, two HPO42âˆ’ groups and half of one [tmpip]2+ cation (Fig. 2b). In both structures, the Al3+ ions are tetra-coordinated by O atoms from phosphate, forming typical tetrahedral geometries. The inorganic frameworks of 1 and 2 are similar, and both show one-dimensional chain-like structure. The alternating connection of AlO4 and HPO4 tetrahedra gives rise to typical four-membered ring (4 MR), which is linked with each other to form 1D inorganic chains along the [100] direction (Fig. 3a). According to the Aufbau principle of high dimensional architectures built from low-dimensional substructures, the generation of low-dimensional units is of peculiar significance. In the present cases, the existences of non-coordinated PO and POH terminal groups in compounds 1 and 2 prevent the expansion of such 1D inorganic chains into 2D or 3D extended framework. However, hydrogen-bond, as one of the characteristic supramolecular driving forces, plays a paramount role in the creation and stability of low-dimensional structures. The inorganic framework of 3 is three-dimensional, consisting of vertex-sharing AlO4, AlO5, PO4 and HPO4 units. The 3D architecture can be conceptually built up from two different secondary building units (SBUs), Al6P6 and Al6P8. As shown in Fig. 4b, the Al6P6 SBUs is composed of two Al(1)O5 trigonal bipyramids, two Al(2)O4, two Al(3)O4, two HP(1)O4 and four PO4 tetrahedral units; the Al6P8 SBUs consists of two Al(4)O5 trigonal bipyramids, two Al(5)O4, two Al(6)O4 and eight PO4 tetrahedra. Such Al6P6 and Al6P8 SBUs are interlinked by sharing phosphate-oxygen atoms to result in a 3D framework with 10-ring along the [100] and [010] directions (Fig. 4c,d), and 8-ring along the [001] and [110] directions (Fig. 4e,f). The framework density of this structure measured by the number of polyhedra per 1000 Ã…3 is 15.6, which is comparable to those of open-framework aluminophosphate sieves with 10-ring channels, such as JIS-1 (15.8), ULM-4 (16.7) and MIL-64 (17.3) [8], [36]. Compared to those anionic AlPOs with Al:P ratios of 1/2, 2/3, 3/4, 3/5, 4/5, 5/6, 11/12, 12/13 and 13/18, the Al:P ratio of compound 3 is 6:7 and rarely encountered. Hitherto, there is only one case of an organically template aluminophosphate with unique Al/P ratio of 6/7 (JIS-1). Further structural analysis indicated that the framework of compound 3 is isostructural to JIS-1. Distinct from the contemplating ionothermal synthesis (ionic liquid 1-methyl-3-ethylimidazolium bromide and aromatic amine 1-methylimidazole as co-templates) in JIS-1 [8], the single tmpip2+ guest templates are in situ generated and found perfectly arranged in the middle region of the 8-ring channels in 3. Charged tmpip2+ groups balance the negative charges and simultaneously interact with the inorganic networks via extensive OHâ‹¯O and CHâ‹¯O types hydrogen bonds. A void space analysis using the program PLATON indicates that these extra-framework organic cations in 3 occupy 40.2% of the unit cell volume.

3.3. Thermal stability

Thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) of the powder samples of 1â€“3 were performed out in air atmosphere in the temperature range of 30â€“800 Â°C to investigate their thermal stabilities (Fig. 5). The first small weight losses of 1.05% before 104 Â°C for compound 1, 2.86% before 97 Â°C for 2, and 2.78% before 186 Â°C for 3, may be respectively due to the physically adsorbed water. On further heating, the organic molecules began to decompose. For 1, a sharp weight loss of 20.50% occurred in the range of 340â€“718 Â°C and this value was a little lower than the theoretical value (24.49%), which may be due to the partial retention of carbon in the solid residue (gray in color). Similar phenomenon was previsouly observed in other reported metal phosphates/phosphites [37], [38]. For compounds 2 and 3, the weight losses of 25.50% and 14.86% were observed in the temperature region 226â€“695 Â°C for 2 and 236â€“800 Â°C for 3, which agree well with the removal of the organic molecules (calcd: 24.75% for 2 and 14.55% for 3). The remaining residue of each sample is amorphous after the calcination and their phases are unidentified.

4. Conclusion

In summary, three AlPOs driven by in situ-template-generation strategy have been solvothermally synthesized. With this strategy, two new organic templates (i.e. dmdabco2+ in 1, tmpip2+ in 2 and 3) were created from the in situ methylation reactions between methanol solvent and the starting organic-amine precursors. Compared with classic Eschweilerâ€“Clarke methylation, the simplicity and environmental friendliness of the in situ direct methylation transformation is noteworthy. Under different in situ generated templates, the inorganic frameworks of compounds 1â€“3 display different dimensions: infinite chains in 1 and 2, and open-framework with three-dimensional channel system in 3. This investigation may open a new method toward designing and synthesizing diverse N-alkylated derivatives, which may function as novel templates for making new crystalline materials. Further exploration on this subject, including the feasibility of methylation reactions or alkylation reactions for other aliphatic amine and aromatic amine, is underway.
