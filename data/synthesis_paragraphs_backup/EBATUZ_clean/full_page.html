    Abstract

    Two fof-type porous metal–organic frameworks (MOFs), [Cu2(bdfdpa)(H2O)2]·8DMF·2H2O (JLU-Liu29) and [Cu2(btadpa)(H2O)2]·7DMF·8H2O (JLU-Liu30) (H4bdfdpa = 5,5′-(benzo[1,2-b:4,5-b′]difuran-2,6-diyl)diisophthalic acid and H4btadpa = 5,5′-(benzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole-4,7-diylbis(ethyne-2,1-diyl))diisophthalic acid), have been successfully synthesized by using different expanded tetracarboxylate ligands. Both of them exhibit good permanent porosity and high surface areas of 2205 and 1791 m2 g–1, respectively. Due to open metal sites, different functional groups, and suitable pore spaces, the two porous MOFs exhibit high adsorption performance for small molecule gases (CO2, CH4, C2H6, and C3H8) and removal of refractory organosulfur compounds.

    Synopsis

    JLU-Liu29 and JLU-Liu30 have been constructed by using expanded tetracarboxylate ligands and Cu2(CO2)4 paddlewheel building units. Both of the two compounds exhibit high surface area, excellent adsorption for small molecule gases, and notable removal behavior of refractory organosulfur compounds.

    Recently, great energy consumption accompanied by the high level discharge of carbon dioxide far beyond the standard line has aroused the global concern. It is necessary to reduce the growth of the carbon dioxide concentration in the atmosphere. Several methods can be considered helpful to dispose carbon dioxide. One is to use clean energy instead of fossil energy, and another can be the separation of carbon dioxide from emission sources. Also, the improvement of the energy conversion efficiency can be an effective method.(1-4) In recent years, the storage of the small hydrocarbons (CH4, C2H6, C3H8) is also highly in demand as promising new energy alternatives. As the predominant ingredient of natural gas (NG), CH4 is always mixed with small amounts of CO2 and hydrocarbon as impurities, further purification is necessary.(5) Especially, CH4 is a considerable cleaner energy source for our daily life as the primary component of natural gas and future vehicles.(6) The organosulfur content in fuels is another environmental concern because, during the combustion, organosulfur is changed into SOx, which not only contributes to acid rain but also has a negative effect on human health. Many studies have been undertaken to develop adsorbents for the desulfurization of transportation fuels using zeolites, mesoporous materials, and activated carbons. Apart from the several methods investigated (geological storage, terrestrial ecosystems storage, biological storage, ocean storage, mineral storage, etc.), the porous materials are also investigated for their possibility of storage of carbon dioxide and small hydrocarbons (CH4, C2H6, C3H8) and removal of organosulfurs.(7-9)
    Porous metal–organic frameworks (MOFs), which offer large surface areas, high void volumes, suitable pore size, and chemical stability, have proven their potential application as a series of promising materials in the gas storage areas over the last ten years.(10-13) A large amount of MOFs have been applied to gas absorption and natural gas purification, such as Al-soc-MOF-1, UTSA-35a, and FIR-7a-ht,(14-16) because of the open metal sites and suitable pore cage for their interactions with the gas molecules. However, only a few MOFs materials are known to possess significant organosulfur adsorption properties, such as benzothiophene (BT), dibenzothiophene (DBT), and 4,6-dimethyldibenzothiophene (DMDBT), which are undesirable to refiners (Scheme 1).(17-20)
    Among diverse investigated MOFs, those constructed with isophthalate included carboxylate acid ligands have drawn great attention. For instance, since the first report by Chen and Schröder in their pioneering works,(21, 22) those MOFs assembled from inorganic copper paddlewheel Cu2(CO2)4 secondary building units (SBUs) and organic tetracarboxylate acid ligands are greatly developed.(23-29) Such kinds of MOFs can provide available open Cu2+ sites and variable and suitable pore spaces through the selection of different sized ligands, which can provide the efficient gas storage and sulfur adsorption. Furthermore, the frameworks can be easily decorated with functional groups embedded in the ligands, which is believed to be beneficial for special of gases by enhancing their interactions with the pores. Here in this work, two functional groups, dibenzofuran and benzothiadiazole, have been incorporated between two isophthalate acid moieties to form two new tetracarboxylates ligands, 5,5′-(benzo[1,2-b:4,5-b′]difuran-2,6-diyl)diisophthalic acid (H4bdfdpa) and 5,5′-(benzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole-4,7-diylbis(ethyne-2,1-diyl))diisophthalic acid (H4btadpa). Thus, two MOFs, JLU-Liu29 and JLU-Liu30, with fof topology have been successfully synthesized; they exhibit high performance for adsorption of small molecule gases (CO2, CH4, C2H6, and C3H8) and removal of refractory organosulfur compounds.

    Materials and Physical Characterizations

    The related chemicals and reagents were purchased from commercial sources and used without further purification. Infrared spectra were recorded on a Bruker IFS-66v/S FTIR spectrometer in the range of 400–4000 cm–1 using the KBr pellet. Powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD) data were collected on a Rigaku D-Max 2550 diffractometer with Cu–Kα radiation (λ = 1.5418 Å) over the 2θ range of 3–40° at room temperature. Thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) were carried out with the TA Q500 thermogravimetric analyzer under a heating rate of 10 °C min–1 to 800 °C in air. Elemental analyses (C, H, N, and S) were carried out with a Vario MICRO elemental analyzer.

    X-ray Crystallography

    Single-crystal X-ray diffraction measurements were carried out on a Bruker Apex II CCD diffractometer for JLU-Liu29 and JLU-Liu30 with graphite monochromated Mo Kα (λ = 0.71073 Å) radiation at 293 K. Data processing was accomplished with the SAINT processing program.(33) The structures of these compounds were solved by direct method and refined on F2 by full-matrix least-squares with the SHELEX-2014/7 program.(34) All the non-hydrogen atoms were refined with anisotropic displacement parameters. The hydrogen atoms on the aromatic rings were located geometrically with isotropic thermal parameters of 1.2 times those of the attached carbon atoms. The void volume of these compounds can be obtained by the PLATON/SQUEEZE program,(35, 36) and the related topology analysis is obtained from the TOPOS software.(37) Formulas of these two compounds were designated from the crystallographic data combined with experimental ones (element analyses, TGA). Crystallographic data for JLU-Liu29, JLU-Liu30 (1479873–1479874) have been deposited with the Cambridge Crystallographic Data Centre.     Gas Adsorption Measurements

    The N2, CO2, CH4, C2H6, and C3H8 gas adsorption measurements were performed on a Micromeritics ASAP 2420, Micromeritics ASAP 2020, and Micromeritics 3-Flex instruments. Before gas adsorption measurements, JLU-Liu29 and JLU-Liu30 were exchanged with fresh acetone for 3 days to fully removal the guest DMF and water molecules, which can be proved by TGA analysis (Figure S3). The samples were activated by drying under a dynamic vacuum at room temperature for 1 h. Before the measurement, the sample was dried again by using the “outgas” function of the surface area analyzer for 10 h at 100 °C.

    GC–MS Measurements

    A GC–MS system (GCMS-QP 2010 plus, Shimadzu, Kyoto, Japan) was used. Chromatographic separation was conducted with a DB-5MS capillary column (30 m × 0.25 mm i.d., film thickness of 0.25 μm, J & W Scientific, Folsom, CA, USA). Helium (purity ≥ 99.999%) was used as carrier gas at a constant flow of 1.0 mL min–1. The temperature program was set 140 °C for benzothiophene (BT), 250 °C for dibenzothiophene (DBT), and 4,6-dimethyldibenzothiophene (DMDBT). Injector temperature was maintained at 250 °C, and the injection volume was 1.0 μL in the splitless mode. The ion source and interface temperatures were 200 and 250 °C, respectively, and electron impact ionization energy was 70 eV. The mass spectrometer was operated in the selective ion monitoring (SIM) mode. Full-scan MS data were acquired in the range of m/z 50–900 to obtain the fragmentation spectra of the analytes. The samples of acetone exchanged JLU-Liu29 and JLU-Liu30 were degassed at room temperature for 10 h to activate the samples on a degassing station.

    Crystal Structure Descriptions

    Single-crystal X-ray diffraction and TOPOS analyses suggest that both of the compounds are fof-type MOFs. JLU-Liu29 crystallizes in the trigonal R3̅m space group. As expected, the 4-connected copper paddlewheel Cu2(CO2)4 SBUs are further bridged by 3-connected organic ligands to form a (3, 4)-connected structure, which belongs to fof topology with a Schläfli symbol of (6.82)2(62.82.102.) (Figure 1a–c).(38) A further insight into this structure suggests that there are two kinds of distinct cages alternatively packed with a ratio of 1:1 in this framework. As shown in Figure 1d, the spherical-like cages are composed of 6 Cu2(CO2)4 SBUs and 12 bdfdpa4– ligands, and the cage can be regarded as edge decorated by bdfdpa4– ligands with the diameter of ca. 12 Å. The shuttle-like cages are composed of 6 bdfdpa4– ligands and 12 Cu2(CO2)4 units, while the cage can also be regarded as face-decorated by the ligands, the diameter of such a cage is about 6 Å when the van der Waals radii of the atoms are not taken into account. The accessible void volume of JLU-Liu29 after removal of guest molecules is calculated to be 73.9% when the single crystal structure is used with the PLATON/SQUEEZE program. JLU-Liu30 has the same underlying net with JLU-Liu29, and the spherical-like cage is ca. 11 Å, the shuttle-like cage is ca. 6 Å (Figure 1e), and the calculated void volume is 70.9%.

    Gas Adsorption Behaviors

    The permanent porosity of JLU-Liu29 and JLU-Liu30 were confirmed by nitrogen adsorption–desorption isotherms at 77 K. All the samples were activated with acetone for about 3 days and then degassed under high vacuum at 373 K for about 12 h to obtain the guest molecule free frameworks. From the TGA curves, the guest molecules are almost effectively exchanged and the integrity of the samples was investigated with PXRD patterns, as can be seen in Figure S2. Both the samples exhibit the color change from green to purple, which suggests the generation of open Cu2+ sites in the frameworks. The N2 adsorption isotherms of JLU-Liu29 and JLU-Liu30 exhibit reversible typical type I adsorption behaviors with a large amount of N2 at 77 K (698 cm3 g–1 for JLU-Liu29 and 566 cm3 g–1 for JLU-Liu30, at 1 bar). On the basis of the isotherms, the Brunauer–Emmett–Teller (BET) and Langmuir surface areas were estimated to be 2205 and 2967 m2 g–1, respectively, for JLU-Liu29, while 1791 and 2409 m2 g–1 for JLU-Liu30. The pore volumes calculated from the maximum amount of N2 absorbed were 1.07 and 0.81 cm3 g–1. The values of JLU-Liu29 are lower than those of NOTT-101,(39) but slightly higher than those of ZJU-25,(40) and comparable to those of ZJNU-40.(41) Due to that benzothiadiazole moieties included in the frameworks may block up the cages formed. The values of JLU-Liu30 are lower than those of JLU-Liu29, and comparable to those of Cu2dbip(42) (Table S3).
    The high surface area, large pore volume, open Cu2+ sites, and functional groups moieties of these two MOFs promoted us to investigate their capacities in the gas uptake. To investigate the performance of these compounds for CO2 capture, low pressure CO2 adsorption–desorption measurements were measured at 273 and 298 K, respectively. As illustrated in Figure 2, a high CO2 uptake of about 145 cm3 g–1 can be observed for JLU-Liu29 at 273 K and 1 bar, while JLU-Liu30 reaches a value of 49 cm3 g–1. The uptakes of CO2 for these materials at 298 K also reach to 72 and 26 cm3 g–1, respectively. Remarkably, though the CO2 uptake of JLU-Liu29 and JLU-Liu30 is lower than that of NU-135,(27) the relatively higher uptake value of JLU-Liu29 is still significantly higher than that of NOTT-101(39)(Table S3).
    The potential of these compounds for small hydrocarbons adsorption is also explored. The adsorption isotherms of CH4, C2H6, and C3H8 are measured at 273 and 298 K under 1 bar, respectively. The maximum uptakes of JLU-Liu29 for CH4 are 25 and 15 cm3 g–1, for C2H6 are 201 and 127 cm3 g–1, and for C3H8 are 236 and 207 cm3 g–1, respectively. Another compound, JLU-Liu30, also exhibits notable adsorption capacities to CH4, C2H6, and C3H8. Adsorption of CH4 is 29 and 17 cm3 g–1, C2H6 is 199 and 116 cm3 g–1, and C3H8 is 237 and 208 cm3 g–1 at 273 and 298 K under 1 bar, respectively, which overpass the values of JLU-Liu15,(43) JLU-Liu22,(44) and BIF-24(45) (Figure 3).

    Organosulfurs Removal Experiments

    Most MOF materials show a high affinity to water or hydrogen, but only a few are known to possess significant organosulfurs removal properties. In this study, we have investigated JLU-Liu29’s and JLU-Liu30’s performance for adsorption of benzothiophene (BT), dibenzothiophene (DBT), and 4,6-dimethyldibenzothiophene (DMDBT) molecules, which were dissolved in n-hexane solvent. A 5 mg portion of the samples was measured and added into 1 mL of n-hexane solvent containing 0.01 mmol of BT, 0.01 mmol of DBT, and 0.01 mmol of DMDBT. A series of the same mixture were made while increasing the sample weight by 5 mg each time until the sample weight reaches 60 mg. The mixture was standing for 12 h at room temperature. The amount of BT, DBT, or DMDBT in the n-hexane phase was determined by GC–MS analysis. The results clearly show the decrease of BT, DBT, and DMDBT in the n-hexane solvent with increasing the amount of the samples. The concentration of the BT treated by JLU-Liu29 was reduced from 10 to 2.14 mmol L–1. The DBT and the DMDBT were reduced to 0.59 and 0.8 mmol L–1, respectively. As for the other compound, JLU-Liu30, the final concentrations of BT, DBT, and DMDBT were 3.09, 2.17, and 1.58 mmol L–1 (Figure 4). These results are better than HKUST-1(46) and comparable to the reported porous MOFs, such as [In3(bpdc)3(HCOO)(H2O)3/2]·xDMF.(17) Both the compounds exhibiting great adsorption for organosulfurs can be attributed to the π-complexation between the organosulfur compounds and the functional groups, dibenzofuran and benzothiadiazole.(17-19)

    In summary, by utilizing two tetracarboxylate acid ligands including functional moieties such as dibenzofuran and benzothiadiazole, two fof-type MOFs, JLU-Liu29 and JLU-Liu30, have been obtained. Both of these compounds exhibit high permanent porosity with the pore volume of about 1.07 and 0.81 cm3 g–1. Combined with high surface areas, open Cu2+ sites, and functional groups, these compounds show high capacity of CO2 and their abilities for sulfur removal of different benzothiophene, dibenzothiophene, and 4,6-dimethyldibenzothiophene were evaluated. This work can further demonstrate the potential application of the fof-type MOF families in the gas storage and organosulfur purification areas. Thus, the further development of fof-type MOFs is worth doing in the future.
