Two coordination polymers of benzene-1,2,4,5-tetracarboxylic acid (H4BTC): in situ ligand syntheses, structures, and luminescent properties†

Di-Chang Zhong *a, Hua-Bin Guo a, Ji-Hua Deng a, Qing Chen *b and Xu-Zhong Luo a
aCollege of Chemistry & Chemical Engineering, Gannan Normal University, Ganzhou 341000, China. E-mail: zhong_dichang@hotmail.com; Fax: +86 797 8393670
bInstitute of Chinese Materia Medica, Guangxi University of Chinese Medicine, Nanning 530001, China. E-mail: qing0082@163.com

Received 6th March 2015 , Accepted 27th March 2015

First published on 27th March 2015

Solvothermal reaction of Co(II)/Zn(II) salt with 1,2,4,5-benzene tetracarboxylic dianhydride (BTD) leads to the formation of two coordination polymers based on benzene-1,2,4,5-tetracarboxylic acid (H4BTC), [Co2(BTC)(DMA)(H2O)]n (1) (DMA = N,N′-dimethylacetamide) and [Zn4(BTC)2(H2O)6]n·3nH2O (2). Both 1 and 2 have been characterized by infrared spectra (IR), elemental analyses (EA), thermogravimetric analyses (TG) and single-crystal/powder X-ray diffraction (XRD). The results of the single-crystal X-ray diffraction analyses reveal that both compounds are 3D coordination polymers. In 1, 1D Co(II)-carboxylate chains are linked together by the organic moiety of BTC4− ligands, forming a 3D coordination polymer. In 2, 1D Zn-BTC4− ribbons are connected together by [Zn2(H2O)3] building units, generating a 3D porous framework with 1D rectangular channels. Gas sorption measurements reveal that the two compounds can not adsorb N2. Photoluminescent study indicates that 2 exhibits luminescent properties with an emission peak at 527 nm.

Introduction

Recently, considerable efforts have still been devoted to the design and syntheses of porous coordination polymers (PCPs) that have incredible huge surface areas, tunable pore diameters and a pore surface that is accessible to be modified.1 The main reasons are that these pore characteristics endow these materials with potential applications in gas separation and storage,2 catalysis,3 photoluminescence,4 molecular magnetism,5 and so on. Use of varied organic linkers and diverse metal ions have led to the formation of increasingly complicated frameworks with a variety of proposed applications.6 The changing of reaction conditions such as solvent, pH value, ratio of reactants, temperature, and counteranion has also afforded diverse polymer materials.7 Additionally, the selection of different synthetic approaches has also resulted in numerous complex materials with intriguing structures and diverse functions.8 In all, with the combined knowledge accumulated in coordination chemistry over the past two decades, function-directed structure predictability, an important requirement for the synthesis of functional materials, has even become possible, in many cases by combining topological principles laid out by Wells with new computational models.9

In situ ligand synthesis, with advantages in simplicity, effectiveness, and environmental friendliness, is regarded as an ideal and efficient approach for the preparation of new functional materials.10 The reactions of in situ ligand syntheses usually include [2 + 3] cycloaddition reaction, substitution reaction, hydrolysis reaction, decarboxylation reaction and so on.11 During the past several years, in situ ligand synthesis has been developed rapidly in the construction of coordination polymers, as it usually generates complex materials with unexpected structures and functions, which can not be obtained by the direct reactions of the ligands with the corresponding metal salts.12In situ hydrolysis ligand synthesis is one of the most powerful techniques for the construction of coordination polymers among the in situ ligand syntheses. A number of coordination polymers based on carboxylic ligands have been constructed by in situ hydrolysis of ligand precursors containing cyano, ester or phosphonic ester groups.13 However, to the best of our knowledge, an example of a coordination complex of carboxylate synthesized by in situ hydrolysis of anhydride compounds has not been reported yet. Very recently, we, by in situ hydrolysis of a carboxylic precursor (anhydride), obtained two porous coordination polymers, [Co2(BTC)(DMA)(H2O)]n (1) and [Zn4(BTC)2(H2O)6]n·3nH2O (2) (H4BTC = benzene-1,2,4,5-tetracarboxylic acid, DMA = N,N′-dimethylacetamide). In this article, we report their syntheses, structures and properties of gas adsorption and luminescence.

Experimental section

Materials and physical measurements

All of the chemicals are commercially available and were used without further purification. 1,2,4,5-Benzene tetracarboxylic dianhydride (BTD) was purchased from Nanjing Tianzun chemical Co., Ltd. Elemental analyses were determined using an Elementar Vario EL elemental analyser. IR spectra were recorded in the 4000 to 400 cm−1 region using KBr pellets and a Bruker EQUINOX 55 spectrometer. Thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) were carried out on a NetzschTG-209 instrument in air atmosphere. Powder X-ray diffraction patterns (XRD) were recorded on a D8 ADVANCE X-Ray diffractometer. Photoluminescent measurements were conducted on a RF-5301PC spectrometer.
Synthesis of [Co2(BTC)(DMA)(H2O)]n (1). A mixture of Co(NO3)2·6H2O (0.5 mmol, 0.146 g), 1,2,4,5-benzene tetracarboxylic dianhydride (BTD) (0.2 mmol, 0.044 g), dimethylacetamide DMA (3 mL), and H2O (2 mL) was heated at 85 °C for 48 h in a 20 mL sealed Teflon-lined stainless steel vessel. After the autoclave was cooled over a period of 11 h at a rate of 5 °C h−1, purple block-shaped crystals of 1 were isolated by filtration, washed with water, and dried in air. Yield: 0.037 g, 31% base on Co. Anal. calcd for C14H13NO10Co2: C, 35.54; H, 2.77; N, 2.96%. Found: C, 35.41; H, 2.85; N, 2.84%. Synthesis of [Zn4(BTC)2(H2O)6]n·3nH2O (2). A mixture of Zn(NO3)2·6H2O (0.5 mmol, 0.149 g), 1,2,4,5-benzene tetracarboxylic dianhydride (BTD) (0.5 mmol, 0.104 g), NH3·H2O (0.5 mL), and H2O (7.5 mL) was heated at 110 °C for 48 h in a 20 mL sealed Teflon-lined stainless steel vessel. After the autoclave was cooled over a period of 16 h at a rate of 5 °C h−1, colorless cubic-shaped crystals of 2 were isolated by filtration, washed with water, and dried in air. Yield: 0.066 g, 57% base on Zn. Anal. calcd for C20H22O25Zn4: C, 26.00; H, 2.40%. Found: C, 26.16; H, 2.33%. Determination of crystal structures

Single-crystal data for 1 and 2 were collected at 293 K on a Bruker SMART APEX II diffractometer, with Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å). The empirical absorption corrections were applied using the SADABS program.14 Both structures were solved using direct methods, which yielded the positions of all non-hydrogen atoms. These were refined first isotropically and then anisotropically. The hydrogen atoms of the ligands were placed in calculated positions with fixed isotropic thermal parameters and included in the structure factor calculations in the final stage of full-matrix least-squares refinement. The hydrogen atoms of lattice water molecules in 2 were not assigned. All calculations were performed using the SHELXTL system of computer programs.15 The crystallographic data were summarized in Table 1, and the selected bond lengths and angles were listed in Table S1.†

Results and discussion

Preparation chemistry

Under solvothermal conditions, reactions of Co(II) and Zn(II) salts with 1,2,4,5-benzene tetracarboxylic dianhydride (BTD), which is easily hydrolyzed to yield 1,2,4,5-benzene tetracarboxylic acid (H4BTC), afforded two coordination polymers of [Co2(BTC)(DMA)(H2O)]n (1) and [Zn4(BTC)2(H2O)6]n·3nH2O (2), respectively. To our knowledge, a number of H4BTC-based Co(II)/Zn(II) complexes have been directly synthesized through reactions of the corresponding metal salts with the H4BTC ligand.16,17 However, 1 and 2, prepared by in situ hydrolysis ligand reactions, could not be obtained by the direct synthetic method. These results illustrate that in situ ligand synthesis is really an effective approach for the construction of new functional complex materials. Especially for commonly used ligands, new functional complexes may be afforded in in situ synthesis conditions. Therefore, by using an in situ hydrolysis synthesis method, more new H4BTC-based complex materials with good chemical or physical properties can be expected in the future.

Crystal structures

X-ray single-crystal diffraction analysis reveals that 1 crystallizes in an orthorhombic crystal system, with the space group of Pbcn. As shown in Fig. 1a, there are two crystallographically independent Co(II) ions in 1. Both Co1 and Co2 are coordinated by six O atoms and show slightly distorted octahedral geometries. The six O atoms around Co1 come from four individual BTC4− anions (O1, O2, O4v, O5vi, O8viii) and one H2O molecule (O10). Co1 is situated at the centre of the octahedron. O2, O4v, O5vi and O8viii locate the equatorial plane. O1 and O10 occupy the two axial positions. The six O atoms surrounding Co2 are from three individual BTC4− anions (O2, O3, O6vii, O7vii, O7viii) and one DMA molecule (O9). O2, O3, O6vii and O7vii locate the equatorial plane. O7viii and O9 occupy the two axial positions. Co2 sits on the C2 symmetry axes. As we expected, the ligand H4BTC has been in situ generated from the hydrolysis of BTD under solvothermal conditions. The four carboxylic groups of the resulted H4BTC are deprotonated and bond to seven Co(II) ions. The Co–O bonds, ranging from 2.000(4) to 2.276(7) Å, are unexceptional (Table S1†). Through the bridging of the carboxylic groups, all the Co(II) ions are connected together, forming a 1D Co(II)-carboxylate chain along the c axis (Fig. 1b). These 1D chains are further linked together by the organic moiety of the BTC4− anion (aromatic ring), generating a 3D coordination polymer. DMA and H2O molecules are immobilized on the polymer framework by coordination interactions (Fig. 1c).

Compound 2 crystallizes in a monoclinic crystal system, with the space group of C2/c. As shown in Fig. 2a, the structure of 2 contains two crystallographically independent Zn(II) ions. Zn1 is coordinated by five O atoms (O1i, O2i, O3ii, O7, O8) from three individual BTC4− anions, and one coordinated H2O (O9) molecule, resulting in a greatly distorted octahedral geometry. Zn2 shows a five-coordinated greatly distorted trigonal bipyramidal geometry. The five coordinated O atoms come from two individual BTC4− anions (O5, O6, O5i) and two coordinated H2O molecules (O10, O11). The distances of Zn1–O2 (2.634 (1)Å) and Zn1–O7 (2.872(5)Å) are much longer than those of other Zn–O (1.950(1) Å ~2.405(4) Å), indicating that O2 and O7 weakly interact with Zn1 (Table S1†). Through the bridging of BTC4− anions, Zn1 ions are connected together to generate a 1D ladder-like ribbon (Fig. 2b). O11 sits on the C2 symmetry axes, by which two Zn2 are bridged together to form a [Zn2(H2O)3] building unit (Fig. 2c). The adjacent ribbons are further connected together by [Zn2(H2O)3] building units through coordination interactions (Fig. 2d), forming a 3D porous structure (Fig. 2e). The size of the pore window is about 8.7 Å × 2.6 Å. Lattice H2O molecules are embedded into the surface of the pores. About 10% potential solvent-accessible volume is estimated by using PLATON software (radius of probe sphere is set at 1.2 Å).

Powder X-ray diffraction and thermal analyses

XRD was used to check the purity of 1 and 2. The results show that all the peaks displayed in the measured patterns at room temperature closely match those in the simulated patterns generated from single-crystal diffraction data, indicating single phases of 1 and 2 have been formed (Fig. 3a and b). The thermogravimetric (TG) curve of 1 shows a weight loss of 3.5% from room temperature to 170 °C, corresponding to the removal of one coordinated water molecule per formula unit (calcd. 3.8%). Upon further heating, a continuous weight loss up to 520 °C is observed (Fig. 4). The TG analysis for 2 reveals that 2 loses its lattice and coordinated water molecules when heated to 290 °C. The weight loss found of 17.3% is consistent with that calculated (17.5%). Upon further heating, the desolvated 2 decomposes through another two continuous weight loss processes (Fig. 4).

Effect of ligand precursor

To further investigate the influence of ligand precursors on the reactions final products, we have performed two controlled experiments under the same reaction conditions as those of the in situ reactions. One uses H4BTC as the ligand instead of BTD, and the other uses a mixture of H4BTC and BTD (H4BTC and BTD in molar ratio of 1:1) instead of BTD. Unfortunately, we have not obtained single crystals suitable for X-ray diffraction analyses. Powder XRD measurements clearly show that the diffraction peaks of the micro-crystalline products do not match to those of 1 or 2 (Fig. S2†), which indicates that under the same reaction conditions, when the reaction precursor of BTD is replaced with H4BTC or a mixture of H4BTC and BTD, new crystalline phases are generated, i.e., after exchanging the ligand precursor, 1 or 2 can not be successfully synthesized under the same reaction conditions. These results clearly illustrate that the final products are influenced closely by the ligand precursors. As the reaction intermediates are hardly captured, the reaction mechanisms are not readily uncovered. Therefore, the reason why the products are different is not easily to be figured out. We speculate that pH value may be a very important reaction factor in both reaction systems for the formation of 1 and 2. Different ligand precursors will obviously result in different pH values of the reaction systems, which further influences the reaction processes and final products. These results further reveal that in situ ligand synthesis is a useful approach for the preparation of new functional materials.

Gas adsorption and photoluminescence

To examine the possible porous nature in 1 and 2, N2 sorption properties for desolvated 1 and 2 were investigated. The results show that neither 1 nor 2 exhibits N2 uptake at 77 K, which indicates that there are no pores in 1 or 2 after the removal of coordinated DMA/H2O molecules. These observations also reveal that the coordinated DMA/H2O molecules play important roles in the stability of 1/2, which can be understood from the theory of coordination chemistry. In 1 and 2, the metal centres have typical coordination numbers and suitable coordination geometries. Once the coordinated solvent molecules are removed, the coordination numbers around the metal centres are reduced and the suitable coordination geometries are broken, leading to the instabilities of 1 and 2. Therefore, with the removal of these coordinated solvent molecules, the frameworks collapse concurrently.

Constructed from Zn(II) and a conjugated organic ligand, 2 may exhibit excellent luminescent behavior. The photoluminescent measurement for 2 was carried out in the solid state at room temperature. The result depicted in Fig. 5 reveals that 2 exhibits photoluminescence upon excitation at 365 nm. The emission peak at 527 nm can be attributed to ligand-to-ligand charge transition (LLCT), which has been observed in other Zn(II)-carboxylate coordination polymers.18


Fig. 5 Emission spectrum of 2 recorded in the solid state at room temperature (λex = 365 nm).

Conclusions

Two coordination polymers based on benzene-1,2,4,5-tetracarboxylic acid (H4BTC), which can not be obtained by the direct reactions of the corresponding metal salts with H4BTC, have been successfully prepared through in situ hydrolysis ligand reactions. Both 1 and 2 are 3D coordination polymers. 1 features 1D Co(II)-carboxylate chains linked together by the organic moiety of BTC4− ligands, and 2 features 1D Zn-BTC4− ribbons that are connected together by [Zn2(H2O)3] dimer building units. The photoluminescent study indicates that 2 is a potential green light material.
