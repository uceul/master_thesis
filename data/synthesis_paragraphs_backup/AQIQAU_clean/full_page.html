Journal of Molecular Structure

Volume 1123, 5 November 2016, Pages 394-399

Two novel CPs with double helical chains based rigid tripodal ligands: Syntheses, crystal structures, magnetic susceptibility and fluorescence properties

Author links open overlay panelXiaoWangabXiang-YangHouaQuan-GuoZhaibMan-ChengHub
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.molstruc.2016.07.039Get rights and content

Highlights

•

Multi-functional tripodal rigid ligands.

•

The 1-2 reveal new (3,5)-net and alb-(3,6)-net topology, and containing a novel double-helical chains respectively.

•

Good fluorescence properties of 1-2, and antiferromagnetic magnetic for 2.

Abstract

Two three-dimensional coordination polymers (CPs), namely [Cd(bpydb)- (H2bpydb)]n·0.5nH2O (1), and [Cu2(bpydb)2]n (2) (2,6-di-p-carboxyphenyl-4,4’- bipyridine1 = H2bpydb), containing a novel double-helical chains, which have been solvothermal synthesized, characterized, and structure determination. CPs 1-2 reveal the new (3,5)-net and (3,6)-net alb topology, respectively. The fluorescence properties of CPs 1-2 were investigated, and magnetic susceptibility measurements indicate that compound 1 has dominating antiferromagnetic couplings between metal ions.

1. Introduction

Rapid development of CPs has been made in recent years not only their fascinating architectures [1], [1]a), [1]b), [1]c), [1]d) but also potential applications as functional materials, such as Photocatalytic [2], electrochemical [3], [3]a), [3]b), luminescence [4], [4]a), [4]b), magnetism [5], [5]a), [5]b), porosity properties [6], [6]a), [6]b), [6]c), etc. Luminescence is the term used to describe the process in which light is produced through the absorption of energy. Which contains two basic forms, fluorescence and phosphorescence. Fluorescence refers to the emitting of light between energy states of the same spin multiplicity, and the process lasts no more than 10 ns So far, many of luminescent CPs have been reported, and the luminescence can exist in several forms [7], [7]a), [7]b), [7]c), [7]d), [7]f), [7]g). There are four different types of luminescence mechanism, which are ligand-based luminescence, lanthanide metal-centered luminescence, charge-transfer luminescence (ligand-to-metal charge transfer (LMCT) and metal-toligand charge transfer (MLCT)), and guest-induced luminescence. The magnetic properties of the metal complexes Is the most studied molecular magnets. Generally, which center atoms is transition metal, rare earth metal ions and transition-rare earth metal ions, and mononuclear, dinuclear or polynuclear as its basic building blocks, through intermolecular interactions that can get a one-dimensional, two-dimensional and three-dimensional molecular magnets [8], [8]a), [8]b), [8]c). Metal complexes have good magnetic properties, must meet the following conditions: 1)larger spin ground state; 2)ignore the magnetic properties of the opposite sex; 3)the lower level of spin excited states; 4)the weaker ferromagnetic interactions; 5)high levels of metal ions. However, helicity, as an important factor of crystal engineering, has also been successfully introduced into complexes architectures field [9]. Although, helicity-CPs containing of one or several factors, such as discrete helices, single spiral chain, double helix chain, multi-helical-array, have been reported [10]f), [10]h), [10], [10]a), [10]b), [10]c), [10]d), [10]e). However, especially assembly of the helical units into a multi-helical-array is still a great challenge. About higher interest is the nexploratio of symmetrical rigid tripodal ligands, which may adopt various coordination modes to satisfy the requirements of assembly process and lead to interesting architectures. Along with our work of assembly of CPs based upon symmetrical rigid tripodal ligand [11]. Herein, we have focused on H2bpydb, a rigid tripodal ligand reported two unprecedented three-dimensional architectures [Cd(bpydb)(H2bpydb)]n·0.5nH2O (1), and [Cu2(bpydb)2]n (2), with weaving double - helical chains from a versatile rigid tripodal ligand H2bpydb and Cd2+ and Cu2+ ions. Moreover, solid-state photoluminescence measurements show 1 and 2 are good candidates for luminescence materials, and magnetic susceptibility measurements indicate that 1 has strong dominating antiferromagnetic couplings between Cu2+ metal ions.

2. Experimental

2.1. General

All available solvents and starting materials of analytical grade in the experiments was purchased from Sigma-Aldrich. The typical PXRD patterns were recorded using Cu-Kα radiation on a PANalytical X'Pert PRO diffractometer. The infrared spectra were measured between 4000 and 400 cm−1 on a Bruker EQUINOX-55 spectrophotometer using KBr pellets. Elemental analysis(C, H, N) was determined on a Perkin-Elmer 2400 type elemental analyzer. The thermogravimetric analyses were performed in platinum crucibles on a NETZSCH STA449C thermogravimetric analyzer under a atmosphere with a heating rate of 10 °C min−1. The solid samples photoluminescence analyses were performed on Edinburgh Instrument FLS920 fluorescence spectrometer at ambient temperature. Magnetic measurement was performed of 1 using a MPMS-XL-7 magnetometer under an applied field of 1000 Oe over the temperature range of 1.8–300 K.

2.2. Synthesis of 1

Synthesis of [Cd(bpydb)(H2bpydb)(H2O)]n (1): A mixture of CdCO3 (0.05 mmol, 0.009 g), H2bpydb (0.05 mmol, 0.020 g) was dissolved in 5 mL distilled water. The resulting solution was stirred for about 60 min at room temperature, sealed in a 20-mL Teflon-lined stainless steel autoclave under autogenous pressure at 180 °C for 4 days to yield 12 mg yellow sheet-shaped crystals. Anal. Calcd (Found) for C96H62Cd2N8O17: C, 63.22 (63.20); H, 3.41 (3.43); N, 6.17 (6.14).

2.3. Synthesis of 2

[Cu2(bpydb)2]n (2): A mixture of CuCl2·2H2O (0.06 mmol, 0.010 g), H2bpydb (0.06 mmol, 0.024 g) was dissolved in 5 mL distilled water. The resulting solution was stirred for about 60 min at room temperature, sealed in a 20-mL Teflon-lined stainless steel autoclave under autogenous pressure at 140 °C for 6 days to yield 8 mg light blue block crystals. Anal. Calcd (Found) for C48H28Cu2N4O8: C, 62.28 (62.95); H, 3.12 (3.08); N, 6.09 (6.12).

2.4. X-ray structural determinations

Data were collected with a Bruker APEX II CCD using Cu-Kα radiation and corrected for absorption with SADABS. The structure was solved by the direct methods and successive Fourier difference syntheses, and refined by the full-matrix least-squares method on F2 with anisotropic thermal parameters for all non-hydrogen atoms (SHELXTL Version 5.1). All hydrogen atoms were added according to theoretical models. Crystal structures and packing figures were drawn with the program diamond 3.1. The crystallographic refinement data are listed below.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Structure description of 1

The single-crystal X-ray analysis reveals that complexe 1 crystallizes in the Monoclinic space group C2/c. The asymmetric unit of complexe 1 consists of one Cd12+ center, one completely deprotonated bpydb2− ligand, one H2bpydb ligand, and half of lattice water molecule. As shown in Fig. 1, the structure consists of one kind coordination mode of Cd12+ center, each Cd12+ center is seven coordinated by one nitrogen (N1a) from a bpydb2− ligand, and six carboxyl oxygens (O1, O2, O3c, O4, O7b, O8b) from two bpydb2− and H2bpydb ligands, respectively, to form a distorted single-cap octahedron geometry of Cd[NO7] moieties (Fig. S1), in which O1, O2, O4, and N1a occupied the equatorial plane position, O3c located in the side of the plane, O7b and O8b in the other side of the plane, Cd2+ ion lies in the center. The CdO/N bond lengths vary from 2.269(4) to 2.528(4) Å, and the OCdO/N bond angle vary from 53.40(19) to 155.97(19) °. The Cd12+ center is alternately coordinated by bpydb2− and H2bpydb ligands to generate a double-1D chains along the b axis direction (Fig. S2 and Fig. 2). The most striking feature of double-1D chain is novel interweaving helical chain formed by the tripodal bpydb2−, H2bpydb ligands, and metal Cd12+ centers. Four carboxylate groups from bpydb2− and H2bpydb ligands are bonded to three Cd12+ centers in bidentate chelating and monodentate fashion, giving rise to infinite right-handed and left-handed helical chain (-Cd-H2bpydb-Cd-bpydb2--Cd-)n, which is generated based on the repeated unit containing three metal Cd12+ centers and bpydb2− and H2bpydb ligands with a long pitch of 25.874(6) Å. Notably, the rigid tripodal ligand acts as a bisdentate linker in constructing the helical chains. Viewed down the b axis, two helical chains are arranged as a exceptional irregular quadrilateral. Interweaving double-helical chains leads to a two dimensional network by carboxylate groups of bpydb2− and H2bpydb ligands to bridge the metal Cd12+ possessing c axis direction (Fig. 2). Further, the double-helical chains by nitrogen atoms of bpydb2− bonded adjacent double-helical chains along the a axis come into being a three-dimensional network (Fig. 3 and Fig. S3). Moreover, bpydb2− ligand adopted coordination modes (Fig. S4) are μ3-kO,O: kO,O: kN to link three Cd12+ ions, and H2bpydb are in μ2-kO: kO modes to link two Cd2+ ions, and Cd12+ center bonded five ligands. Hence, each metal center can be considered as a node and the two kinds of ligands as a node and linear spacers, the whole structure is a new (3,5)-connected network (Fig. 4) with a Schlafli symbol of {8.102}{84.106}.

3.2. Structure description of 2

Complexe 2 crystallizes also in the monoclinic C2/c space group. The asymmetric unit of 2 contains two Cu2+, and two bpydb2− ligands (Fig. 5). Each Cu12+ and Cu22+ ions coordinates to four carboxylate oxygen atoms (O1,O2, O3a, O8b for Cu12+, O4a, O5, O6, O7b for Cu22+) and one nitrogen atom (N3c for Cu12+, N4d for Cu22+) to give a distorted triangular bipyramid of Cu[NO4] moieties (Fig. S2). The CoO/N distances are in the range of 1.938(4)-2.347(5) Å and OCuO/N bond angles are in the range of 59.59(16)-159.86(19)°. Two carboxylate groups of bpydb2− ligands are linked two adjacent Cu12+ and Cu22+ via the bidentate bridging coordination modes, to form a [Cu2(CO2)2] binuclear cluster with the Cu⋯Cu separation of 2.900(1) Å, and each binuclear cluster is coordinated by six bpydb2− ligands through the bidentate chelating, bidentate bridging, monodentate coordination modes (Fig. S5). On the other hand, each bpydb2− ligands adopts the μ4-kO,O: kO: kO: kN modes to bridge three binuclear clusters (Fig. S4c). Thus, which led to the novel 3D network for complexe 2 (Fig. 6). The interesting structural feature of 2 contains of one infinite interweaving double helical chains with a long pitch of 26.8650(12) Å, one is right-handed and the other one is left-handed (Fig. S6). Further, the double-helical chains by nitrogen atoms of the bpydb2− bonded adjacent double-helical chains come into being a two-dimensional network (Fig. S7). To analyze the net, we can define bpydb2− ligand and the binuclear clusters as three-connected and six-connected nodes, respectively. As shown in Fig. 7, these two nonequivalent nodes lead to a (3,6)-connected network of complexe 2 with Schlafli symbol of {4.82}2{42.812.10}, which corresponds to the alb topology.

3.3. Powder X-ray diffraction, IR, and thermogravimetric analysis

XRPD atterns for the samples of 1 and 2 are depicted in Fig. S8. The well-matched patterns between the experimental and simulated results indicate the phase purity of the products.

In the FT-IR spectrum for 1, 2 and H2bpydb ligand (Fig. S9), the asymmetric stretching vibration (vas-COO-) was observed at 1637 and 1644 cm−1 and symmetric stretching vibration (vs-COO-) strong absorptions at 1381 and 1390 cm−1, respectively. However, the vCO and δO-H of carboxyl about H2bpydb ligand two characteristic absorption peaks appeared in 1660 and 1005 cm−1,two characteristic absorption peaks of H2bpydb ligand are disappear in 1 and 2. The observed broad peak at 3490 cm−1 in the FT-IR spectrum is attributed to the OH stretching vibration.

The TGA curve of complexes 1-2 show that 1 and 2 have good thermal stability (Fig. S10). For 1, the first weight loss in the temperature range of 80–280 °C is consistent with the removal of lattice water; The second process began from 406 °C, corresponding to the decomposition of organic ligands, which is nearly in agreement with the single-crystal analysis. For 2, no obvious weight loss was observed until 416 °C, on further heating, until 610 °C, which is attributed to the loss of bpydb2− organic ligands.

3.4. Luminescent properties of 1 and 2

The fluorescence properties of CPs are great interest due to their various applications in light-emitting diodes, and chemical sensor [12]. CPs 1, 2 and H2bpydb ligand were investigated in the solid state at room temperature, as shown in Fig. 8, Fig. 9, Fig. 10, respectively. The H2bpydb ligand (excitation at 325 nm) reveals an intense emission with the maximum peaks at 397 nm. CPs 1 shows a strong emission maximum peaks approximately at 506 nm upon the excitation maximum peaks at 403 nm. The emission peaks of complexe 2 be located at 508 nm upon excitation at 404 nm. The emission fluorescence of CPs 1 and 2 show a red shift compared with that of the H2bpydb ligands, which are probably due to enhanced conjugation in the ligands upon metal coordination, may be arise from ligand-to-metal charge-transfer (LMCT) transitions [7], [7]a), [7]b), [7]c), [7]d).

3.5. Magnetic properties of complex 2

The variable temperature magnetic susceptibilities of complex 2 are shown in Fig. 11. At room temperature the XmT value of complex 2 is 0.81 cm3 K mol−1. Which value is consistent with that of the reported about dinuclear Cu2+ ions 0.78 cm3 K mol−1 [13]. With the lowering of temperature, XmT values slowly decrease until about 50 K and then falls drastically reaching a value of 0.15 cm3 K mol−1 at 1.8 K. The Xm value of 2 increases less from 1.69 × 10−3 cm3 mol−1 at 300 K to 5.53 × 10−3 cm3 mol−1 at 90 K. And when the temperature drops to 2 K, the Xm value of Xm significantly increase is 4.90 × 10−2 cm3 mol−1. The analysis of the magnetic data through the Curie -Weiss law in the equation, χM = C/(T - θ), led to C = 1.9056 cm3 K mol−1, θ = - 0.6676 K for 2. Such behavior indicates the antiferromagnetic coupling between adjacent Cu2+ centers for complex 2 [14].

According to the crystal structure, complex 2 can be considered as dinuclear from the viewpoint of magnetism, in which the adjacent Cu2+ centers are linked by two carboxylate bridges since the coupling through bpydb2− ligand. It is known that comparing the two carboxylate bridges play a negligible role in propagating the magnetic exchange. Therefore the susceptibility date were analyzed by Bleaney-Bowers expression based on a Heisenberg Hamiltonian H = 2JS1S2:(1)χM′=2Ng2β2kT13+exp(−2J/kT)(1−ρ)+Ng2β22kT·ρ(2)χM=χM′1−zJ′χM′(3)y=((1−P3)∗0.75∗P1∗P1/(3+exp(−20882∗P2/x))/x+(0.1875P1∗P1∗P3/X)/(1−P4∗((1−P3)∗0.75∗P1∗P1/(3+exp(−2.882∗P2/X))/X+0.1875∗P1∗P1∗P/x)))where N, g, β and k have their usual meanings, ρ is a variable fraction of paramagnetic impurities, zj accounts for the interdimer interactions. The least-squares analysis of magnetic susceptibility date led to g(P1) = 2.08, J(P2) = −22.64, ρ(P3) = 0.13, zJ′(P4) = −2.05. The results indicatethat the magnitude of the magnetic coupling between the dinuclear Cu2+ ions is strong. As is well known, the magnetic properties are closely related to the bridging conformation adopted by the carboxylate group in the dinuclear systems, the syn-syn carboxylate bridge is able to mediate very strong antiferromagnetic interactions in complexes [15].

4. Conclusion

In summary, an unprecedented CPs 1 and 2 have been successfully constructed by using a rigid tripodal H2bpydb ligand. Which are three-dimensional framework based on novel interweaving double helical chains. Furthermore, CPs 1 and 2 have the strong fluorescent properties.
