Inorganic Chemistry Communications

Volume 24, October 2012, Pages 237-240

A cobalt(II) metal–organic framework with all-cis 1,2,3,4,5,6-cyclohexanehexacarboxylate ligand: Synthesis, structure, and magnetic properties

Author links open overlay panelZhi-GuoGuabChun-YanPangaZaijunLiaHong-PingXiaoc
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.inoche.2012.07.027Get rights and content

Abstract

A new three-dimensional metal–organic framework, [Co3(cis-CHHC)(H2O)8]n·5nH2O (1), was synthesized by assembling Co(II) ion and all-cis 1,2,3,4,5,6-cyclohexanehexacarboxylate (cis-CHHC) ligand in hydrothermal condition. X-ray crystallography reveals that each cis-CHHC ligand connects six Co(II) atoms, and each Co(II) atom is linked to two cis-CHHC ligands, leading to a 3D structure with channels filled with lattice water molecules. Magnetic analysis of 1 indicates that the antiferromagnetic coupling dominates the magnetic properties.

Graphical abstract

A new three-dimensional metal–organic framework, [Co3(cis-CHHC)(H2O)8]n·5nH2O (1), has been synthesized and structurally characterized. X-ray crystallography reveals that each cis-CHHC ligand connects six Co(II) atoms, and each Co(II) atom is linked to two cis-CHHC ligands, leading to a 3D structure. Week antiferromagnetic coupling dominates the magnetic properties of 1.

Great interest has been focused on metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) due to their interesting topologies, and their potential applications in gas storage, catalysis, photoluminescence, magnetism, conductivity, non-linear optics, ferroelectricity, and biomedicine [1], [2], [3]. In the rational design and synthesis of MOFs, the rigid aromatic ring-based multicarboxylic acid ligands have been extensively employed to construct extended structures with useful properties [4], [5]. In contrast, only a few coordination polymers based on flexible alicyclic ring-based multicarboxylic acid ligands, such as tetrahydrofuran-2,3,4,5-tetracarboxylate, 1,2,3,4-cyclopentanetetracarboxylic acid, and 1,2,3,4,5,6-cyclohexanehexacarboxylate (CHHC) have been reported [6], [7], [8]. Recently, Tong and Thuéry prepared a series of Cd(II), Ni(II), Co(II), Cu(II) and Ln(III) MOFs based on CHHC ligand with diverse structures, and explored the conformation conversion of the ligand [8]. CHHC has six carboxylic groups on the flexible ring skeleton, and possesses diverse conformations and rich coordination modes which make it versatile for constructing MOFs. Furthermore, with twelve coordinating oxygen atoms CHHC is conducive to the close interlinking and clustering of more metal centers, which may usually lead to stronger magnetic exchange interaction and promote the construction of magnetic metal–organic frameworks. Thus, it is interesting to introduce paramagnetic metal ions in the CHHC systems, which will bring particularly novel structures and supply excellent models for studying magnetic interaction of high dimensional compound. In our previous work, we employed the flexible CHHC ligand under hydrothermal conditions to generate a Co15 cluster with trans-CHHC acting as a counteranion, and a 3D framework by specific linking of the Co15 clusters with partially deprotonated cis-CHHC ligand [9]. In a continuous effort to prepare new magnetic MOFs with CHHC ligand, we present herein the synthesis and magnetic study of a new cobalt(II) 3D framework [Co3(cis-CHHC)(H2O)8]n·5nH2O (1).

Complex 1 was obtained by the self-assembly hydrothermal reaction of CoCl2·6H2O, NaOH and cis-CHHC in a molar ration of 6:6:1 at 100 °C [10]. Phase purity of the bulk materials was confirmed by comparison of its powder X-ray diffraction pattern with that calculated from the single crystal study (Fig. S1). Single-crystal X-ray determination reveals that 1 crystallizes in monoclinic P21/c space group with the asymmetric unit consisting of three crystallographically unique Co(II) atoms, one deprotonated cis-CHHC ligand, eight coordinated and five uncoordinated water molecules [11]. As shown in Fig. 1, the cyclohexane ring adopts a chair conformation, and the six carboxylate groups all lie on e-bond positions, suggesting an all-cis conformation of the CHHC ligand. The three Co(II) atoms all display a distorted octahedral geometry with the CoO bond distances in a range of 2.022(2)–2.151(2) Å. Co1 is coordinated by three carboxylate oxygen atoms from two different cis-CHHC ligands and three terminal coordinated water oxygen atoms. Co2 is surrounded by two carboxylate oxygen atoms from different cis-CHHC ligands and four water molecules, in which two symmetry-related ones coordinate in a bridging mode while the other two coordinate in a terminal mode. Co3 is coordinated by four carboxylate oxygen atoms from two different cis-CHHC ligands and two terminal coordinated water oxygen atoms. Each cis-CHHC ligand adopts a μ6-η2:η2:η1:η2:η1:η1 bridging mode connecting six Co(II) atoms through three monodentate and three syn-anti bridging carboxylate groups (Fig. 2). A novel 3D framework structure is therefore generated by the Co(II) carboxylate coordination, displaying flat channels extending along the c axis direction, which are occupied by the hydrogen bonded uncoordinated water molecules (Fig. 3). Topologically, the Co(II) ions and cis-CHHC anions can be viewed as two- and 6-connected nodes, respectively. Therefore, the topology of the framework can be simplified to a (2,6)-connected net with an Vertex Symbol of (12)(42.1213)(4)2 (Fig. S2) using the software TOPOS [12]. Both the lattice water molecules and aqua ligands are found to be engaged in extensive hydrogen bonding interaction, which are responsible for the formation of the hydrogen bonded 1D water chains extending along a axis direction. Additionally, abundant intramolecular hydrogen bonds are found between the aqua ligands O13, O14, O16, O17, O18, O20 and carboxylate oxygen atoms with d(OH⋯O) = 2.523–2.998 Å and ∠(OH⋯O) = 126.3–168.9°. Obviously, the extensive hydrogen bonds make a considerable contribution to stabilization of the crystal structure of 1.

The temperature dependence of magnetic susceptibilities for complex 1 was measured on polycrystalline samples in the temperature range of 2–300 K in a constant magnetic field of 2 kOe (Fig. 4). At room temperature, χMT value of 8.38 emu K mol− 1 is significant higher than the expected spin-only value of 5.625 emu K mol− 1 for three high-spin CoII (SCo = 3/2) ions without any exchange coupling due to the orbital contribution of the Co(II) ion. The χMT value decreases monotonically as the temperature is lowered and reaches 5.83 emu K mol− 1 at 6.0 K, and then drops to 4.93 emu K mol− 1 at 2 K, indicating an overall antiferromagnetic interaction. The antiferromagnetic coupling is further confirmed by a negative Weiss constant (θ = − 15.88 K) by fitting of the 1/χM − T above 50 K using the Curie–Weiss law χM = C/(T − θ) (Fig. 4). The value of the Curie constant (C = 8.86 emu K mol− 1) is consistent with that expected for three six-coordinated high-spin CoII ions [13].

Taking into consideration the 3D structure of 1, neglecting the cyclohexane rings from a viewpoint of magnetic superexchange pathway, each Co(II) atom is connected by two syn-anti bridging carboxylate into a 1D magnetic cobalt(II) chain (Fig. S3). These chains are further connected through the double water bridges to generate an extended 2D magnetic interaction network. No appropriate model is available in the literature to determine the magnetic coupling constant between CoII ions with spin orbit coupling in such a 2D network. In order to evaluate the magnetic interactions in 1, we employed a simple phenomenological Equation χMT = A exp(− E1/kT) + B exp(− E2/kT) that has been used in the previous work [14], where A + B equals the Curie constant, and E1, E2 hold for “activation energies” corresponding to the spin orbit coupling and to the antiferromagnetic exchange interaction, respectively. With this simple model, magnetic susceptibilities of 1 were simulated, giving the best fitting with parameters: A = 3.07 emu K mol− 1, B = 6.09 emu K mol− 1, − E1/k = − 73.36 K, − E2/k = − 0.34 K, and R = 3.7 × 10− 3. The fitted values for A + B agree with Curie constant obtained from the Curie Weiss law, and the value for − E1/k are consistent with those given in the literature for the effect of spin orbit coupling [15]. The value − E2/k = − 0.34 K indicates the antiferromagnetic interaction in 1 is very weak, which can be comparable with the reported cobalt(II) compounds with syn-anti carboxylate brigdes [15].

The magnetization was measured in the field up to 70 kOe, at a fixed temperature of 2 K for 1 (Fig. 5). The unsaturated value of 5.99 Nβ mol− 1 at 7 kOe is significantly lower than the Brillouin curve corresponding to the value of three noninteracting CoII ions with SCo = 3/2, further confirming the antiferromagnetic coupling between the metal ions in 1.

The thermal stabilities of complex 1 have been measured by thermal gravimetric analysis (TGA) under an atmospheric environment (Fig. S4). The first weigh loss until 365 °C was due to the coordinated and uncoordinated water molecules (obsd. 31.43%, cacld 31.09%). The decomposition of the organic links in the anhydrous compound occurs at 365 to 447 °C. The remaining weight corresponds to the formation of Co3O4 (obsd 31.48%, calcd 31.97%).

In summary, we successfully prepared and characterized a novel three-dimensional Co(II) magnetic metal–organic framework with all-cis 1,2,3,4,5,6-cyclohexanehexacarboxylate ligand. Further exploration for more coordination polymers based on flexible alicyclic ring-based multicarboxylic acid ligands with interesting magnetic properties are currently underway in our laboratory.

Preparation of 1: A mixture of CoCl2·6H2O (357 mg, 1.50 mmol), NaOH (60 mg, 1.50 mmol), C6H6(COOH)6·H2O, H6L (92 mg, 0.25 mmol), and 6 mL of H2O was stirred in air for 0.5 h. The resulting solution was heated in a stainless steel reactor with Teflon liner at 100 °C for 2 days. After a period of approximately 24 h cooling to room temperature, purple block crystals of single-phase products for 1 were collected, washed with H2O, and dried in air. Yields: 52%.
