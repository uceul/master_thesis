Introduction

The self-assembly of metalâ€“organic frameworks (MOFs) based on multidentate organic ligands and metal ions is of great interest for material chemists in recent years, due to not only their enormous variety of impressive structural topologies,1 but also their significant potential applications as functional materials in the areas of gas storage and separation,2 magnetic materials,3 luminescence and/or chemical sensors4 and so on. Although studies on the design and synthesis of MOFs have been very prosperous, many attempts have been made to prepare functionalized crystalline materials with highly-connected nodes with the aid of crystal engineering.

On the other hand, the selection of appropriate multifunctional ligands in the design and synthesis of MOFs is a key step. 1H-1,2,3-triazole-4,5-dicarboxylic acid (H3TDA), with one triazolate group and two carboxylate groups, is a versatile multi-chelating ligand, which can provide abundant coordination modes and generate various interesting structures and functional properties.5,6 For instance, we have reported a 2D stair-like transition-metal coordination polymer {[Mn3(TDA)2(H2O)6]Â·6H2O}n,5a which displays antiferromagnetic behavior. The group lead by Chen has also reported two MnII coordination polymers {[Mn3(TDA)2(H2O)2(NH3)2]}n and {[Mn3(TDA)2(H2O)4]}n,6b which exhibit spin-competing behavior as well as unusual multi-step magnetization jumps. There are also many examples of coordination polymers based on 1H-1,2,3-triazole-4,5-dicarboxylic acid, however, most of these MOFs are MnII-based crystalline materials with various magnetic properties.6aâ€“d The investigations on ZnII and CdII MOFs assembled by 1H-1,2,3-triazole-4,5-dicarboxylic acid as luminescent materials are still limited.6e,f

Herein, in this contribution, three new 3D d10-metal-ion-based MOFs, formulated as {[Zn3(TDA)2(bpy)3]Â·(bpy)Â·10.5H2O}n (1), {[Zn3(TDA)2(azopy)2.5(H2O)]Â·4H2O}n (2) and {[Cd2K3(TDA)(HTDA)2(H2O)4]}n (3), have been successfully prepared from the KH2TDA ligand in the presence of three different length-controllable auxiliary ligands, respectively. MOFs 1â€“3 are characterized by infrared spectra, elemental analysis, powder X-ray diffraction, thermogravimetric analysis and single-crystal X-ray diffraction. In 1, ZnII ions are connected by TDA3âˆ’ to generate 2D layers, which are further bridged by bpy pillars to build a 3D KAVGAQ framework. In 2, three ZnII ions are connected by TDA3âˆ’ to generate [Zn3(TDA)2] SBUs, which can be extended to a 1D wave-like infinite chain. Such 1D wave-like chains are further bridged by azopy to build a 3D framework. In 3, two CdII ions and three KI ions are bridged by two TDA3âˆ’ and four HTDA2âˆ’ to form pentanuclear [Cd2K3(TDA)2(HTDA)4] SBUs, which further generate a 3D framework. The luminescent properties of 1â€“3 are also studied.

Results and discussion

Syntheses of 1â€“3

Solvothermal reactions of ZnII with KH2TDA and bpy/azopy in water lead to the formation of 1 and 2, respectively, while the reaction of CdII with KH2TDA and HTZ in a 1:1 mixture of water and ethanol, in the presence of one drop of NH3Â·H2O, affords the framework of 3. ICP measurement of 3 reveals the coexistence of CdII ions and KI ions with a ratio of ca. 2:3, in agreement with the ratio calculated from the single crystal structure. In 3, HTZ coordinates neither CdII ions nor KI ions. The pH before the reaction is ca. 6, while after the reaction, the pH is ca. 7 and the crystals of 3 could not be obtained in the absence of HTZ, indicating that HTZ might serve as a structure-directing agent and/or weak base to slightly adjust the pH value. The guest molecules and phase purities of MOFs 1â€“3 are confirmed by TGA (Fig. S1, ESIâ€ ) and XRPD (Fig. S2â€“S4, ESIâ€ ).

Luminescent properties

It is well known that coordination polymers with d10 metal centers have excellent luminescent properties and potential applications as luminescent sensing materials.4,15 Therefore, the luminescent properties of 1â€“3 as well as the KH2TDA ligand have been investigated in the solid state at room temperature (Fig. 4). Upon excitation at 306 nm, two intense emission peaks at 379 nm and 386 nm are observed for the KH2TDA ligand, which are ascribed to Ï€* â†’ Ï€ and/or Ï€* â†’ n electronic transitions.15 Excitations at 280 nm for 1 and 2 and 285 nm for 3 lead to two maximum emission peaks at 362 nm, 383 nm for 1, 364 nm, 389 nm for 2 and 362 nm, 387 nm for 3, respectively. On comparison with the free KH2TDA ligand, the emission bands for 1 are similar to those of the free KH2TDA ligand and show blue shifts of 17 nm at the 362 nm band and 3 nm at the 383 nm band. The emission bands for 2 are also similar to those of the free KH2TDA ligand and show a blue shift of 15 nm at the 364 nm band and a slight red shift of 3 nm at the 389 nm band compared with the free KH2TDA ligand. The emission bands for 3 are similar to those of the free KH2TDA ligand as well and show a blue shift of 17 nm at the 362 nm band and a slight red shift of 1 nm at the 387 nm band compared with the free KH2TDA ligand. The emission bands for 1â€“3 are neither ligand-to-metal charge transfer (LMCT) nor metal-to-ligand charge transfer (MLCT) in nature because the ZnII ions and CdII ions are difficult to oxidize or reduce due to their d10 configuration.15,16 Therefore, 1â€“3 may be assigned to the intraligand Ï€* â†’ Ï€ or Ï€* â†’ n transition of the KH2TDA ligand that is modified by the ZnII or CdII ions. In the documented literature, such two similar emission bands can be also observed in the coordination polymer {[K2Cd(HTDA)2]}n, which displays a strong blue-violet emission maxima at ca. 378 nm with a shoulder at ca. 392 nm.6f It is apparent that the emission bands for 1â€“3 are blue shifted compared with that of {[K2Cd(HTDA)2]}n.

Conclusions

Three d10-metal-ion-based MOFs were successfully constructed from the KH2TDA ligand in the presence of three different length-controllable auxiliary ligands. MOF 1 shows an interesting KAVGAQ topology, which can be extended by 2D layers and bpy pillars. MOFs 2 and 3 can be considered as a 6-connected {44Â·611} net and a (6,12)-connected {312Â·430Â·520Â·64}{36Â·48Â·5} net, respectively. On comparing MOFs 1â€“3, it is notable that the introduction of auxiliary ligands has a significant influence on the final products. This reveals that the auxiliary ligands play an important role in constructing the structural diversities of metalâ€“organic frameworks and provide a greater possibility for the construction of unusual topological structures.
