    Abstract

    A new 3D MOF, [Zn5(μ3-O)2(bpdc)4(DMF)2(EtOH)2]·3(DMF)·3(EtOH)·5(H2O) (1) (bpdc = 4,4‘-biphenyldicarboxylate, DMF = N,N-dimethylformamide and EtOH = ethanol), has been synthesized under mild conditions. X-ray crystallography reveals that polymer 1 consists of a 3D framework with an expanded diamondoid topology constructed from double 4-connected pentanuclear SBUs linked by rigid bpdc ligands. For an unusual pentanuclear framework, polymer 1 shows the favorable CH3OH adsorption behavior and good optoelectronic effect, which will facilitate the exploration of new types of multifunctional materials.

    Synopsis

    A novel 3D MOF has been synthesized and it exhibits an expanded diamondoid topology constructed from double 4-connected pentanuclear SBUs linked by rigid bpdc ligands and the multifunctional properties in the CH3OH adsorption and optoelectronic effect.

    Metal−organic frameworks (MOFs) through self-organization have currently attracted considerable attention because of their enormous variety of fascinating structural topologies and great potential applications as functional solid materials.1 Learning from natural minerals, a variety of MOFs with mineral structures, such as quartz,2 perovskite,3 rutile,4 PtS,5 and feldspar,6 have been artificially produced by utilizing the well-defined coordination geometries of metal centers as nodes and polyatomic organic ligands as linkers. Especially, some MOFs with the diamondoid topology that are usually assembled by the copolymerization of 4-connected mononuclear metal−carboxylate secondary building units (SBUs) with organic linkers have been synthesized.7 Lin and co-workers, for example, have rationally synthesized some noncentrosymmetric solids based on 3D infinite diamondoid networks by using unsymmetrical bridging ligands and metal centers with well-defined coordination geometries.7b Recently, Moorthy et al. reported a novel MOF with the diamondoid topology by the combination of a D2d-symmetric 3,3‘,5,5‘-tetrakis(4-pyridyl) bimesityl (tpb) ligand with Td metal center.7d However, a successful synthetic strategy for preparing large, diamondoid networks is still a tremendous challenge.
    As a powerful and effective synthetic method, the process of replacing a vertex in a network by a clustered metal−carboxylate entity as a SBU has been shown to design and synthesize a new class of MOFs with robust structures and high porosity.8 In our previous work, we have synthesized two novel MOFs, [Zn7O2(pda)5(H2O)2]·5(DMF)·4(EtOH)·6(H2O)9a with heptanuclear zinc−carboxylate SBUs and [Cd11(μ4-HCOO-)6(bpdc)9]·9(DMF)·6(H2O)9b with undecanuclear cadmium−carboxylate SBUs. which is the largest transition-metal carboxylate SBUs reported so far. As a sequel, we attempt to construct multinuclear zinc−carboxylate SBUs as nodes and use a rigid and linear organic ligand containing O-donors, H2bpdc (bpdc = 4,4‘-biphenyldicarboxylate), as a linker, which has been shown to exhibit a variety of flexible coordination forms.10 Here, we report a novel 3D MOF, [Zn5(μ3-O)2(bpdc)4(DMF)2(EtOH)2]·3(DMF)·3(EtOH)·5(H2O) (1) (DMF = N,N-dimethylformamide and EtOH = ethanol), which possesses the expanded diamondoid topology assembled by pentanuclear zinc−carboxylate SBUs connected with eight bpdc ligands. Of particular interest is that polymer 1 shows the multifunctional properties in CH3OH adsorption and optoelectronic effect.
    For a typical procedure to synthesize polymer 1, 0.03 g (0.10 mmol) of Zn(NO3)2·6H2O, 0.06 g (0.30 mmol) of bpdc and 15.0 mL of DMF were placed in a 25 mL vial to form a solution under stirring. After being stirred in air for 4 h, the vial was set in a 50 mL beaker containing an EtOH solution (5.0 mL) of DIPA (DIPA = diisopropylanmine, 0.30 mL), and then sealed and left undisturbed for 3 days at 60 °C. The resulting colorless block-shaped crystals of polymer 1 were collected in 65% yield based on zinc. This complex was insoluble in common organic solvents, such as acetone, methanol, ethanol, dichloromethane, acetonitrile, chloroform, and DMF. Elemental analysis and ICP analysis for C81H107N5O33Zn5 (2005.65):  C, 48.53; H, 5.22; N, 3.44; Zn, 16.25. Calcd:  C, 48.51; H, 5.38; N, 3.49; Zn, 16.30. FT-IR (KBr):  ν̄ 3388 (s), 1658 (s), 1581 (s), 1531 (s), 1400 (s), 1178 (w), 1103 (m), 1007 (w), 850 (m), 768 (s), 685 (m), 578 (w), 538 (w), 469 (w) cm-1.
    X-ray crystallography11 reveals that polymer 1 consists of a 3D framework with the extended diamondoid topology. As shown in Figure 1, the fundamental building unit of polymer 1 contains one Zn(II) (Zn1) center in tetrahedral, two (Zn3 and Zn3a) in trigonal bipyramidal, and two (Zn2 and Zn2a) in octahedral coordination geometries that are linked by two μ3-O atoms, six bidentate, and two chelating bidentate carboxylate groups of bpdc and completed by the coordination of two terminal DMF and two terminal EtOH. Clearly, five Zn(II) centers form a [Zn5(μ3-O)2(OCO)8] cluster with 6.9 × 6.9 × 9.2 Å3, which can been seen as an unusual double 4-connected SBU. Thus, these SBUs are interlinked by the biphenyl groups of bpdc ligands to generate an expanded diamondiod net (Figure 2a) with the large cavity of approximately 19.4 × 31.2 Å2 (measured between opposite SBUs). Figure 2b shows the 3D network with the hexagonal channels of 19.7 Å × 24.5 Å expanded from the diamondiod net. However, polymer 1 avoids extremely large void space by constructing a threefold interpenetrating framework (Figure 2c). Even so, this compound still exhibits significant void space that is filled with disordered DMF, EtOH, and H2O guest molecules, as established by elemental analysis, ICP analysis, and TGA. The TGA curve for 1 shows that the weight loss of 22.11% during the first step between 35 and 115 °C corresponds to the loss of five guest H2O, three guest EtOH, and three guest DMF (calculated 22.31%), and the weight loss of 11.93% during the second step between 150 and 270 °C corresponds to the loss of two coordinated EtOH and two coordinated DMF (calculated 11.88%). Decomposition of 1 began above 270 °C. The void spaces can be derived from open channels of about 8.6 × 8.6 Å2 viewed along the [100] direction (Figure 2d) and about 7.1 × 7.1 Å2 and 6.8 × 6.8 Å2 viewed along the [001] direction (see the Supporting Information).
    The adsorption isotherm of polymer 1 for CH3OH was measured at room temperature (293 K). Before the measurement for CH3OH adsorption was recorded, the sample of polymer 1 was soaked in CH2Cl2 for 24 h and then calcinated at 60 °C for 1 h to remove the included solvent molecules. The powder XRD pattern showed the framework of the calcinated sample still kept unchanged (see the Supporting Information). As shown in Figure 3a, type I behavior is observed for the CH3OH adsorption isotherm of polymer 1, which is characteristic of solids with micropores. The result indicates that incoming guests can move freely into the channels and the framework maintains its rigidity and porosity throughout the analysis. At saturation, the CH3OH amount adsorbed by polymer 1 is about 150 mg/g and is equivalent to the adsorption of 29 CH3OH molecules per unit cell. So, the pore volume for polymer 1 is estimated to be approximate 0.19 mL/g. Additionally, we tried to measure the adsorption isotherm of polymer 1 for N2. However, very limited sorption of N2 was observed, possibly due to the flexibility of the framework, which is similar to some reported MOFs, MOF-69A and MOF-69B (see the Supporting Information).12 Further research on the gas storage (such as H2 storage) of 1 is currently underway.
    Interestingly, photovoltage (PV) transients of polymer 1 with pentanuclear zinc clusters can be obtained under atmospheric pressure and at room temperature (293 K).13 Figure 3b shows PV transients for polymer 1 at different intensities of the exciting laser pulse (355 nm). The transient PV signals of polymer 1 display retardation in time, which is caused by slow and independent diffusion of excess electrons and holes. Although the intensities of the exciting laser pulses are different (0.10, 0.20, and 0.30 mJ), the arisen time of their maxima has a similar value (2 × 10-4 s). The maxima are related to the separation of charge at the polymer 1/ITO (ITO = indium tin oxide) interface, and the gradient of excess electron and hole concentrations is caused by the nonhomogeneous absorption of light with photons of hν > EG (EG represents the optical band gap). Similar to the heptanuclear zinc−carboxylate clusters,9a the signals of the PV transients show negative, and it means that the photoexcited electrons move slower than holes toward the polymer 1/ITO interface. Additional, the maxima of the transients are linearly enhanced when the intensities of the PV signals are increased from 0.10 to 0.30 mJ, which implies that polymer 1 possesses the characteristic of diffusion PV transient.14
    In summary, we have succeeded in synthesizing a 3D MOF, [Zn5(μ3-O)2(bpdc)4(DMF)2(EtOH)2]·3(DMF)·3(EtOH)·5 (H2O) (1), which exhibits an expanded diamondoid topology constructed from double 4-connected pentanuclear SBUs linked by rigid bpdc ligands. For an unusual pentanuclear framework, polymer 1 shows the favorable CH3OH adsorption behavior and good optoelectronic effect, which will facilitate the exploration of new types of multifunctional materials.
