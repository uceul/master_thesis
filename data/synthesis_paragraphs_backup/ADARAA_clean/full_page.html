Abstract

A 12‚Äêconnected fcu metal‚Äìorganic framework (MOF), MMPF‚Äê3, has been prepared using a CoII metalloporphyrin. MMPF‚Äê3 is comprised of the same polyhedral supermolecular building blocks as the prototypal fcu‚ÄêMOF, fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1, and its nanoscale cavities feature 18 catalytically active cobalt centers. The high density (ca. 5 cobalt sites/nm3) affords MMPF‚Äê3 superior performance in catalytic epoxidation of trans‚Äêstilbene compared to other MOFs.

A major driving force behind the recent surge of interest in metal‚Äìorganic frameworks (MOFs)1 lies with their amenability to design using crystal engineering2a‚Äì2d strategies. In particular, MOFs with specific composition and topology can be targeted by judicious selection of organic linkers and metal‚Äêbased molecular building blocks2e‚Äì2g (MBBs) that serve as nodes.2 Furthermore, their modular nature means that prototypal MOFs can serve as blueprints or platforms for a plethora of derivatives with controlled pore size and surface area, as exemplified by the practice of ‚Äúreticular synthesis‚Äù.3 Such features make MOFs stand out over traditional porous materials, and afford them with potential for use in gas storage,4 separation,5 CO2 capture,6 sensor,7 catalysis,8 and other areas.9

High‚Äêsymmetry MOFs based upon high‚Äêconnectivity polyhedral cage MBBs that are in effect supermolecular building blocks (SBBs) can provide exquisite control over structure because of their high connectivity and also afford the features of confined nanospace10 and extra‚Äêlarge surface area.11 Such MOFs have afforded superior performance in the context of gas storage for hydrogen, methane, CO2, and other gas molecules.4,‚Äâ6,‚Äâ11,‚Äâ12 The nature of the nanospace in SBB‚Äêbased MOFs is such that they can encapsulate catalytically active species, for example, organometallics,13 polyoxometallates,14 metalloporphyrins (porph@MOMs),15 and enzymes.16 Along with encapsulation of catalysts, it is possible to generate porphyrin‚Äêwalled MOFs by custom‚Äêdesigning metalloporphyrin moieties so that they can serve as vertices and/or edges and/or faces. In principle, the metal clusters residing on the vertices could also contribute as active sites to achieve an even higher density of active sites than that of porph@MOMs.15 Polyhedral MOFs are therefore attractive targets as catalyst supports for heterogeneous catalysis. However, although metallosalens and metalloporphyrins have been utilized as linkers for catalytically active MOFs17 and polyhedral cage‚Äêcontaining metal‚Äìmetalloporphyrin frameworks exist,18 MOFs sustained by catalytically active metalloporphyrin linkers and catalytically active MBBs remain unexplored.

Herein, we report such a MOF that based upon a previously reported fcu topology net built from 12‚Äêconnected cubohemioctahedral SBBs of formula [Co2(Œº2‚ÄêH2O)(H2O)4]6(bdc)12 and benzoimidephenanthroline tetracarboxylate (bipa‚Äêtc) linkers (Scheme‚ÄÖ1‚Äâa), fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1.19 The new compound, MMPF‚Äê3 (MMPF denotes metal‚Äìmetalloporphyrin framework), was prepared solvothermally from a novel porphyrin ligand that is an analogue of bipa‚Äêtc, 5,15‚Äêbis(3,5‚Äêdicarboxyphenyl)‚Äê10,20‚Äêbis(2,6‚Äêdibromophenyl)porphyrin (dcdbp, Scheme‚ÄÖ1‚Äâb),20 and Co(NO3)2. Cube‚Äêshaped, dark‚Äêred crystals of MMPF‚Äê3, [Co2(Œº2‚ÄêH2O)(H2O)4](Co‚Äêdcdbp)‚ãÖ(H2O)6‚ãÖ(C2H5OH)12‚ãÖ(DMF)12, (as determined by x‚Äêray crystallography, elemental analysis, and thermogravimetric analysis) were thereby harvested. Single‚Äêcrystal X‚Äêray diffraction studies were conducted at the Advanced Photon Source, Argonne National Laboratory and revealed that MMPF‚Äê3 crystallizes in the same space group, Pa, as fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1, with a=38.805(3)‚ÄÖ√Ö, (39.288(2)‚ÄÖ√Ö in fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1). As expected, MMPF‚Äê3 is based upon Co2(Œº2‚ÄêH2O)(H2O)4(COO)4 MBBs (Supporting Information, Figure‚ÄÖS2) that afford the cubohemioctahedral SBBs that serve as 12‚Äêconnected nodes (Figure‚ÄÖ1‚Äâa) in the resulting fcu topology network (Supporting Information, Figure‚ÄÖS3).

fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1 was targeted as a blueprint because it exhibits three types of polyhedral cage: a cubohemioctahedron with a window size of ca. 5.9‚ÄÖ√Ö and inner dimensions of about 7.3‚ÄÖ√Ö (atom‚Äêto‚Äêatom distance); a truncated tetrahedron in which six linkers form the edges of the tetrahedron (2,6‚Äêdibromophenyl moieties occupy the inner space of the cage, Figure‚ÄÖ1‚Äâb); a truncated octahedron, with a window size of about 9.2‚ÄÖ√Ö (atom to atom distance) and a cavity with an internal volume of about 4000‚ÄÖ√Ö3 (Figure‚ÄÖ1‚Äâc). In the truncated octahedral cage, twelve bipa‚Äêtc linkers form the main edges of the octahedron. Furthermore, the SBBs occupy the vertices in such a manner that one CoII cation of each Co2(Œº2‚ÄêH2O)(H2O)4(COO)4 MBB orients towards the center of the cage. The equivalent nanoscopic polyhedron in MMPF‚Äê3 possesses twelve cobalt centers that orient towards the center of the cage and therefore exhibits eighteen metal centers with a density of about 5‚ÄÖCo/nm3. The three types of polyhedral cage are interconnected (Figure‚ÄÖ1‚Äâd), and MMPF‚Äê3 possesses a solvent‚Äêaccessible volume of 60‚Äâ% as calculated from PLATON.20

That MMPF‚Äê3 exhibits permanent porosity was confirmed by CO2 adsorption at 273‚ÄÖK, which revealed a surface area of about 750‚ÄÖm2‚Äâg‚àí1 using NLDFT analysis (Supporting Information, Figure‚ÄÖS5).21 Given the high density of cobalt metal centers in the truncated octahedral cages of MMPF‚Äê3, we evaluated its catalytic performance in the context of epoxidation of trans‚Äêstilbene, which has recently been studied in cobalt‚Äêbased MOFs.22 We conducted assays for the epoxidation of trans‚Äêstilbene using tert‚Äêbutyl hydroperoxide (tbhp) as oxidant in acetonitrile at 60‚Äâ¬∞C. Control experiments were conducted for homogeneous cobalt(II)‚Äêmetalated dcdbp (Co(dcdbp)), fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1, and a blank under the same conditions. As revealed by Figure‚ÄÖ2 and Table‚ÄÖ1, MMPF‚Äê3 demonstrated the most efficient catalytic activity for epoxidation of trans‚Äêstilbene of the compounds studied in terms of both yield (95.7‚Äâ% over 24‚ÄÖh) and selectivity (87.1‚Äâ% epoxide product). This compares favorably to the corresponding values for homogeneous Co(dcdbp) (60.4‚Äâ% yield, 67.0‚Äâ% epoxide), fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1 (47.1‚Äâ% yield, 76.7‚Äâ% epoxide), and the blank (9.0‚Äâ% yield, 55.8‚Äâ% epoxide). The average turnover frequency (TOF) in the first 12 hour was 69‚ÄÖh‚àí1 for MMPF‚Äê3, almost 3.5 times faster than that of fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1. We attribute the enhancement of catalytic performance in MMPF‚Äê3 versus fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1 to the higher density of active cobalt centers in the former and/or cooperative interactions between the active centers.17m We also determined the catalytic activity for the 3D‚Äêchannel cobalt‚Äêbased porphyrin MOF MMPF‚Äê2, which was recently reported by our group,23 and the 2D‚Äêchannel MOF PPF‚Äê1Co that is based upon cobalt‚Äêmetalated tetrakis(4‚Äêcarboxyphenyl)porphyrin and Co2(COO)4(H2O)2 MBBs.24 Figure‚ÄÖ2 reveals that MMPF‚Äê2 converts 67.2‚Äâ% of substrate after 24‚ÄÖh (Table‚ÄÖ1), whereas PPF‚Äê1Co converts only 23.7‚Äâ% (Table‚ÄÖ1). We attribute the reduced catalytic activity in these two porphyrin‚Äêbased MOFs to misalignment (that is, not oriented directly towards the channel center) or non‚Äêalignment (oriented parallel to the channels) of the cobalt centers, respectively. No detectable leaching of active site or cobalt metal in the reaction solution was observed after removal of MMPF‚Äê3 by filtration, and MMPF‚Äê3 can be reused for eight cycles without significant drop in its catalytic activity (Table‚ÄÖ1). Our results therefore highlight how appropriately designed polyhedral cages can serve as efficient nanoreactors. However, it should be noted that several factors, for example, amount of catalyst, oxidant, solvent, reaction temperature, and time, can profoundly influence performance (for example conversion, epoxide selectivity, TOF) in catalytic epoxidation of trans‚Äêstilbene.25 We are currently investigating these factors in the context of MMPF‚Äê3 and are also studying epoxidation of different olefin substrates with various molecular sizes and shapes to assess whether or not size‚Äê and shape‚Äêselectivity can be effected. These studies will be reported separately in the near future.

In summary, polyhedral cage‚Äêbased nanoreactors that exhibit a high density of about five catalytically active cobalt centers per nm3 have been generated in MMPF‚Äê3 by decoration of a previously known 12‚Äêconnected MOF, fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1, with a custom‚Äêdesigned CoII metalloporphyrin ligand. MMPF‚Äê3 exhibits permanent microporosity and demonstrates superior performance in the context of both selectivity and overall conversion in catalytic epoxidation of trans‚Äêstilbene when compared to the parent fcu‚ÄêMOF‚Äê1 and two other cobalt‚Äìporphyrin MOFs. The crystal engineering approach for the generation of polyhedral cage‚Äêbased nanoreactors with a high density of catalytically active centers is expected to be a broadly applicable approach for the development of new classes of highly efficient heterogeneous catalytic systems for epoxidation and related reactions.
