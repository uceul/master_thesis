A polythreading array formed by a (3,5)-connected 3D anionic network and 1D cationic chains: synthesis, structure, and catalytic properties†

Min Li a, Shan Zhao a, Yan-Fen Peng ab, Bao-Long Li *a and Hai-Yan Li a
aKey Laboratory of Organic Synthesis of Jiangsu Province, College of Chemistry, Chemical Engineering and Materials Science, Soochow University, Suzhou, 215123, P. R. China. E-mail: libaolong@suda.edu.cn
bDepartment of Material and Chemical Engineering, Chizhou University, Chizhou, Anhui, 247000, P. R. China

Received 7th March 2013 , Accepted 19th April 2013

First published on 22nd April 2013

An unusual copper(II) coordination polymer {[Cu(tmtz)(H2O)4][Cu2(tmtz)2(sip)2]·4H2O}n (1) (tmtz = 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,3,4,5-tetramethylbenzene, sip = 5-sulfoisophthalate) is synthesized by the hydrothermal reaction. X-Ray structural analysis shows that 1 is comprised of two distinct and crystallographically independent polymeric motifs polythreading together. The first motif of 1 is an unusual (3,5)-connected 3D anionic network [Cu2(tmtz)2(sip)2]. The second motif in 1 is the [Cu(tmtz)(H2O)4]n 1D cationic chain. A polythreading array formed by a (3,5)-connected 3D anionic network and 1D cationic chains. The catalytic performance exhibits that 1 is active as a catalyst for the degradation of methyl orange. The thermal analysis is also observed.

Introduction

The rational design and construction of metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) is of current interest in the fields of supramolecular chemistry and crystal engineering because of their potential applications as functional materials for gas storage, separation, microelectronics, nonlinear optics and catalysis, and their intriguing topologies.1 Many coordination networks are particularly intriguing because of presence of periodic entanglements, in which independent motifs are entangled together in different modes. Besides the usual interpenetrating networks, polycatenation and polyknotting species, another more interesting subgroup of entanglement system described as polythreading still remains less explored that is reminiscent of molecular rotaxanes or pseudo-rotaxanes.2–11 Polythreaded structures are characterized by the presence of closed loops, as well as of elements that can thread through the loops, and can be considered as extended periodic analogues of molecular rotaxanes and pseudorotaxanes.4–11 Polythreaded structures with 3D components are really rare.11

Our synthetic approach starts by focusing on the construction of new topological frameworks using flexible ligands such as 1,2-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-yl)ethane (bte),12 1,3-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-yl)propane (btp),8d 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-yl)butane (btb)3e and 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene (bbtz).3b The organic ligand 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,3,4,5-tetramethylbenzene (tmtz), a derivative of bbtz, has four extra methyl groups with the steric hindrance and electron pushing ability and can play the key role in the constructing MOFs.

In the present work, we reported an unusual MOF {[Cu(tmtz)(H2O)4][Cu2(tmtz)2(sip)2]·4H2O}n (1) (sip = 5-sulfoisophthalate) which exhibits a polythreading array formed by a (3,5)-connected 3D anionic network and 1D cationic chains.

Experimental section

Materials and physical measurements

All reagents were of analytical grade and used without further purification. Elemental analyses for C, H and N were performed on a Perkin-Elmer 240C analyser, and Cu was determined with a Leaman inductively coupled plasma (ICP) spectrometer. IR spectra were obtained for KBr pellets on a Nicolet 170SX FT-IR spectrophotometer in the 4000–400 cm−1 region. X-Ray powder diffractions (XRPD) were performed on a D/MAX-3C diffractometer with the Cu Kα radiation (λ = 1.5406 Å) at room temperature. TGA was carried out using a Thermal Analyst 2100 TA Instrument and SDT 2960 Simultaneous TGA-DTA Instrument in flowing dinitrogen at a heating rate of 10 °C min−1.

Preparation of {[Cu(tmtz)(H2O)4][Cu2(tmtz)2(sip)2]·4H2O}n (1)

A mixture of tmtz (0.3 mmol), CuCl2·2H2O (0.3 mmol) and NaH2sip (0.2 mmol) was dissolved in CH3OH (5 mL) and H2O (10 mL), and then the pH was adjusted to 6 by the addition of 1 mol L−1 NaOH. The resulting mixture was sealed in a 25 mL Teflon-lined stainless steel vessel and heated at 130 °C for 24 h, and then cooled to room temperature. Blue crystals were obtained in 72% yield (based on Cu). Anal. Calc. for C64H82Cu3N18O22S2 (1): C, 44.95; H, 4.83; N, 14.75; Cu, 3.72. Found: C, 44.98; H, 4.79; N, 14.72; Cu, 3.68%. IR data (cm−1): 3558m, 3133w, 2928m, 2360m, 2340m, 1727w, 1631s, 1562m, 1530m, 1439m, 1383s, 1363s, 1286w, 1248w, 1209w, 1161m, 1126s, 1030s, 1001m, 883w, 783m, 721m, 625m, 450w.

Crystal structure determination

A suitable single crystal of 1 was carefully selected under an optical microscope and glued to thin glass fibers. The diffraction data were collected on the Rigaku Saturn CCD diffractometers with graphite monochromated MoKα radiation. Intensities were collected by the ω scan technique. The structures were solved by direct methods and refined with full-matrix least-squares technique (SHELX-97).13 The hydrogen atoms of organic ligands were located and included at their calculated positions. The hydrogen atoms of the water molecules were obtained from the successive Fourier syntheses. No hydrogen atom of disordered water was obtained. Catalytic activity of 1 for the degradation of methyl orange

The catalytic activity is studied according to the literature method.14 0.080 mmol of 1 and 2 mL of 30% H2O2 were added into a 200 mL of methyl orange (MO) solution (10 mg L−1), of which the pH value was adjusted to 3 with sulfuric acid (1 mol L−1), and the temperature was maintained at 318 K using a thermostat. At a given interval, aliquots of the reaction mixture were periodically taken and analyzed with a UV-vis spectrophotometer at an absorption wavelength of 506 nm. This procedure was repeated in the absence of 1 as a blank comparison experiment and using equal amounts of CuCl2·2H2O as the catalyst instead of 1 as the comparative experiment.

Results and discussion

Crystal structure of {[Cu(tmtz)(H2O)4][Cu2(tmtz)2(sip)2]·4H2O}n (1)

X-Ray single-crystal diffraction analysis reveals that the structure of 1 is comprised of two distinct and crystallographically independent polymeric motifs polythreading together.

The first motif of 1 is an unusual (3,5)-connected 3D anionic network. The asymmetry unit consists of one Cu(II) atom, one sip3− anion and two halves tmtz. Each Cu(II) atom displays a distorted trigonal bipyramidal coordination geometry, coordinated by three oxygen atoms from three sip3− anions and two nitrogen atoms from two tmtz (Fig. 1). One carboxylate group (O1O2) of one sip3− anion exhibits the bidentate bridging mode and joins two symmetry-related Cu(II) atoms. The other carboxylate group (O3O4) acts as a monodentate ligand and links one Cu(II) atom. The sulfo group is uncoordinated. Each sip3− anion acts as a tridentate ligand to link three Cu(II) atoms. The Cu(II) atoms are joined by 3-connected sip3− ligands and extend to form the [Cu2(sip)2]n ladder (Fig. 2).


Fig. 1 The coordination environment of the Cu(II) atom in the first motif of 1.

Fig. 2 The [Cu2(sip)2]n ladder in 1.

Two kinds of tmtz ligands all show the trans-conformation and two-monodentate mode with the Cu⋯Cu separations of 15.1256(9) and 13.3617(8) Å for N3 and N6 tmtz ligands. Each tmtz ligand bridges two [Cu2(sip)2]n ladders. Each [Cu2(sip)2]n ladder connects four identical [Cu2(sip)2]n ladders (Fig. 3) and extend to generate an unusual 3D anionic network (Fig. 4).


Fig. 3 One [Cu2(sip)2]n ladder connects four identical [Cu2(sip)2]n ladders in the first motif of 1.

Fig. 4 The [Cu2(tmtz)2(sip)2]n 3D anion network in 1.

Each Cu(II) atom connects five Cu(II) atoms through three sip3− anions and two tmtz ligands and is 5-connected. The sip3− ligands are 3-connected and tmtz ligands are two-connected nodes. According to the simplification principle, the structure of one motif in 1 is binodal with 5-connected (Cu units) and 3-connected (ligands sip3−) nodes and exhibits a fascinating 3D structure. The point symbol for the first motif in 1 is (42·65·83) (42·6) (Fig. 5).15


Fig. 5 Schematic representing the [Cu2(tmtz)2(sip)2]n 3D anion network in 1. The brown balls at the benzene ring center of sip3− ligands exhibit the 3-connected sip3− ligands. The pink sticks represent the tmtz ligands.

The second motif in 1 is the [Cu(tmtz)(H2O)4]n 1D cationic chain. The asymmetry unit consists of half Cu(II) atom, half tmtz and two coordination water molecules. Each Cu(II) atom displays a distorted octahedral coordination geometry, coordinated by four oxygen atoms from four water and two nitrogen atoms from two tmtz ligands (Fig. 6). Each tmtz ligand shows the trans-conformation and links two Cu(II) atoms to form a 1D chain along the [1 1 0] direction with the Cu⋯Cu distance of 14.8270(12) Å.


Fig. 6 The [Cu(tmtz)(H2O)4]n 1D cation chain in 1.

As far as we know, it is quite rare to observe distinct coordination polymers, especially with different chemistry, within the same crystal, affording an unusual packing lattice. The interesting structural feature of 1 is that there are two chemically and structurally distinct coordination frameworks, the [Cu2(tmtz)2(sip)2]n 3D anionic network and the [Cu(tmtz)(H2O)4]n 1D cationic chains stacking in the lattice. The entanglement of two motifs in 1 can be described as polythreading of a 3D network with 1D chains running along the [1 1 0] direction (Fig. 7). The hydrogen bonding interaction between the coordination water molecules from the 1D cationic chains and the oxygen atoms from uncoordinated sulfo groups of 3D anionic network stabilize the polythreading array (Table S1, see the ESI†).


Fig. 7 The [Cu(tmtz)(H2O)4]n 1D cation chains polythread the [Cu2(tmtz)2(sip)2]n 3D anion network.

Polythreaded structures with finite components are unusual.4–11 The few species known include polythreaded 0D rings with side arms that give 1D or 2D arrays,5 1D chains of alternating rings and rods (1D → 1D),6 molecular ladders with dangling arms, resulting in (1D → 2D) or (1D → 3D) polythreaded arrays,7 1D chains and 2D sheets (1D + 2D → 2D) or (1D + 2D → 3D)8 and 2D sheets (2D → 3D).9 Polythreaded structures with 3D components are really rare.11 As to the best of our knowledge, six examples involving the 3D component polythreaded frameworks have been synthesized. Five examples are polyoxometalate-based 3D networks [Cu(bbi)2V10O26][Cu(bbi)]2·H2O,11a [CuII(bbi)2(α-Mo8O26)][CuI(bbi)]2 and [CuIICuI(bbi)3(α-Mo8O26)][CuI(bbi)],11b [CuI(bix)][CuI(bix)(δ-Mo8O26)0.5] and H(CuIbix)[(CuIbix)2(β- Mo8O26)]·2H2O11c (bbi = 1,1′-(1,4-butanediyl)bis(imidazol), bix = 1,4-bis(imidazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene) which are polythreaded with 1D single chains. The sixth example is {[Cd(btre)Cl][CdCl(dca)2]·0.5H2O}n (btre = 1,2-bis(1,2,4-triazol-4-yl)ethane, dca = dicyanamide) which exhibits a polythreading coordination array formed from a 3D microporous cationic network and 1D anionic ladders.11d1 is a new polythreading array by the 1D cationic chains polythreading a (3,5)-connected 3D anionic network.

Powder X-ray diffraction

The measured PXRD of 1 is in good agreement with the simulated PXRD from the X-ray data for a single crystal which confirms the purity of the crystalline phase (Fig. S1, see the ESI†).

Catalytic activity of 1 for the degradation of methyl orange

To date, most attention has been focused on the Fenton and Fenton-like reaction to oxidize contaminants of concern such azo dyes, and is largely dependent on transition metal based catalysts.14,16 The degradation experiment of methyl orange (MO) was tracked by visible spectroscopy and the results are depicted in Fig. 8 (Fig. S2, see the ESI†). It was found that the degradation rate of MO is up to 97.0 and 97.3% at 170 and 200 min in the presence of 1. However, when the research was conducted in the blank experiment and comparative experiment (CuCl2·2H2O), the degradation efficiency of the reaction was reduced 17.3% and 32.5% in 200 min, respectively. Clearly, 1 is an effective catalyst for the wet hydrogen peroxide catalytic oxidation of MO by hydroxyl radicals in the Fenton-like process.14
Fig. 8 The experiments of the degradation of methyl orange using 1, CuCl2·2H2O and blank.

H2O2 is the precursor of hydroxyl radicals, which are effective and highly active oxidizing species. Generation of hydroxyl radicals occurs through the use of a catalyst (copper ions) by the well-known Fenton type mechanism, where Cu2+ reacts with H2O2 to generate hydroxyl radicals. Due to the presence of a reaction cycle, Cu2+ ions could also be used as Fenton catalysts. It is possible to represent the MO degradation by the following simplified scheme (Scheme 1).14 A reduction–oxidation cycle of Cu(II)–Cu(I) generating ˙OH radicals seems to be the possible route for the Fenton-like degradation of MO.14,161 shows catalytic activity not only due to Cu(II) metal center, but also the directionality of copper–ligand interaction and the angles defined by the multipodal linker, which generate micro cavities/channels that can be accessed from the exterior allowing mass transfer and the incorporation of substrates inside the crystal.14,16

Zhang and coworkers report the hybrid zeolitic imidazolate frameworks M4(im)6TO4 with catalytically active TO4 (T = Mo6+ or W6+) building blocks which show the catalytic activity for the degradation of methyl orange in the presence of H2O2.17a Cao and coworkers synthesised a water-insoluble photocatalyst constructed from Wells–Dawson polyoxometalates and hexacopper phosphates which exhibiting good photocatalytic activity in the presence of H2O2 under visible light irradiation.17b Zhang and coworkers synthesised two isomeric two-dimensional copper(I) coordination polymer materials based on an in situ generated 5-(3-pyridyl)tetrazole ligand showing different catalytic activity for the degradation of organic dyes in the presence of H2O2 under visible light irradiation.17c

Thermal analysis

To characterize the compounds more fully in terms of thermal stability, the thermal behavior of 1 was examined by TG. The experiment was performed on samples consisting of numerous single crystals with a heating rate of 10 °C min−1 (Fig. S3, see the ESI†). In the TG curve of 1, the lattice and coordination water molecules were lost from 90–190 °C (Calcd: 8.43%, found: 8.84%). The remaining substance was stable upon heating to 290 °C. Then the gradual and rapid weight decrease happened. The weight of remaining substance (14.06%) is stable upon heating to 668 °C which is attributed to the formation of CuO (Calcd: 13.95%).

Conclusions

In summary, we synthesized an unusual polythreading array {[Cu(tmtz)(H2O)4][Cu2(tmtz)2(sip)2]·4H2O}n formed by the 1D cationic chains polythreading a (3,5)-connected 3D anionic network, which displays high catalytic activity in the Fenton-like process to degrade methyl orange (MO), implying its application potential as a solid catalyst. The successful synthesis of 1 not only provides an intriguing example of a polythreaded system but also provides new perspectives to devise novel extended entanglements.
