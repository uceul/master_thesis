Luminescent cadmium phenylenedipropionate coordination polymers with long-spanning dipyridine ligands

Author links open overlay panelGregory A.FarnumbCurtis Y.WangaChaun M.GandolfobRobert L.LaDucab
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.molstruc.2011.05.013Get rights and content

Abstract

Hydrothermal synthesis has generated oxoanion-free divalent cadmium 1,4-phenylenedipropionate (phdp) coordination polymers containing either bis(4-pyridylmethyl)piperazine (bpmp) or bis(4-pyridylformyl)piperazine (bpfp) ligands. The coordination environment at cadmium appears to dictate the overall dimensionality of the resulting coordination polymer crystal structure. {[Cd(phdp)(bpfp)(H2O)]Â·4H2O}n (1) possesses pentagonal bipyramidal coordination at cadmium with trans pyridyl donors and displays a simple 2-D (4,4) grid network. {[Cd(phdp)(bpmp)]Â·5H2O}n (2) exhibits distorted octahedral coordination with cis pyridyl donors, resulting in a 3-D 3-fold interpenetrated diamondoid network. Both 1 and 2 show visible-light luminescence upon ultraviolet excitation, with the emission maximum depending on the nature of the dipyridyl coligand.

1. Introduction

Coordination polymer design and characterization remains under intensive investigation because of the potentially industrially important applications of these phases [1], including gas storage [2], adsorption and separation [3], ion exchange [4], catalysis [5], fluorescence and sensing [6], and second harmonic generation [7]. Aromatic dicarboxylates such as terephthalate [8] or isophthalate [9] have proven very advantageous for the construction of divalent metal coordination polymers. These ligands provide both the structural rigidity and charge balance important for the self-assembly of crystalline extended networks. Metal coordination environment and carboxylate group binding mode can be tailored to provide access to a host of possible framework topologies [10]. For divalent zinc- and cadmium-containing coordination polymers, the absence of crystal field stabilization in the d10 electronic configuration allows the specific coordination environment to respond sensitively to the geometric and steric requirements of the dicarboxylate ligands [11].

Expansion of the scope of coordination polymer topologies has been made possible by the use of more conformationally flexible phenylenediacetate (phda) ligands [12], [13], [14], [15], [16]. A recently published family of divalent cobalt coordination polymers with phda and long-spanning bis(4-pyridylmethyl)piperazine (bpmp, Scheme 1) ligands reveals an important topological dependence imparted by the carboxylate pendant arm disposition [15]. {[Co(1,4-phda)(4-bpmp)(H2O)2]Â·2H2O}n exhibits a very common 4-connected 2-D grid topology, while its ortho-acetate analog {[Co(1,2-phda)(4-bpmp)1.5(H2O)](H24-bpmp)0.5(ClO4)Â·12H2O}n possesses a unique 5-connected layered framework with an Archimedean topology based on rectangular and triangular circuits. Topological variances are also seen across a series of cadmium phda coordination polymers containing the seldom used [16], [17], [18], [19] neutral tethering ligand bis(4-pyridylformyl)piperazine (bpfp, Scheme 1), structurally similar to bpmp, but possessing hydrogen bonding accepting ability at its two amide groups. [Cd(1,2-Hphda)2(bpfp)]n shows 2-D polymeric layers with embedded cadmium carboxylate ribbon motifs, {[Cd(1,3-phda)(bpfp)(H2O)]2}n has 2-fold parallel interpenetrated (4,4) grids, and {[Cd(1,4-phda)(bpfp)(H2O)]}n manifests acentric 1-D chains with an uncommon 4-connected 33425 topology [16].

In this follow-up contribution we present the results of our attempts to prepare analogous phases with the longer and more conformationally flexible 1,4-phenylenedipropionate (phdp, Scheme 1) ligand, with the synthesis and single-crystal X-ray structures of the {[Cd(phdp)(bpfp)(H2O)]Â·4H2O}n (1) and {[Cd(phdp)(bpmp)]Â·5H2O}n (2). The coordination environment at cadmium appears to dictate the overall dimensionality of the resulting coordination polymer crystal structure in this system, resulting in a switch from a 2-D network in 1 to a 3-D framework in 2. Luminescent properties of these new materials are also reported herein.

2. Experimental

2.1. General considerations

Cadmium salts and 1,4-phenylenedipropionic acid were obtained commercially from Aldrich. Bis(4-pyridylformyl)piperazine (bpfp) [17] and bis(4-pyridylmethyl)piperazine (bpmp) [20] were prepared via modification of published procedures. Pyridine was refluxed with CaH2 under flowing N2 and distilled immediately prior to use. Water was deionized above 3 MÎ© cm in-house. Elemental analysis was carried out using a Perkin Elmer 2400 Series II CHNS/O Analyzer. Infrared spectra were recorded on powdered samples on a Perkin Elmer Spectrum One instrument. The luminescence spectra were obtained with a Hitachi F-4500 Fluorescence Spectrometer on solid crystalline samples anchored to quartz microscope slides with Rexon Corporation RX-22P ultraviolet-transparent epoxy adhesive.

3. X-ray crystallography

Single-crystal diffraction data for 1 and 2 were collected using a Bruker-AXS Apex2 CCD instrument. Diffraction data was acquired using graphiteâ€“monochromated Mo KÎ± radiation (Î» = 0.71073 Ã…). The diffraction data were integrated with SAINT [21]. Lorentz and polarization effect and empirical absorption corrections were applied with SADABS [22]. The structures were solved using direct methods and refined on F2 using SHELTXL [23]. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined with anisotropic thermal parameters. Hydrogen atoms bound to carbon atoms were placed in calculated positions and refined with isotropic thermal parameters using a riding model. The crystal of 1 was non-merohedrally twinned, and the twin law was obtained using CELLNOW [24]. Only reflections from the major twin component were used during refinement. 4. Results and discussion

4.1. Synthesis and spectral characterization

Hydrothermal reaction of a cadmium salt, 1,4-phenylenedipropionic acid, and the requisite dipyridine ligand generated crystalline samples of 1 and 2. Their infrared spectra were consistent with their single crystal structures. A strong band at 1638 cmâˆ’1 indicates the CO formyl groups in the bpfp ligands in 1. Sharp, medium intensity bands in the range of âˆ¼1600 cmâˆ’1 to âˆ¼1200 cmâˆ’1 are attributed to stretching modes of the pyridyl rings of the bpfp or bpmp ligands and the aromatic rings of the dicarboxylate ligands. Features corresponding to pyridyl ring puckering modes of the bpfp or bpmp ligands are evident in the region between âˆ¼930 and âˆ¼600 cmâˆ’1. Asymmetric and symmetric CO stretching modes of the fully deprotonated phdp ligands are present as broadened bands at 1603 cmâˆ’1 and 1471 cmâˆ’1 (1) and 1539 cmâˆ’1 and 1408 cmâˆ’1 (2). Broad, weak spectral features at âˆ¼3100âˆ’3400 cmâˆ’1 (OH stretching modes) denote the presence of the bound and unbound water molecules.

Two chelating phdp carboxylate groups complete the coordination sphere. One type of phdp ligand has an anti conformation of its propionate groups (four-C atom torsion angle = 179.8Â°), while the other type has a very twisted gauche conformation (torsion angle = 50.7Â°). Individual [Cd(phdp)]n chains in 2 are oriented parallel to the [1 1 1] crystal direction. These are connected into a 4-connected 66 topology diamondoid 3-D [Cd(phdp)(bpmp)]n coordination polymer lattice through tethering bpmp ligands (Fig. 6). The cis disposition of the pyridyl ligands in 2 results in a preference for the 3-D diamondoid topology at the expense of the 2-D grid topology seen in 1, in which the pyridyl donors are oriented in a trans fashion. The Cdâ‹¯Cd distances through the crystallographically distinct bpmp ligands are 16.370 and 16.923 Ã…, respectively. These varying distances are imposed by slight differences in the conformation of the pyridylmethyl groups of the bpmp ligands (58.1Â° and 61.2Â° CNCC torsion angles, respectively).

Incipient voids within a single [Cd(phdp)(bpmp)]n coordination polymer lattice contain two identical networks, resulting in mutual 3-fold interpenetration (Fig. 7). Crystallographic inversion centers within both the phdp and bpmp ligands in 2 prevent chirality, in contrast to several other examples of acentric diamondoid coordination polymers with an odd numbered level of interpenetration [26]. The remaining accessible inter-framework space (21.1% of the unit cell volume) is filled with hydrogen-bonded â€œinfiniteâ€ water chains (Fig. 8) with C5 classification [27]. The water chains are anchored to the coordination polymer framework via hydrogen bonding donation to phdp carboxylate oxygen atoms and bpmp piperazinyl nitrogen atoms (Table 3). The 3-D topology of 2 differs greatly from the 2-D network of its shorter-armed 1,4-phda analog {[Cd(1,4-phda)(4-bpmp)]Â·1.5H2O}n, which displays a (4,4)-grid layered structure based on 4-connected {Cd2O2} dinuclear units [28].

4.4. Luminescent properties

Polycrystalline samples of 1 and 2 underwent visible light fluorescence upon excitation with ultraviolet light at 375 nm. According to a recent review of d10 metal coordination polymer luminescence [6a], the emissive behavior can be plausibly ascribed to Ï€â€“Ï€* or Ï€â€“n electronic transitions within the phenylenedipropionate and dipyridyl ligands. Different emission maxima were observed for the bpfp derivative 1 and bpmp derivative 2, at âˆ¼500 and âˆ¼475 nm respectively. The redshift of the emission of 1 is apparently caused by adjustment of molecular orbital energy levels imparted by the formyl group of the bpfp ligand. Emission spectra for 1 and 2 are shown in Fig. 9.

5. Conclusions

Cadmium 1,4-phenylenedipropionate coordination polymers with long-spanning dipyridyl/piperazine ligands show different dimensionalities depending on the coordination environment at the metal, along with geometric disposition of the pyridyl donor atoms. In both cases, the structural topologies differ from related cadmium coordination polymers containing the shorter-armed 1,4-phenylenediacetate (1,4-phda) ligands. Additionally, aggregations of co-crystallized water molecules can be tuned, with short and â€œinfiniteâ€ water chains accessible. The presence of the amide functional group in the bis(4-pyridylformyl)piperazine ligand appears to result in a red shift of the ligand-based emission maximum, when compared to the amide-free related bis(4-pyridylmethyl) piperazine ligand. Efforts to prepare other divalent metal coordination polymers containing the under-utilized bpfp ligand are underway in our laboratory.
