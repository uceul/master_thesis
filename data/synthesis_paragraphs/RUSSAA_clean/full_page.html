Crystal structure and magnetic property of a 3D heterometallic coordination polymer constructed by 3-cyanobenzoate and 3-(5H-tetrazol) benzoate ligands

Author links open overlay panelFengChenabFa-KunZhengaGuang-NingLiuabA-QingWuaMing-ShengWangaSheng-PingGuoaMei-FengWuabZhi-FaLiuabGuo-CongGuoaJin-ShunHuanga
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.inoche.2009.12.002Get rights and content

Abstract

Hydrothermal reaction of Cd(NO3)2, Co(NO3)2, Na(3-cba) and NaN3 yielded a 3D heterometallic coordination polymer, [Cd(μ5-3-tzba)(μ2-3-cba)(μ3-OH)Co(H2O)]n·2nH2O 1 (3-H2tzba = 3-(5H-tetrazol)benzoic acid and 3-Hcba = 3-cyanobenzoic acid), which was characterized by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. The in situ [2 + 3] cycloaddition reaction of nitrile and azide in presence of Cd(II) salt afforded tetrazolate-based 3-tzba2− ligand. In 1, metal-oxo chains [Cd(μ3-OH)Co]n are linked by 3-tzba2− in an unprecedented μ5-κN1:κN2:κN3:κN4:κO1,O2 coordination mode to form 2D layers, which are further pillared by 3-cba− to generate a 3D open framework encapsulating 1D chiral channels. Variable-temperature magnetic studies show that 1 exhibits antiferromagnetic interactions between Co(II) ions bridged by μ3-OH group.

Graphical abstract

A novel heterometallic Cd(II)/Co(II) coordination polymer based on 3-cyanobenzoate and 3-(5H-tetrazol)benzoate ligands features a 3D open framework encapsulating 1D chiral channels, exhibiting antiferromagnetic interactions.

Metal–organic coordination complexes with tetrazolate- or carboxylate-based ligands have been the subject of intense research efforts for decades, owing to their interesting structural topologies, and wide physical properties such as molecular magnetism, molecular absorption and recognition, catalysis, non-linear optics and luminescence [1], [2]. It is well-known that organic bridging ligands, which contain adjustable components and connectivity information with single metal ions, metal clusters or inorganic chains, play a key role in building extended networks [3]. Tetrazolate–carboxylate ligands, simultaneously containing carboxylate and tetrazolate groups both being able to bind metal ions in versatile coordination modes, can be used as excellent bridging linkers for the construction of novel metal–organic frameworks possessing intriguing structural varieties and potential applications [4], [5]. Especially, tetrazolate-5-carboxylate ligands, carboxylate-introduced 5-substituted tetrazolate ligands with four N and two O potential electron-donating centers, can act as polydentate ligands and exhibit diverse coordination modes (Scheme S1) [5], [6]. On the other hand, plenty of homometallic coordination polymers with tetrazolate-based ligands have been explored through [2 + 3] cycloaddition reaction of organic nitriles and inorganic azide in the presence of metal salts as Lewis acid catalysts [1](a), [7]. However, few instances of heterometallic tetrazolate-based polymers have been reported so far [8], [5](c). Moreover, the investigation of tetrazolate–carboxylate coordination complexes or polymers remains less developed. Recently, we have obtained a novel 3D Zn(II) coordination polymer [Zn(4-tzba)]n (4-H2tzba = 4-(5H-tetrazol)benzoic acid) with the gsi (gamma silicon) net, which shows high thermal stability, second-order non-linear optical effects, and interesting heat-induced photoluminescence [6a]. As an extension of our research, we turn our attention to another tetrazolate-5-carboxylate ligand, 3-(5H-tetrazol)benzoic acid (3-H2tzba), whose relative position of two kinds of coordination groups is different from that of 4-H2tzba. Here, we report a novel 3D heterometallic coordination polymer [Cd(μ5-3-tzba)(μ2-3-cba)(μ3-OH)Co(H2O)]n·2nH2O 1 (3-Hcba = 3-cyanobenzoic acid), in which a μ5-κN1:κN2:κN3:κN4:κO1,O2 coordination mode of 3-tzba2− ligand is first observed in tetrazolate-5-carboxylate complexes (Scheme 1).

Polymer 1 was hydrothermally synthesized [9], and determined by the experimental powdered X-ray diffraction (PXRD) pattern and IR analyses (Figs. S1 and S2). The single-crystal X-ray structural analysis reveals that 1 features a 3D open framework [10], and the selected bond distances and bond angles of 1 are listed in Table S1. Each asymmetric unit contains one Cd(II) atom, one Co(II) atom, one hydroxyl group (μ3-OH), one 3-cba− ligand, one 3-tzba2− ligand, one coordinated water and two lattice water molecules, as shown in Fig. 1. The coordination environment of Cd(II) is six-coordinated and can be described as a distorted octahedron. Four equatorial sites are occupied by three carboxylate O atoms (O11, O21A, O22A) from one 3-cba− and one 3-tzba2− ligand, and one μ3-OH group (O2). Two axial sites are held by two tetrazolate N atoms (N1 and N4B) from two symmetry-related 3-tzba2− ligands. The Co(II) center is also six-coordinated and has a slightly distorted octahedral configuration. The equatorial plane is defined by two symmetry-related μ3-OH groups (O2, O2C), one cyano N atom (N5D) from 3-cba− ligand and one water O atom (O1 W). Two tetrazolate N atoms (N2C, N3B) from two symmetry-related 3-tzba2− ligands furnish a distorted octahedral coordination geometry of Co(II). The Cd/Co–N/O distances and angles lie in the normal range compared with those reported previously [11]. The striking structural characteristic in 1 is that 3-tzba2− ligand adopts an unusual μ5-κN1:κN2:κN3:κN4:κO1,O2 coordination mode (Scheme 1), which has not been found for tetrazolate-5-carboxylate complexes. Each 3-tzba2− ligand acts as a hexadentate linker to connect two Cd(II) atoms through the tetrazolate group in a μ2-1,4 N mode, two Co(II) atoms in a μ2-2,3 N mode, and one additional Cd(II) atom through carboxylate group in a chelated fashion. To the best of our knowledge, 1 is the first instance of coordination complexes in which tetrazolate-5-carboxylate ligand utilizes all potential binding centers to link metal atoms. For 3-cba− ligand in 1, its carboxylate group exhibits a terminal monodentate coordination mode and links one Cd(II) atom through one oxygen atom. Meanwhile, each cyano group is bound to the Co(II) atom by its nitrogen atom. Notably, cyanobenzoate complexes with both of two functional coordination groups involved with coordination have been rarely observed [12].

The intricate framework of 1 is best understood by first considering metal-oxo chains [Cd(μ3-OH)Co]n, in which a μ3-OH anion serves as a capped center connecting one Cd(II) and two Co(II) ions to form a highly compressed [Cd(μ3-OH)Co2] tetrahedron. The neighboring [Cd(μ3-OH)Co2] tetrahedral polyhedra are joined by sharing a Co(II) vertex to form metal-oxo chains [Cd(μ3-OH)Co]n propagating along the b-direction. Furthermore, the metal-oxo chains are linked by 3-tzba2− ligands to produce a 2D sheet parallel to the bc-plane (Fig. S3). The adjacent sheets are further pillared through 3-cba− ligands to achieve a 3D open framework (Fig. 2a). Interestingly, there exist chiral channels (L/R) built by left-/right-handed helical chains formed by interrelation of metal-oxo chains [Cd(μ3-OH)Co]n and 3-cba− ligands (Figs. 2b and c). Both of left- and right-handed helical chains extend along the 21-helical axis with a pitch of 6.958(2) Å and are stacked alternately to construct a whole chiral 3D open framework.

Extensive hydrogen-bonding interactions exist in 1, as shown in Fig. S4. The geometrical parameters pertaining to hydrogen bonds are collected in Table S2. Hydrogen bonds between lattice water molecules (O2 W and O3 W) and uncoordinated carboxylate oxygen atoms (O12) from 3-cba− ligand create 10-membered rings O5H5, which are linked to form a 1D water chain along the b-direction by sharing hydrogen-bonded O2 W⋯O3 W (Fig. S4a). Such a hydrogen-bonded configuration in 1 has rarely been encountered in supramolecular compounds [13]. The further interlinkage of water chains and metal-oxo chains through O1 W⋯O2 W hydrogen bonds affords a 2D supramolecular net parallel to the ab plane (Fig. S4b).

Thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) experiment for 1 was performed to investigate its thermal stability (Fig. S5). A weight loss of 6.14% (calc. 6.24%) from 75 to 115 °C corresponds to the removal of two lattice water molecules. Then, the release of one coordinated water molecule happened in the temperature range of 115–180 °C with a weight loss of 2.86% (calc. 3.12%), followed by a plateau of stability between 180 and 340 °C. Upon heating above 340 °C, the rapid collapse of the framework took place.

The temperature-dependent molar magnetic susceptibility of 1 was measured on a crystalline sample in an applied field of 1000 Oe from 300 to 2 K, and the corresponding curves of χM-1 and χMT versus T are shown in Fig. 3. The χMT value of 2.43 cm3 mol−1 K at 300 K is larger than the expected value of 1.875 cm3 mol−1 K for a free high-spin Co(II) ion (S = 3/2), which is typical for high-spin Co(II) ions with significant orbital contribution [14]. When the temperature decreases, the χMT value smoothly decreases to a minimum value of 1.77 cm3 mol−1 K at 22 K, related to the depopulation of higher-energy spin–orbit levels of six-coordinated Co(II) centers and weak anti ferromanetic coupling between the Co(II) centers. Upon further cooling, the χMT value increases rapidly and reaches a maximum value of 3.77 cm3 mol−1 K at 2.6 K, and finally decreases to 3.647 cm3 mol−1 K until the lowest temperature 2 K owing to saturation effects. The data of χM-1 above 50 K obey the Curie-Weiss law (χM = C/(T − θ)), with a Curie constant C = 2.38(1) cm3 mol−1 K and a Weiss constant θ = –12.17(41)  K. The C value is larger than the corresponding one for a high spin-only Co(II) ion, indicating the obvious orbital contribution of the Co(II) ion. The negative θ value suggests an overall antiferromagnetic interaction at high temperature between the Co(II) ions and/or the spin–orbit coupling through the 4Tg state of the octahedral Co(II) center. Based on the structural analyses, the magnetic data were fitted by 1D Co chain model (Eq. (S1)), leading to J/k = −6.46(9) cm−1, g = 2.33(0), R = 2.6 × 10−4. The small negative J/k value confirms that the weak antiferromagnetic exchange interaction exists between Co(II) ions in 1. Both the real (χM′) and imaginary (χM″) components of the alternating current (ac) susceptibility (Fig. S6) show the sharp peaks at about 2.2 K. It confirms that the ac data are agreeable to the observation in the direct current (dc) measurement (2.6 K), which indicates the occurrence of the antiferromagnetic ordering.

In summary, we have successfully prepared a novel 3D heterometallic coordination polymer based on 3-cyanobenzoate and 3-(5H-tetrazol) benzoate ligands. The in situ synthesized 3-tzba2− ligand exhibits an unprecedented μ5-κN1:κN2:κN3:κN4:κO1,O2 coordination mode in tetrazolate-5-carboxylate complexes. Variable-temperature magnetic susceptibility data indicates that there exist antiferromagnetic interactions between the Co(II) atoms bridged by hydroxyl groups in the metal-oxo chain [Cd(μ3-OH)Co]n.
