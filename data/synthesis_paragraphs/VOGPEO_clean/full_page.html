1. Introduction

With the booming development of economy, the traditional fuels such as oil have been excessively exploited. What is more, the burning of fossil fuels could release a large amount of CO2 which is the main cause of global climate change. So looking for new cleaner energy resources is becoming an urgent goal in environmental protection. Natural gas, which mainly consists of methane about 80â€“95%, as preferable alternative fuel meets the requirement. Methane is valuable not only as a clean-burning, renewable and inexpensive source of energy for future vehicle and residential use but also as a chemical feedstock in chemical and petrochemical industries.1 However, natural gas contains also other impurities, such as carbon dioxide and acetylene. And the coexistence of CO2 with CH4 reduces the energy content of natural gas and causes pipeline corrosion,2 so selective removal of carbon dioxide and acetylene from methane is very important industrial processes for natural gas purification.3,4 Furthermore, the traditional separation technology of the cryogenic distillation at low temperature is very energy-intensive.5 Thus there is a need to seek a new method or material for the purification of natural gas. The emergence of the microporous materials with selective adsorption properties has greatly met such a need to reduce cost for the purification of methane.

Microporous metalâ€“organic frameworks (MOFs) are a new class of porous materials and have attracted great attention in recent years.6 MOFs can be self-assembled from metal ions or metal-containing clusters as secondary building blocks (SBUs) bound by multidirectional organic ligands through solvothermal synthesis or other synthetic methods to form networks.4â€“8 Compared with traditional porous materials, such as zeolite and activated carbon materials, microporous MOFs have specific advantages with (a) significantly high surface area and large pore spaces,9 (b) tailorable structures and pores;10 (c) modifiable functional sites in pore surface such as â€“COOH and open metal sites.11 Therefore, microporous MOFs have a lot of potential in their applications for gas separation.12 Both experiments13 and computational methods14 have demonstrated that the introduction of polar functional groups such as â€“COOH in the frameworks can enhance the CO2 adsorption capacity and CO2/CH4 separation performance of MOFs. Moreover, open metal sites in pore surface of some MOFs have been demonstrated play important roles in the aspect of selective adsorption. For example, HKUST-1 (ref. 15) exhibits high CO2/N2 and CO2/CH4 selectivities, while MIL-100 and MIL-101,16 with coordinately exposed Cr3+ sites, display high adsorption capacities for CO2 over CH4, though the enthalpy values of adsorption at zero coverage for HKUST-1 (35 kJ molâˆ’1), MIL-100 (62 kJ molâˆ’1) and MIL-101 (44 kJ molâˆ’1) are relatively high, which are not so beneficial from the perspective of a reduced energy requirement for adsorbent regeneration. Herein, we report a new microporous MOF, Cu6(DDCBA)3 (ZJU-72; H5DDCBA = 3,5-di(3â€²,5â€²-dicarboxylpheny)-benozoic acid, ZJU = Zhejiang University) from a new organic linker (Scheme 1). It displays the well-known topological point symbol of {4.64.8}2{42.64}-{64.82}2{66}. Interestingly, ZJU-72 has low binding energy of 9.7 kJ molâˆ’1 for C2H2 and of 15.1 kJ molâˆ’1 for CO2 but still display quite high selectivity of 39.7 for C2H2/CH4 at 298 K and 8.2 for CO2/CH4 at 273 K.

3. Results and discussion

ZJU-72 was synthesized by the solvothermal reaction of H5DDCBA and Cu(NO3)2Â·2.5H2O in a mixture solvent of DMF/H2O with addition of a small amount of HNO3 at 80 Â°C for 72 h as blue cuboid shaped single crystals. The structure is characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction studies, and the phase purity of the bulk material is independently confirmed by powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD, Fig. S1â€ ) and thermogravimetric analysis (TGA, Fig. S2â€ ).

The crystal structure of ZJU-72 has been solved by X-ray crystallography. This compound crystallizes in the orthorhombic space group Cmcm. As except, ZJU-72 adopts a three-dimensional (3D) framework which is built from the paddle-wheel Cu2(CO2)4 SBUs formed in situ bridged to H5DDCBA linkers via their carboxylate groups (Fig. 1) to have the well-known topological point symbol of {4.64.8}2{42.64}{64.82}2{66}. There are one-dimensional channels along the c axis with the uncoordinated free carboxylate functional groups point to the pores of the framework (Fig. 1a) and one-dimensional channel along the a axis of about 4.8 Ã… in diameter with the open copper sites exposed to the pores (Fig. 1b). The total accessible free volume is 10424 Ã…3, or 66.78% of the unit volume 15608.7 Ã…3 by PLATON analysis.19

As shown in Fig. 2a, ZJU-72a exhibits type I reversible sorption isotherms and takes up N2 to 410 cm3 gâˆ’1 at 77 K and 1 bar, corresponding to a BET surface area of 1184 m2 gâˆ’1 (Langmuir surface area of 1750 m2 gâˆ’1), and a pore volume of 0.635 cm3 gâˆ’1. The combined feature of the open metal sites, exposed COOH groups and optimized pore size of ZJU-72a highlights the potential application in storage and separation of gas molecules. As shown in Fig. 2b and c, ZJU-72a can take the amount of C2H2 (179.7 cm3 gâˆ’1), CO2 (138.8 cm3 gâˆ’1) and CH4 (32.3 cm3 gâˆ’1) at 1 atm and 273 K; and C2H2 (167.7 cm3 gâˆ’1), CO2 (103.9 cm3 gâˆ’1) and CH4 (25.1 cm3 gâˆ’1) at 1 atm and 298 K.

The coverage-dependent adsorption enthalpies of ZJU-72a to these three gases, calculated based on virial method, a well-established and reliable methodology from fits of their adsorption isotherms at 273 and 298 K. It is remarkable to note that the values of the binding energies of those three gases are quite low. The significantly lower binding energy of C2H2 and CO2 implies significantly lower regeneration energy requirements using ZJU-72a. The amount of absorbed C2H2 in ZJU-72a at 298 K is higher than NOTT-102,20 UTSA-20,20 MOF-74-Fe,7e and that of CO2 is higher than MOF-505 (ref. 21) and UTSA-20.7d These data illustrated that the combined feature of open metal sites and carboxylic acid groups do lead to the efficient adsorption of acetylene and carbon dioxide molecules in ZJU-72a micropores.

ZJU-72a exhibits higher adsorption capacities of C2H2 and CO2 than those of CH4 at both 273 and 298 K. Such difference of adsorption capacities has enabled ZJU-72a to be selective for C2H2/CH4 and CO2/CH4 separation. In order to confirm its gas selectivities in ZJU-72a, the well-known ideal adsorbed solution theory (IAST)22 calculation is further applied to estimate the C2H2/CH4 and CO2/CH4 adsorption selectivity. Mixture adsorption isotherms and the selectivities at different pressures and temperatures calculated by IAST for mixed C2H2/CH4 (C2H2/CH4 = 50:50) and CO2/CH4 (CO2/CH4 = 50:50) in this activated ZJU-72a as a function of total bulk pressure are shown in Fig. 4 and S3.â€  The IAST calculation gives the gas selectivity value of 33.4 and 39.7 for C2H2/CH4 and 8.2 and 6.8 for CO2/CH4 at 273 and 298 K, respectively (Table S3â€ ). These selectivity values of CO2/CH4 adsorption separation is 8.2 at 273 K is consistent with those selectivities (7.2â€“8.8) of some reported PCN-305-308 (ref. 23) calculated from the experimental single-component isotherms by using IAST method. The selectivity value of CO2/CH4 of ZJU-72a on account of introduction of â€“COOH groups at 273 K is higher than that of PCN-306 without â€“COOH functional group, featuring the promise of this new microporous MOF for practical C2H2/CH4 and CO2/CH4 separation in the near future. It needs to be mentioned that among diverse porous MOFs, those with open copper sites are stable in the presence of small amount of water vapour (humid conditions),24 which enables this kind of porous MOFs to be promising for their practical applications.

4. Conclusions

We successfully synthesized one new microporous metalâ€“organic framework Cu6(DDCBA)3 (ZJU-72) with open metal sites, immobilized â€“COOH groups on the pore surfaces. These features enable ZJU-72a to have high selectivities for C2H2/CH4 and CO2/CH4 separation. This MOF also has quite low values of binding energies for C2H2 and CO2, which are beneficial to the low regeneration energy requirements for such important gas separations.
