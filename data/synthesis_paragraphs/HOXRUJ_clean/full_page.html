Abstract

Four 3D lanthanide organic frameworks from potassium pyrazine‚Äê2, 3, 5, 6‚Äêtetracarboxylate (K4pztc) or potassium pyridine‚Äê2, 3, 5, 6‚Äêtetracarboxylate (K4pdtc), namely, {[KEu(pztc)(H2O)2]¬∑H2O}n (1), {[KTb(pztc)(H2O)2]¬∑1.25H2O}n (2), {[KLn(pdtc)(H2O)]¬∑H2O}n [Ln = Gd (3), Ho (4)], were synthesized by reaction of the corresponding lanthanide oxides with K4pztc or K4pdtc in presence of HCl under hydrothermal conditions, and characterized by elemental analysis, TGA, IR and fluorescence spectroscopy as well as X‚Äêray diffraction. In complexes 1 and 2, the dodecadentate chelator pztc4‚Äì links four LnIII ions and four KI ions. The coordination mode of the pztc4‚Äì ligand is reported for the first time herein. Complexes 3 and 4 are isostructural with earlier reported Nd, Dy, Er complexes. Moreover, the EuIII and TbIII complexes exhibit the characteristic luminescence.

1 Introduction

Lanthanide elements are well‚Äêknown as important candidates to construct metal‚Äêorganic frameworks (MOFs) because of their ability to form multifunctional materials with the interesting magnetic, optical, and catalytic properties.1 Moreover, lanthanide elements have higher coordination numbers and more flexible coordination geometry, which are helpful in the formation of unusual MOFs.2 On the other hand, depending on the instinctive high oxophilicity (hard acid‚Äêhard base interaction),3 lanthanide ions are strongly coordinated by oxygen donor ligands. Hence, ligands with carboxylate groups are expected to be good organic ligands in constructing lanthanide‚Äêorganic hybrid networks.4

Pyrazine‚Äê2, 3, 5, 6‚Äêtetracarboxylic acid (H4pztc) and pyridine‚Äê2, 3, 5, 6‚Äêtetracarboxylic acid (H4pdtc) are suitable ligands for the construction of MOFs due to the presence of nitrogen and oxygen atoms.5 Furthermore, compared with the corresponding di‚Äê, tri‚Äêcarboxylic acids, they contain richer coordination sites and are more symmetrical. Thus, it is aimed to obtain more fascinating and richer structures by the self‚Äêassembly between H4pdtc/H4pztc and lanthanide metals.

However, metal‚Äêorganic frameworks containing lanthanide are far less studied6 than d‚Äêblock elements because the unspecific coordination properties of the lanthanide ions render the design of lanthanide‚Äêbased coordination frameworks with specific properties very challenging. Some 2D/3D MOFs from polycarboxylate ligands and lanthanide metals have been prepared7 through taking certain factors into account, such as (1) the coordination nature of the metal ions; (2) the shape, functionality, flexibility, symmetry of the organic ligand; (3) different synthesis methods, solvent, conditions (pH temperature) etc. They usually adopt diverse binding modes as multidentate chelators to obtain interesting polymeric forms. To continue the research on 4f MOFs, H4pztc/H4pdtc and four heavy lanthanide metals were employed in the self‚Äêassembly process to construct high‚Äêdimension coordination polymers.

2 Experimental Section

2.1 General

Pyrazine‚Äê2, 3, 5, 6‚Äêtetracarboxylic acid (H4pztc) and pyridine‚Äê2, 3, 5, 6‚Äêtetracarboxylate (K4pdtc) were synthesized according to the reported methods,8,9 respectively. K4pztc was obtained from H4pztc by treatment with KOH. Other reagents and solvents employed were commercially available and used as received without further purification. The C, H, and N microanalyses were carried out with a Perkin‚ÄêElemer model 240 elemental analyzer. The FT‚ÄêIR spectra were recorded from KBr pellets in the range of 400‚Äì4000 cm‚Äì1 with a Magna‚Äê560 FT‚ÄêIR spectrophotometer. TG analyses were performed with a NETZSCH TG 209 instrument with a heating rate of 10 ¬∞C min‚Äì1. The photoluminescence spectra were measured with a Cary Eclipse EL06063917 fluorescence spectrophotometer with a xenon arc lamp as the light source.

3 Results and Discussion

3.1 Structure Description of 1 and 2

X‚Äêray single crystal diffraction reveals complexes 1 and 2 are isostructural. They have similar structure except for the numbers of uncoordinated water molecules (2 has 0.25 uncoordinated water molecule more than 1). So, complex 1 is taken as an example to introduce the coordination structure. The EuIII ions show a nine‚Äêcoordinate arrangement, which comprises six OCOO‚Äì atoms, one N atom, and two Owater atoms and shows a slightly distorted mono‚Äêcapped trigonal prism (Figure 1).

The Eu‚ÄìO bond lengths range from 2.34(2) to 2.535(9) √Ö, and the average bond lengths of Eu‚ÄìO and Eu‚ÄìN are 2.438(14) and 2.546(12) √Ö. The Tb‚ÄìO bond lengths range from 2.366(9) to 2.499(9) √Ö, and the average bond lengths of Tb‚ÄìO and Tb‚ÄìN are 2.403(17) and 2.500(13) √Ö. The bond lengths of Tb‚ÄìO and Tb‚ÄìN are slightly shorter than the bond lengths of Eu‚ÄìO and Eu‚ÄìN because of the lanthanide contraction effect. All the carboxyl groups of the ligand are deprotonated forming pztc4‚Äì anion. Each pztc4‚Äì ion bridges four EuIII ions and four KI ions through dodecadentate mode. The coordination mode of the pztc4‚Äì ligand has not been reported previously (Figure 2). The KI atom adopts a hexacoordinate mode in compounds 1 and 2. The coordination modes of KI are shown in Figure S5 (Supporting Information). The average K‚ÄìO bond lengths are 2.896(5) and 2.903(15) √Ö in 1 and 2, respectively, Selected bond lengths and angles are listed in Tables S1 and S2 (Supporting Information).

As shown in Figure 3, the carboxylate group at C4 of the reversed arranged pztc4‚Äì ligand doubly bridges EuIII ions with Eu‚ÄìEu separation 6.7260(13) √Ö to yield an infinite column running along the b axis. This columns are further stitched to each other by the carboxylate oxygen atom (O4) of pztc4‚Äì belonging to different columns to give rise to an ordered 3D lanthanide organic framework with 1D channels along the b axis. A space‚Äêfilling representation of 1 clearly indicates the formation of porous, nano‚Äêsized channels, which are occupied by free water molecules. When KI atoms are shown, the channels with 11.7‚Äâ% porosity still exist (Figure 4).

3.2 Structure Description of 3 and 4

Complexes 3 and 4 show similar 3D architectures and they are isostructural with three NdIII, DyIII, ErIII complexes with the ligand pdtc4‚Äì reported earlier,11 in which LnIII ions are eight‚Äêcoordinate and adopt square antiprismatic arrangements. All the carboxyl groups of the ligand are deprotonated, and show rich coordinated modes, whereas each pdtc4‚Äì participate coordination through a tridecadentate mode (Figures S6 and S7, Supporting Information).

Lanthanide contraction effect can be observed through comparing the average Ln‚ÄìN and Ln‚ÄìO bond lengths among the reported three complexes and 3 and 4 (Table 2). Selected bond lengths and angles for complexes 3 and 4 are listed in Tables S3 and S4 (Supporting Information). Like the other three complexes, KI ions in 3 and 4 also participate in coordination, which makes the structures of 3 and 4 much richer. The coordinated environments of KI ions in complexes 3 and 4 are different (Figure S8, Supporting Information). In 3, KI ion is seven‚Äêcoordinate and adopts the same coordination mode as in the NdIII complex. In 4, KI is eight‚Äêcoordinate and adopts the same coordination mode as in the DyIII complex. Owing to the role of KI ions, the 3D organic frameworks appear much richer and fascinating (Figure S9, Supporting Information).

3.3 Thermal Analysis

The TGA were carried out and the TG curves are shown in Figure S10 (Supporting Information). The structures of 1 and 2 are very similar, therefore only the TGA of 2 was operated in a nitrogen atmosphere in the temperature range of 30 to 750 ¬∞C. For 2, the first weight loss below 135 ¬∞C is 5.04‚Äâ%, corresponding to the loss of 1.25 uncoordinated water molecules (calcd. 4.43‚Äâ%). The second weight loss, 8.15‚Äâ%, occurred from 135 to 300 ¬∞C, which was due to the loss of two coordinated water molecules (calcd. 7.08‚Äâ%). After the loss of water molecules, a continuous weight loss occurred in the temperature range 300‚Äì705 ¬∞C, which was due to the degradation of pztc4‚Äì ligand. Above 705 ¬∞C, terbium oxide and K2CO3 were obtained representing the remaining mass of 49.78‚Äâ% (calcd. 46.01‚Äâ%).

The structures of 3 and 4 are similar. Compound 3 is taken as an example to study the thermal behavior in an atmosphere of air in the temperature range of 30 to 750 ¬∞C. For 3, the first weight loss from 70‚Äì170 ¬∞C is 4.53‚Äâ%, corresponding to the loss of one uncoordinated water molecule (calcd. 3.73‚Äâ%). The second weight loss, 3.87‚Äâ%, occurred from 170 to 320 ¬∞C, which was due to the loss of one coordinated water molecule (calcd. 3.73‚Äâ%). From 320 to 680 ¬∞C, the weight loss was due to the degradation of pdtc4‚Äì ligand. Above 680 ¬∞C, gadolinium oxide and K2CO3 were obtained representing the remaining mass of 48.18‚Äâ% (calcd. 51.78‚Äâ%).

3.4 Fluorescent Properties

The fluorescent spectra for complexes 1 and 2 are measured at room temperature in aqueous solution with the concentration of 10‚Äì5 mol¬∑L‚Äì1. Complexes 1 and 2 exhibit the corresponding characteristic luminescence when the excitation wavelengths are 350 nm and 330 nm, respectively. The emitting spectra of 1 and 2 are shown in Figure 5. The fluorescent spectrum of 1 exhibits the characteristic transition of the EuIII ion. The peaks at 570, 591, 615, 649, and 692 nm are attributed 5D0 to 7FJ (J = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4) transitions. It is well‚Äêknown that the 5D0 to 7F0 transition is strictly forbidden by symmetry.12 As the 5D0 to 7F0 transition is observed, the spectra reveal that the EuIII ion does not lie in a centrosymmetric coordination site, which agrees with the result of X‚Äêray single‚Äêcrystal diffraction. The magnetic and electric dipole transitions of 5D0 to 7F3 are very weak, whereas the electric dipole transition of 5D0 to 7F2 is the strongest. 5D0 to 7F2 is affected by symmetry, it increases as the site symmetry of the central EuIII atom decreases.12a,12d The 5D0 to 7F2 transition became the strongest emission only when the EuIII ion was in the asymmetric center.13 In emitting spectra of TbIII in 2, the four main bands at 491, 545, 584, 622 nm are assigned to the transition of 5D4 to 7FJ (J = 6, 5, 4, 3), and it can be seen that the emission band at 545 nm (5D4 to 7F5) is obviously stronger than the other emission bands, which is consistent with the literature.[12b‚Äì12d, 14] The EuIII and TbIII complexes show characteristic luminescence.

4 Conclusions

Four lanthanide(III) coordination polymers were synthesized by using N‚Äêheterocyclic pztc4‚Äì and pdtc4‚Äì as the organic linkers. Ligands pztc4‚Äì and pdtc4‚Äì coordinate to s‚Äêblock and f‚Äêblock metals by dodecadentate and tridecadentate chelating modes, respectively. Owing to the participation of KI ions, complexes 1‚Äì4 show much richer frameworks. In 1 and 2, a porous, nano‚Äêsized channel with 11.7‚Äâ% porosity occupied by free water molecules were observed. Furthermore, the coordination mode of the pztc4‚Äì ligand is reported for the first time. EuIII and TbIII complexes show characteristic luminescence emission in the visible region.
