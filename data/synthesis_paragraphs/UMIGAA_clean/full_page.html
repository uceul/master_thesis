Abstract

The ZnII‚ÄêCoII organic framework [Me2NH2][Zn2Co(Œº3‚ÄêOH)(BTC)2(H2O)]¬∑2H2O (FJI‚Äê6) (H3BTC = 1, 3,5‚Äêbenzenetricarboxylic acid and DMA = N.N‚Ä≤‚Äêdimethyl acetamide), was synthesized and structurally characterized. FJI‚Äê6 shows a three‚Äêdimensional heterometallic microporous framework with coexisting octahedral cages and one‚Äêdimensional channels assembled by Zn2Co(Œº3‚ÄêOH)(CO2)6 secondary building units. In addition, the sorption behavior and magnetic properties of FJI‚Äê6 were investigated.

Introduction

In recent years, the design and construction of cluster‚Äêbased frameworks has attracted great interest, not only because of their interesting structures but also due to their potential applications in the fields of catalysis, gas adsorption and separation, luminescence, and magnetism.1‚Äì3 Such cluster‚Äêbased frameworks can be constructed by combining the secondary building units (SBUs) with various organic linkers under solvothermal conditions.4,5 Among these typical SBUs, the M2(O2CR)4, M3O(O2CR)6, and M4O(O2CR)6 SBUs are the most commonly used ones in the syntheses of cluster‚Äêbased framework materials.6 In addition, the introduction of different metals in one kind of SBU may lead to the formation of multifunctional heterometallic frameworks.7 Such as the flexible lanthanide ions can combine with the alkali metal ions or 3d metal ions, resulting in fascinating and interesting heterometallic cluster‚Äêbased structures.8 To date, a large number of 3d‚Äê4f, 3d‚Äê1s, 3d‚Äê2s, or 4f‚Äê1s heterometallic frameworks have been reported, while the 3d‚Äê3d heterometallic frameworks still remain a great challenge, due to their similar radii and properties.9,10

Herein, the heterometallic organic framework [Me2NH2][Zn2Co(Œº3‚ÄêOH)(BTC)2(H2O)]¬∑2H2O (FJI‚Äê6) (H3BTC = 1, 3,5‚Äêbenzenetricarboxylic acid and DMA = N,N‚Ä≤‚Äêdimethylacetamide) was synthesized and the single crystal structure, the sorption behavior, and the magnetic properties of FJI‚Äê6 were systematically investigated.

Experimental Section

Materials and Methods: All the chemicals were purchased commercially and used as received. Thermogravimetric experiments were performed with a TGA/NETZSCH STA‚Äê449C instrument heated from 30‚Äì1000 ¬∞C (heating rate of 10 K¬∑min‚Äì1, nitrogen stream). The powder X‚Äêray diffraction (XRD) patterns were recorded on crushed single crystals in the 2Œ∏ range 5‚Äì50¬∞ using Cu‚ÄêKŒ± radiation. Scanning transmission electron microscopy (STEM) and energy dispersive X‚Äêray spectroscopy (EDS) analyses were carried out with a JEOL JEM‚Äê2100F microscope operating at an accelerating voltage of 200 kV. Single component gas adsorption measurements were performed in the Accelerated Surface Area and Porosimetry 2020 System (ASAP2020). Magnetic susceptibility data were collected with a Quantum Design MPMS (SQUID)‚ÄêXL magnetometer and a PPMS‚Äê9T system.

Synthesis of [Me2NH2][Zn2Co(Œº3‚ÄêOH)(BTC)2(H2O)]¬∑2H2O (FJI‚Äê6): A mixture of Zn(ClO4)2¬∑6H2O (0.10 mmol, 38 mg), Co(ClO4)2¬∑6H2O (0.05 mmol, 19 mg), H3BTC (0.10 mmol, 20 mg) in DMA (5.0 mL) with additional HBF4 (0.4 mL, tetrafluoroboric acid, 40‚Äâ% in water) was sealed in a 25 mL glass vial, which was heated at 100 ¬∞C for 5 d, and cooled down to room temperature. After washed by fresh DMA, the red crystals of FJI‚Äê6 were obtained in ca. 50‚Äâ% yield based on the H3BTC ligand. C18H6CoO14Zn2: calcd. C 34.41; H 3.04; N 2.01‚Äâ%; found: C 33.31; H 2.94; N 1.94‚Äâ%. EDS data: Zn 15.67‚Äâ% and Co 7.36‚Äâ% (Figure S1 and Table S2, Supporting Information).

Crystal Structure Determination: Single‚Äêcrystal X‚Äêray diffraction data of FJI‚Äê6 was collected with a Rigaku Diffractometer with a Mercury CCD area detector (Mo‚ÄêKŒ±; Œª = 0.71073 √Ö) at 173 K. Empirical absorption corrections were applied to the data using the Crystal Clear program.11 The structure was solved by the direct method and refined by the full‚Äêmatrix least‚Äêsquares on F2 using the SHELXTL‚Äê97 program.12 Metal atoms were located from the E maps and other non‚Äêhydrogen atoms were located in successive difference Fourier syntheses. All non‚Äêhydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. The organic hydrogen atoms were positioned geometrically, while those of the solvent molecules were located using the difference Fourier method and refined freely. PLATON/SQUEEZE was employed to remove the heavily disordered water molecules and Me2NH2+ cation, and the final formula of FJI‚Äê6 was calculated from the TGA and elemental analysis. Results and Discussion

Description of the Crystal Structure

Single crystal X‚Äêray diffraction analysis reveals that FJI‚Äê6 crystallizes in the tetragonal space group I4cm. Its asymmetric unit contains one independent ZnII ion, one independent CoII ion, one BTC3‚Äì ligand, one Œº3‚ÄêOH anion, one coordinated H2O molecule, two guest H2O molecules, and one Me2NH2+ cation. The Me2NH2+ cations are generally decomposed from DMA molecules under solvothermal conditions and confirmed by TG‚ÄêMS data (Figure S2, Supporting Information). The central ZnII ion is five‚Äêcoordinate with a distorted trigonal bipyramidal arrangement coordinated by four carboxylate oxygen atoms from three different BTC3‚Äì ligands and Œº3‚ÄêOH‚Äì anion. The central CoII atom is six‚Äêcoordinate in a square‚Äêbipyramidal arrangement surrounded by four carboxylate oxygen atoms from four BTC3‚Äì ligands, two oxygen atoms from the coordinated water molecules and Œº3‚ÄêOH‚Äì anion (Figure 1a). Each BTC3‚Äì ligand is connected to three ZnII ions and two CoII ions with a (Œ∫1‚ÄêŒ∫1)‚Äê(Œ∫1‚ÄêŒ∫1)‚ÄêŒ∫2‚ÄêŒº5 coordination mode. Six carboxylate groups and the Œº3‚ÄêOH‚Äì anion linked by two ZnII and one CoII ions tend to form Zn2Co(Œº3‚ÄêOH)(CO2)6 SBUs (Figure 1b).

In addition, an octahedral cage is assembled from eight Zn2Co(Œº3‚ÄêOH)(CO2)6 SBUs and sixteen BTC3‚Äì ligands with one inserted sphere of an approximate diameter of 5.2 √Ö (Figure 1c). The octahedral cages are expanded further into three‚Äêdimensional heterometallic porous framework with one‚Äêdimensional channels (Figure 1d).

XRD Patterns and Thermal Properties

Powder X‚Äêray diffraction (XRD) for FJI‚Äê6 was performed to confirm its purity. The diffraction peak positions on the curves correspond well with the simulated XRD patterns (Figure S3, Supporting Information). The thermo‚Äêgravimetric analysis (TGA) experiments for FJI‚Äê6 were conducted in the temperature range of 30‚Äì1000 ¬∞C in a flow of nitrogen with a heating rate of 10 K¬∑min‚Äì1 (Figure S4). The TGA curve of FJI‚Äê6 indicates that two guest water molecules are lost from 30 to 100 ¬∞C (obsd 4.98‚Äâ%, calcd. 5.03‚Äâ%). A weight loss of 8.64‚Äâ% is found between 100 to 250 ¬∞C, which is in agreement with the departure of one lattice Me2NH2 cation and one coordinated H2O molecule (calcd. 8.56‚Äâ%). Finally the frameworks begin to collapse from 350 ¬∞C.

Sorption Behavior

The total porosity of FJI‚Äê6 is calculated to be 53.2‚Äâ% (solvent area volume 3983.4 √Ö3; unit cell volume 7483.0 √Ö3), however only some part of this volume can be considered as solvent‚Äêaccessible, because the cavities are partially occupied by charge‚Äêbalancing Me2NH2+ components, and the theoretically calculated pore volume of this structure is 0.471 cm3¬∑g‚Äì1. To confirm the permanent porosity, the nitrogen adsorption‚Äêdesorption isotherms of desolvated samples at 77 K are determined. Prior to the sorption experiment, microcrystals (100 mg) were acetone‚Äêexchanged everyday and thoroughly, and then loaded into the sample basket within the ASAP 2020 System. The pseudo‚ÄêType I isotherm curves show a saturated N2 uptake of 126.3 cm3¬∑g‚Äì1 (Figure 2), corresponding to Brunauer‚ÄêEmmett‚ÄêTeller (BET) and Langmuir surface area of 468 m2¬∑g‚Äì1 and 544 m2¬∑g‚Äì1, respectively. Meanwhile, the pore size distribution curves conducted by DFT Pore Size method show that there is a wide distribution of micropores centered at 0.5‚Äì1.0 nm (Figure S5, Supporting Information).

Magnetic Properties

The variable‚Äêtemperature magnetic susceptibility measurement was performed with the crystalline sample of FJI‚Äê6 at the field of 1000 Oe. The œámT value of FJI‚Äê6 is 3.42 cm3¬∑K¬∑mol‚Äì1 at 300 K, which is greater than that expected for the spin‚Äêonly value (1.88 cm3¬∑K¬∑mol‚Äì1 with S = 3/2 and g = 2.0).13 Upon cooling, the value of œámT continuously decreases, reaching a minimum value of 1.26 cm3¬∑K¬∑mol‚Äì1 at 2 K (Figure 3a). In addition, the plot of œám‚Äì1 vs. T of FJI‚Äê6 follows Curie‚ÄêWeiss behavior (C = 3.55 cm3¬∑mol‚Äì1 and Œ∏ = ‚Äì17.19 K).

The nearest neighboring CoII atoms are separated by the H3BTC ligands in FJI‚Äê6 and the gradual decrease of œámT upon lowering the temperature is mainly due to the zero‚Äêfield splitting (ZFS).14 The best fit for FJI‚Äê6 was obtained with g| = 3.48, g‚ä•= 2.12 and |D| = 32.84 cm‚Äì1, which is comparable to that observed for similar mononuclear CoII complexes.14

Additionally, the M vs. H plots (at 2 K) show that M increases rapidly with the increase of the magnetic field, and does not reach saturation even at H = 8 T (Figure 3b). In order to characterize the low‚Äêtemperature behavior of FJI‚Äê6, the temperature dependencies of field‚Äêcooled (FC) and zero‚Äêfield cooled (ZFC) magnetization were tested under a field of 50, 100, and 500 Oe. In Figure S6 (Supporting Information), all the FC curves are consistent with the ZFC curves and no bifurcation points are observed. These results indicate the absence of any long‚Äêrange magnetic ordering in FJI‚Äê6.15

Conclusions

The ZnII‚ÄêCoII organic framework (FJI‚Äê6) with coexisting discrete cages and one‚Äêdimensional channels, which are assembled by the Zn2Co(Œº3‚ÄêOH)(CO2)6 SBUs, wasobtained. The sorption behavior and magnetic properties of heterometallic cluster‚Äêbased framework FJI‚Äê6 were investigated. The preparation of FJI‚Äê6 may provide a useful and promising approach to synthesize new heterometallic cluster‚Äêbased frameworks.
