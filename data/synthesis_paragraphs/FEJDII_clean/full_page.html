    Abstract

    Two new organically templated zinc phosphites, [H3N(CH2)4NH3]·[Zn3(HPO3)4]·H2O (1) and [C3N2H5]2·[Zn3(HPO3)4] (2) have been synthesized under mild hydrothermal conditions. Their structures were determined by single-crystal X-ray diffraction and further characterized by FTIR, elemental analysis, inductively coupled plasma (ICP) analysis, powder X-ray diffraction, and thermogravimetric analysis. In addition to the same anionic framework compositions of [Zn3(HPO3)4]2-, both phases consist of strictly alternating ZnO4 tetrahedra and HPO3 pseudo-pyramids linked through their vertexes to generate the three-dimensional frameworks with intersecting 8- and 12-membered ring channels, where the template molecules reside. Compound 1 crystallizes in the triclinic system, space group P1̄ with the following cell parameters:  a = 7.7703(4) Å; b = 9.1499(4) Å; c = 14.4685(3) Å; α = 89.965(2)°; β = 87.050(2)°; γ = 65.332(2)°; V = 933.31(7) Å3; Z = 2. Compound 2 crystallizes in the triclinic system, space group P1̄ with the following cell parameters:  a = 9.0238(4) Å; b = 9.0392(4) Å; c = 13.8071(6) Å; α = 95.965(2)°; β = 96.049(2)°; γ = 110.228(2)°; V = 1038.92 (8) Å3; Z = 2.

    Synopsis

    Two new organically templated zinc phosphites, [H3N(CH2)4NH3]·[Zn3(HPO3)4]·H2O (1) and [C3N2H5]2·[Zn3(HPO3)4] (2) have been synthesized under mild hydrothermal conditions. Both phases possess the same anionic framework compositions of [Zn3(HPO3)4]2- and consist of strictly alternating ZnO4 tetrahedra and HPO3 pseudo-pyramids linked through their vertexes to generate the three-dimensional frameworks with intersecting 8- and 12-membered ring channels, where the template molecules reside.

    Microporous materials are of considerable interest due to their rich structural chemistry and wide applications in the areas of catalysis, adsorption, and separations.1 Since the discovery of crystalline aluminophosphate molecular sieves in 1982,2 a great deal of attention has been paid to the synthesis of open-framework metal phosphates, and this has resulted in the preparation of many new compounds with novel architectures.3,4 These compounds occur as zero- (clusters), one- (chains or ladders), two- (layers), and three-dimensional (continuous) structures. Several metal phosphates possessing helical channels,5-7 chiral frameworks,8-11 and gigantic pores12-14 are more interesting. Generally, these materials have been synthesized under mild hydrothermal conditions in the presence of organic amines, alkali metal cations, or metal complexes as templates to avoid the formation of dense phases.
    The possibilities of replacing the tetrahedral phosphate group by a pseudo-pyramidal [HPO3]2- unit was first demonstrated by Clearfield and co-workers several years ago.15 Compared to phosphate, the phosphite group can only make three P−O−M (M = Zn, etc.) bonds, which might be expected to lead to a new class of compounds with interesting architectures. There are (NC5H12)2·Zn3(HPO3)4 with polyhedral 16-membered ring windows16 and (C5H6N2)Zn(HPO3) and (4,4‘-bipy)[Zn(HPO3)]2 containing left-handed and right-handed helical chains in a sheet.17,18 [H2N(CH2)2NH2]0.5·ZnHPO3 possesses an unprecedented architecture of two independent, interpenetrating mixed inorganic−organic networks.19
    In this paper, we report the synthesis, crystal structure, and some properties of two new zinc phosphites prepared in the presence of either 1,4-diaminobutane or imidazole:  [H3N(CH2)4NH3]·[Zn3(HPO3)4]·H2O (1) and [C3N2H5]2·[Zn3(HPO3)4] (2). Although the two compounds have the same inorganic framework compositions of [Zn3(HPO3)4]2-, they show different crystal structures due to the utility of different templates.

    Materials and Methods. All reagents were purchased commercially and used without further purification. Elemental analysis (C, H, and N) was conducted on an Elementar Vario EL III analyzer. Inductively coupled plasma (ICP) analysis was carried out on a Perkin-Elmer Optima 3300 DV spectrometer. The infrared spectrum was obtained on an ABB Bomen MB 102 spectrometer in the 400−4000 cm-1 region with pressed KBr pellet. Powder X-ray diffraction (XRD) data were collected on a Philips X'Pert-MPD diffractometer with Cu−Kα radiation (λ =1.540 56 Å).     Synthesis of [H3N(CH2)4NH3]·[Zn3(HPO3)4]·H2O, 1.     X-ray Crystallography. Suitable single crystals with dimensions of 0.18 × 0.12 × 0.12 mm3 for 1 and 0.56 × 0.30 × 0.24 mm3 for 2 were carefully selected for single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis. Data collection was performed on a Siemens SMART CCD diffractometer with graphite-monochromatized Mo Kα (λ = 0.710 73 Å) radiation in the ω and φ scanning mode at room temperature. Intensity data were collected by the narrow frame method. The number of frames and the width of a frame is 1300 and 0.3°, respectively. Also the time of exposure is 5 s. No decay of intensities were observed in the process of data collection. An empirical absorption correction was applied using SADABS program.20 Both structures were solved by direct methods. The zinc and phosphorus atoms were first located, and the carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen atoms were found in the final difference Fourier map. The hydrogen atoms attached to C, N, and P3 in compound 1 were placed geometrically, and the remaining H atoms in the P−H groups in compounds 1 and 2 were located by Fourier maps. H atoms associated with the extraframework water molecule in 1 were not located. The structures were refined on F2 by full-matrix least-squares methods using the SHELX97 program package.21,22 All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically.     The asymmetric unit of 1 contains 26 non-hydrogen atoms, as shown in Figure 2, three Zn and four P atoms being crystallographically distinct. The zinc atoms are tetrahedrally coordinated by oxygen atoms with Zn−O contacts varying between 1.900(9) and 1.959(9) Å and O−Zn−O angles lying between 96.0(4)° and 118.4(4)°. The phosphorus atoms each share three oxygen atoms with adjacent Zn atoms with P−O bond lengths ranging from 1.502(9) to 1.533(9) Å. The fourth tetrahedral vertex about each P atom is assumed to be a P−H bond [1.2742−1.47(10) Å]. The O−P−H and the O−P−O bond angles are in the range of 102(5)°−115.1(5)°, indicating a pseudo-pyramid of HPO32-. Assuming the usual valence of Zn, P, O, and H to be +2, +3, −2, and +1, respectively, the network stoichiometry of [Zn3(HPO3)4] creates a net charge of −2 and the diprotonated 1,4-diaminobutane molecule per formula unit balances this negative charge.
    The framework structure of 1 is built up from strictly alternating ZnO4 tetrahedra and HPO3 pseudo-pyramids that are linked through their vertexes giving rise to a 3D architecture possessing channels. The anionic part in the asymmetric unit can be considered as secondary building units, which are connected to each other via vertex oxygens forming a 4,8,12-network parallel to the ac plane (Figure 3). The layers are stacked along the [010] direction in an −AAAA− sequence to generate the three-dimensional framework with 8-ring channels along the crystallographic [100] (Figure 4a) and [010] (Figure 3) directions and 12-ring channels along the [010] and [001] (Figure 4b) directions. The diprotonated 1,4-diaminobutane molecules are located in the channel system and interact with the inorganic framework and water molecules through hydrogen bonds.     Two new three-dimensional open-framework zinc phosphites, [H3N(CH2)4NH3]·[Zn3(HPO3)4]·H2O and [C3N2H5]2·[Zn3(HPO3)4], have been obtained from hydrothermal systems. Structural analyses indicate that both compounds are constructed from strictly alternating ZnO4 tetrahedra and HPO3 pseudo-pyramids by sharing vertexes. Compounds 1 and 2 have the same compositions of [Zn3(HPO3)4]2- and each encapsulates a complex three-dimensional channel system with intersecting 8- and 12-membered ring channels. But their crystal structures are different due to the influence of the corresponding organic template.
