Polyhedron

Volume 122, 28 January 2017, Pages 55-60

Constructing three new metal–organic frameworks based on a mixed-donor ligand: Topological analysis and fluorescence properties

Author links open overlay panelXiao-FangWangYingPanZhanLiXiuLiShi-LunQiuMingXue
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.poly.2016.10.041Get rights and content

Abstract

Three new metal–organic frameworks (MOFs), [Zn2(TZI)(μ3-OH)(H2O)2]·(CH3)2NH (JUC-165), [Cd5(TZI)3(μ3-OH)(H2O)7]·4H2O (JUC-166), and [Cd(TZI)]·(CH3)2NH2·H2O (JUC-167) (JUC = Jilin University China) have been synthesized by self-assembly of Zn2+/Cd2+ ions and a prominent ligand, 5-tetrazolylisophthalic acid (H3TZI). JUC-165 exhibits fascinating three-dimensional (3D) structure containing 1D rectangular channels with dimensions of 6.5 × 3.0 Å2 and it performs (5,5)-connected topology with a Schläfli symbol of 4664, JUC-166 displays a 2D sandwich-layer which further forms into a 3D supermolecular structure by H-bonds reactions and JUC-167 exhibits (4,4)-connected 3D frameworks with a Schläfli symbol of 42638. Furthermore, we research the fluorescence properties of these new MOFs measured at room temperature, and the results reveal that JUC-165, JUC-166 and JUC-167 display strong main emission spectra peaks at 410, 437 and 415 nm, respectively.

Graphical abstract

Three new MOFs comprising one mixed-donor ligand (5-tetrazolylisophthalic acid, H3TZI) are synthesized and structurally characterized. JUC-165 features 3D frameworks including 1D rectangular channels and it performs (5,5)-connected frameworks with a Schläfli symbol of 4664, JUC-166 shows a 2D rarely sandwich-like tri-layer, and JUC-167 exhibits (4,4)-connected 3D frameworks with a Schläfli symbol of 42638. The fluorescence properties of these three new MOFs were measured at room temperature.

1. Introduction

The crystal engineering of metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) have developed at an unusually fast pace in recent years due to their fascinating topologies and various potential applications, such as adsorption, separation, catalysis, fluorescence and magnetic properties [1], [2], [3], [4], [5]. Many factors are known to affect the self-assembly of coordination structures. However, the construction of MOFs is determined primarily by metal centres and organic ligands. Organic ligands acting as bridging linkers between metal centres play a key role in tuning the structural topology and functionality of MOFs when the coordination number and geometry of the metal centres are relatively fixed [6], [7], [8], [9], [10], [11], [12]. Nitrogen containing tetrazole subunits have been utilized to generate a wide diversity of structural motifs ranging from discrete molecular rings to 3D frameworks. Tetrazoles and their derivatives, such as five-membered N-heterocyclic azoles, are prominent of multi-connectivity abilities and high structural stability [13], [14], [15]. For O-donor ligands, the outstanding ones of containing m-benzenedicarboxylate subunit are of special interest. It is an intense evidence of an important role on targeting functional MOF materials that many excellent MOFs which constructed by the linkers containing m-benzenedicarboxylate subunit [16]. Meanwhile, it has to be proved that mixing different donors/subunits in one ligand is a wisdom strategy [17], thus, based on elaboration above mixed m-benzenedicarboxylate and tetrazole subunits in one ligand will be extraordinary to create ideal materials [18], [19], [20], [21], [22]. From another point of view to achieve structural diversity is the incorporation of linkers with flexibility into the framework. And in common sense the flexible nature of typical flexible or semi-rigid ligands containing such as -CH2- spacers or at least one sp3 hybrid atom (usually C, N, or O) have been noted and mentioned in many researches which allows the ligands to bend and rotate freely when coordinating to metal centers [23], [24], [25], [26]. But it is interesting that so as to conform to the coordination geometries of metal ions, rigid ligands can also perform similar flexible-like features for the twist and tent of different motifs in one linker which will bring on unforeseen various frameworks and functions [27]. The detailed study and discussion about the effects of flexibility in rigid ligands are meaningful.

Inspired by the aforementioned above, we opted to use the 5-tetrazolylisophthalic acid (H3TZI) ligand to coordinate with Zn2+ or Cd2+ ions and explore its potential in directing the assembly of extended frameworks based on its flexibility. Successfully, three new MOFs, [Zn2(TZI)(μ3-OH)(H2O)2]·(CH3)2NH (JUC-165), [Cd5(TZI)3(μ3-OH)(H2O)7]·4H2O (JUC-166) as well as [Cd(TZI)]·(CH3)2NH2·H2O (JUC-167) were constructed. It’s notable that for the rotation and bending of the mixed motifs in the ligand, H3TZI behaved various coordination modes and different configurations in the three structures. JUC-165 featured 3D frameworks including 1D rectangular channels with dimensions of 6.5 × 3.0 Å2, which performed (5,5)-connected frameworks with a Schläfli symbol of 4664, JUC-166 performed 2D sandwich-layer which further form to 3D supermolecular structure by H-bonds reactions, JUC-167 exhibited 3D frameworks with a Schläfli symbol of 42638. Moreover, we describe the luminescence properties of the three MOFs materials measured at room temperature and the results reveal strong main emission spectrum peaks at 410, 437 and 415 nm of JUC-165, JUC-166 and JUC-167, respectively.

2. Experimental section

2.1. Materials and methods

All the chemical reagents in this work were acquired from commercial sources and used without further purification. Elemental analyses (C, H and N) were carried out with a Perkin-Elmer 240 analyzer. The Powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD) data were collected on a SHIMADZU LabX XRD-6000 diffractometer with Cu-Ka radiation (λ = 1.5418 Å) at 40 kV and 30 mA at room temperature. Thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) were performed on a Perkin-Elmer TGA thermogravimetric analyzer under air flow. Fourier-transform infrared spectra were obtained on a Nicolet Impact 410 FT-IR spectrometer by using KBr pellets in the 4000–400 cm−1 range. Fluorescent data were obtained using a Cary Eclipse fluorometer at room temperature.

2.2. Synthesis of JUC-165

A mixture of Zn(NO3)2·6H2O (0.08 mmol, 0.012 g), H3TZI (0.05 mol, 0.012 g), DMF 4 mL, and H2O (4 mL) was stayed at 80 °C for 48 h. Light yellow block crystals were obtained and dried in air (70% yield based on Zn). EA (%) Anal. Calc. for C11H15N5O7Zn2: C, 28.72; H, 3.29; N, 15.22. Found: C, 28.81; H, 3.16; N, 15.28%. 2.3. Synthesis of JUC-166

A mixture of CdCl2·2.5H2O (0.14 mmol, 0.032 g), H3TZI (0.05 mmol, 0.012 g) and H2O (8 mL) was adjusted to pH about 4.0 with 0.05 M NaOH solution. It was then sealed in a 25 mL Teflon reactor and heated at 150 °C for 72 h. Finally, the mixture was cooled to room temperature and colorless block-shaped crystals were obtained by filtration. The crystals were washed with distilled water and dried in the air. (yield: 51% based on Cd). EA (%) Anal. Calc. for C27H23N12O24Cd5: C, 22.17; H, 1.59; N, 11.49. Found: C, 22.23; H, 1.41; N, 11.35%. 2.4. Synthesis of JUC-167

A mixture of Cd(NO3)2·6H2O (0.06 mmol, 0.02 g), H3TZI (0.05 mmol, 0.012 g), and DMF/EtOH/H2O (4:2:1, 7 mL) was adjusted to pH about 4.0 with 2 M HNO3 and 0.5 M NaOH solution. It was then sealed in a 25 mL Teflon reactor and heated at 120 °C for 72 h. Finally, the mixture was cooled to room temperature and colorless needle crystals were obtained by filtration. The crystals were washed with DMF and dried in the air. (yield: 55% based on Cd). EA (%) Anal. Calc. for C11H12N5O5Cd: C, 32.49; H, 2.98; N, 17.22. Found: C, 32.87; H, 2.74; N, 17.73%. 2.5. X-ray structure determination and refinement

Diffraction intensities for JUC-165, JUC-166 and JUC-167 collected at 293 K on a computer-controlled Bruker SMART CCD diffractometer equipped with graphite-monochromated MoKα (λ = 0.71073 Å) radiation by using the ω-scan technique. The structures ware solved using the shelxs-97 program and refined anisotropically by a full-matrix least-squares technique based on F2 using shelxl-97 [28]. All non–hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically and the hydrogen atoms of organic ligands were located by geometrically. And the guest H2O molecule number was determined by elemental analysis and TG analysis. 3. Results and discussion

3.1. Description of the crystal structure of JUC-165

Single crystal X-ray diffraction of JUC-165 reveals that it crystallizes in the orthorhombic space group Cmcm. The asymmetric unit contains 0.5 Zn2+ cations, 0.25 TZI3− ligand, 0.25 μ3-OH and 0.5 coordinated water molecular (Fig. S1). And the 0.25 (CH3)2NH of guest comes from the hydrolysis of DMF under high pressure and temperature [29]. TZI3− ligand coordinates with six mental sites by the coordination mode which can be described as μ6-η1: η0: η0: η1: η1: η1: η1: η1 (Fig. 1a). Its conformation is mode-b by the dihedral is 90.0° between the m-benzenedicarboxylate and tetrazolyl ring (Scheme S1a). Two kinds of zinc centers (Zn1 and Zn2) are found in the framework. The Zn1 atom is five-coordinated by three oxygen atoms which consist of two carboxylate oxygen atoms from two TZI3− and one bridging oxygen atom from a μ3-OH ligand in the equatorial plane, and two nitrogen atoms from two TZI3− ligands distribution in the axial position, which forms triangle biconical ZnN2O3 coordination geometry. While the Zn2 atom is six coordinate by four oxygen atoms from two μ3-OH ligands, two coordinated water molecules and two nitrogen atoms from two different TZI3− ligands, forming an octahedral geometry. The Zn–O bond distances are from 1.962(4) to 2.128(6) Å, and the Zn–N bond distances are 2.110(5) and 2.362(5) Å (Table S1). For the interactions between metal sites and ligands described above, triangle [Zn3(μ3-OH)] SBU are formed by μ3-OH connecting three metal centers (Fig. 1b), these isosceles triangles connected by TZI3− spacers to form 1D strip-shaped chains further. Adjacent chains linked by m-benzenedicarboxylate groups to form 3D frameworks with 1D rectangular channels (6.5 × 3.0 Å2) with taking van der Waals radii into consideration run along the c axis (Fig. 1c). A better insight into the nature of JUC-165 can be achieved by the application of a topological approach, that is, reducing multidimensional structures to simple node-and-linker nets (the Topos4.0 program is a powerful tool for the analysis, comparison, and design of network structures) [30]. As shown in Fig. 1, the trimetallic [Zn3(μ3-OH)] SBU can simply be regarded as a 5-connected node since the terminal H2O molecules do not contribute to the network topology. Likewise, each TZI3− ligand coordinated to five [Zn3(μ3-OH)] clusters can act as a 5-connected node. On the basis of the simplification principle, the resulting structure of JUC-165 is a rare binodal five-connected net, and its Schläfli symbol is 4664 (Fig. 1d).

3.2. Description of the crystal structure of JUC-166

Single crystal X-ray diffraction of JUC-166 reveals that it crystallizes in the orthorhombic space group P212121. The asymmetric unit contains five Cd2+ cations, three TZI3−anion, one μ3-OH and seven terminal coordinated water molecular (Fig. S2). For the TZI3− ligand, three kind of coordination modes were found which can be described as μ5-η1: η0: η1: η1: η1: η1: η1 η0 (type (I)), μ5-η1: η1: η1: η1: η1: η1: η1: η0 (type (II)), and μ7-η0: η1: η2: η2: η1: η1: η0: η1 (type (III)), respectively (Fig. 2a and b). It is worth noting that the dihedral between the m-benzenedicarboxylate and tetrazolyl ring is 58.8°, 45.5°, and 2.2° of the three modes, respectively (Scheme S1b-d). In this structure, there are five kinds of Cd2+ sites. Among them, Cd1, Cd2, Cd3, Cd5 are all six-coordinated octahedral geometry. For Cd1, it is coordinated by three oxygen atoms from two terminal coordinated waters and one bridging oxygen μ3-OH ligand, and three nitrogen atoms from three TZI3− ligands. The Cd2 atom is coordinated by three oxygen atoms from one μ3-OH ligands, two carboxylate oxygen atoms from two TZI3− ligands and three nitrogen atoms from three different TZI3− ligands. Cd3 atom is coordinated by five oxygen atoms from one TZI3− ligand, three terminal coordinated waters and one μ3-OH ligand, and one nitrogen atom from one TZI3− ligand. For Cd5, it is six-coordinated by four oxygen atoms of three TZI3−, one terminal coordinated water oxygen atom, and one nitrogen atom from TZI3− ligand. While the Cd4 is seven-coordinated which consist of five carboxylate oxygen atoms from three TZI3−, one terminal coordinated water oxygen atom, and one nitrogen atom from TZI3− ligand, forming a single-cap triangular prism geometry. The Cd–O bond distances are from 2.267(8) to 2.567(7) Å, and the Cd–N bond distances are 2.346(8) and 2.457(7) Å (Table S1). The type (I) and type (II) linkers connect Cd1, Cd2, Cd4 and Cd5 centers to form two different 2D layers which are in the top and bottom, respectively. The two layers are like twins for they are very similar which form to a double-layer by sharing Cd1, Cd2 centers (Fig. 2c). In addition, the type (III) linkers as well as μ3-OH ligand connect Cd1, Cd2, Cd3 and Cd4 centers to form another layer in the middle (Fig. 2d). And the intermediate layer is like a mirror which is caught in the middle of the twins-like layers (Fig. S3). Thus, the three monolayers form an overall sandwich-like 2D layer by sharing Cd1, Cd2, Cd4 and Cd5 centers. As we know, the sandwich-like tri-layer structure is rare [31], [32], [33]. There are strong hydrogen bonding interactions (O6W–H⋯O1, O6W–H⋯O3 and O7W–H···O7 distance of 2.9350, 2.9136 and 2.7421 Å, respectively (Table S2)) between adjacent 2D layers. The layers stack each other to form 3D supramolecular frameworks further (Fig. 2e).

3.3. Description of the crystal structure of JUC-167

Single crystal X-ray diffraction of JUC-167 reveals that it crystallizes in the monoclinic space group P21/c. The asymmetric unit contains one Cd2+ cations, one TZI3− anion, and one (CH3)2NH2 of guest comes from the hydrolysis of DMF under high pressure and temperature (Fig. S4) [34]. For the TZI3− ligand, its coordination mode can be described as μ4-η1: η1: η1: η0: η0: η1: η0: η1 (Fig. 3a). The dihedral between the m-benzenedicarboxylate and tetrazolyl ring is 56.1° (Scheme S1e). So the m-benzenedicarboxylate motifs connect metal centers to form chains along the a axis, and tetrazoly units link Cd2+ to construct chains along c axis. For metal center, it is five-coordinated with three carboxylate oxygen atoms from two TZI3−, and two nitrogen atoms from other two TZI3− ligands, forming a trigonal bipyramidal geometry (Fig. 3b). The Cd–O bond distances are from 2.234(5) to 2.462(5) Å, and the Cd–N bond distances are 2.221(6) Å (Table S1). For the interactions between metal sites and ligands described above, JUC-167 shows a 3D framework (Fig. 3c). A better insight into the nature of JUC-167 can be achieved by the application of a topological approach, that is, reducing multidimensional structures to simple node-and-linker nets (analyzed by the Topos4.0 program) [35]. The TZI3− ligands and the mental centers could be simplified as 4-connected nodes. After the analysis by the software package TOPOS, the network belongs to a (4,4)-connected bimodal net with point symbol 42638 (Fig. 3d).

3.4. Coordination modes of the ligands

From the structure descriptions above, the mixed-donor ligands can adopt a variety of coordination modes as we expect. It connects four (JUC-167), five (JUC-166), six (JUC-165), and seven (JUC-166) metals by different ways in this paper (Scheme S1). In JUC-165, two carboxylate groups bridge two Zn2+ ions in a μ1-η1:η0 mode, and all of the nitrogen atoms of the tetrazole are involved in coordination connecting four Zn2+ ions. In JUC-166, three types of TZI3− ligands show different coordination modes. The type (I) ligand bridges five Cd2+ atoms with one of two carboxylate groups in a μ1-η1:η0 coordination mode, the other one in a μ1-η1:η1 coordination mode, and the tetrazole connects three Cd2+ ions in 1, 2, 3-position. The coordination mode of type (II) ligand is very similar with type (I) ligand except that the two carboxylate groups are all in μ1-η1:η1 coordination modes. While type (III) ligand connects seven Cd2+ centers with one of two carboxylate groups in a μ1-η1:η0 coordination mode, the other one in a μ3-η2:η2 coordination mode, and the tetrazole connects three Cd2+ ions in 1, 2, 4-position. In JUC-167, four Cd2+ atoms with one of two carboxylate groups in a μ1-η1:η0 coordination mode, the other one in a μ1-η1:η1 coordination mode, and the tetrazole connects two Cd2+ ions in 1, 4-position.

3.5. Flexible effects of the ligands on the frameworks

The H3TZI can be regarded as a partially flexible ligand, where the carboxylate group and the tetrazole/phenl ring can twist and bend freely. In theory, for H3TZI, there are two ultimate conformations (Scheme S2), the planar conformation (mode-a) and the non-planar conformation (mode-b) with the maximum torsion angle achieves to 90.0° of the two parts belong to the ligand. From the structure descriptions above, it exhibits mode-a conformation with the maximum torsion angle of 90.0° in JUC-165. However, two conformations are found in JUC-166, one is the approximative mode-a conformation with torsion angle of just 2.2°, and the other is mode-b conformation with torsion angle of 58.8°and 45.5°. And it is notable that the carboxyl gropes of the three types of linkers twist different angles around the benzene ring in this structure, and even the C–C bond which connect benzene and tetrazolyl rings bent 9.8° and 10.9° respect to the m-benzenedicarboxylate plane in type (I) as well as type (II) linkers, respectively (Fig. S5). Thus, H3TZI performs remarkable flexibility in the framework. In JUC-167, the dihedral between the m-benzenedicarboxylate and tetrazolyl ring is 56.1° (mode-b). Obviously, the outstanding flexible effects of H3TZI meet the complex of the various frameworks.

4. Thermal analysis and PXRD results

Thermal stabilities of three new MOFs materials have been measured (Fig. S6). For JUC-165, it is found that there was a weight loss of 8.94% before 110 °C, which corresponds to the departure of guest (CH3)2NH2 molecules (calculated: 9.23%), and the temperature range of 100–280 °C can be attributed to the removal of the coordinated waters (experimental: 9.31%; calculated: 7.83%), and then the structure decomposed gradually. The residue was 34.73 % corresponding to the formation of ZnO (calculated: 35.38%). For JUC-166, the weight loss of 12.91% corresponding to the release of water molecules is observed from 30 to 252 °C (calculated: 13.56%), and then the structure decomposed. The departure of the structure finally leads to the formation of CdO (experimental: 45.08%; calculated: 43.93%). For JUC-167, the weight loss of 9.41% corresponding to the departure of guest (CH3)2NH molecules is observed from 50 to 152 °C (calculated: 11.07%), and then the structure decomposed. The departure of the structure finally leads to the formation of CdO (experimental: 33.24%; calculated: 31.58%). The PXRD experimental and computer-simulated patterns of the corresponding complexes are shown in Figs. S7–S9. The peak positions of the measured PXRD patterns are in good agreement with the simulated patterns.

5. Luminescence properties

Luminescent properties of MOFs containing d10 metal centers have attracted intense interest due to their potential applications, such as in chemical sensors, photochemistry, electroluminescent display, and so on [36], [37], [38]. In this work, the solid-state photoluminescent spectra of H3TZI ligand, JUC-165, JUC-166 and JUC-167 have been carried out at room temperature (Fig. 4). The main emission peak of H3TZI is at 377 nm (λex = 326 nm), which can be attributed to the π∗ → π or π∗ → n transitions. On complexation of the ligands with Zn2+ or Cd2+ atom, the emission peaks occur at 410 nm (λex = 372 nm) for JUC-165, 437 nm (λex = 337 nm) for JUC-166, and 415 nm (λex = 320 nm) for JUC-167. The Cd2+ ions and Zn2+ ions are difficult to oxidize or reduce because of the d10 configuration [39], [40]. So the emissions of these compounds are neither metal-to-ligand charge transfer (MLCT) nor ligand-to-metal charge transfer (LMCT) in nature. However, for the three complexes, the main emission peak is highly red-shifted by 33, 60 and 38 nm, respectively, relative to the free H3TZI ligand. It is known that the replacement of the hydrogen proton by Zn2+ or Cd2+ ions decreases the π∗ → π or π∗ → n gap of the carboxylate ligands, resulting in a red shift of the emission peaks [41], [42], [43]. So the emission can be assigned to the chelation of the ligands to the central metals.

6. Conclusions

In conclusion, we have described three MOFs with the 5-tetrazolylisophthalic acid ligand (H3TZI). JUC-165 and JUC-167 show different 3D frameworks: in JUC-165, infinite strip-shaped chains in which Zn3(μ3-OH) triangles are interlinked by TZI3− ligands into a 3D structure with the point symbol 4664 topology; in JUC-167, the unique Cd2+ ions are linked by TZI3− ligands with the point symbol 42638 topology. And JUC-166 performs a 3D supermolecular structure by strong H-bonds containing rarely 2D sandwich-like tri-layer. Meanwhile, we discussed the interesting flexibility features of this rigid ligand in details, the mixed-donor ligand exhibits wonderful characteristics coordinated with mental centers. Moreover, the luminescence of JUC-165, JUC-166 and JUC-167 indicated that these MOFs materials may be good candidates for functional materials.
