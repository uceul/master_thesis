Abstract

Five new Mn(II)/Co(II) coordination polymers [Mn2(Adi)2(DMA)]n (1), [Mn2(Adi)2(DMF)]n (2), [Mn4(Adi)4(DMF)2]n (3), [Co4(Adi)4(DMF)2]n (4) and {[Co3(Cit)2(H2O)3]Â·(H2O)}n (5) [Adi=adipate anion, Cit=citrate anion, DMA=N,Nâ€²-dimethylacetamide and DMF=N,Nâ€²-dimethylformamide] have been successfully constructed from two flexible multicarboxylate ligands under solvothermal conditions. Complexes 1 and 2 exhibit 2-D network featured 1-D MnII chain, 3 and 4 are 3-D frameworks containing different 1-D carboxylateâ€“metal chain, while 5 shows a 3-D structure based on Co6 wheel clusters. Magnetic investigations indicate antiferromagnetic behaviors for 1â€“4 and weak ferromagnetic behavior for 5 because of distinct linkage modes of metal ions.

1. Introduction

The investigation of functional coordination polymers has stimulated researchers' considerable interest, not only for their diverse structural architectures and intriguing physical/chemical properties, but also by virtue of their promising broad applications in gas storage [1], separation [2], drug delivery [3], catalysis [4], luminescence [5], magnetism [6], etc [7]. More chemists focus on how to obtain expected products through a variety of chemical methods. Rational selection of ligands to construct such materials is a key problem for researchers to contemplate, in which rigid or aromatic carboxylate is one of the most used types of ligands [8]. As for the common flexible organic acids with long aliphatic chain, they are relatively less documented [9]. Moreover, flexible multicarboxylate ligands are good candidates in preparing molecular magnetic materials with architectural beauty of their structures.

Adipic and citric acids (Scheme 1) have been employed to construct functional coordination polymers along with other auxiliary aromatic ligands [10]; however, the complexes constructed only from adipic/citric acid have been rarely reported [11]. These can be attributed to the lack of Ï€âˆ’Ï€ interactions, which is a very important driving force to single crystals growth in these systems [12]. Thus this implies a challenging task to construct coordination polymers based on flexible carboxylate ligands with long aliphatic chain. Both adipic acid and citric acid include multiple carboxylate groups and could provide more coordination sites to construct novel molecular magnetic materials. In addition, long aliphatic chain of adipic acid is favor for synthesizing chain-based coordination polymers, and one more carboxylate and hydroxyl groups of citric acid is helpful to obtain more complicated products.

Herein, we report the five coordination polymers, [[Mn2(Adi)2(DMA)]n (1), [Mn2(Adi)2(DMF)]n (2), [Mn4(Adi)4(DMF)2]n (3), [Co4(Adi)4(DMF)2]n (4) and {[Co3(Cit)2(H2O)3]Â·(H2O)}n (5) [Adi=adipate anion, Cit=citrate anion, DMA=N,Nâ€²-dimethylacetamide and DMF=N,Nâ€²-dimethylformamide], synthesized under solvothermal conditions. All complexes have been analyzed by infrared spectroscopy, elemental analysis and PXRD (X-ray powder diffraction), and 1â€“3 and 5 have been characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction. Complexes 1 and 2 have a similar structure with the exception of different solvent molecules coordinated to MnII ions and contain 1-D MnII chains connected by carboxylates which are further extended to 2-D network through adipate. Complex 3 has a 3-D network with 1-D MnII chains along the b axis which is different from that of 1 and 2. Complex 4 is analogue of 3, which was determined from PXRD. Complex 5 displays a 3-D structure based on Co6 wheel clusters. Magnetic studies indicate that 1â€“4 display global antiferromagnetic behaviors due to the antiferromagnetic couplings between metal ions, while 5 shows weak ferromagnetic behavior.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Synthesis

Colorless block-shaped crystals of 1â€“3 and pink/red crystals of 4â€“5 are obtained by reacting CoII/MnII salts and Adi/Cit under solvothermal conditions. In our efforts to obtain target complexes, the kinds of metal salts and solvents, the ratio of reagent and reaction temperature were altered. Complexes 1â€“5 are air-stable and almost insoluble in common organic solvent. The IR spectra of 1â€“5 show the strong vibrations appearing in the range of 1500 and 1700 cmâˆ’1, indicating the existence of carboxylate groups. Generally, adipic acid with long aliphatic chain is a good candidate in synthesizing chain-based coordination polymers. Compared with adipic acid, citric acid contains more coordination groups (one carboxylate and one hydroxyl), which is helpful to obtain cluster-based coordination polymers. The synthetic methods allow for the assembly of other related coordination polymers with interesting magnetic properties.

3.2. Description of the crystal structures

[Mn2(Adi)2(DMA)]n (1). Complexes 1 and 2 have a similar structure, thereby, only 1 will be described in detail as an example. The single crystal X-ray determination reveals that 1 crystallizes in the monoclinic space group P21/c. The crystallographic asymmetric unit of 1 consists of two MnII ions, two adipate anions and one DMA molecule (DMF for 2, the range of Mnâ€“O length: 2.065(4)â€“2.372(4) Çº. Table S1 in the SI) (Fig. 1a). As shown in Scheme 2(A, B, and D), adipate anions exhibit three coordination modes. The Mn(1) centers have six-coordinated octahedron geometry, coordinated with four oxygen atoms from four heptadentate Î¼6 mode adipate anions, and two oxygen atoms from two Î¼4 mode adipate anions. Mn(2) centers also adopt octahedron geometry, connected to three oxygen atoms from two heptadentate Î¼6 mode adipate anions, two oxygen atoms from two Î¼4 mode adipate anions, and one oxygen atom from a DMA molecule. The Oâ€“Mnâ€“O angles are ranging from 174.00(14)Â° to 56.03(12)Â° (Table S1). Mn(1) and Mn(2) are bridged by adipate anions with heptadentate Î¼6 mode and Î¼4 mode to form a 1-D chain along the c axis (Fig. 1b), then one 1-D chain is connected with adjacent chains by the carbonic framework of Î¼4 mode adipate anions, as a result, forming a 2-D layer in ac plane (Fig. 1c).

[Mn4(Adi)4(DMF)2]n (3). Although 3 possesses of the same molecular formula with 2, there are huge differences between them in structural aspect. Complex 3 crystallizes in space group P21/c, and the crystallographic asymmetric unit consists of four MnII ions and four adipate anions (the range of Mnâ€“O length: 2.119(6)â€“2.276(5) Çº. In 3, adipate anions adopt two coordination modes: One is tetradentate Î¼4 mode, the other one is hexadentate Î¼6 mode (see Scheme 2B and C). {[Co3(Cit)2(H2O)3]Â·(H2O)}n (5). Complex 5 crystallizes in the orthorhombic space group Pbca, and has three crystallographical CoII centers in the lattice: Co1, Co2 and Co3.
