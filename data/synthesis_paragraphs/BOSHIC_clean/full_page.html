1. Introduction

Metalâ€“organic frameworks (MOFs) have been rapidly emerging as potential materials for storage and separation of small gas molecules such as H2, CH4, CO2 and C2H2.1 To develop high-performance porous MOF materials for such applications, a series of strategies, such as improving surface area and pore volume, making use of framework interpenetration and catenation, and optimizing activation conditions, has been extensively explored.2 Recently, the strategy of synthesizing polymorphous frameworks constructed from the same organic ligand and secondary building units (SBUs) provides another approach for investigating the interaction between the framework and gas molecules. Actually, the research of polymorphous frameworks has been carried out by several groups. Mirkin and co-workers have systematically synthesized three types of MOFs through metallacarborane-basedtetratopic bis(isophthalicacid) linker.3a Meanwhile, two polymorphous MOFs (UHM-8 and UHM-9) whose structures are dependent on the solvents used during the solvothermal synthesis have been constructed from a linear tetracarboxylate linker containing CC backbones.3b Generally, the series of MOF materials assembled from such a tetratopic bis(isophthalic acid) linker and a copper paddle-wheel Cu2(COO)4 cluster exhibit either NbO (niobium(II) oxide) or PtS (platinum(II) sulfide) topologies.4 Since the first report of a NbO topology in MOF-505, this family of isoreticular MOFs has been studied extensively and shows bright promise for the storage of H2, CH4 and C2H2.5 This strategy of synthesizing polymorphous frameworks will encourage us to design and synthesize many more types of MOF materials.

As a part of our research into the synthesis and properties of multidentate carboxylate linker based MOFs, we have synthesized numerous porous MOFs assembled from tetratopic bis(isophthalicacid) linker and copper paddle-wheel Cu2(COO)4 clusters.5h,6 This series of MOF materials exhibits excellent gas sorption properties because of their high surface area, tunable pore size, and open copper(II) sites suitable for gas adsorption. For example, the NbO type MOF ZJU-5 has been established for its high absolute volumetric methane storage (190 cm3(STP) per cm3) at room temperature and 35 bar.5h

To expand our investigations in this area, herein we report a new MOF [Cu2(C26H12O8)(H2O)2]Â·(DMF)2Â·(MeCN)3Â·(H2O)4 (ZJU-7, ZJU = Zhejiang University; H4L = 5,5â€²-(naphthalene-1,4-diyl)diisophthalic acid; DMF = N,N-dimethylformamide; MeCN = acetonitrile). Interestingly, ZJU-7 and NOTT-109 (ref. 5c) are polymorphous frameworks constructed from the same tetratopic ligand H4L decorated with a naphthalene group (Scheme 1). The activated ZJU-7a exhibits moderately high porosity with the Brunauerâ€“Emmettâ€“Teller (BET) surface area of 2198 m2 gâˆ’1. With open metal sites, suitable pore spaces and moderately high permanent porosity, ZJU-7a exhibits moderately high CH4 storage of 160 cm3(STP) per cm3 at 35 bar and 298 K. Furthermore, ZJU-7a exhibits moderate C2H2 gravimetric storage of 180 cm3 gâˆ’1 at 1 atm and 298 K.

2.2. Gas sorption measurements

A Micromeritics ASAP 2020 surface area analyzer was used to perform the gas sorption measurements. 2.3. X-ray collection and structure determination

Crystallographic measurements for ZJU-7 were taken on a Bruker APEX-II diffractometer with an CCD detector using graphite-monochromatic Mo KÎ± radiation (Î» = 0.71073 Ã…) at 293 K. The determination of the unit cell and data collection for the crystal of ZJU-7 was performed with CrysAlisPro. The data sets were corrected by empirical absorption correction using spherical harmonics, implemented in the SCALE3 ABSPACK scaling algorithm.7 The structure of ZJU-7 was determined by direct methods and refined by the full-matrix least-squares method with the SHELX-97 programpackage.8 All non-hydrogen atoms were located successfully from Fourier maps and were refined anisotropically. The naphthalene unit of L ligand is highly disordered, which is split into four parts during structural refinement. The disordered lattice DMF, MeCN and water molecules could not be located successfully from Fourier maps in the refinement cycles. The scattering from the highly disordered lattice guest molecules were removed using the SQUEEZE procedure implemented in the PLATON package.9 The composition of the as-synthesized ZJU-7 was figured out based on the elemental analysis, TGA and single crystal structure.â€

3. Result and discussion

The organic linker H4L was readily synthesized by Suzuki cross-coupling followed by hydrolysis and acidification. ZJU-7 was synthesized as blue rhombic crystals via a solvothermal reaction of Cu(NO3)2Â·2.5H2O and organic ligand H4L in DMF/MeCN/H2O with addition of hydrochloric acid at 80 Â°C for 2 days. The structure was characterized by single-crystal X-ray diffraction studies, and the phase purity of the bulk material was independently confirmed by powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD, Fig. 1). The similarity of the simulated PXRD pattern from single-crystal data to those for both as-synthesized and activated samples reveals that the single crystal is representative of the pure bulk sample, and the framework is robust and its crystallinity can be retained after the removal of guest molecules and the coordinated water molecules. Based on the single-crystal structure determination, elemental analyses and thermogravimetric analysis (TGA, Fig. 2), ZJU-7 can be formulated as [Cu2(C26H12O8)(H2O)2](DMF)2(MeCN)3(H2O)4. TGA under a nitrogen atmosphere reveals a weight loss of 39.37% up to 513 K, corresponding to the loss of free solvents and terminal water molecules (calcd 39.44%). Above this temperature, the complex starts to decompose rapidly.

Single crystal structure study indicates that the framework is indeed constructed from paddle-wheel Cu2(CO2)4(H2O)2 clusters with organic carboxylate Ligand. ZJU-7 crystallizes in the rhombohedral space R-3m with a = b = 18.6843(7) Ã… and c = 38.6052(12) Ã…. This is different from the 4284 PtS type framework NOTT-109, which is constructed from the same organic ligand and [Cu2(CO2)4] paddle-wheel center, but crystallizes in the tetragonal space group I4/mmm.11 The [Cu2(CO2)4] SBUs (Fig. 3b) are bridged by the four-connected rectangular organic building block ligand to give rise to a 3D (4,4)-connected structure with NbO type topology. Furthermore, there exist two types of cages which are alternately stacked along the c axis. The first cage (Fig. 4a) with a spherical-like pore of about 14.0 Ã… in diameter (according to the space-filling models) is formed from twelve ligands and six paddle-wheel SBUs; while the second one (Fig. 4b) consists of six ligands connecting twelve paddlewheel SBUs to form a shuttle-shaped pore with an inner sphere diameter of 13.4 Ã…. Meanwhile, each cage has open triangular windows of 5.0 Ã… in dimension when viewed along c axis. PLATON calculations9 showed that the solvent-accessible volume in ZJU-7 is about 6221 Ã…3 out of the unit cell of 11671.6 Ã…3, which constitutes 53.3% of the total volume of the crystal. The framework density of ZJU-7 is 0.750 g cmâˆ’3 upon removal of the coordinated water molecules.

The high surface area, large pore volume and open copper sites within the framework of ZJU-7a encouraged us to study its gas-uptake capacity. Methane gas is an important alternative on-board fuel that has attracted much interest. MOFs offer great promise for CH4 storage and several MOF compounds have been reported to exhibit exceptional CH4 uptake and work capacity. To investigate the CH4 storage capacity of ZJU-7a, volumetric CH4 adsorption isotherms have been also recorded up to 60 bar at 273, 298 and 308 K. As shown in Fig. 6, ZJU-7a exhibits moderately high volumetric methane uptakes of 160 cm3(STP) per cm3 under 35 bar 298 K, reaching approximately 89% of the DOE standard (180 cm3(STP) per cm3) at 35 bar and room temperature. The methane storage capacity of ZJU-7a is slightly lower than some MOFs for extraordinarily high methane storage,1b but comparable to most MOF materials at same condition (NOTT-140,10a 159 cm3(STP) per cm3; UTSA-34b,10b 159 cm3(STP) per cm3; MIL-53(Cr),10c 165 cm3(STP) per cm3). The methane storage in ZJU-7a increases to 188 cm3(STP) per cm3 under 60 bar. Taking 5 bar as a specific lower pressure limit and 60 bar as the upper limit, the methane delivery amounts of ZJU-7a is 110 cm3(STP) per cm3, that is, a tank filled with ZJU-7a can deliver 42.4% as much fuel as the CNG tank operating on the same lower pressure limit and 248 bar as the up limit, indicating ZJU-7a as a potential material for CH4 storage. The initial Qst of CH4 adsorption is âˆ¼16.6 KJ molâˆ’1, comparable to those in other MOFs with copper paddlewheels.1f As shown in Fig. 6 inset, the Qst of CH4 adsorption does not decrease with the CH4 loading.

ZJU-7a can reversibly adsorb 245 cm3 gâˆ’1 and 180 cm3 gâˆ’1 C2H2 at 273 K and 298 K under 1 bar, respectively. Considering the framework density of 0.750 g cmâˆ’3, the volumetric capacity will be 135 cm3 cmâˆ’3 at 298 K. The initial Qst of C2H2 adsorption is âˆ¼28.8 KJ molâˆ’1, comparable to those in other MOFs with copper paddlewheels.1f As shown in Fig. 7 inset, the Qst of C2H2 adsorption decrease with the C2H2 loading. 4. Conclusions

In summary, we have constructed a novel NbO three-dimensional microporous metalâ€“organic framework ZJU-7 from tetratopic ligand H4L decorated with naphthalene group. The activated ZJU-7a exhibits high porosity with BET surface area of 2198 m2 gâˆ’1 and pore volume of 0.8945 cm3 gâˆ’1. Accordingly, it can storage quite large amount of CH4 and C2H2 under room temperature. This work is expected to motivate more extensive studies on the design and synthesis of polymorphous frameworks constructed from the same organic ligands for their gas storage and separations.
