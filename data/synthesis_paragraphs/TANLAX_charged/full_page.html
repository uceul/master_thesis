Second structural directing agent induces the formation of 1D organic templated terbium sulfate†

Lei Zheng , Xinmin Qiu , Yan Xu *, Jie Fu , Yan Yuan , Dunru Zhu and Su Chen
College of Chemistry and Chemical Engineering, State Key Laboratory of Materials-Oriented Chemical Engineering, Nanjing University of Technology, Nanjing, 210009, PR China. E-mail: yanxu@njut.edu.cn; Tel: (+86)25-83587857

Received 26th November 2010 , Accepted 5th January 2011

First published on 15th February 2011

Two organic amine templated 1D terbium sulfates(H3O)2(C2H8N)[Tb(SO4)3] 1 and 3D (C2H8N)9[Tb5(SO4)12]·2H2O 2 have been solvothermally synthesized and structurally characterized by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. Compound 1 exhibits a 1D anionic chain structure compensated by protonated dimethylamine and water cations occluded in the helical [Tb–O–S]n chains, while the 3D inorganic framework of 2 contains extra-large 20-membered ring channels. The synthesis of both compounds demonstrates that a large organic amine (1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine) may be used as the second structural directing agent (SDA) to prevent the formation of a high dimensional inorganic framework as well as inducing crystal growth of 1D structural terbium sulfate chain of 1. Because of the excellent luminescent properties of Tb3+, compounds 1 and 2 were investigated. The very strong luminescence in the green light region indicates 1D compound 1 is an excellent candidate for green fluorescent materials.

Introduction

Over the past two decades, great efforts have been made to obtain new inorganic solid state materials with different compositions and structures owing to their wide applications such as precursors for oxides and catalysis,1 ion exchanging2 and magnetic properties.3 As a building unit, the SO4 tetrahedra can link metals to form new 1D, 2D, and 3D solid state materials with novel topological structures and interesting properties. Since the first organically templated cadmium sulfate synthesized by Rao, there has been a growing interest in the study of organic amine templated sulfate framework architectures.4–7 Compared with 3d transition metals such as Fe, Mn, V and Zn, the rare-earth elements can adopt a large range of coordination numbers from 8 (LnO8) to 12 (LnO12) and flexible Ln–O bond lengths allow the formation of new inorganic lanthanide sulfate topological frameworks.7–22 Up to now, successful examples including organic amine templated lanthanide sulfates materials have been reported. For example, [(C4H16N3)La(SO4)3]·H2O7,20,6a is a 1D lanthanum sulfate containing infinite zigzag {-La-μ-O–La-μ-O–La-} chains and the inorganic framework of [C6N4H22]1.5[Ho2(SO4)6H2O]21 is an interesting 1D triple ladder; [C2N2H10]1.5[Eu(SO4)3(H2O)]·2H2O23 is a 2D europium sulfate which contains interesting corrugated layers in the inorganic framework; (C2H8N)9[Eu5(SO4)12]·2H2O24 is the first 3D rare earth sulfate which contains two kinds of intersecting extra large 20-membered ring channels.

More recently, other topics in solid state materials chemistry are inorganic materials with helical pores or chains due to their particularly desirable applications in optical materials and enantiotopic selective separation. Among the reported frameworks, germanates,24 vanadates25 and phosphates26–28 have such chiral structural features. For example, [(CH3)2NH2]K4[V10O10(H2O)2(OH)4(PO4)7]·4H2O25 contains interpenetrating double helices.

Although terbium is an important rare earth element due to its excellent optical properties, organic templated terbium sulfate has not been reported up to now. Therefore, it is vital to design novel terbium sulfates with helical chains and microporous materials in order to explore their catalytic and optical properties as well as understand their formation mechanism. In this work, we used a large amine (1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine) as a second SDA (structure directing agent) to prevent the formation of 3D Ln-O–S frameworks. A novel 1D rare earth sulfate (H3O)2(C2H8N)[Tb(SO4)3] 1 with helical [Tb–O–S]n chains was successfully synthesized, while the 3D, with intersecting extra-large 20-membered ring channels, organic templated terbium sulfate (C2H8N)9[Tb5(SO4)12]·2H2O 2 was obtained without the second SDA under similar conditions.

Experimental

Materials and apparatus

Both compounds were prepared from a mixture of rare earth oxide (99.9%), sulfuric acid (95–98 wt%), N,N-dimethylformamide (DMF, 99.5%), and 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine (99.7%) under solvothermal conditions. All chemicals purchased with reagent grade were used without further purification. The element analyses were performed on a Perkin-Elmer 2400 element analyzer and the inductively coupled plasma (ICP) analysis was performed on a Perkin-Elmer optima 3300 DV ICP spectrometer. The infrared (IR) spectrum was recorded within the 400–4000 cm−1 region on a Nicolet Impact 410 FTIR spectrometer using KBr pellets. A NETZSCH STA 449C unit was applied to carry out the TGA analyses under nitrogen atmosphere with a heating rate of 10 °C min−1.

Preparation

In a typical synthesis of compound 1, a solution was prepared by dissolving 0.1055 g (0.14 mmol) of Tb4O7 into 7.0873 g (96.96 mmol) DMF and 1.5381 g (15.33 mmol) sulfuric acid under constant stirring for an hour. Then 0.1013 g (0.80 mmol) 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine was added into the solution under constant stirring for 30 min. The resulting mixture was transferred into a 25 mL Teflon-lined stainless-steel autoclave and heated at 433 K for 6 days. After cooling to room temperature, the product was washed with ethanol and dried in air for one day. Finally, the block crystals (yield 63%, with respect to Tb) were obtained. The element analysis shows that the C, H and N contents are 4.47, 2.60 and 2.74 wt%, respectively (calculated: C, 4.52 wt%; H, 2.64 wt%; N, 2.64 wt%). IR (KBr pellet, cm−1): 3442 (b), 3178 (b), 1653 (w), 1400 (m), 1384 (m), 1119 (m), 651 (w), 599 (w). The IR spectrum of compound 1 shows the characteristic bands for dimethylamine in the region 1400–1600 cm−1. The strong bands around 1119 cm−1 can be attributed to the sulfate ion. Absorption at 599 cm−1 is due to Tb–O vibration. A band around 3450 cm−1 can be attributed to the presence of water.

The block crystal of compound 2 was prepared in the same way. 0.1066 g Tb4O7 (0.14 mmol) was dissolved into 7.0905 g (96.96 mmol) DMF and 1.5641 g (15.34 mmol) sulfuric acid under constant stirring for an hour while 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine was not added into the solution. The resulting mixture was transferred into a 25 mL Teflon-lined stainless-steel autoclave and heated at 433 K for 6 days. After cooling to room temperature, the product was washed with ethanol and dried in air for one day to give the block crystals (yield 44%, with respect to Tb). The elemental analysis shows that the C, H and N contents are 9.13, 3.08 and 5.31 wt%, respectively (calculated: C, 9.01 wt%; H, 3.17 wt%; N, 5.26 wt%). IR (KBr pellet, cm−1): 3448 (b), 3206 (vs), 2781 (m), 1638 (m), 1466 (m), 1405 (w) 1112 (b), 657 (m), 603 (m). The IR spectrum of compound 2 shows the characteristic bands for dimethylamine in the region 1400–1600 cm−1. The strong bands around 1112 cm−1 can be attributed to the sulfate ion. Absorption at 603 cm−1 is due to the Tb–O vibration. A band around 3450 cm−1 can be attributed to the presence of water.

X-ray crystallography

The single crystals of both compounds 1 and 2 were transferred onto a thin glass fiber by epoxy glue in air for data collection. The diffraction data were collected on a Bruker Apex 2 CCD with Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at 296 K using ω−2θ scan method. An empirical absorption correction was applied. All the non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. The hydrogen atoms of the organic molecules were refined in calculated positions, assigned isotropic thermal parameters, and allowed to ride on their parent atoms, while the H atoms of protonated water in 1 were located from difference map. The O–H distances are restrained as 0.85(2) Å in 1. The O21, O22, O23, O24, O21a, O22a, O23a and O24a in 2 are disordered with occupancy factors of 0.5 and higher thermal parameters. All calculations were performed using the SHELX97 program package.29 Further details of the X-ray structural analyses for compound 1 and 2 are given in Table 1 and selected bond lengths and angles are listed in Table S1†.

Result and discussion

Synthesis

The solvothermal synthesis method has been demonstrated to be a useful technique in the formation of inorganic solid-state materials. During the solvothermal synthesis, many factors, including reaction temperature, pH values, solvent and time, affect the nucleation and the crystal growth of the final products. In the formation of the title compound, the solvent (DMF) plays an important role. In the reaction, DMF decomposed into dimethylamine and formic acid. Solvothermal reaction of Tb4O7, DMF, H2SO4 and 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine in a molar ratio of 1:693:110:6 were mixed to produce the 1D inorganic terbium sulfate 1. While 3D, with intersecting extra-large 20-membered ring channels, organic templated terbium sulfate (C2H8N)9[Tb5(SO4)12]·2H2O, 2, was obtained under similar conditions, only without the second SDA (1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine). Therefore, large organic molecules (1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine) prevent the formation of a 3D framework as a second template. Also, when we use some small molecules, such as dimethylamine, trimethylamine, and piperazidine, to replace 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine, the product obtained was only (C2H8N)9[Tb5(SO4)12]·2H2O 2.

Description of the structure

Crystal structure of 1. Compound 1 crystallizes in triclinic space group P. The structure of 1 consists of inorganic anionic 1D [Tb(SO4)3]n3n− chains, charge compensated by the protonated dimethylamine cation and protonated water cations. The asymmetric unit of compound 1 contains one terbium atom, three sulfate groups, one protonated dimethylamine and two H3O+ cations, as shown in Fig. 1. In the inorganic chain of 1, Tb is eight-coordinated by eight O atoms from five sulfates. The metal atom surrounded by three bidentate sulfate anions and two monodentate sulfate anions to form a distorted bicapped trigonal-prismatic geometry. The bond distances of Tb–O vary from 2.310(4) to 2.461(4) Å, whereas the angles of O–Tb–O are between 57.13(13) and 153.24(13)°. There are three crystallographic independent S atoms: S1 and S2 form three S–O–Tb linkages with three 2-coordinated oxygen atoms; S3 makes two S–O–Tb linkages as a ligand of one Tb atom through two μ-2 oxygen atoms (Tb–O–S bridges). All the S atoms are tetrahedrally coordinated by four O atoms with the S–O distances of 1.437(5)–1.496(4) Å, which is similar to the reported lanthanide sulfates.7–19
Fig. 1 ORTEP view of the (H3O)2(C2H8N)[Tb(SO4)3] structure showing the atom labeling scheme.

The reported [(C4H16N3)La(SO4)3]·H2O7,20,6a is a 1D lanthanum sulfate containing an infinite zigzag {-La-μ-O–La-μ-O–La-} chain, while the inorganic framework of [C6N4H22]1.5[Ho2(SO4)6H2O]21 is an interesting 1D triple ladder. The structure of the inorganic chain of 1 is different, which can be described as double helical chains with terminal SO4 tetrahedra attached to them. Tb atoms were connected by the bridging sulfates (S1, S2 and their crystallographic partners) to form a single helical [-Tb–O–S–O-]n chain and other helical chains were further linked by O–Tb–O linkages to generate intertwined -Tb–O–S–O- double helices of the same handedness, as shown in Fig. 2. These kinds of double helices are particularly rare in inorganic materials, however, one germanate and two phosphates were reported, formulated as Ge7O12(OH)4(C4N3H13)0.5(H2O)5,24[(CH3)2NH2]K4[V10O10(H2O)2(OH)4(PO4)7]25 and [Zn2(HPO4)4][Co(dien)2]H2O.26 The remaining SO4 tetrahedra are attached to the double helices. There are two crystallographically independent protonated water (H3O+) cations. To the best of our knowledge, a protonated water (H3O+) cation was not observed in the previously reported organic templated lanthanide sulfates. Interestingly, O1w makes four hydrogen bonds, O–H⋯O, with the O atoms from the double helices to generate a flat layer along the a axis, as shown in Fig. 3a. In the [-Tb–S–O⋯H3O⋯] layer, Tb atoms are linked with each other by SO4 tetrahedra and hydrogen bonds to form a hexagonal mesh structure. The remaining protonated water cation (O2w) connects adjacent layers by three hydrogen bonds to generate a 3D soft open framework along the b axis, as shown in Fig. 3b. The protonated dimethylamine cations are located in the channels and involved hydrogen bonding interactions with the soft framework and make it more stable (Fig. 4).

It is somewhat curious that compound 1 includes a fully protonated dimethylamine instead of 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine. It must be noted that dimethylamine, along with formic acid,30 would be the product of decomposition of DMF as shown in Scheme 1. The organic amine 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine was not included in the compound, but it is very important during the synthetic process. Without 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine, the product would be 3D porous compound 2 rather than the 1D compound 1.

Crystal structure of 2. Crystal structure analysis indicates that the open-framework 2 is the first porous terbium sulfate with an extra-large 20 membered ring (20MR) channels and it has a 3D inorganic framework constructed from TbO8, TbO9 and SO4 polyhedra. The asymmetric unit of compound 1 contains three terbium atoms, six sulfate groups, one water molecule and protonated dimethylamine cations. As shown in Fig.5, metal atoms Tb1 is eight-coordinated by O atoms; Tb2 is nine-coordinated by O atoms, while Tb3 is connected by disordered SO42−. The bond distances of Tb–O vary from 2.283(9) to 2.738(13) Å, whereas the angles of O–Tb–O are between 38.7(4) and 167.06(14)°. Six crystallographic independent S atoms can be divided into 4 groups: S1 and S5 are bonded by one μ3-O, two μ2-O and one Ot (terminal O atoms) to form 4 S–O–Tb linkages; S2 and S3 are bonded by three μ2-O atoms and one Ot to generate 3 S–O–Tb linkages; S4 is coordinated by four μ2-O to form four S–O–Tb linkages; while S6 is coordinated by eight disordered O atoms (O21, O22, O23, O24, O21a, O22a, O23a, O24a) with occupied factors of 0.5. The S–O distances range from 1.374(9) to 1.562(13) Å, which is similar to those of previous reports about lanthanide sulfates.7–19
Fig. 5 ORTEP view of the (C2H8N)9[Tb5(SO4)12]·2H2O structure showing the atom labeling scheme.

The structure of 2 can be described from two types of secondary building units (SBUs). Tb1 lies on a 2-fold axis (0, y, 0.25), while two Tb3(SO4)6 fragments connect with each other by sharing Tb1 to form the SBU-1 of Tb5(SO4)12 (Fig. 6a). Two adjacent SBU-1 are linked by bridging O atoms to generate an interesting 20-membered ring (Fig. 6b), consisting of 10 TbOx and 10 SO4 polyhedra. SBU-2 is a 10-membered ring constructed by 5 TbOx and 5 SO4 groups. Adjacent 10 and 20-membered rings are connected by bridging SO4 groups (S1O4 and S5O4) to form an open framework with a 3D channel system. There are two kinds of channels with 10 and 20-members extending along the crystallographic c axis (Fig. 7a). Both 10 and 20-membered (MR) channels are arranged in an ordered close packing structure. Each 20MR channel is surrounding by six 10MR, and they are alternately surrounded by three 20MR and three 10MR channels. The approximate window sizes for 20MR and 10MR are 5.3 × 9.5 and 3.8 × 3.8 Å, respectively. Interestingly, there are other rectangular 20MR-r channels along the crystallographic [101] direction, as shown in Fig. 7b. Although the rectangular 20MR also consists of 10 TbOx and 10 SO4 polyhedra, the shape is quite different. The pore size of rectangular 20MR is about 2.8 × 12.8 Å. We used PLATON software to calculate the percent void volume in the 3D network of 2, and the result is 48%. Although the structure of 2 is similar to our previously reported 3D europium sulfate,23 the inorganic framework of 2 is different. Because of the appearance of disordered SO4 in both types of 20-membered rings, the inorganic framework and pore sizes are different. The dimethylammonium ions (with N1, N3 and N6) are in general positions, while the remaining three (with N2, N4 and N5) lie on twofold axes. As shown in Fig. 8, the protonated dimethylamine molecules are located in two kinds of 20MR channels of the inorganic framework by the strong hydrogen bonding interactions between N atoms of organic amine molecules and O atoms of the open-framework of 2.

Luminescence property

Compounds 1 and 2 were investigated because of the excellent luminescent properties of Tb3+. The green luminescence properties of each are shown in Fig. 9 and Fig. 10, respectively. The solid-state emission spectra of the two compounds at room temperature were under excitation at 371 nm. Compound 1 shows four emission bands at 490 nm (5D4 → 7F6), 545 nm (5D4 → 7F5), 585 nm (5D4 → 7F4), and 621 nm (5D4 → 7F3). For compound 2, the bands at 490 nm, 545 nm, 585 nm and 622 nm can be attributed to the 5D4 → 7Fj (j = 6, 5, 4, 3) transitions, respectively. Comparatively, compounds 1 and 2 display strong sharp emission bands for Tb3+, 5D4 → 7F5, which is in agreement with the reported Tb compounds.18d,31,32 The luminescence intensity of compound 1 is much stronger than 2 (about 4 times). The enhanced fluorescence efficiency of 1 is attributed to the fact that the coordination environment and strong hydrogen bonds between inorganic chain and protonated water cations increase the rigidity of Tb atoms effectively and reduce the loss of energy by vibrations. The very strong luminescence in the green light region indicates 1D compound 1 is an excellent candidate for green fluorescent materials.

Thermal behavior

Thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) of complexes 1 and 2 were performed in a N2 atmosphere when heated to 1000 °C at a rate of 10 °C min−1. For compound 1, the total weight loss is 55.5%, which is in agreement with the calculated value 61.0% (Fig.S1†). The weight loss of 36.5% in the range of 40–720 °C corresponds to the removal of two free water molecules, dimethylamine and some SO3. The last step loss of 19.0% in the range of 720–1000 °C can be attributed to loss of SO3. The final product is Tb2O3. For compound 2, the total weight loss is 63.5%, which is in agreement with the calculated value 59.1% (Fig.S2†). The weight loss of 1.6% in the range of 30–100 °C corresponds to the removal of two free water molecules. The second step loss of 31.6% in the range of 100–350 °C can be attributed to all the dimethylamine and some of SO3. The weight loss of 30.3% in the range of 350–800 °C can be attributed to loss SO3. The final product is Tb2O3.

Conclusions

In summary, we have successfully synthesized lanthanum sulfate (H3O)2(C2H8N)[Tb(SO4)3] 1 and (C2H8N)9[Tb5(SO4)12]·2H2O 2 by using DMF as solvent. The DMF decomposed into dimethylamine and formic acid and the dimethylamine acts as an organic template. The 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine as a second SDA is essential to the formation of 1D chain. Without a second SDA, the product appeared to be 3D porous compound 2. The synthesis of the compounds demonstrated that the solvent and 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine play a vitally important role, and new lanthanide sulfates can be designed and prepared by applying the solvothermal method. The luminescent spectra of the compounds reveal that the rare-earth organic template sulfates not only possess a luminescence nature but are also become promising photoluminescence materials.
