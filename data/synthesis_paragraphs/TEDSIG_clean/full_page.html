Abstract

Two coordination polymers based on 1, 6‐bis(2‐methyl‐imidazole‐1‐yl)‐hexane (bimh), namely {[Zn3(BTC)2(bimh)]·(bimh)}n (1) and {[Zn(IPA)(bimh)]·(CH3CH2OH)0.5}n (2) (H3BTC = trimesic acid, H2IPA = isophthalic acid), were synthesized through hydrothermal reactions. In compound 1, the zinc(II) ions are bridged by BTC3– ligands to form an undulating infinite two‐dimensional (2D) polymeric network. The 3D networks of 1 show a twofold interpenetrating net. In compound 2, zinc(II) ions are bridged by IPA2– ligands to form one‐dimensional (1D) helical structures. The 2D structures of 2 are further assembled into 3D networks through aromatic π–π stacking interactions. Both compounds exhibit strong photoluminescence at room temperature and may be good candidates for potential luminescence materials.

Introduction

The design and construction of coordination polymers (CPs) with flexible N‐bridging ligands, especially bis(imidazole) ligands has attracted much more attention. Modern coordination chemists have comprehensively developed and researched their intriguing structural topologies and various potential materials applications, such as nonlinear optics, biological activity, catalysis, magnetism, sensors, and molecular recognition.1 The bis(imidazole) ligand with flexible nature of–CH2– spacers is a good representative of nitrogen‐donor bridging ligands. The ligand is able to bend and rotate freely when coordinating to central metal atoms.2 Generally, CPs with long flexible bis(imidazole) lead to interpenetrating structures and helical modes.3

Recently, significant work has been carried out by using different metal ions assembly with flexible bis(imidazole) ligands, a series of outstanding examples of which are (N‐im)2(CH2)n (n = 1–6).4–12

Upon careful inspection of the reported cases, we found that when n = 6, the exploration of metal–bis(imidazole)–organic counterion (such as BTC3–, IPA2–) system is less developed.13–15 In our previous work, we reported the unusual three‐dimensionnal fivefold interpenetrating microporous structure {[Cd(oba)(bib)]·2H2O}n [bib = 1, 4‐bis(2‐methyl‐imidazol‐1‐yl)butane, oba = 4, 4‐oxybis(benzoic acid)].16 In this contribution, we employed the structurally more flexible bis(imidazole) ligand, 1, 6‐bis(2‐methyl‐imidazole‐1‐yl)‐hexane (bimh) (Scheme 1) with a longer methylene (–CH2–)6 skeleton. It tends to form strong coordination bonds to central metal atoms in the process of coordination and exhibits the ability to produce unique interwoven motifs and larger voids with the more flexible conformation.

In this paper, we chose the ligands bimh, trimesic acid (H3BTC), isophthalic acid (H2IPA) to react with zinc(II) under hydrothermal conditions and report herein the preparations, X‐ray crystal structures, and photoluminescent spectra of two new zinc(II) coordination polymers {[Zn3(BTC)2(bimh)]·(bimh)}n (1) and {[Zn(IPA)(bimh)]·(CH3CH2OH)0.5}n (2).

Results and Discussion

Description of Crystal Structures

Structural Analysis of {[Zn3(BTC)2(bimh)]·(bimh)}n (1)

X‐ray diffraction analysis reveals that compound 1 exhibits a twofold interpenetration structure with space group P21/n. In this structure, the bimh ligand adopts an anti–anti coordinating fashion, and the completely deprotonated BTC3– ligands display a tri(monodentate) coordinating mode. The zinc atoms are four‐coordinate by one bimh nitrogen atom and three BTC3– oxygen atoms to afford a ZnO3N tetrahedral arrangement. The Zn–O/N bond lengths are in the range of 1.9619–2.004 Å [Zn(1)–O(1) 1.9619(17) Å, Zn(1)–O(3) 1.9786(17) Å, Zn(1)–O(5) 1.9488(17) Å, Zn(1)–N(1) 2.004(2) Å] (Figure 1). The selected bond lengths and angles for 1 are listed in Table 1. As illustrated in Figure 2a, polymer 1 affords a 3‐connected two‐dimensional (2D) net, if the metal atoms and the BTC3– anion are viewed to be the 3‐connected nodes, respectively. Furthermore, the 2D frameworks are extended to (3, 4)‐connected three‐dimensional (3D) supramolecular frameworks through Zn–N bonds between 2D nets and bimh molecules. Polymer 1 shows a 3D twofold interpenetrating net (Figure 2c). This compound exhibits void space, but the cavities are largely reduced by guest bimh molecules and the mutual interpenetration of the networks (Figure 2d).

Structural Analysis of {[Zn(IPA)(bimh)]·(CH3CH2OH)0.5}n (2)

In compound 2, zinc(II) ions are bridged by IPA2– ligands to give rise to one‐dimensional (1D) helical structures, which coexist as two kinds of helical chains, a left‐handed and a right‐handed helical chain. The bimh ligand adopts an anti–anti coordinating fashion, and the completely deprotonated IPA2– ligands are coordinating monodentately mode. The zinc atoms are four‐coordinate by two bimh nitrogen atom [Zn(1)–N(1) 2.023(3) Å, Zn(1)–N(4) 1.996(3) Å] and two IPA2– oxygen atoms [Zn(1)–O(1) 1.950(2) Å, Zn(1)–O(3) 1.952(2) Å] to construct the ZnO2N2 tetrahedral arrangement (Figure 3). The selected bond lengths and angles for 2 are listed in Table 1. As shown in Figure 4a, the arrangement of zinc(II) and IPA2– gives the 1D helical chain of {Zn(IPA2–)}n, and such chains are further connected by bimh molecules via Zn–N coordination bonds to form 2D frameworks as illustrated in Figure 4b. A better insight into the 2D frameworks can be accessed by topological methods. Each zinc(II) ion connects to three other zinc(II) ions by two IPA2– and two bimh spacers. Hence, it can be viewed as 3‐connected nodes (Figure 4c). These 2D structures are further assembled into a 3D network through aromatic π–π stacking interactions between imidazolyl rings of bimh ligands (face to face distance: 3.525 Å) (Figure 4d). The resulting 3D network contains one‐dimensional channels, and the effective free volume is only 7.3 %, as calculated by PLATON.17

Thermal Properties of Complexes 1 and 2

Thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) indicates that compound 1 is stable up to 362 °C, and subsequently begins to decompose. Compound 2 is stable up to 295 °C, a total weight loss of 4.7 % occurred in the range 78–132 °C, corresponding to the removal of guest ethanol molecules (calcd. 4.6 %), from 132 to 295 °C, the TGA curve of 2 passes through a flat area, and decomposition of organic components (Figure 5).

Fluorescent Properties of Complexes 1 and 2

The solid‐state photoluminescent spectra of 1 and 2 and the bimh ligand are depicted in Figure 6. An intense band in the emission spectra of 1 and 2 is observed at 408 nm (λex = 320 nm) and 474.8 nm (λex = 320 nm), respectively. Whereas the pure bimh ligand exhibits a fluorescent emission band at 440.2 nm (λex = 320 nm), the emission peak of 1 is blue‐shifted by about 32.2 nm compared to pure bimh ligand, but the emission of compound 2 is red‐shifted by about 34.6 nm relative to that of the bimh ligand. According to the literature,[18, 19] these emission bands can be assigned to the intraligand π–π* transition. In addition, the relatively efficient π–π interactions between imidazolyl rings of bimh ligands in 2 may be a reason for the red‐shift of the emission peak in the spectrum of the free ligand. The lifetime in 1 and 2 was monitored at room temperature upon excitation at 320 nm, all data were well fitted as shown in Figure 7. The lifetime at 408 nm emission of 1 shows 1.34 ns (47.17 %) / 3.93 ns (52.83 %), χ2 = 1.149 and the lifetime at 474.8 nm emission of 2 shows 1.35 ns (31.80 %) / 4.24 ns (68.20 %), χ2 = 1.203. Additionally, in order to confirm the phase purity of compounds 1 and 2, the original samples were both characterized by X‐ray powder diffraction (XRPD) at room temperature. The patterns simulated from the single‐crystal X‐ray data of 1 and 2 are in good agreement with those observed (Figure 8).

Conclusions

Two coordination polymers based on 1, 6‐bis(2‐methyl‐imidazole‐1‐yl)‐hexane (bimh), namely {[Zn3(BTC)2(bimh)]·(bimh)}n (1) and {[Zn(IPA)(bimh)]·(CH3CH2OH)0.5}n (2), were synthesized through hydrothermal reactions. In compound 1, the zinc(II) ions are bridged by BTC3– ligands to form an undulating infinite two‐dimensional (2D) polymeric network. Furthermore, the 2D frameworks are extended to 3D supramolecular frameworks through the Zn–N bonds between 2D nets and bimh molecules, showing a 3D twofold interpenetrating net. In compound 2, the zinc(II) ions are bridged by IPA2– ligands to form one‐dimensional (1D) helical structures, a left‐handed and a right‐handed helical chain, these chains are further connected by bimh via Zn–N coordination bonds to form 2D frameworks. The 2D structures are further assembled into 3D networks through aromatic π–π stacking interactions. Both compounds exhibit acceptable thermal stability and strong photoluminescence at room temperature.

Experimental Section

Materials and Physical Measurements: All commercially available chemicals were of analysis reagent grade and used without purification. The ligand bimh was prepared according to the description in the literature.12 Elemental analyses were made with a Perkin–Elmer 240C automatic analyzer. IR spectra were recorded with a FT‐IR 170 SX (Nicolet) spectrophotometer (4000–400 cm–1 region) by using KBr pellets method. The TG‐DTA measurements were carried out with a NETZSCH TG 209 thermal analyzer from room temperature to 800 °C in a nitrogen atmosphere with a heating rate of 10 K·min–1. X‐ray power diffraction (XRPD) data were recorded with a Rigaku RU200 diffractometer at 60 kV, 300 mA for Cu‐Kα radiation (λ = 1.5406 Å), with a scan speed of 2 K·min–1 and a step size of 0.02° in 2θ. Fluorescence spectra were measured with a Hitachi F‐7000 fluorescence spectrophotometer at room temperature. The luminescent lifetimes were measured with an Edinburgh FLS920 fluorescence spectrometer at room temperature.

Synthesis of 1, 6‐Bis(2‐methyl‐imidazole‐1‐yl)‐hexane (bimh): A mixture of 2‐methyl‐imidazole (4.1 g, 50 mmol) and NaOH (2.0 g, 50 mmol) in tetrahydrofuran (THF) (50 mL), was stirred at 60 °C for 1 h. Afterwards, 1, 6‐dibromohexane (6.1 g, 25 mmol) was added dropwise. The mixture was cooled to room temperature after stirring at 60 °C for another 12 h. After the evaporation of THF, ethyl acetate (15 mL) was added and stirred at 77 °C for 3 h. Finally, the undissolved content was filtered, and the resulting mixture solution was static cooled down in several days. The faint yellow solid was obtained. Yield 55 %. IR (KBr): = 3410 w, 3219 m, 3104 w, 2940 m, 2863 m, 1498 s, 1423 m, 1368 w, 1282 m, 1265 w, 1115 w, 984 m, 757 m, 743 m, 681 w cm–1. 1H NMR (CD3Cl, 400 MHz): δ = 6.90 (s, 2 H), 6.79 (s, 2 H), 3.97–3.83 (t, 4 H), 2.36 (s, 8 H), 1.70–1.73 (s, 3 H), 1.32–1.34 (s, 3 H) ppm.

{[Zn3(BTC)2(bimh)]·(bimh)}n (1): A mixture containing Zn(OAC)2·2H2O (21.9 mg, 0.10 mmol), H3BTC (21.0 mg, 0.10 mmol), bimh (24.6 mg, 0.10 mmol), and water (10 mL) and ethanol (2 mL) was sealed in a Teflon reactor (20 mL), heated at 150 °C for 3 days, and subsequently cooled to room temperature at 10 K·h–1. Colorless crystals of 1 were collected in a 74 % yield [based on Zn(OAC)2·2H2O]. C23H25ZnN4O6: calcd. C 53.19, H 4.81, N 10.79 %; found: C 53.15, H 4.78, N 10.82 %. IR (KBr): = 3445 w, 2925 w, 2857 w, 1636 s, 1577 m, 1409 m, 1332 s, 1807 m, 931 m, 757 m, 714 s cm–1.

{[Zn(IPA)(bimh)]·(CH3CH2OH)0.5}n (2): Compound 2 was synthesized in a procedure analogous to that of 1 except that Zn(OAC)2·2H2O was replaced by Zn(NO3)2·6H2O (29.7 mg, 0.10 mmol), the H3BTC was replaced by H2IPA(16.6 mg, 0.10 mmol). Colorless crystals of 2 were collected in a 56 % yield [based on Zn(NO3)2·6H2O]. C46H52Zn2N8O9: calcd. C 55.66, H 5.24, N 11.29 %; found: C 55.56, H 5.18, N 11.16 %. IR (KBr): = 3482 s, 3136 w, 2936 w, 2862 w, 1617 s, 1576 w, 1362 s, 1148 w, 1008 w, 750 m, 718 m cm–1.

X‐ray Crystallography: Suitable single crystals of 1 and 2 were carefully selected under an optical microscope and glued to thin glass fibers. The diffraction data were collected with a Siemens SMART CCD diffractometer with graphite monochromated Mo‐Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at 298 K. An empirical absorption correction was applied using the SADABS program. The structures were solved by direct methods and refined by full‐matrix least‐squares methods on F2 by using the SHELX‐97 program package.20 The detailed crystallographic data are summarized in Table 2. All non‐hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. Hydrogen atoms of bimh were generated geometrically, and no attempts were made to locate the hydrogen atoms of ethanol, the final formula for the complex was determined by combining single‐crystal structure, elemental microanalysis, and TGA.
