 A mixture of Zn(NO3)2·6H2O (2.0 mmol), NH3·H2O (9.0 mmol) and 5-methylisophthalic acid (4.0 mmol) in 20 mL H2O are stirred for 10 min at room temperature. The mixture is then transferred into a 40 mL, Teflon-lined stainless-steel vessel. The mixture is heated at 200 °C for five days under autogenous pressure. After the reaction mixture has slowly cooled down to room temperature, colorless sheet crystals of Zn-mip are filtered off, washed with distilled water, and dried in air. Yield: ∼55%.
