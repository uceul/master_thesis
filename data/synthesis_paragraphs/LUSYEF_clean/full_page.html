A new NbO type metalâ€“organic framework [Cu2(NPA)(H2O)2](DMF)5.5(H2O)0.5 (ZJU-9, H4NPA = 1,5-naphthalene-m-phthalic acid) with moderately high permanent porosity, open metal sites and appropriate pore sizes was designed and synthesized, exhibiting high storage capacities for both acetylene and methane at room temperature.

1. Introduction

Natural gas (with the main component methane) and acetylene are two potential alternative fuels for future vehicles, thus realization of materials for high methane and acetylene storage is very important.1,2 Among the diverse porous materials for their gas storage, metalâ€“organic frameworks (MOFs) are the most promising ones because of their high porosities, tuneable pore sizes and functional sites.3â€“5 Over the past few decades, a number of MOFs have been explored for their methane and acetylene storage.6,7

MOFs are composed of organic ligands and secondary building units (SBUs), deliberate choices of these two components can lead to MOF structures of certain topologies and porosities.4 Of different types of organic ligands and SBUs, those tetracarboxylic acids containing m-benzenedicarboxylate units and paddleâ€“wheel Cu2(COO)4 clusters are of particularly interesting because the resulting MOFs are robust, open metal sites can be readily generated, pore structures/porosities can be rationally tuned and functional sites can be easily incorporated for their recognition of gas molecules and thus for gas storage.1,4l,8 For example, UTSA-76 with open metal sites and pyramid-ine groups has a very high methane storage capacity of 257 cm3(STP) cmâˆ’3 at room temperature and 65 bar, and a record methane storage working capacity of 197 cm3(STP) cmâˆ’3.9 It has been established that the fused aromatic rings might strengthen their interaction with guest molecules,10 as clearly demonstrated in PCN-14 for its methane storage;11 we thus extend our work to incorporate naphthalene into a new tetracarboxylic organic linker H4NPA (Scheme 1) for its construction of porous MOFs. Herein, we report the first microporous MOF, [Cu2(NPA)(H2O)2](DMF)5.5(H2O)0.5 (ZJU-9; H4NPA = 1,5-naphthaline-m-phthalic acid, ZJU = Zhejiang University) from this new organic linker. Its structure has the well-known NbO type topology. The activated ZJU-9a displays moderately high porosity with the BET (Brunauerâ€“Emmettâ€“Teller) surface area of 2353 m2 gâˆ’1. ZJU-9a exhibits a moderately high methane storage capacity of 217 cm3(STP) cmâˆ’3 at 80 bar and a methane storage working capacity of 159 cm3(STP) cmâˆ’3. Furthermore, ZJU-9a takes up a high amount of acetylene of 193 cm3 gâˆ’1 at room temperature and 1 atm.

3. Results and discussion

We successfully developed a new organic linker H4NPA through Suzuki cross-coupling and hydrolysis reaction. ZJU-9 was prepared by the solvothermal reaction of H4NPA and Cu(NO3)2Â·2.5H2O in a mixture solvent containing N,N-dimethylformamide (DMF), H2O and a small amount of HNO3 at 70 Â°C for 48 h as blue rhombic-prism-shaped crystals. The simulated powder XRD pattern from its single crystal structure matches with the one of the as-synthesized (Fig. S1â€ ), indicating that the phase of the material is pure. The TGA of ZJU-9 shows that ZJU-9 loses the solvent molecules and coordinated water at two steps. The water and DMF molecules were removed with the weight loss of 39.0% (calculated 39.5%) from the room temperature to 240 Â°C. The TGA curve reveals a weight loss of the coordinated water (observed, 3.48%; calculated, 3.53%), and the desolvated framework can be stable up to 290 Â°C (Fig. S2 in ESIâ€ ).

The crystal structure of ZJU-9 is characterized by single-crystal X-ray crystallography. This material crystallizes in the trigonal space group Rm. As typically discovered, ZJU-9 was composed of the paddleâ€“wheel Cu2(COO)4 and NPA linker to have the well-known three-dimensional NbO topology. ZJU-9 has two types of cages along the c axis. The small cage with a spherical-like pore is about 13.5 Ã…, taking into account the van der Waals radii (Fig. 1a). Another one can be visualized as an irregular elongated cage of about 8.0 Ã— 22.6 Ã…, which is surrounded by twelve paddleâ€“wheel SBUs and six organic linkers. There exist triangular windows of 5.0 Ã… along c axis. The windows along the a axes are smaller of about 3.0 Ã— 9.2 Ã… (Fig. 1b). Apparently, once the terminal water molecules are removed, there are accessible open copper sites which have been widely observed in same type of porous MOFs by diffraction studies. The total accessible free volume is 7753 Ã…3, or 65.7% of the unit volume 11788 Ã…3 by PLATON analysis.12

Such MOFs are typically stable, as established before.13 To evaluate the permanent porosity of activated ZJU-9, we carried out N2 sorptionâ€“desorption isotherms at 77 K (Fig. 2). ZJU-9a has open metal sites and moderately high BET surface area, which encouraged us to study its performance for the storage of acetylene and methane. The C2H2 low-pressure adsorption isotherms of ZJU-9a were measured at 273 K and 298 K (0â€“1 atm). As shown in Fig. 3a, The C2H2 storage capacities of ZJU-9a at 1 atm and 273 K is 245 cm3 gâˆ’1, and at 1 atm and 298 K is 193 cm3 gâˆ’1. The storage capacity of ZJU-9a for C2H2 at 298 K is higher than those of NOTT-100 (ref. 15) (148 cm3 gâˆ’1), NOTT-101 (ref. 16) (184 cm3 gâˆ’1), ZJU-7 (ref. 14) (180 cm3 gâˆ’1) and MOF-74-Fe17 (156 cm3 gâˆ’1), and is among those MOF materials with significantly high acetylene storage capacities such as ZJU-5 (ref. 18) (193 cm3 gâˆ’1); HKUST-1 (ref. 15) (201 cm3 gâˆ’1) and CoMOF- 74 (ref. 16) (197 cm3 gâˆ’1). The corresponding adsorption enthalpy for C2H2 is 28.7 kJ molâˆ’1 at zero coverage. This value is slightly lower than HKUST-1 (30.4 kJ molâˆ’1)15 and ZJU-5 (35.8 kJ molâˆ’1),18 while comparable to those in other MOFs with copper paddlewheels, such as ZJU-7 (28.8 kJ molâˆ’1)14 and MOF-505 (25.4 kJ molâˆ’1)15 (Fig. 3a, inset).

4. Conclusions

In conclusion, a new NbO type microporous metalâ€“organic framework ZJU-9 has been successfully synthesized and structurally characterized. The activated ZJU-9a displays moderately high porosity of with the pore volume of 0.887 cm3 gâˆ’1 and the BET surface area of 2353 m2 gâˆ’1. Its moderately high porosity, open copper metal sites and suitable pore spaces has enabled ZJU-9a to store large amount of acetylene and methane which has been established by gas sorption isotherms.
