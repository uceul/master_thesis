A porous sodalite-type MOF based on tetrazolcarboxylate ligands and [Cu4Cl]7+ squares with open metal sites for gas sorption†

Dongmei Wang , Tingting Zhao , Guanghua Li , Qisheng Huo and Yunling Liu *
State Key Laboratory of Inorganic Synthesis and Preparative Chemistry, College of Chemistry, Jilin University, Changchun 130012, P. R. China. E-mail: yunling@jlu.edu.cn; Fax: +86-431-85168624; Tel: +86-431-85168614

Received 12th September 2013 , Accepted 27th November 2013

First published on 3rd December 2013

A porous sodalite-type metal–organic framework based on tetrazolcarboxylate ligands and [Cu4Cl]7+ squares was successfully synthesized, which exhibited permanent porosity and high adsorption abilities of H2, CO2 and organic chemical pollutants.

In recent years, extensive research has been devoted to designing functional metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) with charming properties and potential applications in strategic storage and separation of gases, catalysis, magnetism, luminescence, optical properties and chemical sensing.1–9 As a promising family of MOF materials, zeolitic metal–organic frameworks (ZMOFs) are being vigorously investigated in the areas of catalysis, gas storage and separation.10–12 Up to now, more than 30 kinds of zeolite topologies of ZMOFs have been synthesized,13 including about 23 kinds of ZMOFs with sodalite topology synthesized by using different organic ligands.10–17 To date, there are four different synthetic strategies to construct ZMOFs with sodalite topology: (i) a 4 + 2 strategy by bridging the tetrahedral building units (TBUs) with a longer ditopic organic linker;10a,c,13a,14 (ii) a 4 + 4 strategy by cross-linking the designed inorganic and organic tetrahedral building units through direct coordination interaction;15 (iii) a polyhedral assembly or cage-within-cage strategy;16 and (iv) a face-decorated polyhedral strategy.17 Herein, we present a novel edge-decorated polyhedral strategy to construct the ZMOF with sodalite topology, in which the ligands on the edge of the polyhedral, as compared to having them on the face-decorated polyhedral framework, are quite a significant advance.

The sharp rise in atmospheric CO2 emissions is one of the greatest environmental concerns facing our civilization today. Thus, selective capture of CO2 is of paramount importance for the purification of natural gases and the reduction of CO2 emission. MOFs are of great interest as new materials in this respect.1b The permanent porosity and chemical environment of the internal surfaces of MOFs are crucial for such applications. Several strategies have been introduced to improve the capture of CO2.18 However, the postsynthetic modification (PSM) reveals a powerful tool for anchoring functional groups onto MOFs.19 For instance, Long and co-workers employed a Cu-based MOF bearing coordinatively unsaturated metal sites (CUSs) as a platform to graft amine functional groups to increase the heat of adsorption of CO2,17 and Férey and co-workers also completed a postsynthetic modification of MIL-101.20

Keeping these in mind, our group has successfully synthesised a truncated octahedron-shaped crystal that was designated as JLU-Liu2.‡ It was characterized by single-crystal X-ray diffraction studies and can be formulated as [H3O][(Cu4Cl)(TBA)4(H2O)4]·2DMF·3H2O (see ESI†). Its structure consists of stable chloride-centered square-planar [Cu4Cl]7+ SBUs linked by TBA2− ligands, exhibiting an anionic three-dimensional (3D) framework with large permanent porosity which has been achieved for gas storage and separation media and high absorption abilities of organic chemical pollutants and organic dye molecules.

In the crystal structure of JLU-Liu2§, crystallographically independent Cu2+ with octahedral geometry is coordinated with four ligands at the equatorial positions, while its axial positions are occupied by one terminal water molecule and a Cl− anion. Each TBA2− ligand is connected with four Cu2+ ions via N atoms and/or O atoms from a tetrazolate and/or carboxylate group, respectively (Fig. S5, ESI†). The Cl− is connected with four Cu2+ to form a unique [Cu4Cl]7+ square with the Cl− residing at the center. Thus, the [Cu4Cl]7+ square SBUs via the linear shaped TBA2− ligand generate an anionic 3D framework.

In the structure, tetra-nuclear cluster [Cu4Cl]7+ serves as the vertex, and the TBA2− ligand as the edge; thus, six [Cu4Cl]7+ planars are bridged by twelve TBA2− ligands to produce a sodalite-like cage; then the polyhedron cages are packed via sharing [Cu4Cl]7+ SBUs to extend the structure into a 3D sodalite-type framework (Fig. 1). Topologically, each [Cu4Cl]7+ planar can be simplified as an 8-connected square, and the TBA2− ligand as a linker is connected with the square leading to an 8-connected network with reo topology (Fig. S5, ESI†). It evidently differs from the reported sodalite-type MOFs constructed by [Cu4Cl]7+ SBUs. In the reported MOFs, all the ligands are 3-connected, such as H3BTC,17f,21 H3TTCA,17g H3BTTri,17c H3BTT,17a,e H3TPB-3tz,17d H3TPT-3tz17d (Fig. S6, ESI†), and all of them are located at the face of the sodalite cage. To the best of our knowledge, JLU-Liu2 is the first example of sodalite-like MOFs constructed by [Cu4Cl]7+ squares and a tetrazolcarboxylate mixed ligand, in which the structure has a ligand on the edge.

The TGA measurement for JLU-Jiu2 exhibits high thermal stability as seen in Fig. S4.† In order to analyze the sorption behavior of JLU-Liu2, we executed for N2, H2, CO2 and CH4 four different gas adsorption experiments. The permanent porosity of JLU-Liu2 was confirmed by N2 sorption isotherms at 77 K (Fig. S8, ESI†). The type-I isotherms indicate that it is a microporous material. The BET (Brunauer–Emmett–Teller) and Langmuir surface areas were 2330 and 2545 m2 g−1, respectively. JLU-Liu2 exhibits the highest BET surface area compared to MOFs based on the H2TBA ligand,22 and it is higher than that of most MOFs with [M4Cl]7+ SBUs (Table S2, ESI†).17,21

Low-pressure H2 adsorption isotherms were collected (Fig. S9, ESI†) to explore the potential of JLU-Liu2 as a gas storage material. At 77 K and 760 Torr, a fully reversible uptake of 7.62 mmol g−1 (1.54 wt%) is apparent, which is more than the uptake 7.36 mmol g−1 observed for Cu-BTC under the same conditions.17f,21 The isosteric heat of adsorption of 7.37 kJ mol−1 is considerably higher than that of Cu-BTTri (6.1 kJ mol−1),17c Cu-BTC (6.83 kJ mol−1),21 or Cu-TTCA (3.38 kJ mol−1)17g and comparable to that of Cu-TPT (7.6 kJ mol−1)17d (Fig. S9, Table S2, ESI†). Significantly, the H2 sorption data are higher than that of most MOFs without open metal sites. It has been proved that the presence of CUSs could increase the affinity for binding H2. Therefore, open metal sites allow strong interactions with trapped gases.

On further investigation of this aspect, JLU-Liu2 exhibited a significant uptake capacity for CO2. The CO2 uptakes at 273 K and 760 Torr and at 298 K and 760 Torr reached 3.53 mmol g−1 and 1.86 mmol g−1, respectively (Fig. 2). Postsynthesis based on the interaction between ethylenediamine (en) and exposed CuII sites was carried out to investigate the isosteric heat of CO2 adsorption Qst (Fig. S16, ESI†). For JLU-Liu2, Qst remains at a mean value of 26 kJ mol−1, and it has a significant increase for JLU-Liu2-en, especially at very low loadings. At zero coverage, a maximum value of 45 kJ mol−1 occurs, indicating a strong interaction of CO2 with the functionalized amine. The high binding energy for CO2 in JLU-Liu2-en at low coverage indicated that chemisorption interaction and alkylamine-functionalization indeed can improve the adsorption enthalpy of CO2.

JLU-Liu2 can only adsorb a little CH4 (Fig. S11†), and the CO2/CH4 selectivity is 9:1 at 273 K and 4:1 at 298 K calculated by using the initial slopes of CO2 and CH4 adsorption isotherms (Fig. S12†).

Organic vapor adsorption experiments on hydrocarbons of JLU-Liu2 are shown in Fig. S17, ESI,† featuring typical type-I isotherms. The amounts adsorbed for benzene, toluene, p-xylene, methanol and cyclohexane are 65.5, 57.7, 40.3, 44.2, and 21.7 wt%, respectively. Significantly, the organic vapor sorption capacity of JLU-Liu2 is superior to that of other MOF materials.23 This result was attributed to the steric effect for organic vapor molecules with a proper geometry to fit in the pore shapes and the large pore volume of the framework. The excellent sorption performance of JLU-Liu2 promised a great potential application in the environment. Furthermore, the dye-adsorption experiment was measured (Fig. S18†); JLU-Liu2-RhB (Rhodamine B) presents the luminescent property, which testifies the accessibility of the porosity of JLU-Liu2.

The temperature dependent magnetic property of JLU-Liu2 is measured, and the temperature dependence of the χMT value is shown in Fig. S19.† Fitting this curve to the Curie–Weiss law gives a Curie constant C = 5.259 cm3 mol−1 K and Weiss temperature θ = −174.46 K. The effective magnetic moment μeff = (8/4*C)1/2 is 3.243μB, consistent with four Cu(II) sites per formula unit.24 The negative Weiss temperature indicates the existence of antiferromagnetic interactions.

In conclusion, we have successfully synthesized a porous sodalite-like ZMOF which exhibits higher adsorption capacities of multiple gases and organic chemical pollutants. Significantly, CUSs were grafted by amine molecules that offer a highly selective affinity for CO2 binding and the en-functionalized framework indicates a higher adsorption enthalpy of CO2. Future efforts will focus on materials with exposed metal sites to enhance the capacity for hydrogen storage and the grafting of alkylamine to improve the adsorption enthalpy of CO2.
