A novel 3-D chiral coordination polymer: hydrothermal synthesis and structural characterization of [Co3(μ3-OH)(μ5-btc)(μ6-Hbtc)(H2O)3·6H2O]n

Author links open overlay panelZheng-LiangLüWeiChenJi-QingXuLi-JuanZhangCheng-LingPanTie-GangWang
https://doi.org/10.1016/S1387-7003(02)00751-7Get rights and content

Abstract

A chiral 3-D open-framework coordination polymer [Co3(μ3-OH)(μ5-btc)(μ6-Hbtc)(H2O)3·6H2O]n was successfully obtained from the mixture of Co(CH3COO)2·4H2O, H3btc (where H3btc=benzene-1,2,4-tricarboxylic acid), NaOH and H2O under hydrothermal condition, which contains a novel and unprecedented coordination mode of btc (see Scheme 1

1. Introduction

Potential applications of microporous organic/inorganic hybrid materials are found in catalysis, chemical absorption, magnetism, electrical conductivity and molecular recognition [1], [2], [3], [4]. So, it is of the utmost importance to modify the structures and design new materials. A synthetic strategy widely used in this area is by coordination of metal ions to versatile ligands those are attractive for formation of building blocks of coordination polymers and supramolecular structures, for these ligands promise robust and stable networks with good electronic communication between the metal centers and determine the shape and size of the pores. Presently, there are considerable efforts devoted to the construction of metal–polycarboxylate supramolecular networks self-assembled by covalent, hydrogen bond or other molecular interaction. So far, many topologically promising architectures have been constructed, where polycarboxylates act as building blocks. Specially, many coordination polymers with bta(=benzene-1,2,4,5-tetracarboxylate) [5], [6], tma(=benzene-1,3,5-tricarboxylate) [7] and ta(=benzene-1,4-dicarboxylate) [8] as bridging ligands have been extensively synthesized and reported, but those with btc are very few. As compared to bta, tma and ta, btc possesses higher asymmetry, which makes it possible to synthesize chiral, spiral or high asymmetric molecules or supramolecules. To our knowledge, the only one example of 2-D metal-organic coordination polymer with btc as bridging ligands are found in the literature [9], while the 3-D network coordination polymer with btc has not yet been reported. In this paper, we report the synthesis and structure of a unique 3-D open-framework coordination polymer containing btc, [Co3(μ3-OH)(μ5-btc)(μ6-Hbtc)(H2O)3·6H2O]n 1.

2. Experimental

2.1. Material and physical measurement

The synthesis was conducted using reagent grade Co(CH3COO)2·4H2O, H3btc and NaOH from commercial sources without further purification. Infrared spectrum of the title compound was recorded with Perkin–Elmer SPOECTRUM ONE FT-IR(CsI) spectrophotometer in the 225–4000cm−1 region using a powdered sample on a KBr plate. The elemental analysis was determined using Perkin–Elmer 2400 elemental analyzer. Inductively coupled plasma (ICP) analysis was performed on a Perkin–Elmer Optima 3300DV spectrometer.

2.2. Synthesis of compound 1

A mixture of Co(CH3COO)2·4H2O (0.196 g, 0.787 mmol), H3btc (0.188 g, 0.895 mmol), NaOH (0.100 g, 2.50 mmol) and H2O (21 ml) was stirred for 1 h at room temperature, then the reaction mixture was adjusted to pH 7.0 with the solution of NaOH, and sealed in a 30 ml stainless-steel reactor with Teflon inner, and heated at 160 °C for 72 h under autogenous pressure. After cooling to ambient temperature, the purple tetragonal prismatic crystals were collected by filtration and characterized by crystallographic analysis. Yield: 46% (based on Co). Anal. calcd. For C18H26Co3O22: Co, 22.99; C, 28.12; H, 3.12%. Found: Co, 23.01; C, 28.26; H, 3.09%.

2.3. X-ray crystal structure determination

Crystal data and structure refinement details for 1 are given in Table 1. Selected bond lengths and angles for 1 are given in Table 2. Data were collected with Mo-Kα radation (λ=0.71073 Å) using a Rigaku R-AXIS RAPID diffractometer. The sample selected for investigation has dimensions of 0.189×0.177×0.053mm3. A total of 5446 reflections measured were all indepented reflections (Rint=0.0404). The structure was solved using direct method with SHELXTL program [10] and refined by full-matrix least-squared techniques. The non-hydrogen atoms were assigned anisotropic displacement parameters in the refinement. The hydrogen atoms were treated using a riding model. The structure was then refined on F2 using SHELXL-97. The final R value (on F) for the 4654 observable reflections with I>2σ(I) was 0.0273.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Synthesis

Compound 1 containing protonated carboxylate coordinated to Co atom was synthesized under pH 7.0 condition. We found that under basic condition of pH > 9.0 prepared coordination polymers of late transition metal with polycarboxylate as bridging ligand generally do not contain protonated carboxylate.

3.2. IR spectra

The IR spectrum of 1 exhibits a strong band at 3432cm−1 assigned to ν(H2O) and feature peak at 2850cm−1 attributed to characteristic of ν(COOH) [11]. While absorptions for the antisymmetric stretching vibrations νas(COO−) of the carbonyls in carboxylates appear between 1618 and 1582cm−1, symmetric stretching vibrations νs(COO−) were observed in the range 1421–1395cm−1, Δ(νas(COO−)−νs(COO−))<200cm−1, indicating that all carboxylate groups in compound 1 were coordinated to cobalt [12], [13].

3.3. Structure

The crystal structure of compound 1 reveals the trimetallic center nature and each Co atom is a chiral center. As shown in Fig. 1, the asymmetric unit of compound 1 consists of one μ3-OH, three cobalt atoms, three coordinated water molecules and two btc ligands. The local coordination geometry around each cobalt atom center can be described as a slightly distorted octahedron by six oxygen atoms, one from μ3-OH, one from coordinated water molecule, others from carboxylates of btc. In fact, the four oxygen atoms from btc are in different surroundings, and thus, together with μ3-OH and coordinated H2O molecule, make each Co atom become a chiral center. In Co(1) coordination octahedron, for example, O(5B) belonging to position-1 carboxylate of μ5-btc is coordinated to two Co atoms (Co(1) and Co(2)), O(7A) being part of position-4 carboxylate of μ6-btc is coordinated only to Co(1) atom, whereas O(10) belonging to position-2 carboxylate of μ6-btc and O(12C) being part of position-1 carboxylate of μ6-btc are coordinated to one Co(1) atom and two Co atoms (Co(1) and Co(3)), respectively. Co(2) and Co(3) chiral centers can be similarly described. The coordination octahedron of Co(1) is connected with those of Co(2) and Co(3) by edge-shared, whereas those of Co(2) and Co(3) are linked with each other through sharing corner. The μ3-OH and three Co atoms form a tetrahedron with similar Co–O distances (2.029, 2.037 and 2.082 Å) and 3.097, 3.104 and 3.595 Å of Co⋯Co distances, indicating the lack of obvious metal–metal interaction. It can be seen that the distances of Co(1)–Co(2)(3.097 Å) and Co(1)–Co(3)(3.104 Å) are similar, while that of Co(2)–Co(3)(3.595 Å) is much longer than those of Co(1)–Co(2) and Co(1)–Co(3). The reason producing this situation is probably that there are two bridging ligands, a μ2-O atom and a μ2-COO group from carboxylates, besides μ3-OH, between Co(1) and Co(2), and Co(1) and Co(3), respectively, whereas there is only a μ2-COO group between Co(2) and Co(3) besides μ3-OH. Co–O(from water) bond distances vary from 2.100 to 2.235 Å and those (from btc) is between 2.039 and 2.235 Å, In an asymmetric unit of 1, it is noteworthy that two btc ligands display unprecedented μ5- and μ6-coordination modes, respectively, which is comparatively rare. IR spectrum of 1 shows that there exists undeprotonated carboxyl group coordinated to Co atom. Carboxyl groups at site-4 of μ5- and μ6-btc link with two Co atoms by the same mode, respectively. The relevant bond lengths are: Co(1)–O(7A), 2.058 Å; Co(2)–O(8A), 2.152 Å; Co(2)–O(1), 2.038 Å and Co(3)–O(2), 2.039 Å, in which the bond length of Co(2)–O(8A) is much longer than those of other three bonds. Thus H atom of protonated carboxyl group is considered to be bonded to O(8A) atom. This phenomenon of metal–oxygen bond turning longer can be also found in other compounds in containing protonated carboxyl group linking with metal atoms [14].

A polyhedral diagram of the compound 1 alone b axis and a packing diagram of alone a axis are given in Fig. 2, Fig. 3, respectively. There are many types of channels in crystals of compound 1 possessing 3-D structure. These channels include, along b axis, channels constructed of 36-membered ring with 17.419×6.963Å2 of cross-section, of 20-membered ring with 8.089×5.895Å2 of cross-section and of 18-membered ring with 6.867×5.011Å2 of cross-section, and along a axis those constructed of 20-membered rings with 8.854×3.420Å2 of cross-section. Guest water molecules are filled in the channels. Those guest water molecules are mostly disordered. The microporous character of present structure is enhanced by the above channels.

Due to reaction mechanism under hydrothermal condition having not been understood, attempts to synthesize analogs of compound 1 with H3btc and Ni(CH3COO)2·4H2O or Mn(CH3COO)2·4H2O have not been successful using the similar method.
