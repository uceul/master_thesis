Spontaneous asymmetric crystallization of a quartz-type framework from achiral precursors†

Wenxu Zheng ab, Yongqin Wei a, Xueying Xiao ab and Kechen Wu *a
aState Key Laboratory of Structure Chemistry, Fujian Institute of Research on the Structure of Matter, Chinese Academy of Sciences, Fuzhou, Fujian 350002, P. R. China. E-mail: wkc@fjirsm.ac.cn; Fax: (+86)-591-83792932; Tel: (+86)-591-83792600
bGraduate School of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing 100039, P. R. China

First published on 3rd February 2012

An enantioenriched three-dimensional quartz-type framework {[Ni1.5(tzdc)(H2O)3]·3H2O}n (H3tzdc = 4H-1,2,4-triazole-3,5-dicarboxylic acid) possessing chiral channels is obtained through spontaneous asymmetric crystallization without any enantiopure additive.

Chiral molecules (e.g., L-amino acids or D-sugars) and 1-D polymeric helices (e.g., right-handed DNA) are ubiquitous in our living world, however, three-dimensional chiral frameworks are less common.1 Although in nature many inorganic minerals such as perovskite,2 PtS,3feldspar4 and rutile5 possess interesting structures and topologies, they are not chiral solids. A common inorganic mineral with chiral open framework is quartz with a four-connected net. One unique feature in quartz is the presence of both helicity and chirality, however, unlike to DNA, quartz does not possess chiral building block. Therefore, quartz-type topology is intrinsically chiral topology.6 Tremendous research efforts have been devoted to the development of quartz-type materials not only for their intriguing structure but also for their potential applications in enantioselective catalysis, separation and so on.7 In nature, quartz as well as its isostructural mineral berlinite (AlPO4) which shares a quartz-type topology, is racemic existing in both D- and L-forms.8 Concerning to the industrial application, it is important to prepare the quartz-type product with considerable enantiomeric excess. Several examples in the field of metal–organic coordination polymers show the 3-D frameworks with quartz-type topology, however, with few exceptions,6 most of the products tend to be racemic.9

Homochiral (or enantioenriched) metal–organic coordination polymers have attracted increasing attention because of their potential applications in enantioselective separation and asymmetric catalysis.10 The driving force in asymmetric crystallization can be the conformation of enantiopure organic ligand, induction of enantiopure chiral agent or solvent, even influence of chiral physical environment such as polarized light.11 Without any chiral source, the product tends to be a racemic conglomerate, unless, spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking occurs, in which case the enantiomeric excess (ee) is greatly amplified. Since the first experimental realizations reported by Kondepudi et al.,12 in which NaClO3 can crystallize in homochiral form from achiral precursors through a chiral-symmetry-breaking transition, much attention has been paid on this unusual phenomenon. Actually, the spontaneous symmetry breaking phenomenon can also be found in our living world,13 for example, ribose, one of the components of 1-D biopolymer, is always the D-form, leading the DNA with definite right-handedness. Lacking of convincing evidence leads the origin of the phenomenon of asymmetry in living systems to be a great puzzle.14 A combination of chiral crystallization and formation of helical polymers with preferred chiral conformational structure may be the key to this question.15 Thus, it is of great interest to generate quartz-type 3-D frameworks through spontaneous symmetry breaking not only because enantioenriched quartz-type materials could be generated through a new strategy but also because this could lead to a better understanding of the origin of biohomochirality in our living world.

In this work, for the first time we have prepared a novel enantioenriched coordination polymer with 3-D quartz-type framework, namely, {Ni1.5(tzdc)(H2O)3}·3H2O (1, H3tzdc = 4H-1,2,4-triazole-3,5-dicarboxylic acid), through spontaneous symmetry breaking from achiral precursors without any enantiopure addictive. This result suggests that symmetry breaking could be driven by intrinsic chiral quartz topology without any chiral building block.

Green block crystals of compound 1 were simply obtained by reacting achiral organic ligand H3tzdc with nickel salt under hydrothermal conditions.‡ All major peaks of experimental powder X-ray pattern (PXRD) of compound 1 match quite well with that of the simulated PXRD, indicating reasonable crystalline phase purity (Fig. S1, ESI†). Compound 1§ crystallizes in the enantiomorphic space groupP3121 with one tzdc3− ion, three coordination water molecules, two nickel(II) ions and three lattice water molecules in its asymmetric unit (Fig. S2†). The Ni1 sites on a special position (2-axis) and the local coordination geometry of the six-coordinated Ni1 center is a slightly distorted octahedron bonded by two nitrogen atoms and four oxygen atoms from four individual tzdc3− ligands, while the octahedral coordination sphere of six-coordinated Ni2 is completed by two nitrogen atoms, one μ2-bridging oxygen atom from the carboxylate group and three terminal water molecules. The Ni–O and Ni–N bond lengths fall in the range of 2.022(5)–2.153(3) Å and 1.999(6)–2.069(6) Å, respectively, which are comparable to those of other nickel(II) complexes reported previously. The neighboring Ni2+ ions are bridged by two nitrogen atoms of a triazole ring and a μ2-bridging oxygen atom of the carboxylate group, forming trinuclear secondary building units (SBUs), Ni3N4O2. The distance of Ni1–Ni2 is 3.620 Å and the angle of Ni1–O1A–Ni2 is 117.12(19)° (symmetry code: A 1 + y, x, −z). As shown in Fig. 1, every trinuclear SBU is connected by four tzdc3− ligands, which acts as μ2-bridging ligands. Thus, the trinuclear SBUs are linked by ligand tzdc3− to form a 3-D framework (Fig. 2b). In the structure of compound 1, the trinuclear SBUs replace the Si atoms in the quartz structure, and the ligand tzdc3− replace the O atoms. The 3-D framework is characteristic of a 6482-net representing the α-quartz type topology (Fig. S3,†Fig. 2d). In contrast to the silica prototype, compound 1 also contains a 3-fold screw axis in the c direction, and the trinuclear SBUs along this axis produce a right-handed helix (P-helix) (Fig. 2a). The helicity of compound 1 is comparable to DNA, because both of them adopt the P-helix form. The six P-helices are put together by sharing the trinuclear SBUs, forming a chiral channel with double left-handed helices along the 65 screw axis (Fig. 2c). The hexagonal chiral channel with diameter of 3.8 Å on the basis of relevant van der Waals radius is filled with guest water molecules. To confirm the asymmetric crystallization, 10 crystals were randomly picked out from the same batch and analyzed using single-crystal X-ray diffraction (Table S1†). The nearly zero Flack parameter of each refinement indicated that 9 crystals belong to chiral trigonal P3121 space group, while only one crystal adopts the opposite P3221 space group. This result suggested that the prepared bulk product is enantioenriched.

In order to better evaluate the homochirality and asymmetric crystallization of 1, circular dichroism (CD) spectra (Fig. 3) of bulk samples from 5 parallel syntheses were measured in the solid state using MOS-405 spectropolarimeter. The CD spectra exhibit positive CD signal at about 260 nm for all the 5 bulk samples. CD spectra of each whole batch from these 5 parallel products were also measured in the solid-state, and exhibit positive signal at about 260 nm (Fig. S4, ESI†). These result confirmed the single-crystal analyses, providing an additional evidence for the asymmetric crystallization.

To the best of our knowledge, compound 1 represents the first case where enantioenriched quartz-type framework is generated through spontaneous symmetry breaking process without any enantiopure additive. It is a novel phenomenon that the homochiral material 1 with intrinsically chiral quartz topology can be generated from achiral source without any enantiopure additive, as currently such materials are usually obtained by using enantiopure ligands.5 Because there are no driving force for symmetric breaking crystallization, the spontaneous asymmetric crystallization presented here is unexpected. The origin of the chirality of compound 1 is an inevitable question. Mao et al. demonstrated that the conformation of an organic ligand could be regarded as an enantiopure building unit displaying molecular chirality, which led to the chiral generation in each single crystal.16 Lu et al. have shown that the origin of chirality is closely associated to the asymmetric ligand, the distorted coordination geometry, and the twisted diamond topology.17 There are chiral building blocks in both of the two example. However, there is no chiral building block in compound 1 including the trinuclear SBU and organic ligand. Thus, the conformation of helix and the intrinsic quartz topology both play crucial roles in the chiral generation and symmetry breaking. The 31 [001] helix constructed by trinuclear SBUs and organic ligands, corresponding to the –O–Si–O– helix in quartz, has been spontaneously resolved into two independent helix with P-form or M-form. These helices with the same form are organized to adopt intrinsic quartz topology, whose chirality arises from the 31 [001] screw axes of the same handedness propagating in all crystallographically equivalent directions. Thus, the chirality generation is bound up with the conformation of helix and the quartz topology.

After answering the question about the chiral generation, the next step is to find the reason for the observed enantiomeric excess in the bulk sample. We have checked all starting materials, and found that none of them has optical activity. According to other known examples (e.g.NaClO3), this spontaneous asymmetric crystallization phenomenon can be regarded as a result of chiral autocatalysis, by which the “first crystal” rapidly clones itself through secondary nucleation to dominate the bulk system.11

In conclusion, we have prepared an enantioenriched quartz-type framework with a chiral channel through an unusual spontaneous asymmetric crystallization. The enantiomeric nature of the bulk material was confirmed by both single crystal X-ray diffraction analyses and circular dichroism (CD) spectra. The exploration of such quartz-type material and the relationship between spontaneous asymmetry breaking and helicity of the quartz net could lead to new insights into the generation of helicity and homochirality in both solid-state materials and biopolymers.
