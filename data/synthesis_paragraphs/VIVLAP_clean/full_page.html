Abstract

The TbIII compound [Tb(tci)(H2O)]n¬∑n(DMF) (1) [H3tci = tri(2‚Äêcarboxythyl)isocyanurate, DMF = N,N‚Ä≤‚Äêdimethylformamide] was synthesized by the reaction of terbium oxide, H3tci, and two drops of concentrated nitric acid in the presence of DMF and H2O. Single crystal X‚Äêray analysis reveals that it features a three‚Äêdimensional (3D) framework based on infinite ‚ÄìTb‚ÄìCOO‚ÄìTb‚Äì chains. The tci ligand in 1 links six different TbIII ions with its two carboxylate groups in Œº2‚ÄêŒ∫1O;Œ∫2O,O‚Ä≤ mode and the third in Œº2‚ÄêŒ∫1O;Œ∫1O‚Ä≤ mode. Thermal analysis reveals that it remains high thermal stability until 390 ¬∞C. Luminescence investigation shows that it emits characteristic green light of TbIII ions.

1 Introduction

Recently, lanthanide‚Äêbased coordination polymers have attracted considerable attentions owing to their interest magnetism and luminescent properties.1‚Äì4 Comparing with transition metal ions, lanthanide ions have higher affinity to oxygen atoms. Therefore, the judicious selection of organic ligands will play a key role in the synthesis of lanthanide‚Äêbased coordination polymers. Among the various multicarboxylate ligands, aromatic polycarboxylate ligands (such as terephthalic acid, isophthalic acid, 1,3,5‚Äêbenzenetricarboxylic acid, 1,2,4,5‚Äêbenzenetetracarboxylic acid and so on) have been widely used to construct various lanthanide‚Äêbased compounds, which exhibit interesting luminescent and magnetism properties.5‚Äì7 As flexible multidentate carboxylate ligands, the tripodal symmetrical tris(2‚Äêcarboxythyl)isocyanuric acid (H3tci) ligand (Scheme 1) is particularly interesting owing to its flexible ‚ÄìCH2‚ÄìCH2‚Äì motifs of the carboxylate arms allowing for diversity in coordination modes of the carboxylate groups, and, therefore, the final framework. In the past few years, a few tci‚Äêbased coordination polymers have been documented. However, tci‚Äêbased compounds involving lanthanide ions are less reported.8‚Äì10

Considering the encouraging photoluminescence properties with high quantum yields of lanthanide‚Äêbased compounds, we selected TbIII ions to assemble with H3tci, obtaining a new coordination polymer, namely [Tb(tci)(H2O)]n¬∑n(DMF) (1) [H3tci = tri(2‚Äêcarboxyethyl)isocyanurate, DMF = N,N‚Ä≤‚Äêdimethylformamide]. Single crystal X‚Äêray structural analysis reveals that it features a three‚Äêdimensional (3D) framework based on infinite ‚ÄìTb‚ÄìCOO‚ÄìTb‚Äì chains.

2.2. Synthesis of [Tb(tci)(H2O)]n¬∑n(DMF) (1)

A mixture of Tb4O7 (0.060 g, 0.05 mmol) and H3tci (0.069 g, 0.20 mmol) was placed in a small via. Afterwards, DMF (2 mL), H2O (2 mL), and 2 drops concentrated nitric acid were added. The resulting mixture was kept at 110 ¬∞C for 3 d. After being slowly cooled to the room temperature, colorless prism crystals of 1 were isolated. Yield: 65‚Äâ% based on H3tci. 3 Result and Discussion

3.1. Crystal Structure Description of Compound 1

Single crystal X‚Äêray diffraction analysis reveals that compound 1 crystallizes in the triclinic space group P and exhibits a 3D framework. The asymmetric unit of 1 consists of one TbIII ions, one tci ligand, one coordinated water molecule, and one disordered DMF molecule. As shown in Figure 1, each TbIII ion is nine‚Äêcoordinate by eight carboxylate oxygen atoms from six individual tci ligands and one coordinated water molecule. The coordination arrangement of this TbIII ion can be described as a distorted tri‚Äêcapped trigonal prism with the O‚ÄìTb‚ÄìO bond angles ranging from 50.88(11) to 147.14(11)¬∞ and Tb‚ÄìO bond lengths ranging from 2.283(3) to 2.630(4) √Ö. Two adjacent TbIII ions are linked together through four carboxylate groups to form dinuclear units with the Tb¬∑¬∑¬∑Tb distance being 3.9459(5) √Ö. Individual dinuclear units are further linked by two carboxylate groups into an infinite ‚ÄìTb‚ÄìCOO‚ÄìTb‚Äì chain (Figure 2a). Each infinite chain is further linked to six neighboring chains by the core rings of the tci ligands with its flexible arms, forming an extended 3D framework with 1D channels viewing along crystallographical a axis (Figure 2b). These 1D channels are occupied by highly disordered DMF molecules, which are removed by the PLATON/SQUEEZE. In 1, the tci ligand links six different TbIII ions with its two carboxylate groups in Œº2‚ÄêŒ∫1O;Œ∫2O,O‚Ä≤ mode and the third in Œº2‚ÄêŒ∫1O;Œ∫1O‚Ä≤ mode.

3.2. IR Spectra

The infrared spectrum of 1 displays characteristic bands of the carboxylic groups in the range of 1560‚Äì1687 and 1330‚Äì1458 cm‚Äì1, which can be attributed to asymmetric vibrations and symmetric vibrations, respectively. The presence of the characteristic band attributed to the O‚ÄìH stretching vibration of the coordinated water molecule is observed at 3463 cm‚Äì1.

3.3. Thermal Analysis

In order to investigate the thermal stability of this compound, thermal analysis of 1 was performed in a nitrogen atmosphere with a heating rate of 10 ¬∞C¬∑min‚Äì1 in the temperature range of 30‚Äì900 ¬∞C. As shown in Figure 3, the weight loss of 15.30‚Äâ% occurred in the range of 70‚Äì160 ¬∞C may be attributed to the loss of one coordinated water molecules and one free DMF molecule per unit cell (calcd: 15.22‚Äâ%). The skeleton of this compound can be stable up to 390 ¬∞C. After that the skeleton of it quickly collapse that may be caused by the decomposition of the organic ligands.

3.4. Luminescent Properties

Considering the excellent luminescent performance of TbIII compound, the luminescent property of compound 1 was investigated in the solid‚Äêstate at room temperature. Compound 1 emits green fluorescence and displays the characteristic emission of TbIII ions upon excitation of 285 nm. As shown in Figure 4a, two intense emission bands at 490 and 543 nm are assigned to the electronic transitions from the excited state 5D4 to the multiplets 7F6 and 7F5, and two weak emission bands at 586 nm and 621nm originate from 5D4‚Üí7F4 and 5D4‚Üí7F3. The corresponding decay lifetime is 1138.2 Œºs and the photoluminescence efficiency is 62.61‚Äâ% for compound 1 (Figure 4b), which are all comparable to other related TbIII compounds with the photoluminescence efficiency of 63.68‚Äâ%, 64‚Äâ%, respectively.14‚Äì16

4 Conclusions

A new TbIII compound was successfully synthesized under solvothermal conditions. Compound 1 contains an infinite ‚ÄìTb‚ÄìCOO‚ÄìTb‚Äì chain as building block. Each infinite chain is further linked to six neighboring chains via the core rings of the tci ligands with its flexible arms, forming an extended 3D framework. Thermal analysis reveals that it remains high thermal stability until 390 ¬∞C. Luminescence investigation shows that it emits characteristic green light of TbIII ions.
