Luminescent cadmium dimethylsuccinate and dimethylglutarate coordination polymers self-assembled in the presence of flexible dipyridylamide ligands with capability for nitrobenzene detection

Abstract

Hydrothermal reaction of cadmium nitrate, a dimethyl-substituted aliphatic α,ω-carboxylic acid, and a flexible dipyridylamide ligand afforded four crystalline coordination polymers whose dimensionality and topology depended on the nitrogen donor disposition within the dipyridyl subunit and the length of the aliphatic chain subunit. These new crystalline phases were structurally characterized by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. {[Cd(dmg)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·H2O}n (1, dmg = 2,2-dimethylglutarate, 3-pmna = 3-pyridylmethylnicotinamide) manifests a simple (4,4) grid structure. {[Cd(dms)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·4.5H2O}n (2, dms = 2,2-dimethylsuccinate) displays a 1D coordination polymer ladder motif with looped pairs of struts. {[Cd(dms)(4-pmina)]·2.5H2O}n (3, 4-pmina = 4-pyridylmethylisonicotinamide) shows a 3,5-connected 2-fold interpenetrated binodal net with (426)(426583) topology, wherein the 4-pmina ligands serve as 3-connected nodes. Higher temperature synthetic regimes afforded {[Cd4(dms)4(H2O)4]·H2O}n (4) which manifests a 66 diamondoid topology based on [Cd4(dms)4(H2O)4]n subunits with embedded cuboid {Cd4O4} tetranuclear clusters. Thermal and luminescent properties of these new materials are also presented. The 3-D phase 3 showed potential as a detector for nitrobenzene.

1. Introduction

For the past two decades, there has been a significant basic research focus on the synthesis and structure of divalent metal coordination polymer solids containing dicarboxylate and dipyridyl ligands. These solid state materials show potential as substrates for gas storage [1], shape-selective molecular separations [2], ion exchange [3], heterogeneous catalysis [4], and more recently as detectors for nitroaromatic residues of explosive compounds [5]. Additionally, the aesthetic attractiveness of these materials’ underlying geometric topologies continues to spur exploratory synthesis [6]. Coordination polymers based on divalent cadmium can exhibit useful optical properties, imparted by the closed shell d10 electronic configuration of this metal ion. The full d shell electron population results in forbidden visible light d–d transitions, providing the necessary transparent visible light spectral window required for nitroaromatics sensing [7] or non-linear optical applications [8].

Structural diversity among dicarboxylate/dipyridyl cadmium-based coordination polymers is wide in scope because of the absence of crystal field stabilization and resultant coordination geometry flexibility [9], [10], [11], [12], [13], [14]. Coordination polymer structure direction during self-assembly is thereby finely tuned by the synergistic effects of dipyridyl ligand donor disposition and carboxylate binding mode. Alteration of chain length and substituent-based steric bulk of aliphatic dicarboxylate ligands can also enhance the topological scope of these cadmium-containing coordination polymers [12], [13]. The conformational fluxionality of the aliphatic chains of these ligands instills an active response to specific supramolecular environments, while bulky substituents can restrict access to some conformations or potential binding modes during coordination polymer self-assembly.

Both {[Cd(suc)(bpy)]·0.25H2O}n (suc = succinate, bpy = 4,4′-bipyridine) and {[Cd(suc)(dpa)]·H2O}n (dpa = 4,4′-dipyridylamine) display dimer-based 2-fold interpenetrated 41263 primitive cubic-type nets [9], [10], as does [Cd(glu)(dpa)]n [11]. The related dipyridylamide ligand 4-pyridylisonicotinamide (4-pina) also afforded a 3D dimer-based net in [Cd(suc)(4-pina)]n, but this complex exhibits a rarer 446108 mab self-penetrated topology [12]. The acentric coordination polymer {[Cd(dms)(bpy)(H2O)2]·H2O}n (dms = 2,2-dimethylsuccinate, Scheme 1) exhibits four-fold 1D homochiral right-handed helical [Cd(dms)]n chain motifs conjoined through bpy tethers to construct a 658 cds 3D network [13]. [Cd(dms)(dpa)]n manifests acentric doubly interpenetrated (4,4) rhomboid grid layers [14]. These results indicate that greater steric bulk within the dicarboxylate component can alter 3D topology and can even afford a reduction in coordination polymer dimensionality.

The seldom-used but easily accessible hydrogen-bonding capable dipyridylamide ligand 3-pyridylmethylnicotinamide (3-pmna, Scheme 1) afforded altered topologies due to both its longer shape and kinked donor disposition [15], [16], [17], [18]. Different conformations and through-ligand distances are accessible for 3-pmna ligands when locked in a coordination polymer network, including syn or anti conformations as well as intermediate twisted conformations. The N⋯C⋯C⋯N Ψ torsion angle shown in Scheme 2 can be used to identify the 3-pmna conformation, with the idealized syn conformation having a values of Ψ = 0°. {[Cd2(suc)2(3-pmna)(H2O)2]·3H2O}n possesses {Cd2O2} dimer-based [Cd(suc)]n layers strutted by a twisted anti conformation 3-pmna ligands with Ψ = 140.3° into a 2D 5-connected sandwich structure with 4862 topology [15]. {[Cd(glu)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·3H2O}n manifests a rippled (4,4) 2D grid topology with intermediate conformation (Ψ = 94.5°) 3-pmna tethers [15]. A recently published system of 3-pmna-containing copper coordination polymers has revealed the impact of alkyl group substitution in influencing topology [16]. {[Cu2(glu)2(3-pmna)]·H2O}n contains dimer-containing [Cu2(glu)2]n chain motifs, but retains a 1-D dimensionality due to bridging by perfectly syn-conformation 3-pmna ligands with Ψ = 0°. {[Cu(2-mglu)(3-pmna)]n (2-mglu = 2-methylglutarate) features anti–syn bridged {CuOCO}n chains and a 3,5-connected (3.52)(3253647) 2D topology influenced by syn conformation (Ψ = 21.6°) 3-pmna dipodal tethers.

The isomeric dipyridylamide 4-pyridylmethylisonicotinamide (4-pmina, Scheme 1), with a more linear donor disposition, has not seen much employment in the construction of coordination polymers to date. Either the 3-pmna or 4-pmina ligand can engage in both supramolecular hydrogen bonding donating and accepting mechanisms due to their central amide moieties, unlike the kinked dpa or rigid-rod bpy linkers, affording a plethora of structure-directing capabilities.

In this contribution we report the synthesis, single-crystal structural characterization, luminescent properties, and thermal degradation behavior of four new coordination polymers prepared in the presence of substituted aliphatic dicarboxylate and dipyridylamide ligands: {[Cd(dmg)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·H2O}n (1, dmg = 2,2-dimethylglutarate), {[Cd(dms)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·4.5H2O}n (2), {[Cd(dms)(4-pmina)]·2.5H2O}n (3), and {[Cd4(dms)4(H2O)4]·H2O}n (4). These phases show a very significant change in coordination polymer dimensionality and topology depending on the nitrogen donor disposition if the dipyridylamide ligand is present, and also the length of the dimethyl-substituted aliphatic dicarboxylate chain. Compound 3 also showed promise as a detector for nitrobenzene in solution.

2. Experimental

2.1. General considerations

Cadmium nitrate, dicarboxylic acids, and nitrobenzene were obtained commercially. The dipyridylamide ligands 3-pyridylmethylnicotinamide (3-pmna) and 4-pyridylmethylisonicotinamide (4-pmina) were synthesized by adaptation of the literature procedure for the preparation of 4-pyridylisonicotinamide, using 3-picolylamine and 4-picolylamine as the respective precursors, with nicotinoyl chloride hydrochloride employed for the synthesis of 3-pmna [19]. Water was deionized above 3 MΩ–cm in-house. Elemental analysis was carried out using a Perkin Elmer 2400 Series II CHNS/O Analyzer. IR spectra were recorded on powdered samples using a Perkin Elmer Spectrum One instrument. The luminescence spectra of 1–4 were obtained with a Hitachi F-4500 Fluorescence Spectrometer on solid crystalline samples anchored to quartz microscope slides with Rexon Corporation RX-22P ultraviolet-transparent epoxy adhesive. Thermogravimetric analysis was performed on a TA Instruments Q50 thermal analyzer under flowing N2. Topological analysis of coordination polymer networks was carried out using topos software [20].

2.6. Nitroaromatics detection studies for 3

A 5 mg sample of coordination polymer 3 was suspended in 5 mL ethanol in a volumetric flask, with immersion in an ultrasonic bath for 5 min to ensure an even dispersion. The fluorescence spectrum was recorded with an excitation wavelength of 300 nm. A stock solutions of nitrobenzene and benzene (1 × 10−4 M) in dimethyl sulfoxide were prepared. Aliquots of these stock solution were added sequentially to the coordination polymer ethanol suspension with sonication for 30 s. The emission spectra were measured before any analyte addition and after each aliquot of analyte solution.

3. X-ray crystallography

Diffraction data for 1–4 were collected on a Bruker-AXS SMART-CCD X-ray diffractometer using graphite-monochromated Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å). The data were processed via saint [21] and subjected to Lorentz effect, polarization effect and absorption corrections using sadabs [22] (1, 2, 4) or twinabs (3) [23]. The crystal of 3 was nonmerohedrally twinned. The twin law was ascertained using cellnow [24]; only the data from the major twin component was used for the structure solution and refinement. The structures were solved using direct methods with shelxtl [25] using the olex2 software suite [26]. The dmg ligands in 1 were disordered over two sets of positions and modeled successfully using partial occupancies. All non-hydrogen atoms were refined anisotropically. Hydrogen atoms bound to carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen were placed in calculated positions and refined isotropically with a riding model. Crystallographic details for 1–4 are given in Table 1.

4. Results and discussion

4.1. Synthesis and Infrared Spectroscopy

Compounds 1–4 were prepared cleanly by hydrothermal reaction of cadmium nitrate, the requisite dimethyl-substituted dicarboxylic acid, and either 3-pmna (1, 2, 4) or 4-pmina (3). No 3-pmna was included within the crystal structure of 4, although its presence is required to produce a clean sample of 4. It is plausible that 3-pmna, or its hydrolysis products, are acting as mineralizers. Infrared spectra were consistent with the structural components as determined by single-crystal X-ray diffraction. Broad, weak bands at ∼3300 cm−1 signify the O–H bonds in the water molecules of crystallization and 3-pmna and 4-pmina N–H bonds. Weaker, high energy bands at ∼3000–3200 cm−1 are attributed to C–H bond stretching modes in all cases. Asymmetric and symmetric C−O stretching modes of the dicarboxylate ligands are marked by strong bands at 1543 cm−1 and 1414 cm−1 (1), 1560 cm−1 and 1411 cm−1 (2), 1537 cm−1 and 1398 cm−1 (3), and 1514 cm−1 and 1395 cm−1 (4). Sharp, medium intensity bands in the range of ∼1600 cm−1 to ∼1200 cm−1 are attributed to the stretching modes of the pyridyl rings of the dipyridylamide ligands [27]. Features corresponding to pyridyl and aryl ring puckering are observed in the region between 800 cm−1 and 600 cm−1. Sharp, moderate intensity bands at 1655 cm−1, 1647 cm−1, and 1648 cm−1 denote the CO stretching modes dipyridylamide ligands for 1–3, respectively. There are no dipyridylamide ligands in 4, so such an amide CO band is absent.

4.2. Structural description of {[Cd(dmg)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·H2O}n (1)

The asymmetric unit of compound 1 contains a divalent cadmium atom, a disordered dmg ligand, a 3-pmna ligand, an aqua ligand, and one water molecule of crystallization. The cadmium atoms display a {CdO5N2} pentagonal bipyramidal geometry (Fig. 1), with the apical positions being occupied by pyridyl nitrogen donor atoms from two 3-pmna ligands. Chelating carboxylate groups from two dmg ligands and the bound water molecule fill the equatorial plane. Bond lengths and angles within the pentagonal bipyramidal environment are listed in Table 2.

Adjacent cadmium atoms are linked into [Cd(dmg)(H2O)]n coordination polymer chains by bis(chelating) dmg ligands; these chain subunits are oriented parallel to the a crystal direction with a Cd⋯Cd internuclear distance of 9.0025(8) Å. The major disordered component of the dmg ligand exhibits a twisted gauche-gauche conformation backbone (four-C atom torsion angles = 71.55° and 87.08°). In turn the chain subunits are conjoined into [Cd(dmg)(3-pmna)(H2O)]n coordination polymer layers (Fig. 2a) by pillaring twisted anti-conformation 3-pmna ligands (Ψ torsion angle = 121.88°). The dipyridylamide tethers span a Cd⋯Cd internuclear distance of 11.8970(11) Å, which represents the c lattice parameter. Rhomboid grid apertures with slightly pinched Cd⋯Cd⋯Cd angles of 81.86° and 98.14° are located within the (4,4) grid motifs. The unsubstituted analog of 1, {[Cd(glu)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·3H2O}n, manifests a rippled grid motif due to cis disposition of the dipyridylamide nitrogen donors, although it shares the same overall 4-connected (4,4) layer topology [15].

Neighboring [Cd(dmg)(3-pmna)(H2O)]n layers, which are aligned parallel to the ac crystal planes, aggregate into slab motifs (Fig. 2b) by hydrogen bonding between aqua ligands and 3-pmna carbonyl oxygen atoms (Table S1). The slab motifs stack along the b crystal direction by means of hydrogen bonding patterns situated between the N–H bonds of the 3-pmna ligands, the isolated water molecules of crystallization, and bound dmg carboxylate oxygen atoms (Fig. S1). The water molecules of crystallization occupy small pockets comprising 2.4% of the unit cell volume as computed by platon [28].

4.3. Structural description of {[Cd(dms)(3-pmna)(H2O)]·4.5H2O}n (2)

Similar to 1, the coordination environment is a {CdO5N2} pentagonal bipyramid (Fig. 3), albeit with cis disposed 3-pmna pyridyl nitrogen donor atoms in axial and equatorial positions. The other axial site is occupied by an aqua ligand. Chelating carboxylate groups from two dms ligands fill the remaining four equatorial coordination sites. Bis(chelating) dms ligands link cadmium atoms together to construct [Cd(dms)(H2O)]n coordination polymer chain motifs oriented parallel to the c crystal direction (Fig. 4a), with a Cd⋯Cd internuclear distance of 6.636(1) Å. The four-C atom backbones of the dms ligands adopt a gauche conformation (66.70° torsion angle). Pairs of parallel [Cd(dms)(H2O)]n chains are bridged by pairs of curled anti conformation 3-pmna ligands to form 1-D [Cd(dms)(3-pmna)(H2O)]n coordination polymer ribbons (Fig. 4b). The Ψ torsion angle within the 3-pmna ligands measures 83.0°; the dipyridylamide ligands span a Cd⋯Cd internuclear distance of 10.532(2) Å. The unsubstituted analog of 2, {[Cd2(suc)2(3-pmna)(H2O)2]·3H2O}n, possesses {Cd2O2} dimer-based [Cd(suc)]n layers pillared by 3-pmna ligands into a 5-connected sandwich motif with 4862 topology [15]. Therefore in the case of 2, the presence of dimethyl substituents results in a decrease in dimensionality from 2D to 1D.

Individual ribbons in 2 aggregate to six nearest neighbors by hydrogen bonding involving the numerous unligated disordered water molecules of crystallization (Fig. S2). One particular hydrogen bonding pathway exists between 3-pmna amide N–H groups, water molecules of crystallization, and bound dms carboxylate oxygen atoms (Table S1). The unligated water molecules occupy pockets situated between adjacent [Cd(dms)(3-pmna)(H2O)]n ribbons comprising 15.2% of the unit cell volume.

4.4. Structural description of {[Cd(dms)(4-pmina)]·2.5H2O}n (3)

The asymmetric unit of compound 3 contains a divalent cadmium atom, a dms ligand, a 4-pmina ligand, and net two and one half water molecules of crystallization. Cadmium atoms in 3 display a pentagonal bipyramidal {CdO5N2} coordination environment (Fig. 5), with the axial positions filled by pyridyl nitrogen donor atoms from two 4-pmina ligands. In the equatorial plane reside chelating carboxylate groups from two dms ligands and a carbonyl oxygen donor from another 4-pmina ligand. Water molecules of crystallization are located in channels within the system of interpenetrated nets situated along the a crystal direction, anchored to the coordination polymer frameworks by accepting hydrogen bonds from 4-pmina N–H bonds and by donating hydrogen bonds to ligated dms carboxylate oxygen atoms (Table S1). The unligated water molecules form hydrogen-bonded tapes with alternating cyclic tetramer and hexamer units (Fig. 9), with a T4(0)6(0) classification denoting no sharing of molecules between the ring motifs [29]. According to platon, the water-bearing cavities comprise 11.6% of the unit cell volume.

The cadmium atoms adopt a distorted {CdO8} square antiprismatic coordination environment, comprised of chelating carboxylate groups from two dms ligands, single oxygen atom donors from an additional three dms ligands, and the bound water molecule (Fig. 10a). The dms ligands in 4 adopt a complicated exotetradentate μ4-κ4-O,O′:O′,O″:O″,O″′:O″ binding mode, in which one oxygen atom from each carboxylate group binds only to one cadmium, a third oxygen atom bridges two cadmium atoms, and the fourth oxygen atom bridges three cadmium atoms. These are wrapped by the four full dms ligands that provide the triply bridging dms oxygen atom donors, to construct a larger {Cd4(dms)4(H2O)4} cluster motif (Fig. 10c). Within the clusters, the Cd⋯Cd distances measure 3.931 Å across two opposite cuboid faces, and 3.791 Å across the other four contiguous cuboid faces. Four of the cuboid edges are bridged by the other oxygen atoms of the dms carboxylate groups that bear the triply bridging oxygen donor atoms. Four other cuboid edges are bridged by dms ligands in a 1,4-chelating fashion. Adjacent {Cd4(dms)4(H2O)4} units are connected to four others in a pseudo-tetrahedral arrangement by the full span of the dms ligands (Fig. 11), to construct a 3-D [Cd4(dms)4(H2O)4]n coordination polymer network (Fig. 12a). If individual {Cd4(dms)4(H2O)4} units are considered to be 4-connected tetrahedral nodes, the coordination polymer net simplifies to a commonly encountered 66 diamondoid network (Fig. 12b). Isolated water molecules of crystallization occupy narrow incipient channels throughout the 3-D net comprising 8.4% of the unit cell volume, held to the coordination polymer backbone by weak hydrogen bonding to the bound water molecules (Table S1).

4.6. Luminescence spectra

Solid samples of 1–4 were irradiated with ultraviolet light to examine their potential for photoluminescent behavior. Due to the lack of aromatic ligands in 4, no emissive behavior was observed. The excitation spectra for 1–3 were recorded by monitoring emission at 400 nm, revealing maximum excitation wavelengths of 245 nm, 250 nm, and 253 nm, respectively. Upon excitation at these wavelengths, blue-violet visible light emission was observed in 1–3. The emission maxima for 1–3 were 394 nm, 394 nm, and 407 nm, respectively (Fig. 13). When the pure 3-pmna ligand was irradiated at 235 nm, a weak emission with broad spectral profile centered at 305 nm was observed. For 4-pmina these values were 328 nm and 527 nm, respectively. The fluorescent properties of 1–3 are thus ascribed to π–π∗ molecular orbital transitions within the aromatic ring systems of the dipyridylamide ligands, with a blue-shift caused by donation of the pyridyl nitrogen atoms to cadmium [30].

4.7. Nitrobenzene sensing

Compound 3 shows capability for detection of nitrobenzene (Fig. 14) in ethanol suspension. Addition of small aliquots of nitrobenzene dissolved in DMSO (1 × 10−4 M) resulted in decrease of the coordination polymer luminescence intensity upon excitation with ultraviolet light (λex = 335 nm). This loss of emission signal is attributed to charge transfer from the excited states of the ligand molecular orbitals within the coordination polymer, to those of the adsorbed electron-withdrawing nitrobenzene analyte [5]. The intensity factor (Io − I)/Io (Io = maximum intensity in the absence of analyte) reaches a level about 32% of the initial Io value after 100 μL of the stock analyte solution was added (Fig. S3). As a control experiment, aliquots of a benzene solution in DMSO (1 × 10−4 M) were added to an ethanol suspensions of 3. No decrease in luminescence intensity was observed. Thus, it is averred that the nitro group of the analyte is key for luminescence diminishing within coordination polymer 3.

4.8. Thermal properties

According to thermogravimetric analysis, compound 1 underwent dehydration between 100 and 150 °C, with a mass loss of 7.5% corresponding to ejection of all bound and unbound water molecules (6.9% calc’d). The mass remained stable until 250 °C, whereupon ligand ejection occurred. Compound 2 underwent loss of unbound water between 20 and 70 °C, with a mass loss of 14.0% matching well with the predicted value of 14.3%. The bound water molecules were lost above 100 °C, and ligand ejection occurred above 270 °C. Compound 3 lost its unbound water molecules between 40 and 250 °C, with a mass loss of 7.0%, matching extremely well with the predicted value of 7.1%. Ligand removal occurred above 250 °C. Compound 4 was dehydrated between 140 and 180 °C, with a mass loss of 8.0% matching very closely with the predicted value of 8.1%. The mass of this 3-D phase remained stable until 375 °C. TGA traces for 1–4 are shown in Figs. S4–S7, respectively.

5. Conclusions

The length of an aliphatic dicarboxylate ligand with two methyl group substituents played a large role in topology and dimensionality in a pair of cadmium coordination polymers containing the seldom used 3-pyridylmethylnicotinamide ligand. Altering the nitrogen donor disposition within the dipyridylamide ligand resulted in a shift from a 1-D ribbon topology to a new binodal 3D network, facilitated by amide oxygen atom binding to cadmium. A higher temperature synthetic regime resulted in a dipyridylamide-free tetranuclear cuboid cluster based cadmium dimethylsuccinate 3-D coordination polymer. While all materials containing a dipyridylamide ligand in this study manifested luminescent behavior upon ultraviolet irradiation in the solid state, the cadmium dimethylsuccinate coordination polymer 3 with the longer spanning 4-pyridylmethylisonicotinamide ligand also displayed luminescent quenching in the presence of nitrobenzene. Compound 3 therefore may have potential as an explosives trace residue sensor.
