An unusual eight-connected self-penetrating ilc net constructed by dinuclear lanthanide building units†

Jian-Wen Cheng a, Shou-Tian Zheng a, Wei Liu c and Guo-Yu Yang *ab
aState Key Laboratory of Structural Chemistry, Fujian Institute of Research on the Structure of Matter, Graduate School of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, Fuzhou, Fujian 350002, China. E-mail: ygy@fjirsm.ac.cn; Fax: (+86) 591-8371-0051
bState Key Laboratory of Rare Earth Materials and Applications, Peking University, Beijing, 100871, China
cCollege of Material Science and Engineering, Shandong University of Technology, Zibo, Shandong 255049, China

Received 1st October 2007 , Accepted 26th February 2008

First published on 26th March 2008

Hydrothermal reactions of Ln2(C2O4)3·9 H2O and CuCl2·2 H2O with pyridine-2,3-dicarboxylic acid (H2pdc) in water in the presence of acetic acid lead to two novel three-dimensional lanthanide(III)–copper(I) heterometallic compounds: LnCu(na)2(C2O4)·1.5 H2O (Ln = La1, Ce2; na = nicotinic acid). Both compounds are isostructural and contain two distinct units of 2D Ln-organic layers and nonlinear [Cu(na)2]−, the nonlinear [Cu(na)2]− act as pillars and further link the Ln-organic layers, resulting in two heterometallic metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) with two distinct types of channels along the a and c axes. Further structural analysis reveals that 1 is a scarcely reported eight-connected self-penetrating ilc net with dinuclear {Ln2} subunits as secondary building units. Furthermore, the IR, TGA, PXRD properties were also studied.

Introduction

The crystal engineering of MOFs is of great current interest owing to their potential applications and intriguing network topologies.1,2 Consequently, a variety of coordination polymers with interesting and complicated architectures and topologies have been synthesized successfully.3 The “network topological approach” is an important and essential aspect of the analysis, comparison, and design of MOFs by reducing multidimensional structures to simple node-and-connection reference nets.4 Wells pioneered this approach and classified a great number of natural prototypes in metallic or binary inorganic solids.5 Up to now, a number of uninodal network such as SrSi2 (srs), ThSi2 (ths), diamond (dia), NbO (nbo), quartz (qtz), sodalite (sod), CdSO4 (cds), α-Po (pcu) have been reported in MOFs.6 In these mimic and many other uninodal 3D MOFs without mineral prototypes, the majority are based on three-, four- and six-connected topologies,1–6 Examples of five-, seven-, and eight-connected MOFs are relatively scarce,7–9 and MOFs with twelve-connected MOFs are extremely rare due to the limited coordination numbers of metal centers and steric hindrance issues of the organic ligands.10 To design such high-connected MOFs, the concept of secondary building units (SBUs) is being applied with eminent success because these SBUs generally possess larger sizes, more coordination sites, and smaller steric hindrance for ligands.8–11 Among the numerous topological nets, “simple, high-symmetry” structures are the most important targets for synthesis.3b,6a

The lanthanide(Ln)-transition-metal(TM)-organic frameworks have been of great interest for their fascinating structural topologies and potential applications.12 Recently, we initiated a synthetic attempt to make high-connected heterometallic Ln-TM nets. Our synthetic strategy is based on the following considerations: (1) Ln ions have been used as high-connected nodes to construct novel MOFs for their high coordination numbers;9 (2) the high-connected nets tend to get denser, as with increasing connectivity there will be more nearest neighbours for each node and more links traversing space, a remedy for this could be to introduce a TM node with lower connectivity.13 Therefore, it should be a rational way to construct high-connected nets by incorporation of Ln nodes in heterometallic compounds. Herein, we report two novel 3D hetero-Ln-TM MOFs, LnCu(na)2(C2O4)·1.5 H2O (Ln = La1, Ce2; na = nicotinic acid), utilizing dinuclear Ln cores as nodes, and further linked by [Cu(na)2]− and oxalate ligands to give an uninodal eight-connected net, which defines a rare case of a self-penetrating ilc net.

Results and discussion

Light yellow crystals of 1 and 2 were obtained by hydrothermal reaction of Ln2(C2O4)3·9 H2O, CuCl2·2 H2O, 2,3-pyridine-carbocylic acid (H2pdc) in water in the presence of acetic acid. Compounds 1 and 2 are isomorphous, therefore, only the structure of 1 will be discussed in detail as a representative. The asymmetric unit of 1 contains one La3+, one Cu+, two na− ligands with different coordination modes and two half oxalate ligands with the same coordination mode (Scheme 1). La is nine-coordinated with tricapped trigonal prism configurations: five carboxylate atoms (OCOO) from four na− ligands and four OCOO from two oxalate ligands (Fig. S1, ESI).† The La–O bond lengths range from 2.444(2) to 2.994(3) Å. Interestingly, decarboxylation occurred in the ortho position and pdc2− was transformed into na− under hydrothermal conditions.14 Compound 1 could also be obtained by using nicotinic acid with Cu(I) or Cu(II) salts instead. Two crystallographically equivalent La ions are coordinated by four na− and four oxalate ligands to give a dimer [La2(na)4(C2O4)4] ({La2}) fragment, in which the La⋯La distance is 4.218(1) Å; each dimer {La2} unit is further linked by oxalate ligands to give a 2D layer in the ac plane (Fig. 1,Fig. 2b).

The Cu center has a nonlinear coordination environment made up of two N atoms from two bridging na− ligands with Cu–N distance varying from 1.922(3) to 1.926(3) Å, and N–Cu–N angles of 153.17(13)° (Fig. 2a). In addition to two strong Cu–N links, there is one weaker interaction to symmetry related O5 with Cu–O distances of 2.251(2) Å. Although the starting materials are copper(II) salts, the Cu center has an oxidation state of +1, attributable to a reduction reaction under hydrothermal conditions. The linkage between 2D Ln-organic layers and [Cu(na)2]− pillars gives rise to a pillared-layer framework having two types of channels along the a and c axes (Fig. 3). The guest water chains occupy the void of the interlamellar region and interact with the framework through hydrogen bonds (Fig. S2, ESI).† PLATON calculated a suggested solvent- accessible volume of about 227.1 A3 by excluding the guest water.


Fig. 3 The framework of 1 showing two types of channels along (a) the a and (b) the c axis, respectively.

As shown in Fig. 4, each dinuclear {La2} subunit is linked to eight nearest {La2} cores through four [Cu(na)2]− and four oxalate ligands. Thus, each {La2} core could be considered as an eight-connected node, which is further linked to eight nearest neighbors with distances of 9.382(1)–13.946(6) Å through eight bridging liangds, the overall 3D framework can be rationalized as a uninodal eight-connected net with Schläfli symbol (424·5·63), which is completely different from that of the well-known eight-connected body centred cubic lattice (bcu) with (424·64) topology. The vertex symbol calculation using OLEX15 gives (4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4.4. 4.4.56.*.*.*) for the eight-connected {La2} node. The resulting uninodal eight-connected topology is the rare case of an ilc net (a nomenclature proposed by O'Keeffe et al).16 To date, MOFs with such connectivities are extremely rare, the only reported MOFs with ilc topologies adopt pentanuclear zinc clusters as nodes.8a The ilc net reported herein represents a good example of using Ln based SBUs to construct high-connected intriguing topologies.


Fig. 4 (a) The linkage of dinuclear lanthanide core (purple) with eight adjacent lanthanide cores (green). (b) The topology of eight-connected dinuclear SBU represented as an eight-connected node (green). (c) Schematic representation of the eight-connected non-bcu topology of 1.

According to the new approaches to the analysis of highly connected frameworks based on the visualization of the structures as combinations of interconnected layered 2-D sheets or subnet tectons proposed by Hill et al,3c the structure of 1 can be viewed as the parallel (4,4) nets are cross-linked by zigzag chains in the two adjacent layers, the zigzag chains cross the diagonal of two neighboring windows (Fig. 5). Further analysis indicates that four [Cu(na)2]− and two oxalate ligand connectors link dinuclear {La2} SBUs into two identical interpreting six-connected α-Po-type structures, the two α-Po nets are cross-linked by other two oxalate connectors to form a novel eight-connected 3-D MOF (Fig. 6a). According to the self-penetrating topological definition, the two shortest four-member cycles (pink and yellow four-member cycles) in 1 are catenated (Fig. 6b), therefore, the topological structure of 1 can be classified as an eight-connected self-penetrating network (Fig. 6).

Fig. 6 (a) The topological structure of 1 can be viewed as two identical interpenetrating α-Po-type nets (yellow and purple) cross-linked by oxalate connectors (blue), and each green node represents one dinuclear Ln cores cluster. (b) The detailed catenation of a four-membered ring (purple) with two others (yellow) in 1.

Owing to the similarity of the structures of 1 and 2, compounds 1 were selected to examine thermal stability. The thermal stability of 1 were examined by the TG analysis in N2 atmosphere from 30 to 1000 °C and powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD). Compound 1 undergoes two steps of weight loss. The guest water molecules were gradually lost in the temperature before 200 °C (calcd/found: 4.8/4.1%). Above 320 °C, the weight loss is due to the decomposition of the organic ligands and the collapse of the whole framework (Fig. S3, ESI).† After evaporation of solvent waters, the experimental PXRD patterns of dehydrated 1 correspond well to the simulated PXRD patterns of 1, indicating that the crystal lattice remains intact after the guest water molecules have been removed (Fig. 7).


Fig. 7 Simulated, experimental and dehydrated PXRD patterns of 1, and experimental PXRD patterns of 2.

The IR spectra of 1 and 2 are similar. The characteristic features of na− and oxalate ligands dominate the IR spectrum. The strong and broad absorption bands in the range 3000–3700 cm −1 in 1 and 2 are assigned as characteristic peaks of OH vibration. The strong vibrations appearing around 1611 and 1402 cm−1 corresponding to the asymmetric and symmetric stretching vibra-tions of the carboxylate group, respectively. The absence of strong bands ranging from 1690 to 1730 cm−1 indicates that the ligands are deprotonated (Fig. S4, ESI)† (Table 1).

Conclusions

In summary, two novel 3-D heterometallic Ln-Cu MOFs have been synthesized by incorporating dinuclear {Ln2} cores and copper centers in the framework. The systematic synthetic procedure has been well established. The {Ln2} act as eight-connected nodes giving an unusual self-penetrating ilc net. This work not only provides an intriguing example of topology, but also confirms the significant potential of constructing new highly connected frameworks using Ln based SBUs, work is continuing in this area.

Experimental

Materials and general methods

All chemicals were purchased commercially and used without further purification. Elemental analyses for C, H, and N were performed on a Vario EL III elemental analyzer. The FT-IR spectra (KBr pellets) were recorded on an ABB Bomen MB 102 spectrometer, Thermogravimetric analyses were performed on a Mettler TGA/SDTA 851e analyzer with a heating rate of 10 °C min−1 under N2 atmosphere. Powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD) data were obtained using a Philips X'Pert-MPD diffractometer with Cu Kα radiation (λ = 1.54056 Å).

Synthesis of 1,2: A mixture of La2(C2O4)3·9 H2O (0.298 g), CuCl2·2 H2O (0.034 g), H2pdc (0.167 g), H2O (10 ml), and CH3COOH (0.5 mmol) was sealed in a 30 ml Teflon-lined bomb at 170 °C for 7 d, then cooled to room temperature. Light prismatic crystals of 1 were obtained (yield: 39% based on CuCl2·2 H2O). Elemental analysis (%) calcd (found) for 1: C, 29.94 (29.64); H, 1.98 (2.36); N, 4.99 (4.75). Compound Ce analogue 2 was also prepared following the preceding procedure, the same PXRD and IR spectra prove that this compound is analogous to compound 1 (Fig. 7, S4). IR spectrum (KBr pellet, ν/cm−1) for 1: 3450(vs), 1611(s), 1568(w), 1402(m), 1316(m), 794(w), 758(w); for 2: 3451(vs), 1612(s), 1567(w), 1401(m), 1317(m), 794(w), 758(w).

X-Ray crystallography

The intensity data were collected on a Rigaku Mercury CCD diffractometer with graphite-monochromated Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at room temperature. All absorption corrections were performed using the SADABS program. The structures were solved by direct methods and refined by full-matrix least squares on F2 with the SHELXTL-97 program package. The H atoms of organic ligands were geometrically placed and refined using a riding model. However, the H atoms of water molecules in 1 have not been included in the final refinement. All non-H atoms were refined anisotropically. CCDC 662230 for 1 contain the crystallographic data in CIF format. For crystallographic data in CIF or other electronic format see DOI: 10.1039/b715058j.
