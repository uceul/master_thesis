    Abstract

    This paper presents four interesting entanglement systems, including one self-penetrating and three interpenetrating frameworks, namely, [Ni3(L)3(BTC)2·2H2O]·6H2O (1), [Mn(L)(HBTC)] (2), [Ni2(L)2(BDC)2·2H2O]·H2O (3), and [Mn4(L)4(BDC)4]·H2O (4) (H3BTC = 1,3,5-benzenetricarboxylic acid and H2BDC = 1,4-benzenedicarboxylic acid), based on a rigid 1,4-bis(1-imidazolyl)benzene (L) ligand. Compound 1 is the first example of a (3,4)-connected self-penetrating three-dimensional (3D) polymer containing two types of catenated rings with the unprecedented (6·82)2(62·84)2(64·82) topology. Compound 3 has self-assembled into a 5-fold interpenetrating 3D structure with diamond nets, while both compounds 2 and 4 display 2-fold interpenetrating 3D structures with α-Po structural topology.

    Synopsis

    Four entanglement systems based on the connectivity coeffect between the aromatic carboxylate anions and the rigid 1,4-bis(1-imidazolyl)benzene (L) ligand have been successfully isolated. One is a (3,4)-connected self-penetrating 3D polymer containing two types of catenated rings with unprecedented (6·82)2(62·84)2(64·82) topology, one is a typical 5-fold interpenetrating 3D diamondoid network, and the other two are 2-fold interpenetrating 3D α-Po networks.

    Metal−organic frameworks (MOFs) exhibiting entangled architectures are of great interest because of their intriguing aesthetic structures and topological features as well as their promising applications as functional materials.(1, 2) One of the common phenomena related to the entanglement is interpenetration. Conceptually, interpenetrating networks can be described as a number of individual nets participating in interpenetration with each other. There are some comprehensive reviews by Robson, Batten, and Ciani’s group of interpenetration, which has been the most investigated type of entanglement.(3, 4) However, self-penetrating structures are single nets having the peculiarity that the smallest topological rings are catenated by other rings belonging to the same net. On the basis of a query of the Scifinder (2006) program with the self-penetration, self-penetrated, or self-penetrating topical research and several reviewers in this field,(3b, 4a, 5-7) we find that there are at most 30 three-dimensional (3D) polymers with such self-penetrating motifs. Further analysis indicates that most of them are built on 4-, 5-, 6-, 8-, (3,5)-, or (3,6)-connected nodes; however, only one actual (3,4)-connected self-penetrating 3D net with the (63)2(64·82)2(62·84) topology notation has been observed in MOFs reported by us.(8) Therefore, the study of self-penetrating networks is still a challenging issue in coordination chemistry.
    Up to now, a large number of beautiful MOFs of ingenious design based on flexible bis(imidazole) ligands have been crystallographically characterized by different teams, a series of outstanding examples of which are (N-im)2(CH2)n (n = 1−6) and bix [1,4-bis(imidazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene].(8-11) In particular, we have already reported the formation of six products with different topologies obtained from the reaction of metal cations and (N-im)2(CH2)n (n = 4 or 6) or bix.(8) They have proven to be good candidates for the organization of beautiful MOFs because of their length and flexibility, and a few interpenetrating or self-penetrating networks have been observed in the literature.(8-10) However, the study of the metal−rigid bis(imidazole) ligand system is less developed. By careful inspection of previous cases, we found that only two coordination polymers have been constructed by the rigid bis(imidazole) ligand, 1,4-bis(1-imidazolyl)benzene (L), and they exhibit simple topological features.(12) On the other hand, the multidentate O-donor organic aromatic carboxylate ligands, such as 1,4-benzenedicarboxylic acid (H2BDC) and 1,3,5-benzenetricarboxylic acid (H3BTC), have been extensively employed to construct carboxylate bridged coordination polymers exhibiting high porosity and topological diversity with different dimensions.(13)
    As extending our previous work, our synthetical strategy is to select rigid L as organic linkers in the presence of BTC3− and BDC2− anions as coligands. Thus, four novel polymers [Ni3(L)3(BTC)2·2H2O]·6H2O (1), [Mn(L)(HBTC)] (2), [Ni2(L)2(BDC)2·2H2O]·H2O (3), and [Mn4(L)4(BDC)4]·H2O (4) were successfully isolated. All compounds are characterized by elemental analysis and X-ray crystallography. The topological analysis suggests that they show four interesting entanglement systems, including one self-penetrating and three interpenetrating frameworks. Furthermore, their thermal properties are studied by thermogravimetric (TG) analysis.

    Materials and General Procedures

    Solvents and starting materials for synthesis were purchased commercially and used as received. The ligands L were prepared according to reported procedure.(14) Elemental analysis for C, H, and N was performed on a Perkin-Elmer 240 analyzer. The TG analysis was performed with a Shimadzu TGA-50H TG analyzer in the range of 25−800 °C under a nitrogen flow at a heating rate of 5 °C/min for all measurements.

    X-ray Crystallographic Measurements for 1−4

    Suitable single crystals of 1−4 were selected and mounted in air onto thin glass fibers. Accurate unit cell parameters were determined by a least-squares fit of 2θ values, and intensity data were measured on a rigaku r-axis rapid IP area detector with Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å) at room temperature. The intensities were corrected for Lorentz and polarization effects as well as for empirical absorption based on multiscan techniques; all structures were solved by direct methods and refined by full-matrix least-squares fitting on F2 by SHELX-97. All nonhydrogen atoms were refined with anisotropic thermal parameters.     Synthesis of [Ni3(L)3(BTC)2·2H2O]·6H2O (1)

    The mixture of NiSO4·6H2O (0.26 g, 1.0 mmol), H3BTC (0.21 g, 1.0 mmol), and L (0.21 g, 1.0 mmol) was dissolved in 8 mL of distilled water. The pH was adjusted to 6.0 by the addition of 1 M NaOH solution. Consequently, the resulting mixture was transferred and sealed in a 25 mL Teflon-lined stainless steel vessel, which was sealed and heated at 180 °C for 3 days, and then, the reaction system was cooled to room temperature. Green crystals were obtained; yield, 0.24 g; 52% (based on Ni). Elemental analysis (%) calcd for 1: C, 47.51; H, 3.84; N, 12.31. Found: C, 47.58; H, 3.78; N, 12.36.

    Synthesis of [Mn(L)(HBTC)] (2)

    The mixture of MnCl2·4H2O (0.2 g, 1.0 mmol), H3BTC (0.21 g, 1.0 mmol), and L (0.21 g, 1.0 mmol) was dissolved in 8 mL of distilled water. The pH was adjusted to 5.0 by the addition of 1 M NaOH solution. Consequently, the resulting mixture was transferred and sealed in a 25 mL Teflon-lined stainless steel vessel, which was sealed and heated at 180 °C for 3 days, and then, the reaction system was cooled to room temperature. Colorless crystals were obtained; yield, 0.20 g; 42% (based on Mn). Elemental analysis (%) calcd for 2: C, 53.29; H, 2.98; N, 11.84. Found: C, 53.35; H, 2.91; N, 11.80.

    Synthesis of [Ni2(L)2(BDC)2·2H2O]·H2O (3)

    The mixture of NiSO4·6H2O (0.26 g, 1.0 mmol), H2BDC (0.17 g, 1.0 mmol), and L (0.21 g, 1.0 mmol) was dissolved in 8 mL of distilled water. The pH was adjusted to 6.0 by addition of 1 M NaOH solution. Consequently, the resulting mixture was transferred and sealed in a 25 mL Teflon-lined stainless steel vessel, which was sealed and heated at 180 °C for 3 days, and then, the reaction system was cooled to room temperature. Green crystals were obtained; yield, 0.27 g; 58% (based on Ni). Elemental analysis (%) calcd for 3: C, 52.21; H, 3.72; N, 12.18. Found: C, 52.13; H, 3.79; N, 12.25.

    Synthesis of [Mn4(L)4(BDC)4]·H2O (4)

    The mixture of MnCl2·4H2O (0.2 g, 1.0 mmol), H2BDC (0.17 g, 1.0 mmol), and L (0.21 g, 1.0 mmol) was dissolved in 8 mL of distilled water.     Crystal Structure of [Ni3(L)3(BTC)2·2H2O]·6H2O (1)

    As shown in Figure 1a, there are two independent Ni(II) atoms: Ni1 is coordinated by three BTC3− oxygen atoms, two L nitrogen atoms, and one water molecule to give the NiO4N2 distorted octahedral geometry, and Ni2 also shows the NiO4N2 octahedral geometry but completed by four BTC3− oxygen atoms and two L nitrogen atoms. The Ni−O/N bong lengths are in the range of 2.037(3)−2.251(4) Å, comparable with those observed in other Ni-based complexes,(15) and the distance between the uncoordinated carboxylic oxygen atom and the Ni1 atoms is 3.323(1) Å. The completely deprotonated BTC3− ligands coordinate three Ni(II) atoms with the coordination mode of type I, as illustrated in Scheme 1.
    Furthermore, the Ni(II) ions are in turn connected by BTC3− and L to give rise to the complicated 3D framework with the void space occupied by free water molecules. The distances from 2.699 to 2.986 Å between the O atoms of the water molecules and the O atoms of BTC3− anions indicate the formation of O−H···O hydrogen bonds (Table S1 of the Supporting Information), although the hydrogen atoms of water molecules could not be found.(10d) These hydrogen bonds further stabilize the final framework. Better insight into such elegant frameworks can be accessed by the topology method. If the metal centers and BTC3− are viewed to be the 4-,3-connected nodes, respectively, such special framework features a trinodal (3,4)-connected net with (6·82)2(62·84)2(64·82) for (BTC)2(Ni1)2(Ni2) (Figure 1b). Particularly, in compound 1, there are links not only between the 3- and 4-connected nodes but also between the 4-connected nodes. This is different to the typical (3,4)-connected nets, such as Cu15Si4, boracite, Pt3O4, and the twisted boracite net.(1c, 16)
    Careful inspection of such frameworks suggests the self-penetration for it. As shown in Figure 1b, the catenated 6- and 8-rings are the shortest topological rings. Interestingly, there exist two types of self-penetrating motifs. One is two 6-rings interlocked with one 8-ring, and the other is two 6-rings interlocked with each other. Hence, polymer 1 is the (3,4)-connected self-penetrating 3D polymer containing two types of self-penetrating motifs with the unprecedented (6·82)2(62·84)2(64·82) topology. It is worthy of noticing that the topology of 1 completely differs from the first (3,4)-connected self-penetrating 3D polymer [Co3(bix)3(BTC)2(μ-H2O)3·2H2O] reported by us earlier.(8) Only two 8-rings are interlocked with each other in the latter.

    Crystal Structure of [Mn(L)(HBTC)] (2)

    Polymer 2 crystallizes in monoclinic space group of P21/c. Each Mn(II) ion in 2 exhibits a distorted octahedral environment, composed of four carboxylic O atoms from three HBTC2− anions [Mn−O = 2.1229(16)−2.4328(16) Å] and two N atoms from two L ligands [Mn−N = 2.265(2) Å], as shown in Figure 2a. The Mn−O/N distances fall in the normal range found in other Mn complexes.(17) Each uncompletely deprotonated HBTC2− ligand coordinates three Mn(II) centers (type II in Scheme 1) to form a one-dimensional (1D) ladderlike chain (Figure 2b). As show in Figure 2c, the combination of Mn(II), HBTC2−, and L generates a 3D framework. Further topological analysis suggests the α-Po framework for it, if we define the dinuclear Mn2 unit as a node.
    Of particular interest, the most striking feature of complex 2 is that a pair of identical 3D single nets is interlocked with each other, thus directly leading to the formation of a 2-fold interpenetrated 3D architecture (Figure 2d). Recently, a complete analysis of the 3D coordination networks shows that more than 50 interpenetrated pcu (α-Po) frames have been documented in the CSD database,(18) including 2-fold, 3-fold,(19) and 4-fold(20) interpenetration. In addition, several noninterpenetration motifs with α-Po topology have been reported to date.(21)

    Crystal Structure of [Ni2(L)2(BDC)2·2H2O]·H2O (3)

    As compared to polymer 1, the H3BTC ligands are replaced by H2BDC, and the resulting structure is a diamond framework. The Ni(II) ions in 3 hold the NiO4N2 distorted octahedral geometry, which is provided by three BDC2− oxygen atoms, two L nitrogen atoms, and one water molecule (Figure 3a). The Ni−O/N bond lengths are in the range of 2.030(2)−2.193(2) Å. The completely deprotonated BDC2− ligands adopt two types of coordination modes (Scheme 1, parts III and IV), namely, bi(monodentate) and bi(chelating) coordination modes.
    The extension of the structure into a 3D network is accomplished by connecting four linear ligands to the four-connected Ni(II) nodes, and the free water molecules occupies some void space. On the basis of the concept of chemical topology, the overall structure is a diamond framework containing large adamantanoid cages (Figure 3b).(22) As can be seen, the cages exhibit maximum dimensions (corresponding to the longest intracage Ni···Ni distances) of 13.579 Å × 11.370 Å × 10.738 Å. The potential voids are filled via mutual interpenetration of four independent equivalent frameworks, generating a 5-fold interpenetrating 3D architecture (Figure 3c). Although many diamond-related nets displaying various interpenetration modes ranging from 2- to 11-fold have been reported,(3b) the 5-fold interpenetrating MOFs in the presence of the mixed organic ligands with different lengths is relatively rare.(23) Further inspection of such literature indicates that such networks are constructed from the dicarboxylate and the dipyridyl with certain spacers, such as 1,2-bis(4-pyridyl)ethane (bpe). Thus, compound 3 is the first example of 5-fold interpenetrating diamond net based on bis(imidazole) ligands.

    Crystal Structure of [Mn4(L)4(BDC)4]·H2O (4)

    Single crystal structure analysis reveals that complex 4 also features a 2-fold 3D interpenetrated network with α-Po topology. As illustrated in Figure 4a, two kinds of Mn(II) centers are present in the asymmetric unit: One is five-coordinate with trigonal-bipyramidal geometry, and the other is six-coordinate with a distorted-octahedral geometry. The coordination sphere around the six-coordinate Mn1 cation is composed of four BDC2− oxygen atoms and two L nitrogen atoms. Each Mn2 cation is coordinated by three BDC2− oxygen atoms and two L nitrogen atoms. The Mn−O/N bond lengths vary from 2.074(5) to 2.445(5) Å, comparable with those in 2, and the distance between the uncoordinated carboxylic oxygen atoms and Mn atoms is 2.533(2) Å.
    The BDC2− anions possess two coordination modes of type V and VI (Scheme 1). The Mn ions are linked by the BDC2− anions of type V and L ligands to form 2D double layers (Figure 4b), which are further connected by the BDC2− anions of type VI to generate a 3D framework, and the free water molecules occupy some void space. As shown in Figure 4c, such a 3D framework is apparently different from compound 2. Although eight bridging ligands ligate the dinuclear metal unit, each dinuclear Mn2 unit is bridged to six nearest neighbors because two pairs of ligands L form a “double-bridge”. That is to say, the double layers are considered as single layers. Thus, on the basis of the concept of chemical topology, the overall structure also can be simplified to a primitive cubic (α-Po) network if we define the dinuclear Mn2 unit as a node. Moreover, two of these nets interpenetrate as those in compound 2 (Figure S1 of the Supporting Information).

    Thermal Analyses

    To estimate the stability of the coordination architectures, TG analysis experiments were carried out. Polymer 1 and polymer 3 lost the free and coordinated water molecules at 78−210 °C (calcd, 10.56%; exp., 10.42%) and at 62−176 °C (calcd, 5.83%; exp., 5.61%), respectively, while 4 releases the free water molecules at 68−100 °C (calcd, 1.03%; exp., 0.95%). The frameworks of these polymers can be stable up to about 400 °C for 1, 415 °C for 2, 355 °C for 3, and 320 °C for 4, and this indicates that the H3BTC-based frameworks own the more thermal stability than the H2BDC-based frameworks (Figure 5).

    In conclusion, we have successfully synthesized and characterized four entanglement systems, including one self-penetrating and three interpenetrating frameworks, based on the connectivity coeffect between the aromatic polycarboxylate anions and the rigid L ligand. The completely deprotonated BTC3− ligand affords a (3,4)-connected self-penetrating 3D polymer containing two types of catenated rings with the unprecedented (6·82)2(62·84)2(64·82) topology, whereas the BDC2− anions and uncompletely deprotonated HBTC2− anions yield a typical 5-fold interpenetrating 3D diamondoid topological network as well as two 2-fold interpenetrating 3D α-Po topological networks. This work will give impetus to the investigation of the coordination chemistry of rigid bis(imidazole) ligand. It is believed that more metal complexes containing such rigid ligands and aromatic polycarboxylate anions with interesting structures as well as physical properties will be synthesized in the future.

      Synthesis and characterization of the mixed ligand coordination polymer CPO-5
      Kongshaug, Kjell Ove; Fjellvag, Helmer
      Journal of Solid State Chemistry (2003), 175 (2), 182-187CODEN: JSSCBI; ISSN:0022-4596. (Elsevier Science)
      The synthesis and crystal structures of a novel coordination polymer and its high-temp. variant are described. The as-synthesized material (CPO-5-as), Zn(4,4'-bipyridine)(4,4'-biphenyldicarboxylate)·3H2O, crystallizes in the triclinic space group P‾1 with a 11.0197(2), b 14.2975(3), c 7.6586(1) Å, α 95.9760(9), β 108.026(1), γ 91.373(1)°. CPO-5-as is composed of tetrahedral zinc centers that are connected by the org. linkers to give five independent, interpenetrating diamond networks. In the structure, there is addnl. space for channels that are filled with three water mols. These water mols. can be removed, leading to an anhyd. variant at 130°. CPO-5-130, Zn(4,4'-bipyridine)(4,4'-biphenyldicarboxylate), crystallizes in the triclinic space group P‾1 with a 11.1844(6), b 14.0497(7), c 7.7198(3) Å, α 96.917(2), β 109.527(2), γ 89.115(3)°. The structure of the five interpenetrating networks is virtually unchanged after the dehydration resulting in CPO-5-130 being a porous structure with an estd. free vol. of 19.8%.
