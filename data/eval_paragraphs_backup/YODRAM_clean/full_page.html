A highly-connected metal–organic framework based on [Co2(μ2-OH2)] units and mononuclear Co centers with high gas uptake capacity†

Yao Kang and Fei Wang *
State Key Laboratory of Structural Chemistry, Fujian Institute of Research on the Structure of Matter, The Chinese Academy of Sciences, Fujian 350002, Fuzhou, PR China. E-mail: wangfei04@fjirsm.ac.cn

Received 20th November 2013 , Accepted 21st February 2014

First published on 21st February 2014

A porous 3D framework based on [Co2(μ2-OH2)] clusters and mononuclear Co centers, is presented. The [Co2(μ2-OH2)] units act as 7-connected and 8-connected nodes, respectively. The desolvated form of 1 shows high CO2 and H2 adsorption.

Porous metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) have attracted much attention for their fascinating architectures and potential applications in gas storage, separation, sensor and heterogeneous catalysis.1,2 The architectures of MOFs are mainly dependent on the assembly of metals and organic ligands. The common construction strategy for MOFs is that the mononuclear metal center is linked by an organic ligand to form the terminal compound. For example, as the subclass of MOFs, the ZIFs and BIFs are usually constructed by tetrahedral metal centers with imidazolate derivatives and boron-imidazolate derivatives ligands, respectively.3–10 In addition, many MOFs are formed from the secondary building units (SBUs).11 SBUs with predefined and rigid coordination geometries are generated in situ, which often provide a versatile pool of connectors for the rational synthesis of targeted MOFs.12 Although these two methods have proved useful in building new MOFs, so far efforts focusing on the unity of them to form MOFs have been scarce.13

Herein, by employing isonicotinic acid (Hint) to assemble with Co ions, we successfully synthesized a new porous MOF, Co9(int)18(μ2-OH2)4(H2O)2·(guest)(1). Both of the mononuclear Co and [Co2(μ2-OH2)] SBU were observed in 1, in which they act as 6-connected, 7-connected and 8-connected nodes, respectively. The desolvated form of 1 shows high CO2 and H2 adsorption and remarkable selectivity of CO2 over N2 at 273 K.

Compound 1 was solvothermally synthesized by employing Co(NO3)2·6H2O and isonicotinic acid (Hint) in N,N-dimethylformamide (DMF) and ethanol solvent.‡ X-ray single crystal structural determination reveals that compound 1 crystallizes in the monoclinic space group C2/m.§ In the structure of 1, all Co2+ sites display an octahedral coordination environment, although each of them has a different coordination atom from the ligand. Co1 is coordinated by two O atoms from two int ligands and four N atoms from four int ligands. Co2 is coordinated by four O atoms from four int ligands, one coordinated water and one N atom from one int ligand. Co3 is coordinated by two O atoms from two int ligands, two N atoms from two int ligands, and two coordinated water molecules. Co4 is coordinated by three O atoms from three int ligands, two N atoms from two int ligands, and one water molecule. According to the different coordination modes, five int ligands are classified into the μ2-bridge and μ3-bridge types. One μ2-bridge water molecule bonds to Co2 and Co3 to form a dinuclear [Co2(OH2)(int)7(OH2)] SBU (Fig. 1a). Similarly, two Co4 centers bridge to form another dinuclear [Co2(OH2)(int)8] SBU (Fig. 1b). Such dinuclear SBUs and Co1 atoms are further linked by the int ligands to form a 3D framework with three kinds of 1D channel along the c axis (Fig. 1c–d). The channels show three different windows with sizes of ca. 15.0 Å × 14.0 Å, 6.0 Å × 6.0 Å, 5.3 Å × 5.3 Å, respectively (Fig. 1c). The aforementioned maximum window accommodates one Co atom and six int ligands (Fig. 1e).


Fig. 1 (a and b) Two different [Co2(μ2-OH2)] SBUs in 1; (c) space-filling mode of the 3D porous framework of 1 without Co(int)6; (d) 3D porous framework of 1; (e) the mononuclear Co1 in 1.

From the view of topology, two [Co2(μ2-OH2)] clusters act as 7-connected and 8-connected nodes, respectively, and each mononuclear Co atom acts as a 6-connected node. All int ligands act as linkers. The complex network of 1 can be simplified as a (6,7,8)-connected net with the Schläfli symbol of (32·411·56·62)2(32·418·54·64)2(34·44·54·63) (Fig. 2).14


Fig. 2 (a–c) The defined 6-c, 7-c, 8-c nodes of 1; (d) the 6-connected uninodal net without Co1; (e) the whole topological net of 1.

It is noteworthy, that the [Co2(OH2)] cluster reported here can act as a versatile SBU to build up new networks. Generally, limited by the coordination number and steric hindrance, small clusters often have a low number of connections, and high connectivity nodes (more than six) are often made up by bulk high-nuclear metal clusters (usually higher than hexanuclear).15 Although some high-nuclear clusters, such as [Co3O(μ3-OH)] and [Co4(μ3-OH)4], have been reported to construct extended coordination networks,16 it is the first case of the 8-connected net based on the above dinuclear SBUs.16a

It should be noted that a total of 45.8% of solvent accessible volume for 1 was found, filled by the disordered guest molecules. Though there were weak peaks with residual electron densities inside the channel, attempts to identify and further refine these peaks failed. The contribution of the disordered solvent molecules was subtracted from the reflection data by the SQUEEZE method17 as utilized in PLATON. An accurate determination of solvent can be obtained from the TGA curves and elemental analysis (EA) results, equal to 5.5 DMF guest molecules per Co9(int)18(H2O)6 unit. The TGA curve of 1 indicated that a weight loss of 12.46% occurred in the temperature range of 20–340 °C, corresponding to the loss of 5.5 DMF molecules (expected 12.45%). Beyond that temperature, the framework began to decompose (Fig. S2†).

A freshly prepared sample of 1 was soaked in ethanol at room temperature for several days to exchange guest DMF molecules. The TGA results demonstrated that the guest DMF molecules can be replaced by ethanol completely (Fig. S2†).

To investigate the permanent porosity of compound 1, gas sorption experiments evaluating N2, H2 and CO2 uptakes were measured at different temperatures in the ASAP (Accelerated Surface Area and Porosimetry) 2020 System. The ethanol-exchanged samples were activated at 353 K for 5 h to remove the guest molecules.

The results of the N2 sorption experiments at 77 K showed that they exhibit type I adsorption isotherm behavior (Fig. 3a, b). The Langmuir and BET surface areas were 835 m2 g−1 and 600 m2 g−1, respectively. A single data point at relative pressure 0.98 gives a pore volume of 0.314 cm3 g−1 by the Horvath–Kawazoe equation.


Fig. 3 The sorption isotherms of 1: the N2 adsorption (a) and desorption (b) at 77 K; the H2 adsorption (c) at 77 K; the H2 adsorption (d) and desorption (e) at 87 K; the CO2 adsorption (f) and desorption (g) at 273 K; the CO2 adsorption (h) and desorption (i) at 298 K; respectively.

Low-pressure H2 adsorption isotherms for 1a were measured up to 1 bar at 77 K and 87 K, respectively (Fig. 3c–e). The H2 uptake values were 1.40 wt% (157 cm3 g−1) at 77 K and 1.07 wt% (118 ml g−1) at 87 K, which surpass that of the most favorable zeolite ZSM-5 (0.7 wt%) and are close to those of recently reported ZIFs,1c and moderate compared with that of the [Co4(μ3-OH)4] cluster-based compound.16b At zero coverage, the enthalpy of H2 adsorption for 1a calculated by the virial equation from the adsorption isotherms measured at 77 and 87 K is 6.45 kJ mol−1. Both of the uptake values and the enthalpy of H2 adsorption of 1a are moderate compared with most of the MOFs.1b

The adsorption/desorption isotherms of CO2 were also measured at 273 K and room temperature. The CO2 uptake values were 85.5 cm3 g−1 (3.81 mmol g−1) at 273 K, and 58.6 cm3 g−1 (2.61 mmol g−1) at 298 K (Fig. 3), respectively, which are much higher than that of the [Co3O(μ3-OH)] cluster-based compound.16a The isosteric heats of adsorption (Qst) for 1a was calculated using the adsorption data collected at 273 K and 298 K. At zero coverage, the enthalpy of CO2 adsorption is 24.77 kJ mol−1 for 1a (Fig. S3†), which is moderate compared with other MOFs,1a but much higher than that of the [Co4(μ3-OH)4] cluster-based compound.16a

In conclusion, we have synthesized a porous 3D framework based on [Co2(μ2-OH2)] clusters and mononuclear Co centers. The [Co2(μ2-OH2)] SBUs act as 7-connected and 8-connected nodes, respectively. The desolvated form of 1 shows high CO2 and H2 adsorption.
